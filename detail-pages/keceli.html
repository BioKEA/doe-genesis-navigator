Introduction

I am interested in collaborative research at the intersection of high-performance computing, artificial intelligence, and state-of-the-art quantum chemistry to accelerate discovery in chemistry and materials science. My work focuses on developing and applying autonomous computational workflows that combine large-scale HPC resources, AI-driven decision making, and electronic structure methods to enable efficient, reliable, and scalable studies of complex chemical systems. I have experience working closely with experimental teams and on projects involving membranes, metal-organic frameworks, and other functional materials, as well as high-accuracy quantum chemistry calculations. I am particularly interested in advancing platforms we develeped such as ChemGraph and leveraging codes such as NWChemEx to build next-generation, AI-enabled research workflows for autonomous discovery.
