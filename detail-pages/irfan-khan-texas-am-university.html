    Introduction

    I am the director of the Clean and Resilient Energy Systems (CARES) Lab at Texas A&M University, where my research centers on the security, reliability, and resilient operation of cyber‑physical systems, such as energy networks, autonomous platforms, and industrial control infrastructure. My work integrates machine learning and AI with system and control principles to address real‑world challenges in dynamic, safety‑critical environments, with interests that include cybersecurity, intelligent automation, autonomous systems, and smart grid resiliency. In solving these problems, I and my group leverage machine learning approaches including methods for making data‑driven models more reliable and transparent such as explainable AI, and graph‑based representations to capture complex relationships in structured data, and we explore adaptive decision‑making strategies through reinforcement learning, as well as generative AI concepts where predictive and simulation capabilities can enhance system understanding and planning. These approaches help us build principled, robust, and deployable AI‑enabled systems that can operate reliably under uncertainty and evolving conditions.

    Relevant Projects

    1. Intelligent Cyber-Physical Systems for Autonomous Threat Detection

    Scope: Developed AI-driven intrusion detection systems for industrial and critical infrastructure networks, integrating multimodal machine learning, explainable AI, and graph neural networks. Focused on autonomous detection and mitigation of cyberattacks in real-time operational workflows.
    Achievements: Delivered systems capable of adaptive threat response with minimal human oversight. Key publications include AIS-NIDS, Explainable AI and Random Forest based Reliable IDS, and XG-NID, demonstrating robust, interpretable, and high-performance AI models for real-time decision-making.

    2. Reinforcement Learning for Adaptive UAV Security

    Scope: Applied reinforcement learning to develop autonomous detection and mitigation strategies for UAV GPS spoofing attacks. Focused on real-time adaptation in dynamic, adversarial environments.
    Achievements: Produced RL-based models that continuously learn and adjust to new threats, enabling closed-loop autonomous defense. Demonstrated effectiveness in IEEE Internet of Things Journal (2025) for UAV cybersecurity scenarios, providing a foundation for autonomous experimental control and feedback loops in lab environments.

    3. Edge AI and Real-Time Adaptive Systems

    Scope: Investigated deployment of AI and ML models on edge computing platforms for distributed cyber-physical systems. Emphasized reinforcement learning, generative AI, and multimodal data integration for real-time inference and decision-making.
    Achievements: Developed strategies for optimizing system performance and resilience in resource-constrained settings. Laid groundwork for AI-driven feedback, adaptive control, and high-throughput data curation, aligning with DOE autonomous laboratory objectives in robotics, real-time analysis, and edge intelligence.
