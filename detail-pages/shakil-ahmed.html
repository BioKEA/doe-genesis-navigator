Introduction

Alpha Health AI builds production-grade agentic AI infrastructure for workflow automation, multi-agent orchestration, and autonomous decision support. Our platform, AIDE (Ambient Intelligent Digital Employee), enables a 4-person company to operate a full multi-tenant SaaS ecosystem through AI agent orchestration, delivering work that is cheaper, faster, more reproducible, and fully traceable.

Our capabilities align with the Genesis Mission in two key areas:

Agentic AI and Workflow Orchestration: Our AIDE system uses multi-agent AI with specialized agents, human-in-the-loop approval workflows, and real-time data orchestration, the same patterns required for autonomous laboratory control and experiment automation.

Security-First Architecture: Built for government from day one, following NIST 800-53 controls with MFA, audit logging, and AWS GovCloud-compatible infrastructure, building toward FedRAMP authorization.

Our AI capabilities are FDA-validated: we won the 2025 PrecisionFDA Generative AI Community Challenge, ranking #1 across 39 submissions in both Low Code and Pro Code tiers.

We seek to partner with DOE National Laboratories and academic institutions that need scalable AI workflow infrastructure to accelerate scientific research and apply this capability to the Genesis Mission challenges.

Partnership Request & Project Idea

Partner Type Seeking
Academic institution
DOE National Lab
Capabilities Seeking
Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

We run our own AWS infrastructure but lack access to the kind of HPC resources needed for large-scale scientific AI model training. We are looking for access to DOE supercomputing clusters or national lab compute allocations where we can train and benchmark AI models against real scientific workloads. Our code is containerized and can run on standard HPC schedulers like Slurm. We can also contribute our own cloud resources for hybrid workloads.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We need access to curated scientific datasets relevant to the Genesis challenges we selected. For autonomous labs, we need experimental telemetry and instrument data. For data centers, we need real facility energy and utilization metrics. For scientific reasoning, we need structured research datasets in physics, materials science, or biotechnology. We handle data responsibly and can work within classified or restricted access frameworks.

Experimental or test facilities

We write the AI software but we do not have physical labs. We need a partner with experimental facilities where we can install and test our workflow orchestration system on real instruments. Ideally a lab running repetitive experiments that would benefit from AI-driven experiment design, scheduling, and real-time analysis. We can deploy our edge AI stack on standard Linux hardware connected to lab equipment.

Subject matter expertise

We know AI and software engineering, not nuclear physics or materials science. We need domain scientists who can define what good experiment outcomes look like, validate AI recommendations, and help us train specialized reasoning agents for their field. We work best embedded alongside researchers, not separated from them. Previous experience collaborating with software teams is a plus but not required.

Project Idea Summary

We build AI systems that coordinate multiple specialized agents with human oversight, and we want to apply that capability to accelerating scientific research workflows.

Our AIDE (Ambient Intelligent Digital Employee) platform already orchestrates complex multi-step workflows in production: planning, execution, verification, and coordination across domains. The architecture is domain-agnostic. Scientific research is the next application.

Specifically, we envision an AI agent system that can: 
- Automate literature review and hypothesis generation from research databases (PubMed, arXiv, Semantic Scholar) 
- Design and schedule experimental protocols based on prior results and domain constraints 
- Orchestrate multi-step computational workflows across HPC resources with real-time monitoring 
- Execute automated data analysis pipelines with built-in verification and reproducibility guarantees 
- Generate preliminary reports and identify follow-up experiments, closing the research loop

What we bring: a production-tested multi-agent orchestration engine, FDA-validated AI accuracy, and the engineering to adapt it to any scientific workflow.

What we seek: a National Lab or university team with an active R&D program, domain scientists willing to work alongside our engineers, and access to experimental data. We are flexible on team structure and open to subcontractor, consortium, or teaming roles.
