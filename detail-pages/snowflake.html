Introduction

Snowflake's AI Data Cloud delivers the secure, scalable, and collaborative platform that can turn the nation's vast scientific data assets into breakthrough discoveries. As a FedRAMP High and Moderate authorized platform, Snowflake is built on a Zero Trust architecture with role-based access controls and dynamic data masking — meeting the strictest government security standards. At the core of Snowflake's value is zero-copy data sharing, which enables agencies, national laboratories, universities, and private-sector partners to collaborate on live, governed datasets without duplicating or moving data. This eliminates barriers to interagency data integration and accelerates time-to-insight across complex, multi-stakeholder research ecosystems. Snowflake Cortex AI provides a powerful AI modeling and analysis layer directly within the secure environment. Cortex Analyst enables natural-language querying of complex structured data, Cortex Search powers discovery across vast document and policy archives, and Cortex Agents orchestrate multi-step AI workflows to automate research processes and test new hypotheses at scale. Snowflake's unified platform for data engineering, analytics, and AI development scales instantly for large-scale model training and simulation. Snowflake harnesses decades of federal scientific datasets to train foundation models, deploy AI agents, accelerating discovery across advanced manufacturing, biotechnology, energy, quantum science, and semiconductors.

AI models, tools, or software

Snowflake Cortex AI delivers a fully managed suite of AI services within the secure AI Data Cloud, with no data movement required. Cortex Analyst lets users query structured data using natural language — no SQL needed. Cortex Search enables semantic discovery across unstructured documents and archives. Cortex Agents coordinate these tools to automate complex, multi-step research workflows. The platform supports LLM fine-tuning on proprietary data, open-source model hosting, and vector embeddings — governed by enterprise security and FedRAMP authorization for public sector missions.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Snowflake provides a unified platform for the full AI and data science lifecycle. Snowpark enables data scientists to build ML pipelines in Python, Java, or Scala directly on governed data — no extraction needed. Snowflake ML offers end-to-end model development with feature engineering, training, and a model registry for versioning and deployment. Snowflake Notebooks delivers a collaborative environment for exploratory analysis. The platform natively scales compute for large dataset processing supports structured and unstructured data, and integrates with PyTorch, scikit-learn, and XGBoost.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Snowflake's AI Data Cloud runs on a cloud-native architecture that separates storage and compute, enabling instant elastic scaling without resource contention. Organizations can spin up dedicated compute clusters for large-scale model training, simulation, and inference — scaling to petabytes seamlessly and paying only for what they use. The platform operates across AWS, Azure, and GCP, supporting multi-cloud and cross-region deployments. Snowflake holds FedRamp High and Moderate with built-in replication, failover, and disaster recovery deliver mission-critical resilience and availability.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: Virginia State Police — AI-Powered Operational Analytics (2024) Snowflake partnered with the Virginia State Police to transform their data infrastructure and deploy AI-driven analytics across financial and operational workflows. The scope included consolidating disparate data silos from legacy spreadsheets and disconnected systems into a single, governed platform on the Snowflake AI Data Cloud. Snowflake Intelligence was deployed to enable non-technical staff across departments to query complex financial data using natural language and voice commands. Automated, rule-based reconciliation at scale replaced labor-intensive, line-by-line manual record processing. Semantic modeling categorized expenditure data and enabled predictive spending analysis. Key achievements included 80x faster data processing across all workflows, with critical record processing reduced from two weeks with six personnel to 45 minutes for 82,000 records. The platform delivered over 99% accuracy in predicting recurring costs and identified approximately $3 million in potential vendor negotiation savings.

Project 2: U.S. Census Bureau — Data Cloud Modernization (2023) Snowflake supported the U.S. Census Bureau in modernizing data processing and analytical capabilities for the American Community Survey, one of the nation's largest ongoing demographic research programs. The scope encompassed migrating legacy data infrastructure to the Snowflake Data Cloud, enabling scalable ingestion and processing of massive population datasets with secure, governed access controls. The platform unified structured and semi-structured data while supporting advanced analytical workloads critical to national statistical research. Key achievements included significantly accelerated processing of large-scale survey data, improved timeliness and accessibility of demographic and economic insights for policymakers and the public, and establishment of a modernized cloud architecture positioned to support future AI and machine learning workloads on sensitive federal datasets.

Project 3: Global Energy Company — Engineering Data Analytics at Scale (2024)  Snowflake was deployed by a major global energy company as its core data platform for engineering analytics and operational intelligence across facility operations. The scope included ingesting up to 1.5 million rows per second of real-time sensor, model, and application data from facility instrumentation using Snowpipe Streaming. The platform consolidated operational technology and information technology data into a unified analytical environment, eliminating the need for a separate time series database. Engineers and data scientists leverage the platform for equipment surveillance, energy efficiency modeling, facility production optimization, reliability predictions, condition monitoring, maintenance planning, and complex engineering calculations. The system serves approximately 350,000 time series queries per day supporting real-time operational decisions. Key achievements included real-time data availability for predictive maintenance and production optimization, elimination of legacy time series infrastructure, high-availability operations with reduced need for specialized database resources, and a scalable architecture supporting both ad-hoc engineering analysis and production-grade data science model deployment across global facilities.

Project Idea Summary

Snowflake proposes to serve as a secure, cloud-based data infrastructure and AI platform partner in support of the Genesis Mission's American Science and Security Platform. Our project focuses on unifying massive, siloed federal scientific datasets across DOE National Laboratories, academic institutions, and non-profit research organizations to accelerate AI-driven discovery in energy and advanced manufacturing.

The specific challenge we address is building a governed, scalable data collaboration layer that enables researchers across institutions to securely share, integrate, and analyze diverse scientific datasets — including sensor data, simulation outputs, and experimental results — without copying or moving data between environments. Today, critical research data remains fragmented across disconnected systems, slowing cross-institutional discovery and limiting the potential of AI foundation models.

Snowflake seeks partners with deep scientific domain expertise and research datasets in energy systems, advanced manufacturing, or critical materials. We seek collaborators who can validate AI model outputs, define scientifically meaningful evaluation criteria, and guide development of domain-specific foundation models. Together, we will combine Snowflake's FedRAMP-authorized AI Data Cloud with partner research capabilities to transform federal scientific investment into accelerated breakthroughs.
