    Introduction

    I am a Physics Professor specializing in Elementary Particle Detector R&D and Quantum Observables at Particle Colliders. As part of the Genesis Mission I plan to explore Detector R&D with LLM-driven AI agents, in collaboration with Berkeley Lab. This will be a pathfinder for autonomous particle-physics laboratories. We are looking to collaborate with AI companies and test out leading LLMs in this context.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    Experimental or test facilities

    We have a number of operational detectors of elementary particles that we are setting up for full remote-control and experimentation by AI agents.

    Subject matter expertise

    Our team develops detectors for elementary particles at high and low energies. We specialize in high-resolution ionization imaging in gaseous detector. This allows unique capabilities for detecting neutral particles, including known ones such as neutrons and neutrinos, and ones that may or may not exist, such as particulate dark matter.

    I also teach courses in quantum mechanics and particle physics, and am active in the emerging field of quantum observables at particle colliders, where we're attempting to observe or set limits on quantum decoherence in systems of entangled elementary particles.

    Relevant Projects

    I lead a research group in particle physics, focused on detector R&D and novel measurements in particle physics and fundamental quantum mechanics. Currently I supervise three postdocs, six graduate students, and two undergraduates in these areas. I work very closely with several other professors at the University of Hawaii Department of Physics and Astronomy, which has a strong experimental particle physics group.

    Some of my project experience from the last years are:

    1. I developed the BEAST TPC directional neutron detectors in use at the SuperKEKB particle accelerator in Japan. Development of these detector took about 3-4 years, and they have been in operation ever since, for about 10 years, providing directional measurements of neutrons that no other technology can provide. These detectors can operate in a mode where are sensitive even to single electrons, and are among the highest performance tracking detectors ever constructed. Roughly 15-20 people (postdocs, graduate students, undergraduates) have worked on this project. These detectors have numerous additional applications, see first two articles in my publication list.

    2. I initiated searches for quantum decoherence at the Belle II experiment, and a new technique to actually realize them, which utilizes the strongly focused particle beams at this facility. This work is currently being pursued by multiple university research teams, including my own, and the new technique is being adopted more widely by our 1000+ member collaboration of physicists. First publications are expected this year.

    3. I served on the executive board of the U.S. CPAD (Coordinating Panel for Advanced Detectors) from January 2024-2026. I still serve the a CPAD co-coordinator for gaseous detector R&D in the US. I help organize an annual U.S. detector R&D conference for particle physics and help the physics detector R&D community organize and grow.

    4. I am currently investigating how to best scale up high-resolution 3d particle tracking in gas to very large detectors. Or goal is lot operate large detectors that can count individual electrons in 3d, i.e. reach the fundamental performance limit for ionization detection. This may enable a new class of elementary particle detectors and experiments in the areas of dark matter and neutrino detection. Currently four scientists in my research group are working on this project. Our next target is a direction coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering experiment at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, which would be an experiment with order 30 scientists running for a decade.

    Project Idea Summary

    We plan to investigate autonomous experiments in elementary particle physics, run by partially or fully by AI. This would be a pathfinder for autonomous particle-physics laboratories.

    We already have a DOE national lab and university detector experts in our team. We have good machine learning and some agentic AI experience.  But we are missing a direct connection to a leading developer of AI tools. 

    We are seeking a company that can provide latest generation LLMs and agentic AI platforms, that we can use for high-level control of experimental strategies and evaluation of experimental results, as part of an agentic AI framework.

    We are currently discussing how to configure multi-agent research teams. We would benefit from collaboration with AI developers, who could advise us on specific options, and who might be interested in the observed performance and feasibility of agentic physics experiments in our laboratory.
