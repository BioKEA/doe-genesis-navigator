    Introduction

    Dr. Gutow's research focuses on deciding which robot should do which task and in what order those tasks should be accomplished, specifically when the risks and payoffs of doing those tasks are highly uncertain. In the context of Genesis this applies to automating scientific discovery via robotic experimentation and to the robust physical intelligence necessary for robots to operate in laboratories. We make these challenging optimization problems tractable and scalable by developing customized optimization and search routines that exploit the known physics and geometry of the problem.

    Partnership Request & Project Idea

    Partner Type Seeking
    For-profit company
    DOE National Lab
    Capabilities Seeking
    Datasets or data infrastructure

    Scientific datasets sampling parameter spaces in which additional experiments are expensive in terms of time or money (such as material composition/processing/properties, flight testing, geophysical data, atmospheric flows) but underlying physics constrains the variation of the measured value wrt the parameters.

    Experimental or test facilities

    Experimental/test capability to sample interesting scientific parameter spaces (e.g. material properties, geophysics, atmospheric measurement) that you are open to roboticizing for autonomous experimentation

    Project Idea Summary

    We are very good at turning word problems (eg. "what composition should I try next?" or "where should I launch my next radiosonde?") into math problems and devising tractable algorithms to solve those problems with guarantees on the solution quality. We can do so even when the problem is characterized by substantial uncertainty (aleatoric or epistemic). What we are looking for are partners who possess the accumulated data sets to inform the models over which we optimize, and who have the equipment to test out the recommendations our algorithms will make.
