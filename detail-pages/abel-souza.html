Introduction

I am Abel Souza, an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) Department at UC Santa Cruz.

My research centers on expanding the understanding of computing systems, from small embedded devices to large scale HPC and Cloud data centers, encompassing resource planning, scheduling, and the deployment of optimal strategies for distributed and data-intensive systems that support AI, Machine Learning, and web workloads. Recently, my work has expanded into sustainable, energy- and carbon-aware approaches to designing and managing cloud data centers and cyber physical systems.

A key theme in my research explores the efficient application of predictive analytics methods to process and model vast amounts of data, tailoring resource management holistically to applications. This involves determining the optimal quantity and type of resources to allocate, as well as the most effective deployment times and locations.

Today I supervise and co-advice seven PhD students, two M.S students, and several undergrad students who all help us work on great, exciting projects with great independence.

Prior to joining UCSC, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, and a PhD student in the Department of Computing Science at Umeå University (Sweden).

Relevant Projects

- NFS: CarbonFirst - Decarbonizing Cloud Computing (award 2105494)

The project’s foundation focuses on developing a software-defined energy virtualization layer that provides applications visibility into, and control of, their own energy and carbon usage. The project will then leverage this foundation to develop a number of higher-level systems abstractions for developing and supporting carbon-efficient applications at different geographical scales: local edge, regional edge, and global cloud. 

- Internet of Battlefield Things (IoBT) CRA

A collaborative research alliance between government, industry, and university researchers for the purposes of developing a fundamental understanding of a dynamic, goal-driven Internet of Military Things (IoMT) known as the Internet of Battlefield Things (IoBT).

Project Idea Summary

We would like to propose an intent-aware scientific AI framework for orchestrating complex workflows across energy-constrained, heterogeneous, and distributed HPC systems. Today’s orchestration models rely on imperative resource specifications, requiring users to predict low-level requirements in environments that are increasingly dynamic and opaque, leading to inefficient utilization and high energy costs. Our approach shifts to an intent-driven paradigm, where high-level scientific objectives, potentially expressed in natural language, are translated and parameterized into formal optimization constraints using NLP and large language models, and executed through adaptive, multi-objective scheduling. The key challenge we focus on is robustly inferring and operationalizing user intent under uncertainty, while jointly optimizing performance, energy, and resource heterogeneity in real time. We seek collaborators with expertise and access to (i) large-scale HPC systems, (ii) distributed or edge computing infrastructures, or (iii) scientific AI/ML for modeling and optimization, particularly those with access to real-world scientific workflows or testbeds to evaluate adaptive, orchestration strategies.
