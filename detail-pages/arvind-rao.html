    Introduction

    We are a multidisciplinary academic team at the University of Michigan working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, spatial biology, and multi-omics data science (including bulk RNAseq/scRNAseq, spatial transcriptomics, digital pathology), with a focus on enabling predictive and data-efficient discovery in complex biological systems. Our research integrates transcriptomics, spatial imaging, and computational modeling to better understand how cellular organization, regulatory programs, and environmental context jointly determine phenotype.

    As part of the GENESIS Mission, we aim to advance genotype-to-phenotype mapping by developing AI frameworks that can learn from heterogeneous, high-dimensional biological data and guide experimental design. Our approach emphasizes generative modeling, uncertainty quantification, and principled experiment selection, enabling tighter coupling between data and experimentation. We are particularly interested in leveraging DOE-relevant datasets and infrastructure to accelerate discovery in biology and related systems.

    Project Idea Summary

    We are developing AI methods to improve genotype-to-phenotype mapping by enabling predictive modeling and more efficient experimental design from heterogeneous biological data, aligned with GENESIS Topic 2B. Our goal is to demonstrate that AI can identify the most informative experiments, reducing the number of measurements needed to achieve reliable biological insight.

    We are seeking partners with expertise in experimental biology, or automated experimentation platforms, as well as access to relevant datasets or validation environments, to help test and deploy AI-guided experiment selection strategies.

    We  are able to work with a variety of imaging-based and multi-omic readouts, doing multi-scale, multi-modal bioinformatics across diverse data modalities (imaging, digital pathology, single-cell/bulk transcriptomics)
