Introduction

Joulios is an AI-powered Energy Decision Engine designed to transform how energy infrastructure is planned, analyzed, and deployed. In alignment with the Genesis Mission, Joulios addresses one of the most critical bottlenecks in scaling modern infrastructure: the complexity, fragmentation, and inefficiency of pre-construction decision-making.

Joulios integrates grid analysis, permitting intelligence, market forecasting, environmental constraints, and physics-based simulation into a unified, agent-driven platform. By automating and orchestrating these traditionally siloed workflows, Joulios enables faster, more reliable, and lower-risk development of energy and infrastructure projects.

Built as a decentralized, local-first system with a Modelica-based simulation core, Joulios can dynamically generate and evaluate scenarios across energy systems, data centers, buildings, and industrial environments. This allows stakeholders such as developers, utilities, and infrastructure operators to make high-confidence decisions in a fraction of the time and cost of legacy approaches.

Through its ability to accelerate infrastructure deployment, reduce project failure rates, and unlock scalable decision-making, Joulios directly supports the Genesis Mission’s goals of powering economic growth, modernizing infrastructure, and enabling resilient, intelligent systems at scale.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Joulios is an AI-native software platform featuring a multi-agent decision engine and a Modelica-based simulation core. It dynamically generates, runs, and refines energy system models, integrating grid, market, and environmental factors. Delivered as a modular SaaS platform with APIs, it replaces fragmented tools with a unified environment for planning, simulation, and infrastructure decision-making, supporting scalable deployment and seamless system integration.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Joulios applies advanced AI/ML and optimization techniques to energy system planning, including predictive modeling, constraint optimization, probabilistic risk analysis, and scenario generation. It uses agent-based workflows to automate complex decision chains, combining physics-based simulation with data-driven models to deliver high-confidence insights across grid, market, land assessment, permitting domains, and user custom input, improving speed, accuracy, and decision transparency.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Joulios integrates and structures diverse energy datasets including grid topology, interconnection queues, ISO market data, permitting/zoning records, environmental constraints (e.g., wetlands, species), land/soil data, and weather. It builds a unified, queryable data layer with geospatial indexing and scenario-ready pipelines, enabling rapid, multi-factor analysis for infrastructure siting, feasibility, and risk assessment, with continuous data ingestion, normalization, and versioned scenario tracking.

Project Idea Summary

Joulios is an AI-powered Energy Decision Engine designed to accelerate the planning and deployment of energy and infrastructure systems. Our focus aligns with multiple Genesis challenge areas, including Scaling the Grid to Power the Economy, Securing U.S. Leadership in Data Centers, Reimagining Construction & Buildings, and Autonomous Laboratories.

We address a critical bottleneck: the fragmentation and complexity of pre-construction decision-making across grid interconnection, permitting, market analysis, environmental constraints, and system design. Joulios unifies these domains through a multi-agent AI architecture and a physics-based unified model built on a Modelica simulation engine. This enables dynamic generation, execution, and iteration of infrastructure scenarios, allowing stakeholders to evaluate trade-offs, risks, and feasibility in a single environment with high speed and accuracy.

We recognize that the Scaling the Grid track requires applicants to be utilities or ISOs; therefore, we are actively seeking partners in those categories to collaboratively pursue that pathway. More broadly, we are looking for partners who can provide pilot environments, domain expertise, and access to operational or planning datasets (e.g., grid, permitting or facility-level data). Ideal collaborators include developers, utilities/ISOs, data center operators, and research institutions interested in co-developing and validating next-generation, AI-driven infrastructure planning systems.
