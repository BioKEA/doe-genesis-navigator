    Introduction

    I am an ecosystem modeler at San Diego State University, specializing in ecosystem ecology, biogeochemical cycling, and plant-microbe interactions. My research bridges empirical field measurements with advanced computational modeling and machine learning to decode complex ecosystem dynamics, with a focus on methane flux and the climate resilience of biological systems. 

    San Diego State University (SDSU) is widely recognized as a premier research institution within the 23-campus California State University (CSU) system. It is classified as a doctoral university with high research activity and is uniquely known for blending rigorous academic inquiry with highly applied, real-world impact. Environmental and Climate Sciences is one of SDSU’s standout research areas. The university hosts robust, internationally recognized programs in ecology, biogeochemistry, hydrology, and coastal and marine biology. Researchers frequently focus on climate resilience, complex land-surface modeling, trace-gas fluxes, and conservation.

    Project Idea Summary

    This project aims to develop an AI-driven approach to optimize the phenotype by manufacturing the genotype of a bioenergy crop. Currently, translating high-resolution genomic data (genotype) into predictive, ecosystem-level traits (phenotype) remains a fundamental challenge for ecosystem sciences. Traditional process-based ecosystem models struggle to capture the non-linear, stochastic dynamics of plant-microbe-environment interactions. To bridge this mechanistic gap, our project will strategically leverage diverse multimodal data to develop a novel AI-driven framework. We will integrate plant genomics, microbial multi-omics, and continuous environmental sensor data to map the intricate mechanisms by which genetic variation and root-microbiome interactions jointly govern feedstock resilience and ecosystem-level methane dynamics.
