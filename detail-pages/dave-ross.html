Introduction

I am the lead scientist for the Living Measurement Systems Foundry at NIST. Most of our work in the LMSF has focused on developing measurements and standards for large-scale, AI-ready biological data and machine-learning approaches to predict protein function using large-scale data.

For more details, see:

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our group is actively working on the development of standards for AI-ready biological data, with a focus on data quality assessment and reporting, and approaches to data interoperability for diverse protein types that will enable more efficient data generation and aggregation for generalized predictive model development.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We have generated several large, quantitative protein sequence-function datasets spanning multiple protein families and function types including allosteric transcription factors, proteases, and RNA polymerases. The datasets also span different methods for exploring protein sequence space including comprehensive mutagenesis, surveys of naturally occurring homologs, and AI-generated protein sequences.

Experimental or test facilities

We have developed a platform capability: growth-based quantitative sequence (GROQ-Seq), that enables the generation of quantitative protein sequence-function datasets at scale (>100,000 sequences per experiment). We have used GROQ-Seq to collect datasets for several different types of proteins, and are continuing to expand the capability to new protein types.

For more details see:

https://www.nist.gov/programs-projects/measurements-and-standards-ai-ready-biological-data-unlocking-protein-function
