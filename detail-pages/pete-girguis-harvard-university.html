    Introduction

    Peter Girguis is a Professor of Organismic and Evolutionary Biology at Harvard University. He earned his B.Sc. from UCLA and his Ph.D. from UC Santa Barbara. He was also a David Packard Postdoctoral Fellow at the Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute. He joined Harvard University in 2005, where he now resides as a Full Professor and the Co-Director of the Harvard Microbial Sciences Initiative. He is also an adjunct scientist at the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution’s Applied Ocean Physics and Engineering group.

    Broadly speaking, Professor Girguis studies physiological and biochemical processes in marine organisms, and how genotype maps to phenotype. Girguis is also known for developing novel “open-design” instruments such as underwater mass spectrometers and microbial samplers, and he strives to make these tools available to the broadest research community, with the goal of democratizing science around the world.  

    Professor Girguis is a Merck Co. Innovative Research Awardee, a recipient of the 2007 and 2011 Lindbergh Foundation Award for Science & Sustainability, the 2018 Lowell Thomas Award for groundbreaking advances in Marine Sciences, and a 2024 Capt. Don Walsh award for ocean science and technology.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    Experimental or test facilities

    My laboratory is one of the world's most advanced high-pressure high-temperature facilities for studying life in extreme environments. We are also world leaders in developing novel underwater instruments for measuring biological metabolic activity across a diversity of habitable conditions. Our assets include high-pressure high temperature vessels that can be used to study biotic and abiotic reactions and metabolic activity up to full open depth pressures. We have a state of the art mass spectrometric facility for measuring metabolic processes. 

    Subject matter expertise

    I am trained as a physiologist, biochemist and engineer. I am especially experienced in linking relationships between biological and abiotic processes, with skills in quantitatively allying genes and genomic information to metabolic activity.  I have deep expertise in aquatic ecosystems including coastal wetlands, methane seeps, hydrothermal vents, and the open ocean.  I am also proficient in working with computer scientists in developing models to ally biological/abiotic processes across scales.

    Other expertise

    I have founded two start-ups, one of which was acquired by a multi-national technology corporation. I am thus comfortable working with corporations including start-ups as well as larger entities (e.g. Battelle)

    Other resources/tools

    My lab is ~9,000 sq. ft. in total, including a 40,000 liter saltwater test tank, a 20 foot portable high-pressure laboratory, and access to a full machine shop, The ~1000 sq. ft. lab physiology lab including our mass spectrometric facility, a full molecular lab for biomolecular extractions etc, and access to Harvard's high-performance computing cluster.
