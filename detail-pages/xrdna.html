Introduction

XRDNA is a spatial intelligence and digital trust company developing foundational infrastructure for next-generation energy, logistics, and mission-critical systems. Our core technology—Elastic Vector Addressing (eVa) and Spheres of Influence (SoI)—creates a semantic mesh network (“Mission Fabric”) that embeds location, time, and operational authority directly into data and systems, enabling secure, real-time coordination across physical and digital environments.

We address a critical gap in today’s infrastructure: the inability to reliably coordinate distributed assets, AI agents, and human operators across dynamic, contested, or resource-constrained environments. XRDNA enables “correct-by-construction” orchestration, where systems understand context, enforce zero-trust constraints at the point of action, and maintain resilience without reliance on centralized control.

For the Genesis Mission, XRDNA brings a novel approach to integrating sensing, computation, and control across energy systems, supply chains, and hybrid edge environments. In partnership with research institutions such as the University of Utah, we are advancing deployable prototypes that fuse spatial computing, AI-driven reasoning, and secure distributed networking to support scalable, autonomous, and resilient infrastructure.

Relevant Projects

1. Spatial Intelligence & Mission Fabric Platform Development

XRDNA developed its core platform, Mission Fabric, a semantic mesh network that enables real-time coordination of distributed assets, AI agents, and human operators across physical and digital environments. Built on Elastic Vector Addressing (eVa) and Spheres of Influence (SoI), the platform encodes space, time, and authority directly into data structures, enabling secure, zero-trust interactions at the point of action.

Scope included system architecture, AI integration, and deployment of scalable prototypes supporting multi-domain operations, logistics, and infrastructure coordination. Outcomes include a patented addressing framework, working prototypes for real-time orchestration, and applicability across defense, energy, and industrial systems.

2. AI-Driven Spatial Orchestration & Decision Systems

XRDNA designed and implemented AI-enabled systems (including Neural Voyager and Map of Everything) that fuse spatial data, graph-based reasoning, and large language models to deliver real-time situational awareness and decision support. These systems enable users and autonomous agents to query, navigate, and act across complex environments with contextual understanding.

Scope included AI middleware development, data fusion pipelines, and integration with spatial addressing frameworks. Achievements include improved decision speed, reduced operator burden, and the ability to coordinate across fragmented data sources in real time, supporting applications in logistics, infrastructure, and mission planning.

3. Secure Distributed Systems & Zero-Trust Architecture

XRDNA developed a novel zero-trust framework (Spheres of Influence) that embeds access control, authority, and operational constraints directly into the data and addressing layer rather than relying on external policy systems. This enables secure collaboration across distributed, multi-party environments without centralized infrastructure.

Scope included cybersecurity architecture, identity and access modeling, and system-level integration with AI and distributed networks. Outcomes include a scalable approach to securing AI-driven workflows, enabling bounded autonomy, auditability, and resilience in contested or degraded environments—directly applicable to energy systems, data centers, and scientific computing infrastructure.

Project Idea Summary

XRDNA is developing a foundational coordination layer for next-generation energy, compute, and scientific systems through its Mission Fabric platform. Built on Elastic Vector Addressing (eVa) and Spheres of Influence (SoI), this approach embeds space, time, and operational authority directly into data, enabling secure, real-time orchestration across distributed infrastructure.

Our focus aligns with scaling the grid, securing data centers, and enabling AI-driven scientific workflows. Current systems cannot reliably coordinate assets, AI agents, and operators across dynamic environments without fragile centralized control. XRDNA enables “correct-by-construction” orchestration with contextual awareness, bounded autonomy, and embedded zero-trust constraints.

We seek partners to integrate and validate this capability in real-world environments. Academic institutions and DOE National Labs can support modeling, simulation, and testbeds, while for-profit partners can provide infrastructure integration points such as energy systems, HPC, and data centers.
