    Introduction

    Virta Laboratories builds production-grade and open-source AI and security tools for critical systems and infrastructure. Our passive network monitoring, anomaly detection, and digital twin platforms are validated in complex heterogeneous environments where operational continuity is non-negotiable — a methodology directly transferable to high-performance DOE research networks supporting AI-driven science.

    Relevant Projects

    PROJECT 1: ARPA-H UPGRADE Program — PATCH Project, Northeastern University (2024–Present) Virta Laboratories serves as commercialization partner on Northeastern University's PATCH Project, funded under ARPA-H's $50M+ UPGRADE program. The project constructs high-fidelity digital twins of hospital IT/OT network environments to enable autonomous detection and remediation of vulnerabilities across heterogeneous connected device populations. VirtaLabs contributes open-source passive monitoring infrastructure, working alongside clinical partners including Michigan Medicine, Massachusetts General Hospital, Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center, and UC San Diego. The core technical challenge — maintaining network operational continuity while monitoring and modeling complex heterogeneous device populations at institutional scale — is structurally identical to Focus Area 11B: maintaining high-performance scientific workflow execution while monitoring, correlating, and predicting network behavior across multi-facility DOE research environments. 

    PROJECT 2: NSF SBIR Phase I & II — Passive Network Security Platform (2015–2016) VirtaLabs received NSF SBIR Phase I (2015) and Phase II ($750K, 2016) awards to develop passive, non-disruptive network monitoring tools for heterogeneous device environments. The resulting platform built around the open-source Tapirx engine enables real-time identification and behavioral profiling of networked devices without active scanning or workflow disruption. Validated capabilities include multi-source network event correlation, anomaly detection against behavioral baselines, and root-cause analysis of network disruptions — mapping directly to both 11B Track 1 (multi-modal data curation and event correlation) and Track 2 (anomalous network behavior detection and root-cause analysis). Tapirx openly available at github.com/virtalabs/tapirx under GPL-3.0. 

    PROJECT 3: Blue Ridge Venture Studio / Traverse Management Partnership — Federal AI Commercialization (2023–Present) VirtaLabs collaborates with Traverse Management (Blue Ridge Venture Studio) on AI prototyping and federal commercialization infrastructure. Combined team has supported 30+ startups, managed $20M+ in SBIR/STTR grants across DOD, DOE, NIH, and NSF, and maintains active involvement in the DOE Genesis Mission Federal R&D Innovation Network and Health Sector Coordinating Council Cybersecurity Working Group. This positions VirtaLabs to deliver both technical AIOps infrastructure and the commercialization pathway required under Genesis Mission Criterion 4 — translating validated AIOps research into deployable products for DOE user facilities and commercial research networks.

    Partnership Request & Project Idea

    Partner Type Seeking
    Academic institution
    DOE National Lab
    Capabilities Seeking
    Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

    Access to DOE high-performance research network infrastructure and testbed environments supporting multi-facility integrated science workflows. Specifically seeking partners with operational access to ESnet or equivalent research network environments where AIOps tools can be validated against real network topology, traffic patterns, and workflow execution data. Access to Genesis Mission American Science Cloud (AmSC) infrastructure for model hosting and data sharing is also sought to support Phase I deliverable requirements.

    Datasets or data infrastructure

    Real or historical DOE research network operations data from one or more SC scientific user facilities — including network traffic logs, event records, workflow execution telemetry, and anomaly/incident records from multi-facility integrated science workflows. Data must span at least two SC science domains to satisfy Focus Area 11B's demonstration requirement. Partner should have existing data governance frameworks enabling secure sharing under appropriate data protection protocols consistent with DOE research network sensitivity requirements.

    Subject matter expertise

    Domain science expertise across at least two SC program areas to enable science project tagging and domain science-aware network operations — the ability to distinguish network events by scientific context (e.g., materials characterization vs. atmospheric modeling workflows) and prioritize network resources accordingly. Also seeking research network operations expertise for multi-facility workflow modeling, network service interdependency mapping, and validation of AI-driven predictive mitigations under realistic DOE user facility operating conditions.

    Project Idea Summary

    Virta Laboratories is seeking academic and DOE National Laboratory partners for a Genesis Mission Phase I application targeting Focus Area 11B: AIOps — AI for Network Operations (Topic 11, ASCR). VirtaLabs brings proven passive network monitoring infrastructure, multi-source event correlation, and anomaly detection capabilities validated in complex heterogeneous IT/OT environments under zero-disruption constraints. Our open-source toolset (github.com/virtalabs) provides a production-ready foundation for extending AIOps capabilities into high-performance research network environments supporting AI-driven science workflows. We are specifically seeking partners who can provide: — Access to real or historical DOE research network operations data from one or more SC scientific user facilities — Domain science expertise to enable science project tagging and domain-aware network operations across at least two SC program areas — ESnet or equivalent research network operational expertise for multi-facility workflow modeling and validation The proposed Phase I project will demonstrate AI-enabled curation of multi-modal research network operations data and real-time anomaly detection across DOE user facility network environments, with a quantitative go/no-go milestone at month 6.
