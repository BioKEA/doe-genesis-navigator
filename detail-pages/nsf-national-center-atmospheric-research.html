Introduction

The U.S. National Science Foundation National Center for Atmospheric Research (NSF NCAR) is an FFRDC advancing fundamental and applied Earth system science to support national resilience and energy security. NSF NCAR scientists bring expertise across atmospheric science, hydrology, the water cycle, energy generation operations and resilience, and space and solar physics. 

Our expertise spans multiple science areas relevant to the Genesis mission, including water availability and energy infrastructure; atmospheric turbulence, cloud microphysics, aerosols, and precipitation processes; hydrology and subseasonal-to-decadal prediction for water resource management; space plasma processes relevant to fusion and space weather; and physics-based modeling of complex fluid flows in the Earth system.

NSF NCAR’s expertise is underpinned by observational, modeling, and computing capabilities–along with significant datasets–developed and operated by NSF NCAR which leverage the latest advances in AI, HPC, and GPU-enable coding. With decades of experience in modeling from urban to global scales, including significant experience using and developing the DOE E3SM model, NSF NCAR also contributes expertise in large-eddy simulations of turbulence and atmospheric flows, modeling of space plasma processes, and analysis of environmental drivers relevant to electric grid resilience and energy infrastructure.

As a non-DOE FFRDC, NSF NCAR is unable to lead proposals but we welcome partnership inquiries.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

NSF NCAR develops and supports a range of AI models and software tools for Earth system science, including platforms for AI-based weather, subseasonal-to-seasonal, decadal, and centennial prediction; hybrid AI–physics models, including coupled Earth system models; and ML-based process emulation within models. These efforts span scales from large-eddy simulation (e.g., FastEddy) to global modeling systems. Examples include the CREDIT framework for training and deploying AI models and integration with community models such as CESM, WACCM-X, MPAS, and WRF.  https://ncarprojects.ucar.edu/ai

AI/ML/Data science expertise

NSF NCAR scientists apply AI, machine learning, and data science to advance Earth system understanding and prediction across timescales. Expertise includes hybrid AI–physics modeling, ML-based parameterization and process emulation, AI-assisted data assimilation, uncertainty quantification, and evaluation of predictive skill. NCAR teams also develop scalable workflows for training and deploying models on HPC and GPU systems and collaborate closely with universities, federal agencies, and industry to develop community AI capabilities for Earth and Sun–Earth system science.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

NSF NCAR provides extensive Earth system datasets and cyberinfrastructure to support AI-enabled research. A central component is the Geoscience Data Exchange (GDEX), an integrated research data commons that provides analysis-ready datasets, standardized metadata, and interoperable APIs designed for AI/ML workflows. GDEX connects directly with NSF NCAR HPC systems (e.g., Derecho, Casper, CIRRUS), enabling large-scale analytics and model training without data movement. The platform supports FAIR data principles and provides unified access to observations, reanalyses, and model outputs. 

Datasets or data infrastructure

NSF NCAR curates extensive Earth system datasets spanning atmospheric, hydrologic, oceanic, and Sun–Earth system observations, model outputs, and reanalyses. Through the Geoscience Data Exchange (GDEX), NCAR provides analysis-ready datasets with standardized metadata and interoperable interfaces designed to support AI/ML workflows. These resources enable discovery, integration, and analysis of large multi-source datasets for advancing Earth system science and prediction. https://ncarprojects.ucar.edu/ai

Experimental or test facilities

NSF NCAR operates a range of experimental and observational facilities supporting Earth system and atmospheric research. The Earth Observing Laboratory (EOL) provides deployable ground-based and airborne instrumentation for field campaigns, including radars, lidars, in situ sensors, and integrated observing networks. These capabilities enable detailed studies of atmospheric dynamics, clouds, turbulence, surface–atmosphere interactions, and air quality, supporting both fundamental process understanding and evaluation of emerging modeling and AI approaches.

Subject matter expertise

NSF NCAR scientists contribute expertise in atmospheric microphysics, aerosols, cloud dynamics, and atmospheric turbulence governing precipitation and fluid flows. Additional strengths include hydrology and coupled surface–groundwater processes and subseasonal-to-decadal Earth system predictability. NCAR also advances understanding of Sun–Earth interactions and space weather impacts on power grids and communications, supported by leadership in high-performance computing and AI-enabled scientific discovery.

Relevant Publications

NSF NCAR scientists publish extensively across Earth system science and predictability, atmospheric physics and dynamics, chemistry, hydrology, and Sun–Earth interactions including space weather. A comprehensive list of publications can be found via the UCAR/NCAR Opensjy publications database: https://opensky.ucar.edu/islandora/object/archives%3Aunpc

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

We welcome collaboration on the development of AI models and software tools that advance Earth system science and prediction. This includes methods that integrate AI/ML with physical modeling, data assimilation, and large Earth system datasets to improve predictive capability across spatial and temporal scales.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We are interested in collaborating with partners who bring expertise in AI, ML, and data science applied to complex physical systems. Areas of interest include pure AI and hybrid AI–physics approaches, uncertainty quantification, and scalable methods for analyzing large Earth system datasets.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Collaborations involving advanced computing infrastructure or cloud resources are of interest, particularly for large-scale AI model training, Earth system modeling, and data analytics. Partnerships that enable scalable workflows and efficient access to compute resources for scientific AI applications are especially valuable.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We welcome partnerships involving large observational or model datasets and modern data infrastructure that supports AI-enabled research. This includes collaborations focused on making Earth system datasets more accessible, interoperable, and analysis-ready for machine learning and predictive modeling.

Experimental or test facilities

We are open to collaborations involving experimental facilities or observational platforms that advance process understanding and support the development, testing, and evaluation of AI-enabled Earth system models and prediction systems.

Subject matter expertise

We seek collaborations with partners who bring domain expertise relevant to Earth system processes, including atmosphere, oceans, land systems, hydrology, space weather, and related areas. Integrating domain knowledge with AI and computational approaches is a key priority. We are also interested in efforts that extend scientific advances to user-relevant challenges such as power grid resilience, water availability, and other societal needs.

Other expertise

We welcome additional expertise that advances interdisciplinary collaboration at the intersection of Earth system science, AI, and computational research, including partnerships that support innovation in modeling, prediction, and scientific infrastructure.

Project Idea Summary

NSF NCAR is interested in developing partnerships that advance the use of AI, HPC, and Earth system science to address emerging challenges in energy, infrastructure resilience, and environmental prediction. Rather than a single project, we are pursuing a portfolio of efforts. We are particularly interested in collaborations aligned with the following challenge areas:

- Predicting U.S. Water for Energy 
- Scaling the Grid to Power the American Economy 
- HPC Code Curation, Translation, and Development for Accelerated
- Scientific Discoveries 
- AI for Scientific Reasoning 

We welcome partners who bring expertise in AI/ML, advanced computing, data infrastructure, and domain science, and who are interested in jointly developing new approaches that integrate AI with physics-based modeling and observational data to advance predictive Earth system science.
