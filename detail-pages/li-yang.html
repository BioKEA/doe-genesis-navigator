    Introduction

    I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at UNC Charlotte, where I lead the Efficient AI Computing Lab. My research focuses on efficient, trustworthy, and deployable AI, including foundation model efficiency, reasoning, and agentic AI, on-device and edge intelligence, and adaptive learning under compute and memory constraints. As it pertains to the Genesis Mission, I am particularly interested in developing AI methods that make scientific and engineering workflows more efficient, reliable, and secure, especially in settings involving high-performance computing, real-time decision support, and resource-constrained deployment. I am seeking collaborations where my expertise in efficient AI systems can contribute to DOE-relevant challenges

    Relevant Projects

    1. Efficient Foundation Models for Practical Scientific and Engineering Workflows
    Over the past five years, I have worked on efficient AI methods for foundation models, including model compression, dynamic pruning, adaptive fine-tuning, and low-overhead inference. This work aims to make advanced AI systems more practical in constrained computing environments by reducing memory, compute, and latency costs. Outcomes include top-tier publications and deployable methods for efficient large-model execution, which are directly relevant to Genesis Mission needs in scalable and trustworthy AI for scientific and engineering workflows.

    2. Continual and Adaptive Learning Under Resource Constraints
    I have developed continual learning methods that enable AI systems to adapt to new data and evolving environments without requiring full retraining. The work covers zeroth-order optimization, memory-efficient learning, and adaptive updating for on-device and resource-constrained settings. This has led to several published advances in continual learning and efficient adaptation, including methods that improve stability, reduce forgetting, and support practical deployment in dynamic real-world workflows.

    3. Deployment-Oriented AI via Software-Hardware Co-Design
    A third area of my recent work focuses on co-designing AI algorithms and computing systems for efficient deployment. This includes dynamic neural networks, hardware-aware optimization, in-memory computing, and efficient adaptation mechanisms for specialized platforms. The main achievement is a portfolio of methods that jointly improve performance and efficiency, providing a strong foundation for Genesis Mission applications that require AI to operate reliably under infrastructure, energy, or real-time constraints.
