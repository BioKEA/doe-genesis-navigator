Introduction

Dr. Maria Burns. 

I am a Tenure-Track Assistant Professor of Information Science Technology at the University of Houston and an expert at the intersection of energy security, critical infrastructure protection, and physical threat analysis. My solid industry experience includes managing global transport for Chemical and Crude Oil Tanker Fleets, HazMat/Energy Operations, and serving as a Senior Energy & Maritime Consultant with the American Bureau of Shipping. I have led federal DHS and State Department security research and education programs, with over $1.7 million as PI, and have participated in a $4,000,000 project as Thrust Lead. 
My skills and experience combine in-depth industry knowledge, critical infrastructure security, and academic rigor for the Genesis Mission.

My acclaimed books address the urgent and evolving security risk landscape: 1) Managing Energy Security: An All-Hazards Approach to Critical Infrastructure, 2) Logistics and Transportation Security, and 3) Port Management and Operations are among the global industry's definitive texts on how interconnected vulnerabilities (cyber, physical, and operational) cascade across the full energy value chain. My recent work, 1) "Cybersecurity in the 27-Second Threat Era: Smart Ports & Fleets at Risk", 2) "Access Management and Operational Security in Critical Infrastructure: Preventing Security Breaches", and 3) "Strait of Hormuz: Transportation Security Threats and Military Doctrine."

Project Idea Summary

Energy security is national security. America's upstream, midstream, and downstream energy infrastructure faces an accelerating convergence of cyber and physical threats that no single discipline can solve alone. As AI systems become embedded in energy operations, the attack surface expands. Energy supply chains, offshore platforms, refinery control systems, smart ports, pipeline SCADA networks, etc. These are all potential vectors for adversarial exploitation with catastrophic physical consequences.

I am seeking collaborative partners to form or join interdisciplinary teams pursuing Genesis Mission challenge areas at the intersection of energy security, critical infrastructure protection, and AI-enabled cyber-physical threat detection. Relevant focus areas include energy resilience, national security applications of AI, advanced data analytics for operational technology environments, and workforce development for the next generation of energy security professionals.

Ideal partners bring complementary capabilities in AI/ML modeling, industrial control systems (ICS/SCADA), energy systems engineering, computational science, or National Laboratory research infrastructure. I welcome connections from academia, industry, and DOE National Labs that are actively building Phase I or Phase II teams or exploring future Genesis Mission opportunities beyond the current funding cycle.

If your work touches energy, security, data, or AI, let's connect!
