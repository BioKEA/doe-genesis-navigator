Introduction

I am a computational mathematician. My research interests include the mathematical infrastructure needed to make AI-driven scientific workflows quantitatively reliable: fast probabilistic surrogates that replace expensive forward solves, Bayesian inversion for parameter estimation in PDE-governed systems, agentic orchestration layers that chain simulation, inference, and verification into closed-loop discovery cycles, and uncertainty quantification pipelines that propagate aleatory and epistemic error through coupled models. The common thread across application domains — power-grid planning and operations, atmospheric and hydrological systems, and composite materials under mechanical load — is embedding physical constraints and calibrated uncertainty into AI predictions so that downstream decisions carry quantified risk.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: AI-Driven Constitutive Surrogate Models for Composite Materials (AI4NS)Developed machine-learning surrogates that predict mechanical stress fields from strain fields in two-phase spinodal microstructures, replacing expensive finite-element constitutive solves. The framework maps 4-channel inputs (3 strain components + material mask) to 3 Cauchy-stress components on 2D and 3D grids. Seven deep-learning architectures were implemented and benchmarked — U-Net, Attention U-Net, ResUNet, U-Net++, Swin-UNet, TransUNet, and ConvNeXt U-Net — trained on hundreds of thousands of spinodal decomposition simulations. The codebase supports scalable, multi-node distributed training via PyTorch DDP across NVIDIA CUDA GPUs, Intel XPUs on the Aurora supercomputer, and CPUs, with architecture-specific optimizations for each hardware target. This work directly enables inverse-design and rapid screening workflows for structural materials under extreme mechanical loads.

 

Project 2: Scalable Data Assimilation with Randomized and Machine-Learned Solvers Led a research program on making four-dimensional variational (4D-Var) data assimilation tractable at scale for geophysical systems. Developed randomized preconditioned solvers for strong-constraint 4D-Var that achieve order-of-magnitude speedups over standard Krylov methods, and extended the framework to weak-constraint formulations with data-driven surrogate forward models for downscaling with calibrated uncertainty. Designed optimal sensor placement strategies under model uncertainty within the 4D-Var framework, and built machine-learned reduced-order models that compress high-dimensional state spaces for efficient variational assimilation. Applied these methods to numerical weather prediction and subseasonal climate forecasting, including Gaussian-process models that improved Madden-Julian Oscillation predictability.

 Project 3: GPU-Accelerated Optimization and UQ for Power Grid Operations Developed scalable multi-period AC optimal power flow (AC-OPF) solvers that exploit high-memory GPU architectures to jointly optimize dispatch across coupled time steps — a problem previously intractable at realistic grid scale. Built complementary uncertainty quantification tools including sequential Bayesian parameter estimation for stochastic dynamic load models, trust-region methods for bounding extreme trajectories in power system dynamics, and rare-event probability computation via Rice's formula and ML-based importance sampling. Formulated optimization-based resilience metrics and rare chance-constrained programs for grid planning under low-probability, high-consequence scenarios. This integrated simulation-optimization-UQ pipeline supports real-time decision-making and long-term planning for grid operators.

Project Idea Summary

I am looking for teams where these methods meet real measurement campaigns, operational sensor streams, or testbed facilities, enabling rigorous validation against data. Within the Genesis Mission challenges, my capabilities map onto several focus areas: in Topic 19 (AI for Scientific Reasoning), a verification framework that decomposes scientific claims into formal proof obligations checked by symbolic solvers and returns explicit assumption inventories (19A), agentic hypothesis generation that fuses heterogeneous experimental, simulation, and literature data (19B), and modular foundation-model architectures with plug-in domain tools for cross-discipline reasoning (19C); in Topic 16 (Scaling the Grid), stochastic PDE-based grid foundation models with self-improving calibration (16A), real-time anomaly detection and decision-support tools underpinned by rigorous UQ for grid operations (16B), and systematic uncertainty budgeting across multi-sector energy planning models (16C); in Topic 21 (AI in Fluid Flow for Energy), neural-operator and PINN-based surrogates for multi-physics flow phenomena including turbulence and particle-fluid interactions (21A), generative design exploration for energy components validated against high-fidelity CFD (21B), and digital-twin integration with live sensor data for predictive maintenance and adaptive control of fluid-driven energy systems (21C); in Topic 15 (Predicting U.S. Water for Energy), Bayesian surrogate emulators and data-assimilation
