Introduction

I am a faculty member in Mathematics at North Carolina State University working at the interface of mathematics, machine learning, and scientific data analysis. My research develops computational methods, including optimal transport, multimodal data integration, topological and geometric data analysis, and scientific machine learning, for extracting structure and generating insight from complex data. I have extensive experience working with molecular structures and single-cell and spatial omics data.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Research-grade AI/ML models and software for biomolecular property prediction and biological data analysis, including deep learning methods for protein structure-function relationship prediction and optimal-transport-based tools for spatial/single-cell analysis, multimodal integration, and cell-cell communication inference. I can contribute method development, adaptation, benchmarking, and collaborative prototyping.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Expertise in machine learning and mathematical modeling for scientific data, especially biomolecular, single-cell, and spatial omics datasets. Experience includes biomolecular property prediction, structure-function relationship prediction, optimal transport, topological data analysis, multimodal data integration, and inference of biological interactions from complex, sparse, and heterogeneous data.

Subject matter expertise

Biomolecular structure-function analysis, protein interactions and binding affinity, single-cell and spatial omics, multimodal biological data integration, and cell-cell communication. Particularly interested in linking molecular structure information with higher-level biological interactions and function across scales.
