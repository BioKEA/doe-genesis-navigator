Introduction

ANDECO Institute is a mission-focused nonprofit that enables the discovery, evaluation, and operational integration of commercial and non-traditional data assets to support AI-driven analysis, modeling, and decision workflows for U.S. government and national security missions. ANDECO specializes in translating heterogeneous data ecosystems into structured, mission-ready inputs that can be rapidly consumed by analytic pipelines, AI models, and federated research environments.

Aligned with the Genesis Mission, ANDECO provides technical frameworks for data readiness, provenance, governance, and fitness-for-purpose assessment across large, distributed partner networks. Its work emphasizes scalable data integration, metadata normalization, and taxonomy-driven mapping to enable interoperability across datasets, models, and infrastructure. ANDECO supports the formation of multidisciplinary teams by matching data providers, analytic capabilities, and mission requirements—reducing time-to-integration and lowering barriers to experimentation.

As a consortium partner, ANDECO contributes applied expertise in commercial data orchestration, AI‑ready data preparation, and public–private collaboration, complementing Genesis priorities around data integration and standards, model validation, and secure, federated collaboration across DOE, National Laboratories, academia, and industry.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: AI‑Ready Commercial Data Evaluation and Governance

Scope of Work:
ANDECO supported a U.S. Government sponsor integrating commercial and non‑traditional datasets into AI‑enabled analytic workflows. The effort established a repeatable framework to assess data fitness‑for‑purpose, provenance, lineage, licensing constraints, and AI‑related risks across a fragmented vendor market. ANDECO conducted structured market research, vendor discovery, and side‑by‑side dataset evaluations using anonymized methods to preserve neutrality and mitigate bias. The project addressed risks associated with AI‑generated and AI‑processed data, including disclosure gaps and quality degradation.

Achievements:
ANDECO delivered an auditable data evaluation and governance framework that enabled confident ingestion of external data into AI pipelines. Reusable artifacts—data catalogs, evaluation templates, and governance guidance—reduced time‑to‑integration and established an enduring institutional capability aligned with responsible AI and compliance requirements.

Project 2: Federated Data Ecosystem and Consortium Enablement

Scope of Work:
ANDECO designed and supported a neutral, government‑aligned data ecosystem to enable collaboration among government teams, commercial data providers, and analytic partners. Scope included participant onboarding and vetting, data‑centric requirements definition, standardized metadata and taxonomy development, and governance mechanisms to support federated data access without centralizing sensitive assets. ANDECO served as an independent intermediary, ensuring conflict‑free evaluation and OPSEC‑compliant engagement.

Achievements:
The effort reduced collaboration friction by enabling teams to rapidly identify relevant datasets, align technical and licensing expectations, and experiment with data‑driven approaches without re‑competing agreements. ANDECO’s governance model improved transparency and sustainability while leaving behind reusable processes that supported scalable participation and AI‑enabled discovery.

Project 3: AI‑Augmented Data Risk Advisory

Scope of Work:
ANDECO advised a government program confronting increased use of AI‑processed and AI‑generated commercial data. The work focused on identifying and managing risks related to opaque data transformations, synthetic content, and downstream impacts on AI model performance. ANDECO incorporated AI‑use disclosure into vendor engagement, developed provenance and lineage assessment criteria, and advised on mission‑aligned quality assurance practices.

Achievements:
The project strengthened sponsor confidence in deploying AI‑enabled workflows by embedding governance and risk awareness into acquisition and evaluation processes. ANDECO delivered practical guidance and decision‑support artifacts enabling human‑in‑the‑loop oversight while preserving analytic speed and scalability.

Capabilities Seeking
AI/ML/Data science expertise

Seeking partners with expertise in AI/ML methods applied to scientific reasoning, data integration, modeling, and decision workflows. Relevant capabilities include applied machine learning, data science, model evaluation, and human‑in‑the‑loop approaches that leverage diverse, real‑world data inputs. Partners should be interested in collaborating on AI‑enabled discovery and analysis rather than developing closed or proprietary AI platforms.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Seeking partners that generate, steward, or manage high‑value datasets or data infrastructure relevant to scientific discovery, energy, or national security missions. This includes commercial, public, or non‑traditional data sources, as well as data platforms that support interoperability, metadata, provenance, and federated access. Emphasis is on data readiness and responsible use, not exclusive ownership.

Subject matter expertise

Seeking domain experts across scientific, technical, or mission areas where external data and AI can accelerate discovery, modeling, or decision‑making. Subject matter expertise may span energy systems, materials, manufacturing, environmental science, or related fields, and should complement data‑driven and AI‑enabled approaches rather than operate in isolation.

Other expertise

Seeking partners with expertise in data governance, provenance, standards, interoperability, and public‑private collaboration. Relevant experience includes federated data environments, responsible AI practices, evaluation frameworks, and ecosystem or consortium design that enables transparent, scalable collaboration across government, academia, and industry.

Project Idea Summary

ANDECO Institute is seeking partners to collaborate on Genesis Mission–aligned efforts focused on enabling AI‑driven scientific discovery through improved access to, integration of, and governance for diverse data sources. The core challenge area is accelerating the use of heterogeneous, external, and non‑traditional datasets—commercial, public, and AI‑augmented—as reliable, AI‑ready inputs for scientific reasoning, modeling, and decision workflows.

ANDECO brings expertise in data readiness, provenance, lineage, governance, and public–private ecosystem orchestration. We are seeking partners with complementary capabilities in AI/ML methods, domain science, and data generation or stewardship to jointly address challenges related to data interoperability, fitness‑for‑purpose, and responsible use at scale. Ideal partners are interested in collaborative approaches that support federated access, transparent evaluation, and human‑in‑the‑loop oversight, rather than closed or proprietary solutions.

The goal is to reduce friction between data producers, AI developers, and mission teams, enabling multidisciplinary collaboration and faster experimentation in support of Genesis Mission priorities across energy, discovery science, and national security.
