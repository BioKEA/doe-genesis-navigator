Introduction

JWBA is a science and engineering firm whose principals have nearly sixty years of experience in systems engineering modeling, energy-resource analysis, and applied technology research. We have developed the conceptual framework for a National Artificial Intelligence Economy Resilience Model (NAIERM)—a systems-engineering architecture that treats the economy as a dynamic, data-coupled system with measurable state variables, constraints, and controllable inputs. The ultimate goal of NAIERM is an AI-driven Economic Digital Twin and Supervisory Optimization System for the United States economy. It is designed to measure, simulate, and evaluate the stability, utilization, and efficiency of the national economy under large-scale artificial intelligence (AI) deployment, and provide decision-grade outputs to support human-in-the-loop governance.

Project Idea Summary

JWBA has developed the conceptual and mathematical framework for the National Artificial Intelligence Economy Resilience Model (NAIERM). The system treats the national economy as a dynamic, feedback-controlled system with:

Defined state variables (e.g., productive capacity vs. aggregate demand).A real-time measurement construct (GDP Divergence Index, GDI). A process simulation core representing supply–demand dynamics under AI-driven productivity growth. A supervisory control formulation, including constraints and response pathways. A structured trigger and evaluation framework for assessing system state relative to efficient operation. The architecture is analogous to a large-scale process simulation coupled with supervisory control, extended to a macroeconomic domain.This core system design is complete at the conceptual level and is ready to be implemented, tested, and validated.

We seek a primary technical collaborator supporting:

Data architecture and ingestion pipelines. 

High-performance computing environment for simulation and scaling.

Uncertainty quantification and model validation.

Scenario generation and sensitivity analysis.

Integration into DOE/Genesis computational infrastructure.

JWBA would serve as system architect and model originator, with our partner extending the system into a robust, scalable, and validated implementation.
