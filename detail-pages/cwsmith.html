Introduction

Physical Sciences Inc. (PSI) is a small business R&D company in Andover, MA. For the Genesis Mission, PSI brings a working agentic evolutionary search framework that autonomously discovers verified reasoning patterns — achieving 4× sample efficiency, 87.5% formal verification rates, and compositional transfer across domains. Our neuro-symbolic architecture directly addresses Genesis Mission priorities in trustworthy AI for scientific reasoning (Topic 19-A) and neuro-symbolic agents for code development (Topic 18-C). As a prior DARPA SIGMA+ Lead Integration Team and current DARPA PIE performer, PSI has a proven record of executing high-risk AI programs and is seeking national laboratory partners to apply our framework to DOE science and technology challenges.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

PSI offers a working agentic evolutionary search framework for autonomous discovery of verified reasoning patterns. The system combines PyGol (custom genetic programming for logical rules), SWI-Prolog, LLM-guided search, and formal verification via Z3/Vampire. It achieves 4× sample efficiency, 87.5% verification rates, and compositional transfer across domains (3.0× improvement across 56 task pairs). Includes a reusable bias library with 4 verified pattern families and differentiable ILP with logical regularization for end-to-end trainable neuro-symbolic systems.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Deep expertise in inductive logic programming, neuro-symbolic architectures, evolutionary search, low-shot learning, cross-modal distillation, graph analytics, entity alignment, and multi-modal sensor fusion. Our agentic framework combines LLM-guided hypothesis generation with genetic programming and formal verification — achieving theoretical minimum empirical sample complexity on 22/23 benchmark tasks. Extensive experience developing custom CNNs, Bayesian classifiers, dispersion simulation engines, and ML-based spectral matching algorithms that outperform conventional methods.

Relevant Projects

1. DARPA SIGMA+ — Lead System Integrator & Graph Analytics (12/2019–11/2023)
Contract HR0011-19-C-0014, $14M. PSI served as lead system integrator (TA1) for a large-scale urban chemical sensor network, developing a custom 1D CNN for spectral analysis and a real-time dispersion modeling framework that leverages custom AI for steady state CFD prediction. This networked detection capability achieved 80× false alarm reduction over native sensor capabilities. As a TA3 performer, PSI developed entity alignment and graph search algorithms for multi-source sensor data fusion. PSI integrated PERM-Mobile CBRN units with Tier 0 detection algorithms and successfully deployed the system in urban environments including the Indianapolis 500, World Trade Center, and Port of New York/New Jersey for real-time chemical threat detection.

2. DARPA HEIMDALL — Automated Maritime Targeting (2025)
PSI was a performer on the DARPA HEIMDALL program, developing ML-based software to aid or automate gun system targeting against maritime surface threats currently performed manually by Navy Sailors. The system automatically maintains targeting in dynamic maritime environments from moving ships, reducing operator workload and enabling focus on tactical decisions. The effort integrated EO/IR tracking of USV targets demonstrating real-time detection, tracking, and targeting handoff for surface threats in the EO and IR spectrum.

3. Army LOTT — Low-Shot Object Tracking & Targeting (2020–2023)
PSI developed the Low-Shot Object Tracking and Targeting (LOTT) system for next-generation combat vehicles, integrating state-of-the-art ML object detection and tracking with IR and visible imaging sensors using low-shot training techniques. Key achievements include Phase I and Phase II SBIR completion with algorithms optimized for low-SWaP embedded platforms, cross-modal distillation methods enabling models trained on limited labeled data to generalize across sensor modalities, and integration into PSI's commercial MURS and OWL radiation detection products.

 

Partnership Request & Project Idea

Partner Type Seeking
DOE National Lab
Capabilities Seeking
Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Access to domain-relevant scientific datasets from DOE mission areas (e.g., materials science, computational chemistry, subsurface flow, fusion plasma, or energy systems) for applying our agentic AI framework. We seek datasets that can serve as ground truth for validating discovered reasoning patterns and governing equations, as well as benchmark problems for testing code generation and optimization capabilities.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Access to HPC resources, GPU clusters, or the American Science Cloud (AmSC) for scaling our agentic evolutionary search framework beyond prototype-scale benchmarks. Our system requires compute for running large-scale hypothesis generation, formal verification with Z3/Vampire across thousands of candidate patterns, and integration with scientific software pipelines that may require significant memory or parallel processing.

Subject matter expertise

Domain scientists and engineers from a DOE National Laboratory who can guide the application of our framework to real-world scientific challenges, help identify high-value reasoning patterns or code optimization targets, and validate results against established physical principles and domain knowledge. We seek partners who understand the gap between current AI capabilities and what is needed for trustworthy scientific discovery.

Project Idea Summary

PSI is seeking a DOE National Laboratory partner to apply our verifiable neuro-symbolic AI framework to Genesis Mission challenge areas in HPC Code Curation and/or AI for Scientific Reasoning. We are interested in both areas and will pursue one or both depending on partner fit and program guidance.

We are seeking a lab partner that can provide: (1) domain-relevant scientific datasets and validation expertise; (2) access to HPC resources or the American Science Cloud; and (3) subject matter expertise in a target science domain.
