    Introduction

    Stanford University research group led by Prof. Doug James, specializing in computational physics, large-scale wave propagation simulation, and physics-informed machine learning. Our group develops boundary element methods (BEM), surrogate models, and real-time simulation techniques for complex physical systems. We have extensive experience building digital twins that couple high-fidelity physics solvers with data-driven models for prediction and control of dynamic phenomena.

    We are interested in Genesis Mission opportunities related to Topic 10 (Securing U.S. Leadership in Data Centers), particularly the use of AI and scientific computing to address operational challenges in next-generation data center infrastructure. Our capabilities in physics-based simulation, surrogate modeling, and cyber-physical system design are well suited to collaborative efforts that combine DOE laboratory resources with university research.

    We are seeking DOE/NNSA National Laboratory partners with expertise in adaptive control, real-time sensing and actuation systems, or high-performance computing infrastructure for AI-driven physical simulation.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    AI models, tools, or software

    Custom numerical solvers for large-scale physical simulation, including boundary element methods and fast multipole methods for wave propagation. Experience developing real-time simulation tools and differentiable simulation pipelines for optimization and control of complex physical systems.

    AI/ML/Data science expertise

    Research group with expertise in scientific computing applied to physical systems. Experience developing efficient computational models, data-driven acceleration of numerical solvers, and hybrid approaches that combine classical numerical methods with machine learning for real-time performance.

    Subject matter expertise

    Computational physics, large-scale wave simulation, and digital twin design for cyber-physical systems. Extensive experience in high-performance numerical methods, physically-based modeling, and real-time simulation techniques for prediction and control applications.

    Other expertise

    Physics-based sound simulation and synthesis, including computational methods for acoustic radiation, wave scattering, and sound generation from physical interactions. Extensive publication record in modeling how objects produce sound, with applications spanning computer graphics, virtual environments, and interactive simulation.

    Partnership Request & Project Idea

    Partner Type Seeking
    DOE National Lab
    Capabilities Seeking
    Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

    Seeking access to high-performance computing resources for large-scale physical simulation, including training and evaluation of computationally intensive predictive models. Our methods involve repeated solves of PDEs over complex geometries, which benefit from HPC-scale parallelism. Access to DOE computing infrastructure would accelerate development of real-time surrogate models derived from high-fidelity simulations.

    Experimental or test facilities

    Seeking access to DOE laboratory experimental infrastructure for validating AI-driven control algorithms on physical wave systems. Ideal facilities would support real-time sensor array data acquisition, actuation hardware for closed-loop control experiments, and instrumentation for characterizing dynamic wave propagation environments. We are particularly interested in partners who have experience with acoustic or vibromechanical measurement systems and transitioning from simulation-based prototyping to hardware-in-the-loop testing of adaptive control.

    Subject matter expertise

    Seeking collaborators with deep experience in adaptive control theory, wave propagation physics, real-time sensing and actuation systems, or deployment of AI-driven prediction and control in operational environments. We are especially interested in researchers who have worked on closed-loop control problems involving spatially distributed sensor and actuator arrays, where the control strategy must adapt to changing conditions in real time. Experience with acoustic or elastic wave phenomena is a plus.

    Project Idea Summary

    We are exploring AI-driven digital twin approaches for real-time monitoring and control of physical phenomena relevant to data center infrastructure, under Topic 10 (Securing U.S. Leadership in Data Centers). Our research combines high-fidelity computational methods for wave simulation, including boundary element methods and fast solvers, with efficient surrogate modeling techniques to build predictive models that can operate within closed-loop cyber-physical systems.

    The core technical challenge is bridging the gap between expensive physics-based solvers and the real-time performance requirements of adaptive control in complex, dynamic environments with many degrees of freedom. Data centers present rich opportunities for applying these methods given their scale, operational complexity, and the importance of managing diverse physical phenomena across these facilities.

    We are seeking a DOE National Laboratory partner with expertise in adaptive control systems, real-time distributed sensing, or high-performance computing for physical simulation. The ideal partner would complement our computational modeling strengths with experimental capabilities and domain experience in deploying prediction and control systems in real-world operational settings.

    We are a Category 3 (IHE) applicant seeking to form a multi-category team as required by the RFA.
