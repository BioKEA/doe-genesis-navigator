    Introduction

    Optimuos  is an AI-driven, physics-informed digital engineering platform (MetaXLab) designed to accelerate material discovery, characterization, and deployment. Our platform builds high-fidelity digital twins of materials, enabling virtual testing, performance prediction under diverse operating and manufacturing conditions, and optimization of both formulation and process parameters. In addition to forward modeling, MetaXLab supports inverse design—identifying candidate chemistries and processing pathways based on targeted performance—significantly reducing reliance on costly and time-intensive experimental cycles.

    We are currently at TRL 5, with demonstrated capability and active industry engagement, and are expanding the platform to broader material systems and application domains. We are well-positioned to join a multi-institutional Genesis Mission team, contributing AI-driven modeling, digital twin development, and integrated workflow capabilities.

    We are seeking to collaborate as the AI and digital twin engine within a Genesis Mission team, working alongside national laboratories and domain partners to co-develop and validate mission-relevant use cases. Through these partnerships, we aim to contribute scalable, AI-enabled workflows that integrate into the Genesis ecosystem and accelerate the transition of advanced materials from discovery to deployment.

    Project Idea Summary

    Current manufacturing R&D workflows remain fragmented, with limited integration across process data, experimental measurements, and modeling. This prevents efficient exploration of high-dimensional process spaces and slows the transition from lab-scale development to manufacturing scale-up and deployment. General-purpose AI approaches are insufficient; scalable solutions require physics-informed, process-aware models that capture the coupling between process conditions, system behavior, and performance.

    We are developing an AI-driven platform for digitizing and automating manufacturing R&D workflows by integrating multi-source data with physics-informed models to build digital twins of processes and equipment. The platform enables virtual testing, real-time prediction, and optimization of process parameters, supporting closed-loop, human-in-the-loop workflows that connect experimentation, modeling, and deployment. Our focus is on accelerating scale-up, reducing experimental burden, and enabling robust process control in manufacturing environments.
