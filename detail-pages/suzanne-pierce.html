Introduction

Dr. Suzanne A. Pierce is a Research Professor and Manager of the Decision Support Office (DSO) at the Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC), University of Texas at Austin. Her research program integrates AI-enhanced decision support systems, participatory modeling, and high-performance cyberinfrastructure to address complex environmental and water resource challenges. She is a nationally recognized leader in translating stakeholder knowledge and qualitative field data into executable computational workflows on HPC systems.

Her methodological signature is the Decision Pathways Framework (DPF)—a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) semantic pipeline and Computational Cookbook stack that bridges community narratives, qualitative coding, and physics-based models to produce group decision support systems for use in participatory and policy processes. Current research extends this framework to evaluate whether foundation large language models (LLMs) running in air-gapped HPC environments can replicate and extend expert inductive qualitative coding, with a “verifiability-first” architecture anchoring LLM outputs to human expert codes.

Early in her career, Dr. Pierce served as Environmental Manager for international metals mining operations across the full project lifecycle—exploration, construction, start-up, operation, and closure—providing deep grounding in real-world environmental compliance, multi-stakeholder negotiation, and decision-critical data management under regulatory constraints.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

VO (Scientific Variable Object) semantic pipeline for multi-modal hypothesis generation — a partially automated closed-loop reasoning system connecting NLP-extracted causal hypotheses to physics-based model execution and skill metric feedback. Deployed on TACC infrastructure via TAPIS/DataX science gateway. Computational Cookbooks: containerized, reproducible analytical workflows for NLP narrative analysis, SVO extraction, geospatial analysis, and model integration, accessible via web interface without command-line expertise. Open-source; AmSC-compatible deployment.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Interdisciplinary AI-geoscience methodology: NLP-to-SVO semantic linking, hybrid AI-physical model integration, multi-modal data fusion across text, sensor streams, satellite imagery, and simulation outputs. Seven-year PI of NSF IS-GEO Research Coordination Network (NSF-EAR-1632211) — defined grand challenges and benchmark data formats for AI in geosciences. Publications: Gil & Pierce et al. (2021) ACM TIIS; Gil & Pierce et al. (2019) CACM. Human-in-the-loop decision support methodology for high-consequence environmental and energy systems.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Existing allocations on TACC HPC/GPU infrastructure (Frontera, Lonestar6, Vista GPU cluster) for interdisciplinary environmental and geoscience related research. TAPIS/DataX science gateway: production web-based interface for HPC job submission, workflow orchestration, and data management without command-line requirements. Containerized execution via Apptainer for reproducible, portable workflows. AmSC-compatible data hosting and discoverability via CKAN portal. API-accessible endpoints for plug-and-play integration with other Genesis Mission teams.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Deployed testbed + gateway infrastructure: CKAN-based multi-modal data discovery portal with FAIR-aligned metadata schema (developed under TWDB contract) — operational for heterogeneous environmental and hydrological datasets, extensible to geoscience and critical minerals data types. Multi-modal community knowledge corpora from four regional case studies: Arctic climate adaptation (Nunapitchuk, AK), Rio Grande Valley flood history, southeast Texas groundwater and subsidence (SUBSIDE), and Kerr County rapid-onset flooding. Metadata schema compatible with cross-domain integrations.

Subject matter expertise

Water-energy systems decision support: coupled surface–groundwater modeling, flood risk, land subsidence, and energy infrastructure resilience across Texas and Arctic contexts. Critical mineral environmental management: five years operational experience spanning all mine life-cycle phases at a world-scale copper operation — ARDML prediction, geochemical baseline design, hydrogeological characterization, and regulatory compliance. Participatory modeling: Indigenous knowledge integration, stakeholder engagement, and human-in-the-loop AI for high-consequence decisions.
