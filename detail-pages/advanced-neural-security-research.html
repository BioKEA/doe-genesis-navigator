Introduction

Advanced Neural Security Research (ANSR) is a Washington State nonprofit research institute focused on secure AI agent communications for national infrastructure. We are preparing a Phase I application under DE-FOA-0003612, Topic 20: Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows, Focus Area 20-B (Data Provenance and Integrity Verification). Our team brings expertise in AI architecture, information security operations, and enterprise cybersecurity. We are seeking collaboration with a DOE National Laboratory and academic partner, particularly researchers working in AI security, adversarial robustness, or secure computing for scientific workflows, to validate our cross-architectural protocols against adversarial attack models in HPC environments.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Specialized Neural Network Analysis: Our team is focused on AI/ML model construct enumeration and Model specific processing architectures for security conformed Inference pathways and latent space vector classification. Our tooling framework allows for distributed testing and governed automated communications as a complete inference harness with its own security enclave.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Specialized Neural Research: Our expertise focuses on the intersection of generative AI and secure transmission protocols. We have extensive experience in high-dimensional vector space security, latent space geometry mapping, and abstract encoding architectures.

Subject matter expertise

AI Security & Governance: We provide deep domain knowledge in cybersecurity for autonomous systems. Our team specializes in Zero-Trust security models for automated research environments and adversarial robustness. We have specifically developed protocols that eliminate the transport-layer attack surface entirely, neutralizing statistical side-channel transport analysis and establishing mathematical Proof of Trust (PoT) governance lifecycles, which include automated governance and dispute resolution for contractual interfacing. Expansion of ongoing research expands into Optical Processing.

Other expertise

Strategic Cryptography & Systems Architecture: We bring specialized knowledge in the design of decentralized overlay mesh networks and hardware-anchored identity models. This includes integrating hardware roots-of-trust with neural weight identity abstraction to create immutable identity signatures. Our team also has significant experience in managed IT services and municipal-grade cybersecurity operations through our board-level partnerships.

Other resources/tools

We have our own specialized testing software and AI model inference framework that provides proof of concept in distrubuted AI/Agent system architectures. The Governance modeling system we are working on provides control functionality using natural language processing.

Relevant Publications

Our work up to this point is not externally published, but does exist for review provided an agreement on disclosure security can be mutually agreed to.

Capabilities Seeking
Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

High-Performance Computing (HPC) Utilization: We are seeking access to Tier-1 or Tier-2 HPC resources to perform large-scale stress testing of the VectorGuard protocol. Specifically, we need to simulate high-concurrency agentic workflows to measure the latency and throughput. We require environments capable of running distributed inference across heterogeneous GPU/TPU clusters to validate that our model-bound inference security maintains integrity during cross-model data handoffs in a simulated national laboratory environment.

Experimental or test facilities

Adversarial Simulation Testbeds: We are looking for a partner with established AI security testbeds to conduct Red Team operations against our communication architecture. We aim to utilize existing DOE-managed environments for simulating adversarial injection, "Whisper Leak" data extraction attempts, and unauthorized activation-state manipulation. Validating our protocol’s defensive posture within a controlled, high-fidelity environment is a primary goal for our Phase I proposal to ensure readiness for deployment in critical scientific infrastructure.

Subject matter expertise

Collaborative Research & Security Validation: We seek to collaborate with PIs specializing in AI robustness, data provenance, and secure computing (Topic 20-B). We aim to pair our architecture with a lab’s formal verification and adversarial ML expertise. We are looking for a lead verification partner via a formal subaward to provide the theoretical grounding needed to advance our Proof of Concept into a standardized federal framework.

Project Idea Summary

Project Title: Validation of Inference-Native Security Protocols for Heterogeneous Scientific Workflows 

Challenge Focus Area: We are targeting Focus Area 20-B (Data Provenance and Integrity Verification). Our goal is to demonstrate a scalable framework for verifying the integrity of data and communications within autonomous AI workflows while simultaneously binding the output to the specific model generating the information and securing its provenance over distributed network environments. 

Project Idea: Use the baseline AI/ML model data as a fingerprint substrate via tracking of inference patterns in comparison with probability patterns vs deterministic patterns. Using this as both a provenance system and as a chain of custody of inference data flow through agentic systems.

Partner Request: We are seeking a National Laboratory or academic partner with expertise in AI security, adversarial robustness, or secure scientific computing. Specifically, we require access to HPC infrastructure and specialized testbeds to perform large-scale validation of our cryptographic handshake and latent space parity-check mechanisms. We aim to collaborate with a Principal Investigator to serve as our primary validation partner for a Phase I Genesis Mission application that bridges advanced neural research with practical federal infrastructure.
