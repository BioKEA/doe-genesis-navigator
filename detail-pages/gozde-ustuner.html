Introduction

I am an Assistant Professor at Hofstra University, conducting research at Brookhaven National Laboratory, where I develop advanced systems and novel nanomaterials for both low- and high-temperature energy applications. My research focuses on energy storage technologies, corrosion-resistant materials, and fuel cell systems, with particular emphasis on designing nano-functional materials such as metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) to enhance performance and durability. I actively utilize state-of-the-art user facilities at BNL, including the Center for Functional Nanomaterials (CFN) and the National Synchrotron Light Source II (NSLS-II), to investigate material behavior across multiple scales. I am particularly interested in integrating artificial intelligence and machine learning to accelerate materials discovery, optimize system performance, and extract insights from complex experimental datasets, ultimately enhancing the efficiency and impact of my research. . Through this work, I aim to bridge fundamental materials science with practical energy solutions that support the advancement of efficient and sustainable energy systems.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
Experimental or test facilities

I actively utilize Brookhaven National Laboratory user facilities, including the Center for Functional Nanomaterials (CFN) and the National Synchrotron Light Source II (NSLS-II), and maintain active proposals to run advanced experiments. I also have lab space and funding to acquire new equipment for collaborative projects.

Subject matter expertise

My expertise spans nanomaterials (including MOFs), energy storage, corrosion, and fuel cells across low- and high-temperature systems. I develop novel materials and scalable solutions for advanced energy technologies.

Other expertise

I offer strong collaboration opportunities, including co-development of proposals, joint experiments, and access to unique infrastructure. I am actively seeking partnerships to expand research impact and accelerate innovation.

Other resources/tools

I maintain active collaborations with regional institutions, including Stony Brook University (Advanced Energy Research and Technology Center, AERTC), the Engine Combustion Laboratory, and the Institute for Gas Innovation and Technology (IGIT), as well as other Long Island partners engaged in hydrogen, clean energy and similar field of research. These collaborations provide access to complementary expertise, testing capabilities, and infrastructure, enabling integrated, multidisciplinary projects and expanding opportunities for impactful partnerships.

Relevant Projects

1. M2FCT DOE-Funded Project 
Contributed to the M2FCT (Million Mile Fuel Cell Truck) DOE-funded project focused on advancing materials and systems for heavy-duty fuel cell vehicles. Worked on improving durability, efficiency, and performance of fuel cell components through advanced materials development and system-level integration.

2. MOF-Based Materials Development (BNL)
Developed metal–organic framework (MOF) materials in collaboration with other national laboratories for clean energy and fuel cell applications. Achieved enhanced material performance through tailored synthesis and advanced characterization using national user facilities.

3. HyBlend Project (Hydrogen Infrastructure Initiative)
Contributed to the HyBlend project focused on hydrogen blending in natural gas pipelines. Supported materials and compatibility studies to advance safe and efficient hydrogen integration into existing energy infrastructure.

Project Idea Summary

My research focuses on developing advanced nanomaterials and integrated systems for energy applications, including fuel cells, energy storage, hydrogen technologies, and corrosion-resistant materials across low- and high-temperature environments. A key challenge I aim to address is accelerating the discovery, optimization, and deployment of high-performance materials while bridging the gap between fundamental research and real-world energy systems.

I am particularly interested in integrating AI/ML to enable data-driven materials design, predictive modeling, and efficient analysis of complex experimental datasets generated from advanced characterization techniques at facilities such as CFN and NSLS-II.

I am seeking collaborators with expertise in AI/ML, modeling, and software/tool development who can work at the intersection of data science and experimental materials research. Ideal partners would help develop scalable, interpretable models and integrate them with experimental workflows to accelerate innovation and improve energy system performance.
