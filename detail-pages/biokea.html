Introduction

BioKEA is an interdisciplinary team of computer scientists, programmers, and biologists focused on applying computational methods to complex biological problems. We are especially interested in bringing this combined expertise to Biological and Environmental Research (BER) challenges, where scalable software, rigorous data analysis, and biological insight must work together. Our lead, Sean, brings experience from the U.S. Department of Energy ecosystem, including work at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory and the Joint Genome Institute on metagenomic data and research workflows. That background gives our team direct experience with large, complex biological datasets and the tools needed to turn them into usable scientific insight. In addition to its computational expertise, BioKEA can support biodiversity genomics-focused work through 5,000 square feet of laboratory space. Through the Genesis Mission, BioKEA aims to contribute strong technical execution, biological fluency, and a practical problem-solving mindset to projects that advance BER research.

Relevant Projects

1. DOE KBase AI Research Assistant and Genome Annotation Agent

Sean contributed to DOE-relevant bioinformatics infrastructure by working on the DOE Systems Biology Knowledgebase (KBase) at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. The scope included improving access to reproducible genomic analysis workflows for biologists and bioinformaticians, with emphasis on microbial genome assembly, annotation, quality control, interpretation of workflow outputs, and support for publication-ready scientific narratives. Recent work on the KBase Research Assistant and Genome Annotation Agent applied LLM-based tools, retrieval augmented generation, and agent orchestration to help users move from sequenced reads to annotated genomes within KBase. The achievement was a practical AI-assisted workflow concept for BER-supported cyberinfrastructure, presented through the DOE Genomic Science Program and aligned with the broader KBase mission to democratize bioinformatics analysis and reproducible genomic science.

2. Machine learning for Earth microbiome trait discovery

Sean was part of the LBNL work applying machine learning to standardized metagenome collections across diverse ecosystems. The scope included using metagenomic features to classify source ecosystems, identify microbial traits associated with specific environmental contexts, and improve the interpretation of large environmental genomics datasets. The project identified optimal feature types and model parameters, produced models that performed well in cross-validation, and used model interpretation to identify signature features distinguishing 41 ecosystems. This work is directly relevant to BER priorities because it connects environmental metadata, microbial function, comparative genomics, and machine learning to infer ecosystem-relevant biological signals from complex sequence data. The work was reported through the DOE Genomic Science Program and linked to the peer-reviewed study “A bacterial sensor taxonomy across Earth ecosystems for machine learning applications.”

3. FAIR eDNA and environmental metabarcoding metadata standards

Sean contributed to community standards efforts to make environmental DNA and metabarcoding data more reproducible, interoperable, and reusable. The scope included improving how eDNA datasets are described, formatted, linked to raw and derived data, and prepared for reuse across repositories and biodiversity data systems. This work addressed a major bottleneck in environmental genomics: inconsistent metadata and fragmented data formats that limit cross-study synthesis. Achievements include coauthorship on the 2025 FAIR eDNA metadata checklist and data formatting guidelines, which integrate existing standards and introduce eDNA-specific terms, templates, tools, and example datasets. Sean also contributed to the 2024 MIEM guidelines for environmental metabarcoding reporting. These efforts demonstrate direct experience organizing biological and environmental metadata so that genomic datasets can support downstream inference, synthesis, and AI-enabled analysis.

Project Idea Summary

BioKEA’s project idea focuses on improving genomic prediction and protein function inference across BER-relevant genomics datasets. Current computational approaches often produce incomplete, low-confidence, or incorrect functional annotations because biological context, environmental metadata, experimental provenance, and sequence-derived features are not organized to fully support modern AI and comparative genomics workflows. We want to address this barrier by combining wet-lab biodiversity genomics capacity with dry-lab expertise in software, data engineering, metagenomics, and machine learning.

Our challenge focus area is the development of better infrastructure and methods for organizing, integrating, and using metadata across the BER genomics portfolio to improve biological inference. We are particularly interested in approaches that integrate genomic and metagenomic data with environmental context, trait information, phylogeny, experimental conditions, and validation data to improve the accuracy, explainability, and experimental actionability of protein function predictions.

In a potential partner, we are seeking complementary expertise in BER data assets, metadata standards, protein function annotation, AI model development, or experimental validation. We are especially interested in partners with access to high-value BER datasets, domain-specific scientific questions, or established workflows where improved metadata integration and genomic prediction could help.
