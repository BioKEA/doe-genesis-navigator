Introduction

TekSynap is a federal systems integrator headquartered in Herndon, Virginia, delivering mission-ready AI platforms and enterprise AI infrastructure to U.S. government agencies. Our team designs, deploys, and operates AI solutions across the full stack, from large language model-based applications and agentic workflow platforms to NVIDIA DGX-based AI Factory infrastructure implementation.

Our proprietary AI portfolio includes applications such as TRON (Text Recognition Optimization Nexus), MegaTRON (known as SimplifAI at NRC), and ARDA, with active deployments at the Nuclear Regulatory Commission and within the Intelligence Community. Our TekXlerate Foundry, operating across three locations with NVIDIA DGX infrastructure, serves as our live R&D and prototyping environment for next-generation AI workloads. TekSynap's implementation teams are trained and ready to deliver end-to-end AI Factory deployments covering facility planning, compute infrastructure installation, cluster management, orchestration, and workload scheduling, making us a capable industry integration partner for national laboratory and federal agency AI infrastructure programs.

We hold CMMI Maturity Level 5 across Development, Security, and Services, and are certified under ISO 27001:2022 (Information Security), ISO 20000-1:2018 (IT Service Management), ISO 9001:2015 (Quality Management), ISO 22301:2019 (Business Continuity), and ISO 28000:2022 (Supply Chain Security), as well as CMMC Level 2.

Relevant Projects

Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC): TRON Cognitive Search Platform

TekSynap identified a critical usability gap in NRC's public ADAMS document management system, where search accuracy and response times were generating user complaints. Working through the TekXlerate Foundry, TekSynap obtained permission to scrape a representative subset of ADAMS public data into its lab environment and engineered a purpose-built search infrastructure from the ground up.

The TRON architecture combined Azure Cognitive Search and Azure Functions for metadata extraction, cleaning, and processing with a retrieval design that used cloud-indexed metadata to surface results while pointing users to on-premises document storage for full document access, eliminating unnecessary cloud storage and ingress/egress costs without sacrificing performance. The Foundry pilot succeeded and TekSynap subsequently implemented TRON on the live public ADAMS system. The platform has since been enhanced with generative AI summarization of search results, currently in final stages before public production release. TRON established the foundation for MegaTRON and directly led to SimplifAI.

Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC): SimplifAI Conversational AI Platform

TekSynap co-developed MegaTRON as a pilot inside its TekXlerate Foundry in direct collaboration with NRC stakeholders, then engineered and deployed it into NRC's production environment as SimplifAI, now the agency's primary AI platform.

SimplifAI provides NRC staff with a secure conversational AI environment featuring a Companion Catalog of specialized AI agents, including an Inspection Report Agent, Risk Assessment Agent, and mission-specific companions, each configured with domain-specific system instructions, document groups, and reusable prompt libraries. The platform supports role-based access control, group document management, model configuration, and full audit logging. Built on Azure with OpenAI models and Azure AI Search, SimplifAI moved from concept to production through TekSynap's Foundry-to-deployment model. NRC's depth of AI adoption significantly exceeds what its formal inventory reflects, and SimplifAI is the primary engine behind that adoption.

Defense Threat Reduction Agency (DTRA): AI-Accelerated Data Processing and Discovery Platform

TekSynap deployed a GPU-accelerated AI infrastructure environment for DTRA built on three Blackwell-based compute systems running RHEL 9.6, addressing a critical backlog of scanned PDFs, digital stills, film data, and TIFFs that could not be processed at scale with existing hardware.

TekSynap developed ETL pipelines and integrated commercial containerization and search tooling alongside custom user-facing applications including a browser-based search and chatbot interface, a classification assist chatbot, and a classification marking extractor, enabling automated OCR, intelligent cataloging, and AI-assisted discovery across a large and continuously expanding data volume.

Operational impact was measurable. A workload projected to require more than 12 months with prior hardware was reduced to 90 to 120 days, even while operating under power constraints limiting the deployment to two GPUs. The architecture is designed to scale as physical infrastructure capacity expands.

Project Idea Summary

TekSynap is seeking a DOE National Laboratory partner to co-deploy a mission-ready AI platform modeled on our production implementation at the Nuclear Regulatory Commission. We propose to adapt SimplifAI as the central AI interface for a national laboratory environment, enabling researchers and staff to access specialized AI companions tailored to specific scientific workflows, collaborate through shared document groups, and query large scientific datasets through a secure conversational interface. Backend data infrastructure would be provided through our partnership with Ocient, supporting high-performance analytical workloads at scale. The platform is proven, federally deployed, and configurable to domain-specific use cases without requiring researchers to have data science expertise. TekSynap is seeking a laboratory partner with relevant scientific datasets, subject matter expertise in one or more Genesis Mission challenge areas, and experimental or test facility resources to validate AI-assisted research workflows. Our target challenge areas are AI for Scientific Reasoning, Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows, and Securing U.S. Leadership in Data Centers.
