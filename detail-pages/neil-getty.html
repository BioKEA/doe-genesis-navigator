Introduction

Neil Getty is an Assistant Computational Scientist in the Data Science and Learning Division at Argonne National Laboratory. Neil currently supports the Genesis Mission as a key contributor on the Model Consortium (Modcon) Best Practices for Scientific Workflows, Base Evaluation, and Base Multimodal teams. Neil's research focus areas include model architectures and learning algorithms, domain adaptation of large and multimodal language models, and AI for scientific discovery.

Relevant Projects

·  Genesis Mission Platform: Training multimodal scientific reasoning models end to end, supporting domain science model teams with evaluation frameworks running on American Science Cloud systems and benchmark creation, establishing best practices for scientific workflows for productive AI integration and developing training materials for domain model teams on topics including scaling analysis and model evaluation.

·  Adapting Foundation Models for Scientific Tasks: Created the multimodal, multi-task PtychoBench benchmark for X-ray ptychographic analysis. Systematically compared Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT), In-Context Learning (ICL), and Reinforcement Learning (RL) approaches for multimodal scientific domain adaptation.

·  AuroraGPT, a general purpose foundation model for science: Researched and experimented with alternative architectures to dense transformer-based LLMs including mixture of experts and state space models. Developed pipelines for automatic question generation, LLM judging, and reviewing for a scientific evaluation benchmark. Engaged scientific community crowdsourcing to produce a manual, expert-level evaluation benchmark.

·  Scaling Large Vision-Language Models for Enhanced Multimodal Comprehension in Scientific Discovery: Developed multimodal vision language models for knowledge discovery in low-dose cancer radiation therapy. Built retrieval augmented generation systems for large corpus of scientific texts.

·  MICCAI 2023 SurgToolLoc: Led team competing in surgical tool localization challenge. Expanded pipeline from last year to include object tracking for data augmentation, manual annotations, and the recent YOLOv8 model. 

·  Foundation vision and language models for scientific challenges: Finetuned recent large text-to-image models including stable diffusion on scientific datasets using textual inversion methods. Explored the compositional learning capabilities of diffusion models as well as the capabilities of foundational segmentation models including Segment Anything combined with CLIP for language embeddings. Implemented DINOv2 vision model on SambaNova hardware. 

·  AI for high-throughput functional bioimaging of rhizosphere interactions: Developed data pipeline and models for volumetric segmentation, visualization and featurization of regions of interest in APS root reconstruction volumes.

·  Prognosis modeling for Long COVID: Developed models and visualization tools to predict likelihood of a PASC/Long COVID diagnosis after SARS-CoV-2 infection. As part of the NIH Long COVID Computational Challenge, built a pipeline to curate hundreds of salient features from a harmonized data repository representing patient EHR data from 74 institutions across the US. Advised PhD student at UIC Department of Biomedical and Health Information Sciences on developing models for PASC prediction on private dataset, 2023.

·  Surgical phase and action annotation and learning: Employed deep learning classification, object detection and anatomical segmentation models for manually annotated clinical cholecystectomy videos. Oversaw project design and endoscopic video annotation.

Project Idea Summary

19A. Trustworthy Mathematical and Symbolic Reasoning
We hypothesize that trustworthy scientific reasoning requires shifting from an “AI-as-author” to an “AI-as-verifier” paradigm, treating derivation as program synthesis over formal languages rather than unconstrained text generation. We aim to develop foundation models that synthesize executable symbolic programs explaining observed phenomena, integrating formal verification kernels (e.g., Lean, SMT solvers) to ensure derivations are provably consistent with fundamental principles, physical axioms, and independently auditable.

A critical component addresses reinforcement learning reliability in scientific domains, where evaluating model-generated reasoning is often slow, expensive, or resistant to automated evaluation. Surrogate reward models risk reward hacking, scoring highly under imperfect proxies while failing true scientific objectives. We will integrate formal verification into the reward signal, use verbalized fine-tuning and reward model ensembling to align reasoning chains with ground-truth justifications, and employ test-time training so models can adapt and search candidate symbolic programs during inference. Grounding training and inference in verifiable program synthesis holds the system to correctness rather than plausibility, creating a cross-domain scientific reasoning engine for deriving, verifying, and auditing theories, and providing a stronger foundation for AI-accelerated discovery.
