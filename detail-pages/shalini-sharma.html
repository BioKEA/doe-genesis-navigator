Introduction

Independent Consultant | Semiconductor Strategy & AI-Driven Materials Discovery

I am a semiconductor industry executive and materials scientist with a Ph.D. in Chemistry and 15+ years of experience operating at the intersection of semiconductor R&D, materials science, and AI/ML. My career spans hands-on fab experience to leading enterprise-scale software platforms, most recently as Director of Global Product Management (AIx) at Applied Materials.

At Applied Materials, I led product strategy for an enterprise R&D digital ecosystem spanning Logic and Memory workflows—translating scientific intent into AI-driven process optimization and deploying cloud-based platforms used across global R&D teams.

My expertise aligns closely with the Genesis Mission’s goal of accelerating scientific discovery into deployable technologies:

AI-enabled scientific platforms: Translated semiconductor process workflows into scalable, cloud-based AI systems deployed across enterprise R&D environments.

Semiconductor roadmap & commercialization strategy: Bridging early-stage innovation to high-volume manufacturing in microelectronics and thin-film systems.

Agentic scientific workflows: Designed automated, multi-step AI-driven systems for multi-fidelity, multi-objective materials and process optimization.

Computational materials science: Deep experience applying DFT and simulation-driven approaches to guide materials design

As a consultant, I bridge R&D, AI/ML systems and industrial execution.

Project Idea Summary

I am interested in developing AI-driven, closed-loop platforms for materials discovery and semiconductor process optimization, integrating simulation, experimental data, and machine learning into unified, scalable workflows. The focus is on enabling multi-fidelity modeling and autonomous (“agentic”) systems that accelerate thin-film and microelectronics development from early-stage discovery to manufacturing.

A key challenge is bridging fragmented scientific workflows—spanning simulation (MLIP, DFT, physics based), experimentation, and data systems—into cohesive, production-grade platforms. I am particularly interested in the role of large language models (LLMs) as orchestration and reasoning layers that can enable generative exploration of materials design spaces, automate scientific workflows, and support decision-making across these systems.

I am seeking collaborators who can contribute (1) advanced AI/ML models for scientific discovery, (2) cloud/HPC infrastructure for scalable execution, (3) high-quality experimental and simulation datasets, and (4) access to experimental facilities for validation. I am particularly interested in partners from national labs, academia, and industry focused on translating scientific advances into deployable technologies.
