Introduction

Unstructured provides the GenAI-ready data layer for enterprise and government AI initiatives. Our platform ingests and transforms unstructured content — PDFs, legacy documents, scientific literature, images, and multi-modal data — into clean, structured formats ready for AI model training, RAG, and agentic workflows.

We're FedRAMP High authorized (421 controls), IL-5 ATO, ITAR-ready, and available via NASA SEWP V, ITES-SW2, and GSA MAS through Carahsoft. Unstructured originated as open-source (50M+ downloads) before evolving into a managed SaaS platform built for regulated environments.

Our platform maps directly to several Genesis Mission priorities — converting decades of legacy DOE data into AI-ready formats, curating multi-modal scientific datasets, and enabling document classification and release at scale. We're seeking to collaborate with National Labs, systems integrators, and research teams who need scalable data infrastructure to power their Genesis Mission work.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Unstructured's managed SaaS platform uses vision language models (VLMs) and ML-based partitioning to intelligently parse complex document layouts, classify content types, and extract structured data from unstructured sources. The platform supports configurable workflow nodes for document chunking, embedding preparation, and downstream integration with vector databases and AI frameworks. Originally launched as open-source (50M+ downloads), the platform is now a fully managed enterprise solution deployed in regulated federal environments.

Relevant Projects

PROJECT 1: USASOC Force Modernization Center — CRADA (2022–Present)

Since November 2022, Unstructured has co-developed its core data transformation technology under a Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA) with Army Special Operations Command (USASOC)’s AI Division. Under this partnership, Unstructured and USASOC have collaborated on ingesting and preprocessing a wide range of file types and document formats — including PDFs, HTML, images, and legacy file types — to output clean, LLM-ready data for downstream AI applications. This CRADA served as the foundational development environment for Unstructured’s commercial platform, validating our approach to automated document transformation in operationally relevant settings with real-world DoD data. The open-source library that emerged from this work has since exceeded 50 million downloads and is widely adopted across the AI/ML community.

PROJECT 2: Air Force Test Center (AFTC) — Phase II SBIR

Unstructured is working with the Air Force Test Center under a Phase II SBIR to automate the conversion of thousands of historical test reports into structured JSON format and to develop an end-to-end Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) solution. The project enables Airmen to use historical test data in conjunction with large language models to accelerate testing processes. The scope includes analyzing relevant AFTC data, generating and annotating representative synthetic data, fine-tuning Unstructured’s file transformation models on domain-specific content, and iterating against critical performance thresholds. As part of this deployment, Unstructured will be available in Iron Bank, the hardened container image repository for the USAF’s Platform One environment. This project demonstrates Unstructured’s ability to process large volumes of legacy technical documents and deliver production-grade AI data pipelines within DoD-hardened infrastructure.

PROJECT 3: Space Systems Command TAP Lab — Direct-to-Phase II SBIR

Under a Direct-to-Phase II SBIR with the Space Systems Command TAP Lab, Unstructured developed automated capabilities to extract, clean, and render text from annotated imagery data. Intelligence satellite imagery contains valuable text annotations, but historically it has been difficult to render that text from image-based formats (PDF, JPEG, PNG) into consistent, clean output ready for ingestion by downstream analytics engines. Unstructured worked with the TAP Lab to automate this multi-modal extraction pipeline, converting complex image-embedded text into structured, analysis-ready formats. This project showcased Unstructured’s ability to handle non-standard, multi-modal data types beyond traditional documents — a capability directly relevant to Genesis Mission topics requiring transformation of diverse scientific data sources including imagery, sensor outputs, and legacy visual records.
