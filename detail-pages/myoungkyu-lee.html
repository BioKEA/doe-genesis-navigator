    Introduction

    I am available to join a Genesis Mission team as a senior investigator. I contribute high-fidelity turbulence simulation data, physics-constrained AI model development, and leadership-class computing expertise. I conduct direct numerical simulation (DNS) of turbulent flows on DOE supercomputers. My DNS datasets for turbulent channel flow at friction Reynolds numbers up to 5200 are publicly available through the Johns Hopkins Turbulence Databases. I have published spectral analysis of turbulence budgets at high Reynolds numbers and established that the mean logarithmic velocity profile emerges from a self-similar energy balance. These results provide physical constraints for AI surrogate models. I can generate ground-truth DNS databases that can train and validate AI/ML models for systems governed by turbulent heat and momentum transfer.

    Project Idea Summary

    I develop high-fidelity turbulence simulation databases on DOE supercomputers and extract physical constraints from them. These databases and constraints can train and validate AI surrogate models for turbulent flows. I am looking to join a team that needs ground-truth simulation data or physics-informed model development for any focus area involving fluid mechanics, thermal transport, or energy systems. I can contribute DNS data generation, spectral analysis of turbulence physics, and experience with INCITE/ALCC computing on DOE machines.
