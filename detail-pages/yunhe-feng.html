Introduction

Responsible AI Lab at the University of North Texas develops AI systems that are both powerful and trustworthy, with core strengths in efficient generative AI, multimodal learning, scalable systems, security, privacy, safety, and transparency. We build LLM and multimodal methods for reasoning over text, code, diagrams, and figures, including long-context inference, hallucination-aware retrieval-augmented generation, edge AI, differential privacy, prompt protection, adversarial auditing, and explainable information systems.

These capabilities align closely with the Genesis Mission’s goal of using novel AI models and frameworks to accelerate scientific discovery and R&D workflows for energy, environmental, and nuclear challenges.

Our applied work in science, HPC, smart agriculture, and large-scale earth system modeling (E3SM), together with collaborations across academia, national labs, and industry, positions us to contribute trustworthy, secure, and deployable AI methods for DOE science.

Partnership Request & Project Idea

Partner Type Seeking
For-profit company
DOE National Lab
Capabilities Seeking
Datasets or data infrastructure

We seek partners with curated DOE-relevant datasets and secure data infrastructure spanning scientific codes, compiler/performance traces, simulation outputs, experimental data, sensor streams, images, and literature. Priority includes strong metadata and provenance, governed access, and scalable HPC/cloud storage and pipelines that support training, inference, benchmarking, and integrity verification for AI-driven science workflows.

Experimental or test facilities

We seek partners with facilities that let us validate AI in realistic scientific or engineering workflows, such as HPC user facilities, AI accelerator testbeds, cyber ranges, manufacturing pilot lines, genomics/phenotyping platforms, or grid/digital-twin test beds. Priority includes benchmark tasks, instrumented workflows, ground-truth measurements, and environments for robustness, reproducibility, security, and human-in-the-loop evaluation

Subject matter expertise

We seek collaborators with deep domain expertise in scientific computing, HPC software, cybersecurity, manufacturing, energy systems, biology, or materials, and with experience moving methods into operational practice. Partners should help define high-impact use cases, evaluation metrics, and validation pathways, while our lab contributes trustworthy generative AI, multimodal reasoning, privacy/security, explainability, and robust model development.

Project Idea Summary

We propose a Genesis Mission project under Topic 20: Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows, with a primary focus on Focus Area B: Data Provenance and Integrity Verification. The project will develop trustworthy, provenance-aware AI methods to verify the integrity of data used in scientific model training and inference, detect poisoning or manipulation in large-scale datasets, and support reliable AI-driven decision-making in high-throughput HPC environments. This effort builds on our lab’s strengths in generative and multimodal AI, privacy and security, adversarial auditing, robustness, and explainability. We are seeking a DOE national lab or industrial partner that can provide: (1) access to a compelling scientific workflow and representative datasets with rich metadata and provenance; (2) secure data infrastructure or HPC/test environments for realistic validation; and (3) domain expertise in areas such as materials, energy systems, manufacturing, biology, or scientific computing. Our lab will lead the AI method development, benchmarking, and trustworthy model design, while the partner will help ground and validate the solution in a high-impact DOE-relevant use case.
