Introduction

I am a Postdoctoral Appointee at Argonne National Laboratory, specializing in high-performance computational mechanics and multiphysics modeling. My work focuses on advanced numerical methods, including immersed and shifted boundary techniques, for simulating complex coupled systems in fluid dynamics, solid mechanics, and thermal transport.

I have experience developing scalable simulation frameworks on large-scale HPC systems and integrating physics-based models with data-driven approaches, including machine learning for constitutive modeling and surrogate prediction.

My research interests align with the Genesis Mission in advancing next-generation energy and materials systems, particularly in areas such as microstructure-informed modeling, nuclear energy systems, and multi-scale simulation. I am interested in collaborative opportunities that combine physics-based modeling, AI/ML, and experimental validation to accelerate scientific discovery and engineering design.

Relevant Projects

Project 1 — SCZM and SBM Integration in MOOSE (Argonne)

Contributed to the development of the MOOSE finite element framework at Argonne National Laboratory by integrating the Shifted Boundary Method (SBM) and the Shifted Cohesive Zone Method (SCZM) into the codebase. The work focused on enabling accurate simulations on non-boundary-fitted meshes, particularly for applications such as crystal plasticity.

Achievements include improving solver convergence and enhancing usability of the framework through code contributions (pull requests). The integration of SCZM enables cohesive interface modeling without requiring conformal meshing, expanding MOOSE’s capability for multiphysics simulations involving complex interfaces and evolving geometries.

Project 2 — Parasitic Extraction and Geometry Optimization (Google X)

Worked as a PhD Resident at Google X on parasitic extraction (PEX) and geometry optimization problems involving large-scale PDE-based simulations. The work involved solving PDEs in parallel using deal.II with MPI for distributed computing.

A major contribution was redesigning the meshing workflow by replacing the Gmsh-based pipeline with a Protobuf-based approach, eliminating the need for repeated meshing and improving code maintainability. In addition, a re-meshing-free optimization strategy was developed, reducing optimization iteration time by approximately 50% and significantly improving scalability and efficiency.

Project 3 — Shifted Boundary Method for Multiphysics Simulations

Developed a scalable computational framework based on the shifted boundary method (SBM) for solving partial differential equations on complex geometries using octree-based finite element methods. The work addressed challenges in fluid dynamics, heat transfer, and structural mechanics without requiring boundary-fitted meshes.

Achievements include demonstrating near-optimal parallel scalability on up to 1280 processors and enabling simulations with billions of degrees of freedom. The framework improved solution accuracy through optimal surrogate boundary selection and accelerated geometric queries using k-d trees (up to 10× speedup). The method was extended to thermal flows and thin-shell geometries, leading to multiple first-author publications.

Project Idea Summary

This project focuses on developing scalable multiphysics simulation approaches for complex engineering systems relevant to energy technologies and advanced materials. The work addresses challenges in modeling fluid flow, heat transfer, and material behavior in complex or evolving geometries, where traditional boundary-fitted methods are difficult to apply efficiently.

The approach builds on immersed and shifted boundary methods within high-performance computing frameworks to enable accurate and efficient simulations without conformal meshing. In addition, data-driven approaches, including surrogate modeling and machine learning-based constitutive modeling, are being explored to accelerate scientific discovery and improve predictive capability.

This effort aligns with Genesis Mission challenge areas such as Artificial Intelligence in Fluid Flow for Energy Technologies, Designing Materials with Predictable Functionality, and AI for Scientific Reasoning.

I am seeking collaborators with expertise in experimental validation, domain-specific applications (e.g., energy systems or materials), and AI/ML methods for scientific computing. The goal is to connect high-fidelity simulation with data and real-world applications to enable more reliable and scalable modeling for next-generation energy and materials systems.
