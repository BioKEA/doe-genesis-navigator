Introduction

Corey Oses is an Assistant Professor of Materials Science and Engineering at Johns Hopkins University and leads the Entropy for Energy computational laboratory. His group uses high-throughput simulations and AI/machine learning to accelerate discovery of materials for clean hydrogen, waste-heat conversion, energy storage, grid technologies, and nuclear-waste immobilization.

The lab’s work supports the Genesis Mission by speeding the development of next-generation energy materials. It offers expertise in computational materials design, autonomous discovery workflows, and AI-enabled screening, and is interested in partnerships that connect modeling with experimental validation and deployment.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Develops AI-enabled software, natural-language interfaces, and autonomous computational workflows for materials discovery. Capabilities include high-throughput screening, phase-stability analysis, disorder modeling, and property prediction for clean energy materials. Recent efforts include the CHAOS database and an LLM-enabled interface for intuitive exploration of high-entropy materials.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Expertise in machine learning, AI, and data-driven materials science for accelerated discovery and design. Areas include descriptor development, surrogate modeling, active learning, property prediction, and integration of first-principles simulations with ML. Applications include clean hydrogen, thermoelectrics, catalysis, energy storage, and high-entropy materials.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Extensive experience running large-scale, high-throughput computational materials workflows across research computing environments. Capabilities include workflow automation, autonomous screening, phase-stability analysis, and scalable simulation/ML pipelines for large materials design spaces. Supports reproducible first-principles and AI-enabled materials research.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Develops and uses high-throughput databases of first-principles calculations and related data infrastructure for materials discovery. Capabilities include data curation, feature generation, and creation of machine-readable resources for screening and benchmarking. Recent work includes the CHAOS database for predictive design of high-entropy oxides.

Subject matter expertise

Expertise in computational materials science for clean and renewable energy. Relevant areas include clean hydrogen, electrocatalysis, waste-heat conversion, thermoelectrics, energy storage, high-entropy oxides and ceramics, disorder-enabled materials design, and phase stability. Relevant Projects

Project 1
Led development of the CHAOS database, a high-throughput database of first-principles calculations for high-entropy oxides. Combined quantum-mechanical descriptors, composition-based metrics, and data-driven analysis to guide screening of complex oxide chemistries. Also developed an LLM-enabled natural-language interface to improve access for experimental users.

Project 2
Led computational discovery of catalyst materials for hydrogen-related applications using disorder descriptors, high-throughput modeling, and AI-guided screening. Developed workflows to identify promising compositions while reducing reliance on scarce and expensive materials. Resulted in recent publications on high-entropy fuel-cell catalysts and AI-enabled alternatives to platinum.

Project 3
Led and contributed to projects using first-principles calculations, thermodynamic modeling, and machine learning to discover materials for waste-heat conversion and other clean energy uses. Work included high-throughput screening, disorder modeling, and high-throughput databases of first-principles calculations. Outcomes include publications on high-entropy thermoelectrics and disorder-enabled ceramics.

Capabilities Seeking
Experimental or test facilities

Seeking partners with experimental synthesis, characterization, and validation capabilities for energy materials, especially high-entropy oxides, catalysts, thermoelectrics, and related functional materials. Relevant capabilities include thin-film or bulk synthesis, electrochemical testing, structural and chemical characterization, and performance evaluation to validate computational, AI-guided, and quantum computing-enabled materials predictions.

Seeking collaborators with domain expertise in hydrogen, electrocatalysis, thermoelectrics, energy storage, fusion or extreme-environment materials, and quantum computing for materials science. Especially interested in partners who can help connect computational materials discovery with experimental validation, device-relevant testing, scale-up considerations, and real-world energy applications.

Other expertise

Quantum computing for materials science, focused on its potential to complement first-principles, AI, and high-throughput computational approaches for materials discovery and modeling.

Project Idea Summary

We seek partnerships that combine AI, quantum computing for materials science, first-principles modeling, and high-throughput databases of first-principles calculations with experimental validation to accelerate discovery of next-generation energy materials. Focus areas include clean hydrogen, electrocatalysis, thermoelectrics, energy storage, fusion materials, and high-entropy materials. We are seeking partners with experimental capabilities and application expertise to validate promising candidates and advance them toward practical use.
