    Introduction

    Utilyst Inc. is an AI-native geospatial infrastructure intelligence company headquartered in Raleigh, NC. Our flagship platform, WorldGraph, delivers real-time situational awareness, predictive analytics, and agentic decision support for water utilities — helping operators manage aging infrastructure, detect contamination, and prevent failures before they impact communities.

    WorldGraph integrates IoT sensor networks, geospatial data layers, and large language model reasoning into a four-layer on-premises architecture built on open standards (PostgreSQL/PostGIS, FastAPI, React/Cesium.js). We are currently deployed at two major U.S. utilities serving over 1.8 million residents, with active use cases in predictive pump maintenance, lead contamination intelligence, and sensor quality assurance.

    Our team brings 16+ years of water infrastructure analytics expertise, 24 peer-reviewed publications, and deep partnerships with AWWA national subcommittees on AI, Cybersecurity, and Digital Twins. Utilyst is positioned to accelerate AI adoption across the water sector — a critical infrastructure domain that is significantly underserved by modern data and intelligence tools.

    We seek partnerships in computing infrastructure, federal datasets, domain AI research, and mission-aligned deployment opportunities.

    Relevant Projects

    Project 1: Predictive Maintenance Intelligence — DC Water, Bryant Street Pumping Station 2026–Present

    Deployed an AI-driven predictive maintenance system at DC Water's Bryant Street Pumping Station, serving 700,000+ residents in Washington, DC. Integrated real-time IoT sensor feeds with a geospatial asset registry to enable early detection of pump and mechanical system degradation. Developed agentic root cause analysis workflows layered over time-series sensor data, enabling proactive maintenance scheduling and reducing unplanned failure risk. Platform deployed fully on-premises in compliance with utility cybersecurity requirements. 

    Project 2: Lead Contamination Intelligence & Sensor QA/QC — Greater Cincinnati Water Works 2026–Present

    Partnered with GCWW, serving 1.1 million residents across Southwest Ohio, to build a multi-layer geospatial lead risk model integrating service line material data, historical water quality sampling, and hydraulic residence time. Developed a sensor quality assurance and anomaly detection system to identify faulty or drifting instruments across the distribution network. Accelerated LCRR compliance workflows and improved monitoring data integrity across GCWW's system. 

    Project 3: AI and Data Science for Water Utilities — Hazen and Sawyer 2019–2025

    Served as Director of Data Science, leading applied ML and analytics projects across 50+ U.S. water and wastewater utilities. Developed predictive asset management models, water quality forecasting systems, PFAS risk assessment tools, and operational analytics pipelines. Translated complex utility operational data into decision-ready intelligence, establishing repeatable ML frameworks adopted across a national engineering practice serving millions of residents.

    Project Idea Summary

    U.S. water infrastructure generates vast amounts of operational sensor data yet remains critically underserved by modern AI and predictive analytics — particularly at the intersection of water availability and energy system reliability.

    We are seeking partners to co-develop an AI-driven water monitoring and prediction system aligned with the "Predicting U.S. Water for Energy" challenge area. The project would integrate operational water system data with hydrological, climate, and energy demand datasets to build predictive models of water availability, quality risk, and infrastructure stress under variable energy and climate scenarios.

    Utilyst brings applied AI, geospatial analytics, machine learning expertise, and active deployments at utilities serving millions of U.S. residents. We are seeking partners who can contribute one or more of the following: federal or regional water and energy datasets, domain expertise in hydrology or energy-water systems, high-performance computing resources for model training and validation, or field testbed access at a utility or research facility.

    Target partners include DOE National Labs with water-energy nexus programs, academic groups in hydrology or environmental engineering, and data infrastructure providers with access to relevant public datasets.
