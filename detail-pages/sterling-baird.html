Introduction

Autonomous, remote-access, vertically automated labs for materials and manufacturing discovery. At the Vertical Cloud Lab @ Brigham Young University (Provo, UT USA), we integrate the concepts of vertical automation, cloud experimentation, and frugal twins into self-driving labs focused on discovering new structural, aerospace, and energy materials. We also conduct research on advanced optimization methods to find better materials with fewer iterations.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: Alloy Microstructure Informatics for Fehrmann Materials GmbH (2020–2021)

Developed machine learning software for a German alloys manufacturer (Fehrmann Materials GmbH, $34k contract) to predict joint strength and ductility in aluminum alloys from microstructure data. The scope included creating ML representations that link alloy composition and processing to mechanical properties, enabling targeted discovery of high-performance alloy candidates. This work built on a broader data-driven materials discovery effort that produced a comprehensive survey of 50 experimentally and computationally validated ML-guided materials discoveries across the field. The project demonstrated the viability of using informatics to accelerate alloy development for industry partners with real manufacturing constraints.

Project 2: Acceleration Consortium AI & Automation Lab (2023–2025)

As Director of Training and Programs, then Principal Investigator at the University of Toronto's Acceleration Consortium, built a $600k autonomous experimentation facility (500 line items of equipment) from scratch. Developed dozens of hardware automation APIs (solid dosing, liquid handling, sample transfer) and integrated Bayesian optimization into experimental workflows, including autonomous battery slurry optimization and accelerated electrolysis cell stress testing for fuel cell applications. Partnered with Merck KGaA to create Bayesian optimization benchmarks for chemistry and materials science. Led a team of 12 staff and 8 students across multiple research streams. This work resulted in multiple publications and open-source tools now used across the self-driving lab community.

Project 3: Vertical Cloud Lab — Autonomous Alloy Discovery via Additive Manufacturing (2025–present)

Establishing the Vertical Cloud Lab at Brigham Young University, focused on autonomous alloy discovery through additive manufacturing for aerospace applications. The lab integrates cloud-accessible experimentation platforms with remote programmatic access, enabling AI-driven experimental campaigns without requiring on-site presence. Building on prior work in Bayesian optimization, self-driving lab architecture, and materials informatics, the lab targets closed-loop discovery of novel alloy compositions with tailored mechanical properties. Currently supervising 8 undergraduate research assistants across mechanical engineering and applied mathematics, with research streams spanning adaptive experimentation, in-situ monitoring, and high-throughput characterization.
