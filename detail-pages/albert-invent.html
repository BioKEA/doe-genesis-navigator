Introduction

At Albert, we help scientists change the world by removing every obstacle between curiosity and discovery. As scientists ourselves, we are passionate about enabling researchers with the right technology to use AI effectively in the lab, so we built the first AI-native scientific platform for chemistry and materials science.

Our core capability, Albert OS, provides the digital infrastructure needed to move from fragmented data and disconnected tools to a unified, AI-ready environment—capturing every experiment, formulation, and raw material in a structured format. On top of this structured data foundation, our platform delivers automated data collection and domain-specific AI that can search institutional knowledge using natural language, predict material properties, optimize multi-variable experiments, and guide research decisions.

Beyond software, Albert brings hands-on partnership experience, helping research teams implement data standards, build AI fluency, and operationalize discovery workflows that create lasting impact. Albert OS is currently deployed at enterprise R&D organizations including Henkel, Nouryon, Kenvue, Chemours, Avient, and Solenis.

Within the Genesis Mission ecosystem, Albert is particularly interested in collaborating with labs that are looking to pilot AI-driven research using a mature scientific platform, enabling teams to move quickly without building digital tools from scratch.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Albert provides domain-specific AI tools embedded within its scientific platform to support end-to-end R&D workflows. This includes natural language interfaces (Ask Albert) for searching and synthesizing institutional knowledge, as well as predictive and optimization models (Albert Breakthrough) that guide experimental design, forecast material properties, and optimize complex, multi-variable systems. These tools operate directly on structured experimental data, enabling accurate, context-aware insights within real scientific workflows.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Albert combines deep materials science expertise with advanced machine learning and data engineering capabilities. Our team develops and deploys domain-specific models that understand experimental context, molecular structures, and structure–property relationships. We specialize in building AI systems that learn from complex scientific data, continuously improving as new experiments are conducted, and enabling reliable, quantitative predictions for real-world scientific applications.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Albert provides a structured data infrastructure purpose-built for scientific research, helping teams move from fragmented data and disconnected tools to a unified, AI-ready environment. Our scientific platform captures every experiment, formulation, and raw material in a structured format, creating a unified system of record that connects formulations, materials, processes, and results across workflows. 

Other expertise

Albert brings significant experience implementing data and AI systems within large, complex R&D organizations. We work alongside scientific teams to establish data standards, integrate workflows, and drive adoption through training and change management. Our approach ensures that AI is not just deployed, but operationalized—enabling organizations to scale from initial pilots to sustained, organization-wide impact.

Relevant Projects

Henkel (global adhesives and materials science leader)

Scope: Albert partnered with Henkel to implement a global digital R&D infrastructure across 2,800+ scientists, replacing fragmented systems with a unified, structured data foundation. This included deploying a system of record for experimental data, enabling integration with automated laboratories, and supporting AI-driven experimentation and collaboration across 36 countries.

Achievements: Henkel established a single source of truth for millions of structured data points, enabling global collaboration and AI/ML applications. The transformation has contributed to doubling speed to market while significantly improving efficiency across R&D. In automated lab environments, testing throughput increased 6x, turnaround times decreased from weeks to same-day, and scientists can now test 50+ formulations per week—expanding experimental capacity and accelerating innovation cycles.

Nouryon (global specialty chemicals company)

Scope: Albert partnered with Nouryon to develop BeautyCreations, an AI-enabled natural language portal that integrates structured formulation data with domain-specific models to support customer-facing formulation discovery. The solution was designed to extend Nouryon’s R&D capabilities beyond internal teams and into a scalable digital experience for customers and partners.

Achievements: BeautyCreations enables chemists to generate tailored formulations in minutes based on specific performance and application requirements, improving the speed, consistency, and accessibility of formulation development.

Applied Molecules (advanced materials innovator)

Scope: Albert worked with Applied Molecules to implement a structured data and AI-driven R&D environment, enabling the use of domain-specific AI models for formulation optimization and experimental design.

Achievements: By combining structured data with AI-powered formulation tools, Applied Molecules reduced development timelines from approximately three months to two days. This increase in speed allows the organization to pursue higher-risk, higher-reward projects and explore broader design spaces, fundamentally improving innovation agility and competitiveness.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We are particularly interested in working with labs that are iterating experimentally across multi-variable material systems, such as tuning composition, processing conditions, or microstructure to hit performance targets, and the bottleneck is how efficiently you can move through that design space (polymer labs, composite materials, functional coatings, energy materials, etc).

Project Idea Summary

Albert is interested in partnering on projects that demonstrate how integrated data infrastructure and domain-specific AI can accelerate materials discovery and scale scientific innovation.

Designing materials with predictable functionality:
We aim to enable closed-loop, AI-driven discovery workflows where structured experimental data and AI models operate within the same system. This includes inverse design approaches, where target properties guide new materials design, and experimental results continuously improve model performance.

Achieving AI-driven autonomous laboratories:
We are interested in integrating AI directly into experimental workflows, enabling scientists to design, execute, and learn from experiments within a unified environment. Our platform also supports automated data capture from lab instruments and has served as the digital backbone for high-throughput automated labs, demonstrating significant gains in testing speed and productivity.

Reenvisioning advanced manufacturing and industrial productivity:
We seek to bridge the gap between lab-scale discovery and commercial application by enabling data-driven decision-making across R&D workflows, helping teams navigate complex performance, cost, and regulatory constraints.

We are particularly interested in partners who are looking to pilot AI-enabled discovery workflows using a proven scientific platform as your digital infrastructure.
