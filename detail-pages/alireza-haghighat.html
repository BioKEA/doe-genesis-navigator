    Introduction

    Dr. Alireza Haghighat is the Robert E. Hord, Jr. Endowed Professor and Director of the Nuclear Engineering Program at Virginia Tech, housed within the Department of Mechanical Engineering. From 2001 to 2009, he served as Chair of the Nuclear & Radiological Engineering (NRE) Department at the University of Florida (UF) and directed the UF Training Reactor (UFTR) from 2008 to 2010. Prior to his tenure at UF, Dr. Haghighat was a faculty member in the Nuclear Engineering Department at Pennsylvania State University for 15 years. 

    A Fellow of the American Nuclear Society (ANS), Dr. Haghighat leads the Virginia Tech Theory Transport Group (VT3G). Over his distinguished 38-year career, he has been a pioneer in developing advanced particle transport methodologies and computer codes for modeling and simulation of nuclear systems. His innovative work has impacted diverse fields, including nuclear reactor analysis, security and safeguards, and medical applications. Among his significant contributions are the development of advanced software tools such as PENTRAN, A3MCNP, TITAN, INSPCT-s, AIMS, TITAN-IR, and RAPID, the latter of which features a state-of-the-art virtual reality system (VRS) web application. Ongoing efforts include development physics-informed AI/ML algorithms using RAPID and measurements for design, optimization, and online monitoring nuclear reactor systems. 

    Dr. Haghighat has authored over 280 publications. He is author of the 'Monte Carlo Methods for Particle Transport'.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    AI models, tools, or software

    We are developing physics-based AI-assisted models using our RAPID-AI code system and experimental data for multiphysics modeling of nuclear reactors, and their monitoring and diagnostic during operation. This is a part of our major initiative, POLARIS_TM, which aimed at development of a combined physics and digital reactor system.

    Experimental or test facilities

    Under a MOU with the Josef Stefan Institute, we have performing validation of various algorithms within our RAPID code system, and currently in the process of validating our physics-based AI-assisted model, with eventual implementation of the AI-assisted tools for reactor operation, first in an advisory mode, followed by its incorporation into the reactor control system algorithm.

    Subject matter expertise

    Advanced particle transport methods and codes for application to nuclear systems, nuclear reactor physics, design and optimization, and effective design of neutron detection system for high-fidelity monitoring of nuclear systems. Development of physics-based AI-assisted neutronics tools for high-fidelity determination fuel burnup, reactor kinetics parameters, reactor criticality and power distribution. 

    Other resources/tools

    We have developed advanced neutronics codes for application of modeling nuclear systems. These tools not only are very accurate, but also they are very fast, and therefore enable development of physics-based AI-assisted tools for nuclear systems.

    Project Idea Summary

    This project proposes the development of a physics-based AI-assisted digital reactor ecosystem for autonomous monitoring and operation of advanced nuclear reactors. The system integrates high-fidelity neutronics (RAPID-AI), thermal-hydraulics, multi-modal detection system (CHANDLER), and immersive visualization (VRS-RAPID) within a unified digital twin framework. The primary objective is to enable real-time prediction, anomaly detection, and human-in-the-loop decision support, ultimately leading to autonomous reactor operation.

    In partnership with a nuclear company, I seek funding, or opportunities to jointly pursue funding, to develop AI-assisted tools tailored to their specific reactor design and to support the creation of a high-fidelity, operational (“real”) digital twin for that system.
