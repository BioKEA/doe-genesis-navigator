Introduction

Our Mission
At Ask Sage, our mission is to accelerate decision-making, enhance operational efficiency, and drive mission outcomes by providing a trusted AI platform that ingests organizational knowledge and data, delivers deep analysis, and generates factual, defensible insights — all within a secure and compliant environment.
 

About Ask Sage
Ask Sage is a cutting-edge Artificial Intelligence (AI) platform purpose-built to empower government agencies, defense organizations, and mission-critical enterprises with secure, intelligent, and actionable insights. Designed from the ground up with security, compliance, and operational excellenceat its core, Ask Sage enables organizations to harness the transformative power of AI while maintaining the highest standards of data protection and regulatory adherence

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Ask Sage seeks access to HPC and GPU computing resources at partner labs  and to the American Science Cloud (AmSC) infrastructure to deploy, test, and benchmark AI workflows at the scale required by Genesis Mission standards. Ask Sage will contribute its own FedRAMP High cloud environment (hosted on Azure Government) as the secure inference and orchestration layer, and seeks lab HPC resources specifically for large-scale model training, adversarial simulation, and provenance verification workloads that exceed commercial cloud performance requirements.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Ask Sage seeks access to representative DOE science AI datasets and model artifacts — including existing scientific workflow telemetry, training corpora for domain models, and legacy nuclear document corpora  under formal data-use agreements to be executed during Phase I Months 1–3. For Topic 4H (ATLAS), Ask Sage specifically seeks a pilot corpus of 10,000–50,000 legacy documents spanning multiple decades and formats, along with SME-annotated gold-standard subsets for ECI, UCNI, and CUI classification training and evaluation

Experimental or test facilities

Ask Sage seeks access to live DOE science workflow environments at partner labs —national security AI testbeds — to serve as realistic deployment and validation environments for SENTINEL (Topic 20) and MOSAIC (Topic 19C). These facilities provide the authentic multi-institutional, federated data flow conditions necessary to demonstrate AI advantage metrics under Genesis Mission operational requirements, rather than synthetic or simulated environments.

Project Idea Summary

Executive Summary

Ask Sage, Inc. (operating through its Big Bear AI division) proposes a research and development program to advance the science and practice of securing AI-driven scientific workflows across the DOE complex. The proposed team pairs Ask Sage's production-hardened, FedRAMP High-authorized GenAI platform with the domain science and computational security expertise of [PNNL / LLNL / ANL — select lab partner], and optionally a university research partner, to address all three focus areas under Topic 20: adversarial robustness (20A), data provenance and integrity verification (20B), and real-time attack detection and mitigation (20C).

Ask Sage brings a singular credential to this effort: it is among the very few commercial AI platform providers operating at FedRAMP High authorization — the same security baseline required to process Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) and support operations across DOE, NNSA, and the broader national security enterprise. This is not a theoretical compliance posture. Ask Sage is actively deployed across federal agencies and national security customers, meaning the adversarial threat models, attack surface realities, and operational constraints that motivate Topic 20 are not abstractions — they are problems the team faces and mitigates in production today.
