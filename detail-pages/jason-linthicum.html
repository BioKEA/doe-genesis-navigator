Introduction

Cloudflare supports U.S. Federal customers today in a variety of capacities. Overall, Cloudflare’s approach focuses on three core pillars designed to maximize impact:

Secure Accessibility: Our global network provides the connectivity with post quantum encryption and high-speed environments necessary for researchers to work together across the DOE, the Genesis Mission partnership, and other institutions.

Innovation with Seamless Collaboration: Cloudflare provides government and researchers with a dedicated AI development environment for their AI models. This environment features serverless, edge-based inference, eliminating the need for researchers to manage infrastructure. Instead, they can focus entirely on the development and testing of their applications and agents.

Integrated Security: All capabilities are wrapped in Zero Trust controls, protecting the data, the models and the users while allowing sharing of resources where it is needed. Cloudflare ensures that open collaboration and rapid innovation never come at the cost of security or data integrity.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Cloudflare's AI platform includes AI Gateway (LLM observability, rate limiting, and model fallback), Firewall for AI (real-time prompt injection and abuse detection), Workers AI (serverless GPU inference across 330+ global locations), Vectorize (distributed vector database for semantic search and anomaly detection), and an Agents SDK for building stateful, multi-step AI workflows. Together these tools enable research teams to deploy, monitor, and secure AI-driven workflows at internet scale.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Cloudflare's global network spans 330+ cities across 125 countries, providing serverless edge compute (Cloudflare Workers), GPU inference (Workers AI), and S3-compatible object storage with no egress fees (R2). R2 explicitly supports data lakes, ML model artifacts, and large dataset storage. This infrastructure enables real-time data processing and AI workload execution at global scale without dedicated hardware management.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Cloudflare's network processes traffic representing 20%+ of global internet traffic, generating real-time telemetry on AI model traffic patterns, adversarial attack signatures, and network anomalies at a scale unavailable to academic or government institutions. Aggregate and anonymized derivatives of this telemetry are available to research partners under negotiated data sharing agreements.

Subject matter expertise

Cloudflare brings operational expertise in network security, Zero Trust architecture, AI application security, and large-scale distributed systems. Cloudflare is a charter member of CISA's Joint Cyber Defense Collaborative (JCDC) and holds an active CRADA (CN-24-0564) with NIST-NCCoE. The company operates FedRAMP Moderate-authorized (FedRAMP High-in-Progress) infrastructure serving 40+ federal agency customers and 18 of the 24 Genesis Mission partners.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: NIST NCCoE - Migration to Post-Quantum Cryptography Source: nccoe.nist.gov/applied-cryptography/migration-to-pqc, Cloudflare listed by name as a consortium participant

Cloudflare participated as a named technology collaborator in the NIST NCCoE Migration to Post-Quantum Cryptography project (NIST SP 1800-38), executed under a Cooperative Research and Development Agreement. The project demonstrated practices for migrating from quantum-vulnerable public-key cryptographic algorithms to NIST-standardized post-quantum algorithms. Cloudflare contributed to two workstreams: cryptographic discovery (inventorying quantum-vulnerable algorithm usage across systems) and interoperability testing of post-quantum cryptographic algorithm implementations across vendor products. The project produced draft guidance published for public comment in 2023–2025. Cloudflare's participation is consistent with its public commitment to deploying post-quantum encryption across its global network.

Cloudflare is a named National Cybersecurity Excellence Partnership (NCEP) partner - listed with logo on the NCCoE's formal partners page at nccoe.nist.gov/get-involved/collaborate-us-technical-contributions. NCEP partners sign a Memorandum of Understanding with NIST-NCCoE, pledge hardware/software/expertise, and contribute to projects.

AI models, tools, or software

Cloudflare seeks a partner institution to leverage AI Cloud, our managed inference service. We provide "one-click" access to 50,000+ models, including curated open-source options (Llama, Stable Diffusion), commercial models, and private hosting for DOE/NNSA.

By utilizing Cog, an open-source containerization tool, we simplify the "Bring Your Own Model" (BYOM) process, allowing developers to seamlessly run custom models on Cloudflare’s serverless infrastructure. Whether you need models to work with or have your own to deploy, our platform is built to scale your AI mission.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

The lead institution must supply the PI and the scientific research competency. DOE reviewers will scrutinize whether the team can produce publishable results. Cloudflare brings the instrument; the partner brings the researchers who know how to use it.

Datasets or data infrastructure

The lead institution's existing scientific datasets - network telemetry from SC user facilities, data center operational data, HPC workload traces - combined with Cloudflare's network data is what makes the research proposal compelling.

Experimental or test facilities

DOE/NNSA national labs have the physical research network infrastructure, HPC facilities, and scientific user facilities that Cloudflare cannot provide. For Topic 10 this means data center testbeds; for Topics 20 and 11B this means operational research networks across SC program domains. This is the capability gap Cloudflare most needs filled.

Subject matter expertise

Specifically: HPC network operations (11B), AI workflow security in scientific computing environments (20), and data center energy systems (10). These are domain areas where national labs have decades of operational knowledge Cloudflare does not have.

Project Idea Summary

Cloudflare proposes to serve as an industry partner on a Phase I Genesis Mission application targeting one of three focus areas: Topic 10 (Data Center Load Flexibility or Thermal Management), Topic 20 (Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows), or Topic 11B (AIOps for Network Operations).

Cloudflare's contribution is a production-grade research instrument unavailable to any national lab or university independently: real-time telemetry from a global network processing 20%+ of internet traffic, including AI inference request patterns, adversarial attack signatures, anomaly detection data, and network performance metrics across 330+ cities. This data, combined with Cloudflare's AI Gateway, Firewall for AI, Workers AI, Vectorize, and Agents SDK, provides the technical substrate for measurable AI advantage across all three topics.

Cloudflare will contribute mandatory 20% industry cost share, prepare all required submission materials independently (budget, biosketch, Letter of Commitment), and support operationalization of research outputs at global scale for Phase II.

We are seeking a lead institution, preferably a DOE/NNSA National Laboratory or research university with an active SC program relationship, that brings: an eligible PI with tenure or permanent appointment; operational research network or data center testbed facilities; domain expertise in AI security, network operations, or data center energy systems; and existing scientific datasets from SC user facilities.
