Introduction

Newfound Materials builds physics-driven AI models to predict and optimize the synthesis of inorganic materials. Our core technology is a retrosynthesis engine that generates, ranks, and evaluates thousands of candidate synthesis routes using thermodynamic reasoning: not black-box pattern matching. Given a target material, our platform identifies viable precursor combinations, reaction conditions, and processing sequences, each with a quantified confidence score based on thermodynamic feasibility.

Key capabilities:

- AI-driven retrosynthesis and synthesis pathway prediction for inorganic materials
- Thermodynamic analysis of reaction feasibility, intermediate phase formation, and byproduct selectivity
- Synthetic accessibility scoring of target materials across diverse processing routes
- Integration of multi-modal synthesis, characterization, and processing data into predictive models
- Text mining and structured extraction of synthesis procedures from scientific literature

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Physics-driven AI retrosynthesis engine that generates, ranks, and evaluates thousands of candidate synthesis pathways for inorganic materials. Given a target material, the platform identifies viable precursor combinations, reaction conditions, and processing sequences, each with a quantified feasibility score. This engine is frequently used to rank the synthetic accessibility of compounds and to simulate reaction pathways on physical lattices.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Expertise in graph-based chemical reaction networks, thermodynamic selectivity analysis, and physics-informed ML for materials synthesis prediction. Core methods: reaction pathway enumeration over high-dimensional phase spaces, active learning loops integrating AI predictions with experimental feedback, and multi-component thermodynamic reasoning for complex feedstocks (5-10+ species). Experience building and deploying these models at LBNL and UC Berkeley, including integration with the A-Lab autonomous synthesis facility. Team includes PhD-level researchers in materials science.

Subject matter expertise

Inorganic materials synthesis, solid-state chemistry, AI, and computational materials science. Deep expertise in synthesis thermodynamics: predicting which reactions are favorable, what intermediate/byproduct phases form, and how to steer selectivity. Specific experience with battery cathodes, piezoelectrics, multiferroics, and permanent magnet phases. Published on predictive synthesis of ternary/quaternary oxides with collaborators at LBNL, Colorado State, Columbia, and Michigan.

Relevant Projects

1. Chemical Reaction Network for Solid-State Synthesis Prediction (LBNL/UC Berkeley, 2019-2023)

Developed a graph-based computational framework that enumerates and ranks thousands of candidate solid-state synthesis pathways using first-principles thermodynamic data. Applied to BaTiO3, identifying 82,985 possible synthesis reactions and demonstrating that thermodynamic selectivity metrics can predict which routes yield phase-pure products. Validated against experimental literature for YMnO3, Y2Mn2O7, Fe2SiS4, and YBa2Cu3O6.5. Published in Nature Communications (2021) and ACS Central Science (2023). This work forms the scientific foundation of Newfound Materials' retrosynthesis engine.

2. A-Lab Autonomous Synthesis Integration (LBNL, 2022-2023)

Contributed to the development and deployment of the A-Lab, an autonomous robotic laboratory at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. Integrated computational synthesis planning with robotic execution and automated XRD characterization. Over 17 days of continuous operation, the A-Lab synthesized 41 novel compounds from 58 targets. Published in Nature (2023). This experience directly informs Newfound's approach to closed-loop AI-guided synthesis.

3. Reaction Pathway Simulation for Large Chemicals Company (2024-present)

Deployed Newfound's retrosynthesis engine to simulate and optimize synthesis pathways of catalysts for a major chemicals company. Demonstrates the commercial applicability of Newfound's AI platform to industrial-scale materials manufacturing.

4. Synthesis Optimization for Battery Materials Company (2024-present)

Applied Newfound's pathway prediction and optimization platform to battery materials synthesis for a commercial battery company. Work focuses on simulating reaction pathways for battery materials, optimizing processing conditions to improve phase purity and reduce energy input, and evaluating alternative precursor sources. Validates the platform's utility for critical energy materials and domestic supply chain applications.

Project Idea Summary

Topic 1-B: AI-Driven Materials Processing (BES)

We seek partners with in situ characterization capabilities (especially in situ XRD, XAS, PDF) to demonstrate closed-loop, feedback-controlled synthesis. Our AI predicts phase evolution during a reaction; in situ data is compared in real time; the system adjusts synthesis parameters when intermediates deviate from the predictions. We are interested in university partners with lab-scale in situ XRD for Phase 1, and synchrotron user facilities (SLAC SSRL, APS, ALS) for Phase 2.

**Topic 3-B: AI-Enabled Materials Discovery and Engineering (AMMTO)**

We seek partners in geochemistry, mineral processing, and critical materials science to explore a novel approach: using our retrosynthesis engine to design pathways that go directly from mineral feedstocks to functional materials, bypassing conventional multi-step extraction and purification.

**Topic 12-F: AI-Enabled Materials Discovery, Development, and Qualification (AMMTO)**

We seek partners in materials informatics, NLP for materials science, and experimental synthesis to build the first large-scale, standardized, open synthesis dataset for inorganic materials. Our platform generates computationally predicted pathways; we need experimental validation partners and data curation expertise to create a community resource and benchmark for AI-driven synthesis prediction.
