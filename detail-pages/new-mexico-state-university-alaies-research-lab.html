Introduction

Alaie’s research group at New Mexico State University, led by Seyedhamidreza Alaie, Assistant Professor of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, works at the intersection of miniaturized systems, advanced manufacturing, biomedical devices, and applied machine learning. The lab is hands-on and multidisciplinary, combining design, fabrication, testing, and data-driven modeling, and reflects Dr. Alaie’s training across mechanical engineering, optical science, computer science, and engineering, including three M.S. degrees and a Ph.D. The group has led NIH-supported research, including an R21 Trailblazer project on wireless intracardiac pressure monitoring, and has published in peer-reviewed journals such as Nature Communications, Nature Biomedical Engineering, Advanced Engineering Materials, and Machine Learning with Applications. Dr. Alaie has also developed new academic courses, including Applied Deep Learning for Mechanical Engineers. In the Genesis Mission, the group is developing a compact scientific interface to help autonomous labs and AI tools share scientific context across platforms with less custom integration. The group is especially seeking partners with scientific or engineering workflows that involve high scientific complexity and require deep review of the literature, strong scientific judgment, and decisions across multiple tools or lab platforms.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Alaie’s research group develops models, tools, and software for literature-intensive scientific workflows that require deep scientific judgment across multiple tools. For Genesis, the group is building a compact scientific interface to help AI tools and autonomous labs share scientific state, provenance, and relationships with less custom integration, while supporting literature synthesis, hypothesis generation, evidence tracking, and reproducible multi-step scientific workflows.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Alaie’s research group has practical AI/ML and data science expertise in regression for experimental and sensor-generated data. The group has developed and evaluated dense, CNN, and ResNet-style regressors tested on datasets of ultrasound carrying measurement information passively.  

Datasets or data infrastructure

Alaie’s group maintains data infrastructure for ultrasound-based passive pressure sensing, including a LabVIEW-controlled benchtop acquisition system for synchronized pressure control, ultrasound capture, and metadata logging. The documented dataset includes millions of labeled FFT echo records across 0–158 mmHg for AI/ML development in an NIH-supported project, with supporting code and research repositories available through GitHub and OSF. 

Experimental or test facilities

Alaie’s group has benchtop experimental facilities for fabrication and testing of miniaturized sensors and biomedical devices. Current capabilities include a LabVIEW-controlled ultrasound/pressure characterization setup with a 3D-printed pressure chamber, ultrasound pulser, 10 MHz transducer, oscilloscope, digital pressure regulation, and precision pressure sensing for passive acoustic transponders, 3 robotic systems for automated measurements, and additive manufacturing and custom fixtures for polymeric films, Nitinol structures, and related device prototypes.

Subject matter expertise

Alaie’s group has subject matter expertise in automated measurement systems, including robotic/remote ultrasound platforms and LabVIEW-controlled benchtop setups for synchronized pressure control, sensing, and data acquisition. The group also develops LLM-agent scientific workflows for literature retrieval, synthesis, hypothesis refinement, and evidence tracking, and builds regression models (dense, CNN, and ResNet-style) for inference from complex experimental signals.

Relevant Projects

Alaie’s research group has practical experience building and managing machine-learning-ready experimental datasets and supporting data infrastructure for biomedical and engineering research. In an NIH-supported R21 project on wireless intracardiac pressure monitoring, the group developed a LabVIEW-controlled benchtop platform that synchronizes pressure control, ultrasound acquisition, and metadata logging for passive acoustic transponders. The documented dataset includes abeled FFT echo records collected at randomly selected pressures from 0–158 mmHg. Each datapoint contains a 288-point frequency-domain signal together with metadata such as time, date, setting pressure, and measured pressure. The group has also organized these data for model development through defined training, validation, and test splits, and maintains supporting research infrastructure through GitHub and OSF for code, research prototypes, and open hardware documentation. More broadly, the lab’s work across miniaturized systems, advanced manufacturing, biomedical devices, and applied machine learning positions it to generate, curate, and analyze structured datasets relevant to Genesis workflows.

Project Idea Summary

Our research group is working on Genesis Challenge 11 (AI-Driven Autonomous Laboratories), especially Focus Area 11-C, which is about helping AI move from finding patterns to building real scientific understanding. The project asks a simple question: when different AI tools, software systems, and lab platforms pass work from one step to the next, can they keep the scientific meaning intact? Today, important details such as units, assumptions, where the information came from, and experimental context often get lost, making complex workflows unreliable. The group is developing and testing a compact shared scientific format to carry this context across literature review, hypothesis generation, critique, planning, execution, and evidence updates. Alaie’s group is seeking a partner with a real scientific or engineering workflow that is demanding and spans multiple tools or platforms—especially one requiring deep literature review, multistep reasoning, hypothesis generation, and expert judgment—so the idea can be tested on an important real-world use case.
