Introduction

We’re a proud partner of the Genesis Mission, using our deep expertise in AI,
computational modeling, and digital engineering to strengthen U.S. energy
resilience and enable a future powered by fusion energy. Our involvement with
the Genesis Mission is primarily focused on using AI for fusion - speeding the
development of a fusion pilot plant. We’re developing a new computational
infrastructure called STELLAR AI to speed up computer simulations, a digital twin
or virtual model of the Lab’s primary fusion experiment: the National Spherical
Torus Experiment-Upgrade (NSTX-U), and a critical foundational model for
fusion systems. We’re also leading a national dialogue around AI4Fusion.
 

PPPL’s research brings together theory, computation, and experimentation to
advance the development of practical fusion energy. Across its departments,
PPPL integrates foundational plasma physics, high-fidelity simulation, AI, and
large-scale experimental research to understand and control complex, multi-
scale plasma behavior. This work spans predictive modeling of plasma stability,
the development of advanced computational frameworks and physics-informed
AI, and experimental validation across a global network of tokamak facilities. By
combining data from world-leading experiments with advanced modeling and
machine learning, PPPL is building a data-rich laboratory that accelerates
scientific discovery, supports reactor design, and drives progress toward
commercially viable fusion energy.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

PPPL offers physics-informed AI, surrogate modeling, and large-scale scientific simulation. Computational tools supporting this work include TRANSP (for the interpretive and predictive analysis of tokamak plasmas), SOLPS-ITER (simulation code for the path of the particles under different conditions), and others.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

PPPL has experience in the development and deployment of surrogate models of our high-fidelity tools. Key scientists in the field include Michael Churchill, Shantenu Jha, Alvaro Sanchez-Villar.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

PPPL develops advanced computational frameworks and machine learning models for plasma physics, supported by access to high-performance computing resources such as the STELLAR cluster at Princeton University, and emerging platforms such as the Simulation, Technology and Experiment Leveraging Learning-Accelerated Research enabled by Artificial Intelligence (STELLAR-AI) project. STELLAR-AI will accelerate the design and deployment of a fusion pilot plant that can produce electric power from fusion in the late 2030s. The platform pairs AI systems with high-performance computing (HPC). 

Datasets or data infrastructure

PPPL provides access to data from tokamaks around the world, along with a suite of modeling codes. The laboratory generates large amounts of simulation data spanning extant, planned, and notional magnetic confinement configurations. These simulations encompass extended MHD, kinetic MHD, gyrokinetics for core and edge plasmas, and hybrid models with fully kinetic ions and fluid electrons. PPPL shares data and compute ecosystems through the American Science Cloud, enabling collaborative, AI-driven research and emphasizes AI-ready data through documentation, metadata, and coordinated management.

Experimental or test facilities

PPPL operates NSTX-U, the largest spherical tokamak in the U.S. The laboratory also collaborates with a range of major international tokamak facilities, including ASDEX Upgrade, which serves as a testbed for large-scale fusion experiments, and COMPASS-U, a medium-sized, high-magnetic field experimental tokamak. Additional collaborations extend to DIII-D, ITER, JT-60SA, KSTAR, MAST-U, SMART, SPARC, ST40, and WEST.

Subject matter expertise

PPPL researchers bring expertise across a range of areas, including AI/ML, plasma–materials interactions, wall conditioning, plasma control and diagnostics, MHD instabilities, plasma scenarios and transport, liquid metals, and divertor design. The laboratory is also a leader in the study of burning plasmas, with particular strength in understanding turbulence, energetic particles, and their interactions. PPPL is among the few institutions worldwide capable of performing kinetic simulations of edge plasmas, and maintains additional expertise in high-fidelity extended MHD.

Relevant Projects

Stellar-AI Platform Development: An advanced computing platform supporting large-scale AI workloads for fusion science. This effort integrates high-performance computing with machine learning to enable training on large simulation and experimental datasets, supporting collaboration across national labs, academia, and industry.

The SciDAC collaboration “Computational Evaluation and Design of Actuators for Core-Edge Integration (CEDA)”: focuses on the use of magnetic field geometry, impurity injection and wall coating to achieve core-edge integration.

The SciDAC collaboration “Center for Edge of Tokamak Optimization (CETOP)”: aims to mitigate the damage due to edge-localized modes (ELMs), explosive events observed in tokamaks in high-confinement mode.

The FIRE Collaborative “Mitigating Risks from Abrupt Confinement Loss (MIRACL)”: is the largest US collaboration on the avoidance and mitigation of disruptive events in tokamaks and stellarators.

American Science Cloud Contributions: ASC connects distributed computing resources, datasets, and AI tools. This enables collaborative, large-scale AI-for-Science research across institutions, supporting advances in fusion energy and complex systems modeling.

Physics-Informed AI for Plasma Modeling: The development of machine learning models that incorporate physical laws to improve predictive accuracy and generalization in plasma simulations. These approaches enable efficient modeling of complex, multi-scale systems and support both theoretical and experimental research.

Project Idea Summary

Two AI-driven projects advance real-time control and modeling for fusion.

(Tokamak Experimental Science) Challenge Focus Area 5B, Plasma Facing Materials: Physics-informed AI to improve tokamak performance by integrating diagnostic data and simulations to infer plasma-wall interactions and enable dynamic coupling between edge and core plasma behavior. Using ML to accelerate the design of advanced plasma-facing components through efficient modeling of plasma-material interactions.

(Computer Science) Challenge Focus Area xx: Developing adaptive AI systems for real-time decision-making in resource-constrained environments. Leveraging surrogate models such as Fourier Neural Networks, Physics-Informed Neural Networks, and Principal Component Regression to enable dynamic model selection for accurate, low-latency predictions under varying computational and connectivity constraints.
