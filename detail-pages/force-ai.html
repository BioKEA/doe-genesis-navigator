Introduction

Signal Lake / Resonance is the architecture Force AI is building for Topic 14: Unifying Physics from Quarks to the Cosmos.

Thesis: every physical instrument produces frequency-structured signals — LIGO strain, LHC calorimetry, Fermi gamma, IRIS seismic, solar-wind plasma, optical photometry — and cross-domain coupling is detectable in raw signals through a single unified spectral tokenization. Existing domain-specific foundation models (OmniLearn/OmniCosmos for particles, AION-1 for astronomy, Walrus for PDE dynamics, Universal Spectral Tokenizer for EM spectra) operate within their home domains. None crosses the particle-to-cosmic boundary on raw observational data. That is the gap Topic 14 names and Signal Lake closes.

Preliminary evidence — 25 pre-registered experiments, public data, CPU only: 90/90 pairwise couplings on panchromatic GW170817 across nine wavelength bands plus gravity, cross-event gap +1.92; GW↔gamma +0.932, GW↔seismic -0.216 learned without labels in six seconds; 5-storm solar-seismic coupling Fisher combined p<0.0001, validated theoretically by Kyoto (Mizuno et al. 2026). DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18818197.

Phase I (9 months): four pre-registered benchmark tasks — multi-messenger retrieval, particle-to-cosmic transfer, sample-efficient detection, cross-domain anomaly.

Seeking: (1) DOE National Lab (Cat 1) — HEP, multi-messenger astrophysics, particle-detector ML; (2) academic co-PI (Cat 3) — physics or astronomy with cross-domain ML experience.
