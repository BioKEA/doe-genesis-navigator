    Introduction

    The Parashar Lab at the University of Delaware (Department of Chemistry & Biochemistry; affiliated with Medical and Molecular Sciences, College of Health Sciences) is led by Vijay K. Parashar, Ph.D. (Assistant Professor). We specialize in experimental structural biology, including cryo-electron microscopy (single particle analysis), X-ray crystallography, and biochemical characterization of bacterial signaling enzymes. Our research focuses on c-di-AMP signaling, CRISPR-associated proteins, and quorum-sensing systems in Gram-positive bacteria. We seek AI/ML and computational partners to accelerate the gene-to-structure-to-function discovery cycle in support of the Genesis Mission RFA DE-FOA-0003612, Topic 2 (Scaling the Biotechnology Revolution), Focus Area 2A (Biomolecular Science).

    Offerings
    Datasets or data infrastructure

    Structural datasets from cryo-EM SPA (near-atomic resolution macromolecular complexes) and X-ray crystallography (Rap phosphatases, Rgg receptors with cyclic peptide inhibitors, cOA-bound CRISPR transcription factors). Protein-ligand interaction data for cyclic dinucleotides (c-di-AMP, cOAs, cADPR) bound to signaling proteins. Datasets from bacterial second messenger pathway reconstitution experiments (c-di-AMP, cOA pathways).

    Experimental or test facilities

    Full pipeline for cryo-EM SPA (near-atomic resolution structures of signaling and defense system complexes) and X-ray crystallography (cloning through crystal optimization and structure solution). Anti-phage defense system structural biology (Type III CRISPR Cad2, Thoeris II ThsB/ThsC). Recombinant protein production pipeline for 10+ published systems. Structure-guided inhibitor discovery platform.

    Subject matter expertise

    Structural biology expertise: cryo-EM SPA and X-ray crystallography of bacterial signaling/defense complexes. Bacterial second messenger signaling (c-di-AMP, cOA pathways). Anti-phage defense systems (Type III CRISPR, Thoeris II). Protein-ligand interactions (cyclic dinucleotides, cADPR). Structure-guided inhibitor discovery (cyclic peptide QS inhibitors, PNAS 2015). Recombinant protein production for 10+ published systems.

    Project Idea Summary

    We seek partners for a Genesis Mission Phase I proposal under Focus Area 2A (Biomolecular Science). Our lab specializes in structural biology of bacterial signaling and defense systems using cryo-EM and X-ray crystallography. We aim to apply AI-driven approaches to accelerate structure determination and functional characterization of anti-phage defense systems — a rapidly expanding area where structural data is severely lacking. Our existing structural pipeline (cloning → expression → purification → crystallography/cryo-EM → functional validation) provides an ideal testbed for demonstrating quantifiable AI acceleration. We are seeking AI/ML computational partners and national laboratory collaborators to build a competitive multi-institutional team.
