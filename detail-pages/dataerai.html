Introduction

Dataerai (“data-array”) provides the infrastructure to transform fragmented data into structured, AI-ready assets for AI model development. Our platform automatically captures data and metadata from instruments, simulations, and analysis pipelines at the point of generation, organizing it into consistent, machine-readable formats with built-in traceability.

Designed for distributed environments, Dataerai enables organizations to manage data locally on-premises or in the cloud, while connecting it through a shared platform for refined searchability, discovery, access, and collaboration. This allows teams across organizations to work with the same data foundation without centralizing or duplicating sensitive data, with secure access controls.

By standardizing heterogeneous, multi-modal datasets and linking them through metadata and provenance graphs, Dataerai supports the construction of large-scale, reusable data assets. These assets can be directly leveraged for AI/ML workflows, including for AI model training and validation,  AI-assisted scientific reasoning, digital twins, autonomous labs, and autonomous manufacturing. Our platform is designed to reduce the burden of data curation while securely increasing the accessibility, usability, and impact of data across national labs, academia, and industry.

We are seeking National Lab, academic, and industry partners developing data-intensive workflows where access to streamlined data infrastructure is a limiting factor.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: Development of a Platform for Accessible Data-Intensive Science and Engineering (launched 2023)
Under NSF MRI award 2320600, we successfully deployed the Accessible Data‑Intensive Science and Engineering (DISE) platform, providing a 2‑PB scientific data‑management system and a 1‑PB all‑flash parallel file system integrated with GPU‑accelerated compute. The platform now supports interactive, Jupyter‑based access for students and researchers, enabling low‑latency analysis and training in AI‑driven and data‑intensive science. The infrastructure has already underpinned peer‑reviewed work in areas such as ferroelectric domain imaging and real‑time optical‑mode tracking.
 

Project 2: CRISPS: Cell-Centric Recursive Image Similarity Projection Searching (launched 2022)
CRISPS established an open, AI-enabled platform for materials microscopy that makes valuable image data FAIR by preserving, indexing, and enabling discovery of unpublished microscopy collections through metadata and image-similarity search. The project integrated three key innovations—DataFed for secure federated data management, Schema-Free Search for efficient exploration of unstructured metadata, and Recursive Image Similarity Projections for deep-learning-based, symmetry-aware image analysis—into a full-stack solution applicable to optical, electron, and scanning probe microscopy. It also expanded community access through an openly available software release, a 0.5 PB public DataFed allocation, outreach through conferences and training programs, curricular integration across materials science and computer science, and research opportunities for underrepresented STEM participants.
 

Project 3: Development of Heterogeneous Edge Computing Platform for Real-Time Scientific Machine Learning (launched 2022)
HEC-SML established a purpose-built heterogeneous edge computing platform for real-time scientific machine learning at the extreme edge, enabling low-latency analysis and control of optical, scanning probe, and transmission electron microscopy. By co-locating heterogeneous computing resources with microscopy instrumentation, the project overcame the latency and bandwidth limitations of traditional HPC workflows and created a reconfigurable platform for signal processing, data reduction, machine learning, and experimental control on timescales relevant to active experiments. This work advanced the convergence of microscopy, physics-informed machine learning, and heterogeneous computing, while creating new capabilities for cell counting and sorting, materials characterization and manipulation, personalized medicine, energy conversion, sensing, and quantum materials research, alongside interdisciplinary training and curriculum development spanning biological sciences, materials science, ML, and edge computing.

Project Idea Summary

We are seeking National Lab, academic, and industry partners developing data-intensive workflows where access to streamlined data preparation and management is a limiting factor. This includes teams working on AI/ML model development, large-scale simulation pipelines, and cross-facility experimentation that require integration of multi-modal datasets across instruments, facilities, and institutions.

Ideal partners have access to experimental systems, simulation outputs, or large datasets and are building AI-driven capabilities, such as domain-specific AI models, digital twins, AI-assisted scientific reasoning, autonomous laboratories, and automated manufacturing systems that depend on high-quality, well-structured training data. We are also interested in collaborations where data interoperability, provenance, and secure cross-institutional sharing are core technical challenges.

Dataerai provides the data infrastructure layer that makes it easier for partners to turn complex, distributed data into something usable for AI, without requiring teams to overhaul their existing workflows. Our platform reduces the manual effort of organizing and preparing data while ensuring it is secure, consistent, traceable, and ready for downstream applications. This allows partners to stay focused on building models, running operations, or advancing their core work. Daterai also has experience developing and executing robust Data Management and Sharing Plans (DMSPs).
