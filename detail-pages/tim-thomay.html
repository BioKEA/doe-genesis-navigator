Introduction

We develop AI/ML methods for quantum optical measurements — convolutional neural networks trained on higher-order photon correlations from single-photon detector arrays. Our published results (Analyst 2025, cover article; APL Quantum 2024) demonstrate CNN classification of quantum states from g(2) and g(3) correlation data with >90% accuracy, including in biological systems. We are pursuing two Genesis Mission topics. For Topic 2 (Focus Area 2-E), we are extending our platform from plants to algae for AI-driven bioreactor monitoring, together with collaborators in plant biology and an industry partner in algal cultivation (Helios-NRG). For Topic 14, we propose AI-guided precision measurements of photon statistics to test predictions from a discrete combinatorial model of quantum mechanics, with collaborators in theoretical physics and quantum decoherence modeling. We are seeking a National Lab partner for Topic 14 (based on our PRA 2025)  with expertise in DOE like large-scale AI for fundamental physics, photonic quantum systems, or precision measurement science. A collaborative contribution to the AI-driven experimental design methodology, such as Bayesian optimization of measurement configurations to maximize sensitivity to beyond-standard-quantum mechanics with signatures in photon correlations, would be particularly valuable.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Custom CNN pipeline for classifying quantum states from higher-order photon correlation data. Demonstrated 94% accuracy on multiphoton Fock state classification from g(3) correlation tensors (APL Quantum 2024), and 91%/84% accuracy classifying healthy vs. unhealthy plant cells from g(2) data (Analyst 2025, cover article). Bayesian optimization framework for AI-guided experimental design that maximizes measurement sensitivity per acquisition time. Transfer learning pipeline for adapting trained models across different physical and biological systems with minimal retraining data.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Transfer learning and domain adaptation — demonstrated across quantum optical systems (Fock states) and biological systems (plant cells), now extending to algae. Sparse-data ML achieving high classification accuracy from as few as 800 photon detection events. Neural network architectures designed for tensor-valued inputs (photon correlation tensors of arbitrary order). Experience with CNN training on Empire AI (NYS GPU infrastructure). Bayesian optimization for closed-loop experimental design. Team includes PI with combined quantum optics and ML background, plus student researchers trained.

Experimental or test facilities

Quantum optics lab: multi-pixel Single-Photon Avalanche Diode (SPAD) array detection system, Hanbury Brown and Twiss interferometer with tunable beamsplitter configurations, time-correlated single-photon counting (TCSPC) electronics for photon-number-resolved measurements up to g(4) and beyond. Biological facilities (Dorsheimer Greenhouse): temperature/light-controlled growth chambers, biolistic (gene gun) and electroporation transformation systems, MultispeQ portable fluorimeter. Industry partner (Helios-NRG) contributes proprietary photobioreactor systems.

Other expertise

Experimental quantum optics: 15+ years experience in single-photon measurements, photon statistics, and quantum correlations (Thomay). Fundamental tests of quantum mechanics using higher-order photon correlations — Dejan Stojkovic  has developed a discrete combinatorial model that makes quantitative predictions for photon statistics distinguishable from standard QED at high correlation orders, with connections to cosmology through discrete spacetime models and quantum gravity phenomenology. Herbert Fotso contributes quantum decoherence and noise modeling.

Capabilities Seeking
AI/ML/Data science expertise

Seeking expertise in AI/ML methods for precision physics measurements, particularly: physics-informed machine learning for model comparison (standard quantum mechanics vs. alternative predictions), Bayesian experimental design and active learning for optimizing measurement sensitivity, and scalable neural network architectures for high-dimensional tensor-valued data (higher-order photon correlation tensors). Experience applying ML to fundamental physics datasets or quantum information experiments would be especially valuable.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Seeking access to high-performance computing resources for training and deploying ML models on large photon correlation datasets. Higher-order correlations (g(3), g(4) and beyond) generate high-dimensional tensors that require significant GPU resources for CNN training and Bayesian optimization loops. Access to DOE Leadership Computing Facilities or similar HPC infrastructure would enable scaling our demonstrated proof-of-concept pipeline to the correlation orders and dataset sizes needed for meaningful precision tests.

Subject matter expertise

Seeking domain expertise in fundamental quantum physics measurements at DOE-relevant scales such as connecting tabletop quantum optical experiments to high-energy physics and cosmology facilities. A partner who can help connect our photon correlation measurements to broader DOE physics programs (HEP, NP) would strengthen both the scientific case and the path to Phase II.

Project Idea Summary

We are developing an AI/ML framework for precision quantum optical measurements that could reveal deviations from standard quantum mechanics. Our approach uses convolutional neural networks trained on higher-order photon correlations (g(2), g(3), g(4)) from single-photon detector arrays, combined with Bayesian optimization for AI-guided experimental design that maximizes measurement sensitivity. A discrete combinatorial model of quantum mechanics developed by our theoretical collaborator (Stojkovic, UB) makes specific, testable predictions for photon statistics that differ from standard QED at high correlation orders. We embed these predictions as physics-informed priors in our ML framework, enabling principled model comparison. Quantum decoherence and noise modeling  ensures rigorous separation of physical signals from systematic effects.

We target Focus Area 14-C: Expedited Discovery from High Complexity Datasets. Published proof-of-concept results demonstrate >90% classification accuracy on quantum states from photon correlation data (APL Quantum 2024; PRA 2025).

We are seeking a DOE National Lab partner who can contribute subject matter expertise and/or experimental facilities in one or more of: large-scale AI for physics applications, photonic quantum systems, precision measurement science, or lattice field theory / theoretical predictions testable through photon statistics.
