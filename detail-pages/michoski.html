Introduction

Sophelio is a technology company focused on enabling rigorous, data-driven workflows and advanced ML/AI solutions for fusion energy and related scientific domains. Our work emphasizes reproducibility, physical interpretability, and consistency across heterogeneous data sources spanning experiments, simulations, and control systems. We have experience developing scientific data infrastructure, including MGKDB, which supports multiscale gyrokinetic simulations and cross-code validation across campaigns and devices.

Our Data Fusion Labeler (dFL) framework addresses challenges in data alignment, harmonization, and provenance, enabling reproducible and transparent pipelines that preserve physical meaning and ensure traceability of derived quantities. This supports consistent integration of diagnostic and simulation data and provides a reliable foundation for ML/AI model training and deployment.

In parallel, we develop and validate ML/AI models tailored to fusion applications, with emphasis on domain-informed design, interpretability, and operational relevance. Sophelio is positioned to support the Genesis Mission as an enabling partner across experimental, simulation, and control-oriented efforts.

Relevant Projects

Fusion Data Platform (FDP)
Scope of Work: Sophelio contributed to the design and development of a fusion data platform aimed at integrating heterogeneous datasets across experimental diagnostics, simulation outputs, and derived analysis products. This included development of data ingestion pipelines, time-alignment of multimodal time-series data, and structured workflows for transformation and labeling. A central focus was ensuring reproducibility and physical interpretability through explicit operator ordering and provenance tracking, enabling traceability of all derived quantities.

Achievements: The platform enabled consistent cross-dataset analysis and improved accessibility of fusion data for both physics studies and ML/AI workflows. It reduced ambiguity in preprocessing and supported standardized feature generation across datasets. The FDP effort demonstrated scalable approaches to multimodal data integration and provided a foundation for reproducible analysis, model development, and digital twin workflows.

-----

SciDAC SMARTS Project
Scope of Work: Within the SMARTS SciDAC project, Sophelio supported data infrastructure and analysis workflows for multiscale gyrokinetic simulations and transport studies. This included development and maintenance of MGKDB, a database system for structured storage, retrieval, and comparison of simulation outputs across codes and campaigns. Additional work focused on data alignment and harmonization to support cross-code validation and integrated analysis across parameter regimes and devices.

Achievements: This work improved reproducibility and accessibility of simulation datasets, enabling systematic comparison across gyrokinetic codes and facilitating validation against experimental observations. It supported reduced transport model development and strengthened collaborative workflows across institutions. The resulting infrastructure enabled more effective use of large-scale simulation data and improved consistency in validation and benchmarking efforts.

-----

ARPA-E DeFT Project
Scope of Work: Under the ARPA-E DeFT program, Sophelio developed ML/AI-driven methods for modeling and prediction in fusion-relevant systems. This included construction of data pipelines integrating experimental and simulation data, development of feature representations for complex time-series signals, and implementation of machine learning models for predictive and surrogate modeling tasks. Emphasis was placed on maintaining physical consistency and incorporating domain knowledge into both data processing and model design.

Achievements: The project demonstrated the effective application of ML/AI to fusion datasets while preserving interpretability and scientific rigor. The resulting models improved predictive capability for key quantities of interest and highlighted the importance of structured, reproducible data preparation. This work advanced methodologies for ML-enabled digital twins and provided practical approaches for integrating AI into fusion research and operational workflows.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

We are seeking access to and collaboration on advanced AI/ML models and software frameworks applicable to fusion-relevant problems, including surrogate modeling, reduced-order transport models, and operator-learning approaches. Integration with existing scientific software (e.g., gyrokinetic solvers, transport codes, and control systems) is of particular interest to enable end-to-end workflows from data ingestion through prediction and validation.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We are requesting collaboration with experts in machine learning and data science, particularly those with experience in scientific ML, uncertainty quantification, and physics-informed modeling. This expertise is critical for developing models that are both predictive and physically interpretable, and for ensuring that ML approaches are applied in a manner consistent with fusion domain constraints.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

We seek access to high-performance computing (HPC) and/or cloud resources to support large-scale data processing, multimodal data fusion, and training of ML/AI models. These workflows require scalable infrastructure for handling high-volume time-series data and simulation outputs, as well as for conducting systematic model evaluation and validation studies.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We are seeking access to high-quality experimental and simulation datasets, including diagnostic time-series, equilibrium reconstructions, and gyrokinetic simulation outputs. Additionally, we are interested in collaboration on data infrastructure that supports standardized data formats, cross-device analysis, and reproducible workflows, enabling consistent integration of heterogeneous data sources.

Experimental or test facilities

We request access to experimental facilities or testbeds where developed methods can be evaluated on real or representative fusion data streams. This includes opportunities to test data integration pipelines, validate ML/AI models against measured quantities, and assess applicability to operational or near-real-time scenarios.

Subject matter expertise

We are requesting collaboration with domain experts in experimental fusion diagnostics, plasma theory, transport modeling, and control. This expertise is essential to guide feature construction, ensure physical consistency in data processing and modeling, and support rigorous validation of results against established theory and experimental observations.

Project Idea Summary

Our project focuses on developing a structured, reproducible framework for data fusion, harmonization, and ML/AI-enabled analysis across fusion-relevant workflows. The central challenge we address is the integration of heterogeneous datasets—spanning experimental diagnostics, simulation outputs, and derived signals—into consistent, physically interpretable, and ML-ready representations. This includes resolving issues of time alignment, operator ordering, and provenance, which are critical for ensuring that downstream modeling and analysis remain scientifically valid and reproducible.

We are particularly aligned with challenge areas involving data integration across modalities, development of digital twins and reduced-order models, and the application of ML/AI to experimentally relevant systems. Our approach combines structured data pipelines (e.g., via dFL), domain-informed feature construction, and ML/AI model development with an emphasis on interpretability and validation against physical constraints.

We are seeking partners who can contribute high-quality experimental or simulation datasets, domain expertise in plasma physics and diagnostics, and/or access to computational resources. We are especially interested in collaborators working on gyrokinetic simulations, transport modeling, diagnostic systems, or control frameworks, where integrated data workflows and ML/AI methods can be jointly developed and validated.
