Introduction

My research centers on developing scalable, efficient, and intelligent AI and HPC systems through compression-driven optimization and automated workflows. (1) We conduct research on scalable and efficient AI systems, with a focus on leveraging compression to improve training and inference across GPUs, Cerebras platforms, and federated learning environments, including compression of data, gradients, model weights, activations, and KV cache (HPDC’19, PPoPP’25, TPDS’25). (2) We proposed Retrieval-of-Thought (RoT) (ICLR’26 under review, arXiv:2509.21743), which improves LLM reasoning efficiency by reusing prior reasoning steps, significantly reducing tokens, latency, and cost while maintaining accuracy, alongside ongoing work on long-context LLM optimization. (3) In federated learning, we develop lossy compression techniques to enhance both performance and security (IJCAI’24, ICDCS’24, DSN’26, TPDS). (4) We also design embedding and vector compression methods to reduce RAG storage (CCGrid’26) and improve query efficiency, and build compression-enabled collective communication frameworks such as ZCCL (zccl.org) to accelerate distributed LLM training by reducing MPI communication overhead. (5) We are developing agentic systems for automated performance optimization, including a prototype that parallelizes sequential codes (using lossy compressors as test cases) and a system that autonomously optimizes execution performance for scientific parallel applications on supercomputers.
