Introduction

Loop Scientific builds high-throughput performance testing platforms for heterogeneous catalysts and sorbents. Our systems place candidates into arrays of small-scale reactors for application-specific testing at representative operating conditions, preserving the mass and heat transport phenomena that matter for scale-up. The system is designed to rapidly generate significantly more performance data than traditional bench-scale approaches.

Our API-first software architecture enables seamless data ingestion and, for teams pursuing autonomous workflows, allows AI orchestration tools to programmatically define experiments, execute testing campaigns, and receive structured data back, closing the loop between prediction and physical validation.

We are seeking a supporting role on a Genesis Mission team. Whether a team needs high-volume experimental data to validate their computational models or a physical execution layer for AI-driven experimental workflows, we want to help accelerate the discovery and optimization of new materials and catalysts.

If your team needs high-throughput performance testing, we'd welcome the conversation.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
Datasets or data infrastructure

Loop Scientific is developing high-throughput performance testing platforms that place catalyst and sorbent candidates into arrays of small-scale reactors for application-specific testing at representative operating conditions. Our systems are designed to preserve mass and heat transport phenomena relevant to scale-up, generating performance data that is directly comparable to conventional bench-scale results at significantly higher throughput.

Experimental or test facilities

Our platforms produce structured, machine-readable performance datasets via an API-first software architecture. Experiment configurations are defined as JSON-based specifications submitted by collaborators, enabling batched experimental campaigns tailored to specific research questions. All resulting data, operating conditions, time-series measurements, and performance metrics, is stored in a structured format designed for direct ingestion into AI/ML training and model validation workflows.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

We are looking for teams with computational models, simulations, or AI-driven predictions for heterogeneous catalyst or sorbent performance that need experimental validation. Our platform is designed to generate high-throughput experimental data to benchmark model predictions against real-world performance in gas-solid reaction systems, identify where models break down, and iteratively improve predictive accuracy. We are particularly interested in partnering with groups working on surrogate models, molecular simulations, or foundation models for materials science. 

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We are seeking collaborators with expertise in applying machine learning to experimental materials data, active learning, Bayesian optimization, or similar approaches for guiding experimental campaigns. Our platform generates structured performance datasets for heterogeneous catalysis and gas-solid reaction systems at high volume, and we want to partner with groups who can leverage that data to intelligently direct what gets tested next, closing the loop between prediction and experiment.

Subject matter expertise

We are seeking domain experts and industry stakeholders in heterogeneous catalysis and gas-solid reaction systems, who can define meaningful experimental campaigns and interpret performance results. Our platform is designed to be application-flexible, but generating useful data requires collaborators who understand what questions to ask and what performance metrics matter for their specific domain.

Project Idea Summary

Loop Scientific is developing a high-throughput performance testing platform for heterogeneous catalysts and sorbents, and we are looking to join a team addressing Topic 1: Reenvisioning Advanced Manufacturing and Industrial Productivity, particularly Focus Area A.

Our platform is designed to do one thing well: run large numbers of application specific performance tests on catalyst and sorbent candidates at representative operating conditions, generating structured datasets that can be used to validate computational predictions and train AI models against real-world experimental results. The hardware preserves mass and heat transport phenomena relevant to scale-up, and the software architecture, enabling integration with AI-driven and autonomous experimental workflows.

We are an early-stage company and are seeking partners who would benefit from a high-throughput experimental data generation capability as part of their research program. Specifically, we are looking for:

Teams with computational models or AI-driven predictions for catalyst or sorbent performance that need large-scale experimental validation

Domain experts in heterogenous catalysis or sorbent material science who can help define meaningful experimental campaigns. 

Groups with AI/ML expertise in active learning or similar approaches who want to close the loop between model predictions and physical experiments

We want to validate our platform and contribute valuable experimental data in a real research campaign.
