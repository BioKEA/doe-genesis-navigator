Introduction

Porter Process Technologies (PPT) – Summary

Porter Process Technologies (PPT), based in Austin, Texas, is a specialized process engineering and technology development firm focused on the optimization of energy-intensive chemical manufacturing systems. PPT brings over 20 years of hands-on expertise in catalytic reactor systems, sulfuric acid production, and industrial process operations across multiple sectors including refining, fertilizers, and critical minerals processing.

In support of the DOE Genesis Mission, PPT is leading Project GENESIS, a physics-informed AI digital twin platform designed to optimize real-time performance of industrial process plants. The technology integrates first-principles thermodynamics and reaction kinetics with machine learning models trained on plant operating data to deliver predictive optimization, improved conversion efficiency, reduced energy consumption, and enhanced operational reliability.

Unlike purely data-driven or static simulation approaches, PPT’s hybrid architecture ensures thermodynamic consistency, interpretability, and generalizability across a wide range of exothermic catalytic processes, including sulfuric acid, ammonia, methanol, and sulfur recovery systems. A working software demonstration has already been developed and validated on sulfuric acid process systems, providing a strong foundation for Phase I execution.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: C3 Purification System – Basic Engineering & Modular Process Design

Led the development of a complete Basic Engineering Package for a C3 purification system designed to remove sulfur and arsine contaminants from hydrocarbon streams using catalytic treatment and adsorption technologies. Scope included full process design basis, process flow diagrams (PFDs), heat and material balances, equipment specifications, and piping & instrumentation diagrams (P&IDs).

Project 2: Sulfuric Acid Plant Failure Analysis & Restart Strategy

Conducted an independent technical assessment of a 2,400 MTPD sulfuric acid plant following a major operational shutdown caused by heat recovery system failures. Performed full-site walkthrough and evaluation of superheater corrosion, heat exchanger failures, and downstream process impacts.

Diagnosed root causes including acid condensation-driven corrosion and materials compatibility issues. Evaluated repair strategies, vendor proposals, and code compliance (ASME Section I). Developed recommendations for safe restart, including staged repair plans and operational workarounds to reduce downtime.

Key Achievements:

Delivered full engineering package on accelerated timeline (4–6 weeks)

Enabled modular fabrication with reduced installation risk

Integrated process, mechanical, and automation design into a unified system

Established design basis for downstream procurement and detailed engineering

Key Achievements:

Identified critical failure mechanisms and validated repair strategy

Enabled potential reduction in downtime by ~12 weeks through alternative operating strategies

Provided actionable recommendations for safe restart and long-term reliability

Assessed full plant system with no major barriers to successful operation post-repair

Project 3: Predictive Simulation & Startup Optimization for Sulfuric Acid Systems

Developed and applied custom predictive simulation models for sulfuric acid plant operations, focusing on converter start-up optimization, emissions control, and operational decision support. Models incorporated reaction kinetics, thermodynamics, and mass/energy balances to simulate transient behavior during startup and upset conditions.

These models were used to evaluate optimal sulfur introduction timing, minimize SO₂ emissions, and reduce startup duration. The work emphasized bridging first-principles modeling with real-world operational constraints, supporting commissioning teams and operators in complex industrial environments.

Key Achievements:

Reduced startup uncertainty and improved operational safety

Enabled data-driven decision-making for transient process conditions

Demonstrated improvements in emissions control and startup efficiency

Established foundation for digital twin and real-time optimization applications

Project Idea Summary

We are developing a physics-informed AI digital twin platform for real-time optimization of energy-intensive chemical processes, with initial application in sulfuric acid and catalytic reactor systems. The core architecture integrates first-principles thermodynamics and reaction kinetics with machine learning models trained on industrial operating data to enable predictive control, improved conversion efficiency, and reduced energy consumption.

We are seeking partners to enhance and scale this platform within the DOE Genesis Mission ecosystem. Specifically, we are looking for collaborators with expertise in physics-informed machine learning, advanced AI architectures, and high-performance computing to improve model robustness, generalization, and scalability across multiple industrial processes.

Additional areas of interest include integration with the American Science Cloud and ModCon frameworks, large-scale model training and parameter optimization, and access to pilot-scale or industrial datasets for validation. Experimental or test facilities to validate model predictions under controlled conditions are also of interest.

Our team provides deep domain expertise, existing process models, and a demonstrated industrial use case. We are seeking partners to accelerate AI development, expand computational capabilities, and enable deployment as a scalable, reusable platform aligned with DOE objectives.
