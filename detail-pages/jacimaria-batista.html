Introduction

 Dr. Batista’s research centers on water and wastewater treatment, environmental remediation, and the biological reduction of diverse contaminants. Trained as both a mining and environmental engineer, she brings a strong interdisciplinary perspective and has extensive experience treating spent heap-leaching solutions from the gold mining industry. Her work spans a broad range of industrial waste streams, with particular expertise in advanced treatment processes including ion exchange, zeolite-based systems, and other engineered adsorbents. Her laboratory is well equipped to support both fundamental and applied research, featuring ion chromatographs, total organic carbon analyzers, colorimeters, spectrophotometers, biological safety cabinets, and an anaerobic chamber, among other specialized instrumentation. The facility is fully capable of conducting comprehensive bench- and pilot-scale evaluations of treatment technologies, enabling rigorous technology validation under controlled and field-relevant conditions.
