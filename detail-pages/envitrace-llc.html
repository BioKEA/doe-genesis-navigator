Introduction

Velimir “Monty” Vesselinov is a co-founder of EnviTrace LLC, a geoscience and AI company focused on advancing decision-making in complex subsurface systems. With a background in computational geosciences and extensive experience at Los Alamos National Laboratory, his work centers on integrating physics-informed modeling, machine learning, and uncertainty quantification to address critical challenges in energy, water, and mineral resources.

EnviTrace develops AI-driven frameworks that fuse heterogeneous geoscience data—geophysical, geological, geochemical, and reservoir information—to improve prediction, prospectivity assessment, and risk-aware decision-making. The company’s approach moves beyond traditional modeling by enabling systems that guide exploration strategies, experimental design, and investment decisions under uncertainty.

Aligned with the Genesis Mission, EnviTrace is focused on building decision intelligence systems for subsurface applications, including geothermal energy, critical minerals, and multi-use geologic reservoirs. Through collaborations with national laboratories, academia, and industry, the company aims to accelerate scientific discovery and support the development of resilient, sustainable energy and resource systems.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

AI-driven decision intelligence systems for subsurface energy and mineral applications. We build physics-informed machine learning models that integrate geophysical, geological, geochemical, and reservoir data. Our tools quantify uncertainty and guide data acquisition, experimental design, and investment decisions. Applications include geothermal, critical minerals, carbon sequestration, and multi-use subsurface systems. 

We have AI patents and AI R&D 100 awards.

Some of our codes are open-source on GitHub. Some of our tools are showcased on our web dashboard http://envitrace.com/saas.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Expertise in physics-informed machine learning, Bayesian inference, risk assessment, decision support, and data assimilation for complex geoscience systems. We develop models that integrate heterogeneous, multi-scale data and quantify uncertainty. Capabilities include active learning, optimization, and decision analytics to guide experiments and resource management. Experience spans geothermal, critical minerals, carbon sequestration, subsurface flow, and environmental systems.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We develop automated tools to collect, curate, and integrate publicly available geoscience data from diverse sources. Our SeisDB platform is a purpose-built database for induced seismicity associated with geothermal and subsurface operations, supporting storage, querying, and analysis of seismic events, operational data, and metadata. It enables scalable data integration, quality control, and downstream AI/ML workflows for hazard assessment and decision support.

Subject matter expertise

Expertise in subsurface geoscience, including geothermal, geophysics, geochemistry, water resources, critical minerals, induced seismicity, and fluid flow and transport in porous media. Strong background in inverse modeling, uncertainty quantification, risk assessment, decision support, and multi-physics processes governing geologic systems. Experience integrating geophysical, geological, and geochemical dataset processing and analyses to support exploration, risk assessment, and resource management in complex, data-sparse environments.

Relevant Projects

EnviTrace has participated in U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) SBIR/STTR programs focused on advancing AI-driven subsurface characterization and decision-support technologies. Relevant project areas include:

• AI-enabled geothermal prospectivity and resource characterization, integrating multi-modal geoscience data to improve exploration success and reduce development risk.

• Machine learning frameworks for critical mineral discovery, targeting domestic resource identification using physics-informed models and uncertainty-aware analysis.

• Modeling and analysis of induced seismicity associated with subsurface energy operations, including data integration and risk assessment tools.

• Development of integrated data platforms and workflows to support multi-use subsurface systems, including geothermal, carbon storage, and energy resource optimization.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

We seek access to scalable computing infrastructure, including cloud and HPC resources, to support physics-informed AI/ML workflows for subsurface systems. Experimental or test facilities

We seek access to experimental and field-scale test facilities for validation of AI-driven decision systems in subsurface environments. This includes geothermal testbeds, induced seismicity monitoring sites, and controlled subsurface experiments with integrated geophysical and operational data. Access to instrumentation, real-time data streams, and benchmark datasets will enable calibration, validation, and demonstration of decision-guided exploration and risk management workflows.
