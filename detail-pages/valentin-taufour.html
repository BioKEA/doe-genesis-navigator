Introduction

Taufour's lab combines experimental expertise (crystal growth, solution growth, Czochralski synthesis, high‑pressure/magnetic tuning, anisotropy measurement) with theoretical and data‑driven methods (database aggregation and curation, machine learning, statistical analysis) to discover and engineer various types of materials, including unconventional superconductors, rare-earth‑free magnetic materials with high Curie temperatures and strong magnetic anisotropy, altermagnetic compounds, topological materials.

Experimental or test facilities

The Taufour Laboratory supports targeted synthesis, validation, and property characterization of inorganic materials. Synthesis capabilities include high-temperature box and tube furnaces, vapor-transport and Bridgman furnaces, arc and tetra-arc Czochralski furnaces, an electrosynthesis station, and standard sample-preparation tools. Characterization capabilities include powder and Laue x-ray diffraction, MPMS and PPMS systems with 7 T and 14 T magnets, respectively, and pressure cells compatible with both platforms.
