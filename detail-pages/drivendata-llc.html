Introduction

DrivenData is a data science and AI company specializing in designing and operating AI/ML challenges, building AI-ready data pipelines, and enabling agentic scientific workflows. We are a founding MOU signatory with the DOE for the Genesis Mission as of Dec 2025, with a collaboration scope covering AI, data curation, benchmarking, open science, and evaluation frameworks.

Our core capabilities relevant to Genesis Mission priorities include:

- Challenge design and execution: We have designed and operated 150+ machine learning competitions, of which 20+ were AI challenges with US federal agencies including currently running for DOE/NLR under the American Made Challenges program.
- Data engineering for AI: We prepare complex, heterogeneous datasets for machine learning, building ingestion pipelines and evaluation harnesses that accelerate research workflows.
- Agentic workflow enablement: We develop infrastructure for AI-driven scientific workflows, including API integration, orchestration platforms, and MLOps tooling.
- Open-source tooling: We maintain widely adopted tools for reproducible data science (e.g., Cookiecutter Data Science).

We hold a NASA prime contract (NOIS3) for open innovation services with past performance for NASA, NIST, NOAA, CDC, NIH, and other scientific agencies. We bring deep experience partnering with federal agencies and national laboratories to translate scientific challenges into structured AI problems with measurable outcomes.

Relevant Projects

Water Supply Forecast Rodeo - Bureau of Reclamation / NASA (2023–2024)
We designed and operated a $750K multi-phase ML competition for probabilistic streamflow forecasting across 17 Western states. We integrated data from SNOTEL, CDEC, weather models, and historical streamflow records into an AI-ready dataset, then ran a live forecasting challenge where models were evaluated weekly against real-time observations over an entire water year. The competition included three prize tracks: forecast skill, uncertainty quantification, and explainability. Winning models achieved performance comparable to expensive lidar-based approaches at a fraction of the cost. Bureau of Reclamation expressed strong interest in operationalizing results for water management decisions.

Cyanobacteria Detection in Inland Water Bodies - NASA / EPA / NOAA (2022–2024)
We ran a satellite-based ML challenge to detect harmful algal blooms in small inland lakes using Sentinel-2 imagery. We aggregated 23,570 ground truth measurements from 14 US data providers and built a multi-agency partnership (NASA, NOAA, EPA, USGS, DOD DIU, Microsoft AI for Earth). From competition results, we developed CyFi, an open-source tool that detects cyanobacteria in 10x more water bodies than existing Sentinel-3 tools. The project produced two peer-reviewed publications (SciPy Proceedings, ICHA 2025) and the CAML dataset archived at NASA's SeaBASS. 1,300+ participants contributed solutions.

Mars Spectrometry Challenge - NASA Goddard (2021–2023)
We designed and executed a $230K challenge to improve AI-assisted chemical analysis for NASA's Sample Analysis at Mars (SAM) instrument aboard the Curiosity rover. We developed a custom evaluation metric for geochemical classification and built containerized submission infrastructure compatible with flight hardware constraints. 1,200+ participants submitted 7,800 models, improving baseline classification F-score from 0.61 to 0.87 (43% improvement) and reducing analyst review time by 50%. Results published in RAS Techniques and Instruments.

Crop Type Mapping in Conflict-Affected Yemen - World Bank (2023–2024)
We performed direct data science services for World Bank agricultural monitoring in Yemen, where ground access is limited by conflict. We coordinated manual annotation of 210+ sq km with local experts (2,500+ labeled polygons), trained crop classification models achieving 0.84 weighted F1, and generated annual maps at 10m resolution across 333 districts (2019–2024). The analysis processed 27 billion predictions via Google Earth Engine and identified 1.3M hectares of active agriculture by 2024, roughly double 2019 levels — a key finding for food security planning. Work led to follow-on contracts for infrastructure detection.

Capabilities Seeking
Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Access to DOE HPC systems and scientific computing infrastructure for large-scale ML training, evaluation, and benchmarking. Our work designing ML competitions and building agentic scientific workflows requires compute environments that handle large scientific datasets, GPU-intensive model training, and containerized code execution at scale. Access to leadership-class facilities would enable us to design challenges and evaluation harnesses that reflect real-world scientific computing constraints, and to prototype AI workflows on the platforms where they will ultimately be deployed.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Partners with domain-specific scientific datasets relevant to Genesis Mission challenge areas, particularly materials science, nuclear energy, autonomous laboratories, water/energy systems, and HPC code corpora. Our strength is transforming raw scientific data into AI-ready formats and designing evaluation frameworks, but we need access to source data and domain context. Partners who can provide curated experimental data, simulation outputs, or observational datasets along with expert guidance on data quality and scientific validity would be ideal.

Experimental or test facilities

Partners with laboratory or experimental facilities where AI-driven workflows can be validated against real-world scientific processes. Our expertise is in designing ML challenges and evaluation harnesses, but validating that AI solutions translate from benchmarks to operational impact requires physical testbeds. Partners with autonomous labs, materials characterization facilities, or instrumented experimental environments would help close the loop between challenge-winning models and deployed scientific tools, particularly for autonomous laboratories and materials design.

Subject matter expertise

Domain scientists and engineers with deep expertise in Genesis Mission challenge areas, particularly materials science, nuclear energy, autonomous experimentation, fluid dynamics, HPC code development, and grid-scale energy. DrivenData excels at structuring AI/ML problems, designing evaluation metrics, and building data pipelines, but we need domain experts to define what questions matter, what constitutes a meaningful result, and how AI outputs integrate into research workflows. Ideal partners can articulate domain-specific success criteria and provide scientific mentorship.

Project Idea Summary

We seek to join lab-led or university-led teams, bringing challenge design, data engineering, and evaluation harness capabilities in three primary areas:

1. Lighthouse Challenges and open innovation. We have run 150+ ML challenges for NASA, NIST, NOAA, NIH and other agencies, including live forecasting competitions and containerized code execution on constrained hardware. We can design and operate competitive evaluation infrastructure for any Genesis Mission challenge area. Our past scientific innovation challenges have produced peer-reviewed publications, open-source tools, and productionized AI/ML models.

2. AI-ready data pipelines and benchmarking. We prepare complex scientific datasets for ML and build evaluation frameworks that measure real progress. Track record includes water supply forecasting (Bureau of Reclamation), satellite environmental monitoring (NASA/EPA/NOAA), and planetary instrumentation (NASA Goddard).

3. Cross-cutting platform support (Topics 18-21). We build agentic workflow tooling, API integrations, and orchestration infrastructure for scientific computing, and can contribute to AmSC platform development.

We seek DOE National Lab and academic partners with domain expertise, HPC access, scientific datasets, and experimental facilities, especially teams on Autonomous Labs (11), HPC Code Curation (18), AI for Scientific Reasoning (19), or Cybersecurity for AI Workflows (20).
