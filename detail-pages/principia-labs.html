Introduction

Principia Labs builds foundation models for mathematical reasoning and scientific discovery. Our AI systems are designed to accelerate research in domains where mathematical bottlenecks limit progress - including materials discovery, energy systems optimization, computational physics, and algorithm design for large-scale simulation. 

Our team has pretrained state-of-the-art open reasoning models, built the industry-standard benchmarks for evaluating mathematical AI, and contributed to active research at the frontiers of theoretical computer science, formal mathematics, and computational chemical physics.

We are seeking partnerships with national labs, academic research groups, and mission-driven organizations working on scientific problems where stronger AI reasoning tools could meaningfully reduce time-to-discovery.

We are a research lab based in San Francisco, California.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Principia develops foundation models pretrained from scratch for mathematical reasoning and scientific computation. We build training infrastructure and data curation pipelines optimized for quantitative and formal reasoning domains. Our team created FrontierMath, the leading benchmark for evaluating AI on frontier-level mathematics, now maintained by Epoch AI. We also have experience building autoformalization tools that translate natural-language mathematics into machine-verifiable formal proofs in Lean 4, and agentic coding systems that integrate foundation models with verifiers.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Deep expertise in pretraining large language models for reasoning from scratch, including attention architecture design, data curation pipelines for mathematical and STEM corpora, curriculum and scaling strategies for multi-step deductive reasoning, and reinforcement learning methods for LLM alignment and reasoning. Additional experience in benchmark design and evaluation methodology for mathematical AI, formal verification and automated theorem proving, and building agentic systems that integrate foundation models with symbolic tools and external solvers.

Subject matter expertise

Our team spans pure mathematics, theoretical computer science, and computational chemical physics. In mathematics, expertise includes combinatorics, mathematical logic, formal methods, and frontier problem design across number theory, algebra, and analysis. In physical sciences, expertise in nonadiabatic dynamics, quantum-classical simulation methods, vibrational energy transport in molecular systems, and computational condensed matter physics. This combination enables us to bridge AI capability development with rigorous scientific applications.

Relevant Projects

1. FrontierMath Benchmark (2024) Our co-founder and lead mathematician Elliot Glazer created FrontierMath while at Epoch AI — a benchmark of hundreds of original, exceptionally challenging mathematics problems crafted and vetted by expert mathematicians, covering most major branches of modern mathematics. Problems require hours to days of effort from domain researchers and use automated verification to evaluate AI systems while minimizing data contamination. FrontierMath revealed that state-of-the-art AI solves under 2% of frontier problems and has been widely adopted across the industry as the standard evaluation for mathematical reasoning.

2. LLM Reasoning and Pretraining Research (2023–present) Our co-founder and Head of Pre-Training Yifan Zhang, while at Princeton, led research on foundation model pretraining for mathematical reasoning, publishing at NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, and ACL. Key contributions include novel attention architectures (Tensor Product Attention, NeurIPS 2025 Spotlight), reinforcement learning methods for LLM reasoning (ICLR 2026), preference optimization for alignment (ICML 2025), and construction of large-scale curated STEM pretraining corpora including AutoMathText-V2 (2.46 trillion tokens).

3. MathCode: Mathematical Coding Agent (2026) Team members Yifan Zhang and Jichen Feng built and open-sourced MathCode, a terminal AI coding assistant with a built-in math formalization engine based on the AUTOLEAN pipeline. MathCode converts natural-language math problems into Lean 4 theorems and attempts formal proofs, bridging informal scientific reasoning and formal verification systems.

Partnership Request & Project Idea

Capabilities Seeking
Datasets or data infrastructure

Access to scientific datasets, simulation outputs, and formalized problem sets from DOE-relevant domains that can be used to train, fine-tune, and evaluate mathematical reasoning models. We are particularly interested in curated datasets from computational physics, materials science, or applied mathematics that contain structured quantitative reasoning - problems with verifiable solutions that can serve as training signal and evaluation benchmarks for scientific AI reasoning systems.

Subject matter expertise

Domain scientists working on problems where mathematical reasoning and formal verification are active bottlenecks - for example, in applied mathematics, computational physics, quantum information science, or algorithm design for scientific computing. We need collaborators who can identify high-value scientific workflows where AI-driven mathematical reasoning would provide measurable acceleration, and who can validate model outputs against domain standards.

 

Project Idea Summary

We are targeting Topic 19: AI for Scientific Reasoning. We propose to develop and evaluate foundation models with enhanced capabilities for complex mathematical reasoning, symbolic manipulation, and scientific hypothesis generation, spanning the focus areas of trustworthy mathematical reasoning, hypothesis generation from multi-modal data, and composable foundation models.

Our team brings deep expertise in pretraining reasoning models from scratch, benchmark design for mathematical AI (including FrontierMath), formal verification pipelines connecting natural-language mathematics to machine-checkable proofs in Lean 4, and computational chemical physics. We have published extensively on LLM reasoning architectures, reinforcement learning for reasoning, and data curation for STEM domains.

We are seeking a DOE National Lab partner who can provide: (1) scientific use cases where AI-driven mathematical and scientific reasoning would measurably accelerate existing research workflows; and (2) domain expertise to define evaluation metrics and validation criteria grounded in real scientific practice. The partner need not be funded but must provide significant intellectual contributions to problem definition, evaluation design, and scientific validation of results. We are also open to partnering on applications in other topic areas where our reasoning model capabilities are relevant.
