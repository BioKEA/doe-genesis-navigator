Introduction

The USD Discovery District is an 80-acre university-anchored bioscience and life-sciences innovation community in Sioux Falls, South Dakota. Developed through a public-private partnership among the University of South Dakota (USD), the City of Sioux Falls, the South Dakota Board of Regents, the Governor’s Office of Economic Development, and Forward Sioux Falls, it connects academic research, clinical expertise, industry partners, and a growing talent pipeline in one collaborative location.

With its first 50,000-square-foot purpose-built biotech facility now open and an ambitious multi-building campus underway, the District accelerates translational research, advanced biomanufacturing, and commercialization. It is uniquely positioned to advance the federal Genesis Mission—the U.S. Department of Energy’s national initiative to build the world’s most powerful AI-enabled scientific discovery platform. South Dakota’s cost and speed advantages, combined with USD’s expanding programs in biomedical sciences, engineering, and AI, directly support the Mission’s priorities in translational research, domestic biomanufacturing, AI-driven discovery, and regional infrastructure capacity.

As detailed in the District’s 2025 South Dakota Bioscience Market Study, these strengths position the region to help deliver the Genesis Mission’s goal of doubling American research productivity within a decade.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI/ML/Data science expertise

In partnership with the University of South Dakota, USD’s Computer Science Department, led by Chair Dr. KC Santosh (founding director, AI Research Lab), offers South Dakota’s only AI programs: undergrad/grad specializations, M.S. in AI (machine learning, computer vision, robotics, ethical/trustworthy AI), joint Ph.D. in Data Science & Engineering, and certificates. Faculty specialize in explainable AI, reinforcement learning, medical imaging, and big data. Hands-on, ethics-focused curriculum prepares graduates for high-demand careers in healthcare, analytics, and intelligent systems.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

University of South Dakota’s Research Computing Group manages two on-premises HPC supercomputing clusters for faculty, staff, and sponsored students (free).

Lawrence (2017, NSF #1626516): >2,300 CPU cores, 1.5TB memory nodes, GPUs, 400TB+ high-speed storage via 56Gb Infiniband—ideal for large simulations and AI workloads. Named after Nobel laureate E.O. Lawrence (USD alumnus).

Spirit (new, NSF #2346643, online mid-2026): >1,600 cores, 6 NVIDIA GPUs (2×A100 + 4×L40S), 500TB+ BeeGFS storage.

Experimental or test facilities

The USD Discovery District opened its first 50,000+ sq ft Building 1 in Jan 2025—a purpose-built facility with core and shell square footage for commercialization infrastructure for biotech/life sciences innovators. It enables experimental testing, translational research, advanced biomanufacturing, and AI-driven discovery workflows. Directly linked to USD’s strengths: AI Research Lab, HPC clusters (Lawrence/Spirit for simulation/testing), biomedical engineering, the state’s only medical school, and SDBCC (AI/biomedical computation).

Subject matter expertise

The USD Discovery District demonstrates deep subject matter expertise in public-private partnerships (PPP) through its formation by USD, City of Sioux Falls, SD Board of Regents, Governor’s Office of Economic Development, and Forward Sioux Falls. This model enabled rapid horizontal land development and infrastructure delivery for an 80-acre bioscience research park, culminating in the 2025 opening of its first 50,000+ sq ft modern facility built for initiatives like the Genesis Mission.

Other resources/tools

South Dakota Biomedical Computation Collaborative (SDBCC)
USD’s $6.5M-funded initiative (2024) integrates AI and computation into biomedical research, medical education, and patient care. It builds a statewide network linking Computer Science, Sanford School of Medicine, and clinical partners. Activities include shared HPC/AI infrastructure, student training, the annual SDBCC Symposium, and Healthcare & Biomedical AI Innovation Challenge.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: USD Discovery District Building 1 Development (2023–2025)

Led the public-private partnership and horizontal infrastructure development for the District’s first 50,000 sq ft purpose-built bioscience facility in Sioux Falls. Scope included master planning coordination, site infrastructure (utilities, roads, parking), construction oversight with ISG and McGough, and placemaking to create a collaborative innovation environment. The $25M project (funded by state legislature, City of Sioux Falls, and partners) delivered flexible wet labs, cGMP suites, and office space 13% under budget. Achieved on-time opening in January 2025, secured initial tenants including OmegaQuant, and established the District as South Dakota’s premier bioscience hub. Projected to support 2,800 jobs and $314M economic impact across the full 80-acre park.

Project 2: TRAC – Technology Research and Commercialization Program (2024–present)

Co-led the launch of USD’s TRAC program, a $1.2M initiative ($600k EDA grant + state/USD match) bridging academic research to commercialization. Scope involved designing graduate student fellowship stipends, mentorship networks with Sanford Health, and advisory boards to accelerate tech transfer and entrepreneurship. The program expands USD’s reach across sectors while retaining talent in South Dakota. Key achievements include securing competitive EDA funding (selected from 600+ applications), establishing the first cohort of TRAC fellows, and creating clear pathways from lab to market that directly support Discovery District tenant growth and regional job creation.

Project 3: AI Recruiting & Resource Mapping Platform – USD Discovery District (2024–2025)

Developed an internal AI-powered recruiting and bioscience ecosystem mapping tool for the USD Discovery District. Scope included requirements gathering with leadership, platform architecture, and 1,400+ hours of development to connect early-stage ventures with talent, funding, lab resources, and partners. The tool enhances internal operations and external attraction efforts. Achievements: Delivered a functional prototype that streamlines company recruitment and resource allocation; positioned the District for faster tenant onboarding; and laid digital infrastructure foundation aligned with the Genesis Mission’s AI-enabled discovery priorities. Marc Niamba led core development with team support.

Project Idea Summary

The USD Discovery District seeks partners to establish an AI-enabled accelerator for biomolecular science and translational biotechnology. Our 80-acre research park, anchored by the newly opened 50,000 sq ft Building 1 with flexible core and shell square footage provides the physical infrastructure for collaborative R&D and scale-up.

We leverage an internally developed AI platform for bioscience resource mapping and talent recruiting, alongside resources from the USD Office of Research, to connect early-stage ventures with the right Midwest resources and accelerate lab-to-market pathways.

Specific challenge focus area: Accelerating AI-driven discovery, biomanufacturing optimization, and translational research in biomolecular science by bridging computational tools with experimental facilities and domain expertise.

What we are seeking in a partner: Complementary AI models/tools, advanced datasets or data infrastructure, specialized test facilities (e.g., high-throughput screening or bioprocess development), and subject matter expertise in BER, regulatory pathways, and commercial biomanufacturing to co-develop solutions aligned with the Genesis Mission.

We offer an emerging ecosystem in rural America, talent pipeline from the University of South Dakota, and proven public-private partnership model for rapid project execution.
