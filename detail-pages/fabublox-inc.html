Introduction

FabuBlox is developing an AI-enabled digital backbone for semiconductor process engineering and discovery, accelerated lab-to-fab transitions, and standardized technology transfer, designed to unlock AI advantage in complex, high-flexibility fabrication environments. The platform unifies version-controlled process design, layer stack emulation, and digital twin representations of fab tools and manufacturing execution flows. By transforming traditionally fragmented, “tribal” process knowledge into standardized, transferrable data, FabuBlox enables AI systems to reason directly over fab-context constrained fabrication flows.

Central to this approach is an emerging agentic AI layer capable of evaluating compatibility between process flows and real-world fab environments, retrieving tool capabilities, parameter limits, contamination constraints, and material interactions to guide process development and execution. This accelerates process discovery, unlocks data-driven, inference based recipe translations, resulting in reduced experimental iteration and improved reproducibility within and across facilities.

FabuBlox is a 2024 MIT spin-out, currently deployed across a rapidly growing global user base with nearly 2,000 US-based users across 75+ academic institutions, national labs, and industry teams. With multiple ME Commons partnerships, FabuBlox is currently expanding into secure environments to support next-generation, AI-driven defense-level microelectronics innovation.
