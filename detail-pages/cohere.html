Introduction

As a frontier AI lab, Cohere stands at the forefront of AI innovation, pushing the boundaries of what's possible with language models and natural language processing. We are pioneers in developing foundational AI technologies that directly support the Genesis Mission's three strategic priorities: American energy dominance, advancing discovery science, and ensuring national security. 

Cohere's differentiation includes: (1) Scientific and Defense Language Understanding: We specialize in processing complex technical documentation, research papers, and regulatory texts with domain-specific accuracy—critical for DOE's mission-critical work where precision matters. (2) Secure, Sovereign AI Deployment: Unlike cloud-only providers, we offer on-premises and VPC deployment with full data control, meeting DOE's stringent security requirements for nuclear and national security applications. (3) Multi-Modal Research Acceleration: Our AI doesn't just analyze text, it connects scientific literature, experimental data, and mission parameters to generate novel hypotheses and research pathways. 

Cohere models are developed for organizational complexity. The world's most sensitive organizations trust our models because mission outcomes have real-world impact. We don't just provide AI tools; we redefine what's possible in scientific discovery and national security through language intelligence that understands the unique challenges of energy research and defense mission.

Relevant Publications

Our co-founder and CEO was co-author of "Attention is All You Need", the seminal paper introducing Transformer technology. 

The dominant sequence transduction models are based on complex recurrent or convolutional neural networks in an encoder-decoder configuration. The best performing models also connect the encoder and decoder through an attention mechanism. We propose a new simple network architecture, the Transformer, based solely on attention mechanisms, dispensing with recurrence and convolutions entirely. Experiments on two machine translation tasks show these models to be superior in quality while being more parallelizable and requiring significantly less time to train. Our model achieves 28.4 BLEU on the WMT 2014 English-to-German translation task, improving over the existing best results, including ensembles by over 2 BLEU. On the WMT 2014 English-to-French translation task, our model establishes a new single-model state-of-the-art BLEU score of 41.8 after training for 3.5 days on eight GPUs, a small fraction of the training costs of the best models from the literature. We show that the Transformer generalizes well to other tasks by applying it successfully to English constituency parsing both with large and limited training data.
