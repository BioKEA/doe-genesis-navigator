Introduction

Anchor QEA is an environmental science and engineering partner for Genesis teams that need credible domain expertise in real-world water and subsurface systems. We translate hydrologic, hydrogeologic, geochemical, and remediation problems into scientifically grounded use cases, evaluation frameworks, and field-relevant datasets for AI-enabled research.


Our teams work with heterogeneous monitoring records that reflect the complexity of real-world data: long-horizon, spatially complex, spatially and temporally sparse, and highly autocorrelated datasets with complex legal and regulatory context. We evaluate groundwater-surface water interaction, contaminant fate and transport, water quality, sediment, and related processes using scientific interpretation, model evaluation, and workflow integration suited to operational and regulatory decision contexts.


Anchor QEA is best aligned to Genesis pursuits in Water and Energy, Weeks to Years Prediction, Chemical and Hydrologic Transport in Subsurface, Digitalization of Industrial Processes, and adjacent efforts where model performance depends on applied system understanding. We are strongest as a subject matter expert partner that helps AI entities ground advanced methods in defensible environmental physics, monitoring reality, and deployment constraints while demonstrating fluency in AI/ML, data engineering, and scientific workflow automation. Specialties: PHREEQC, MODFLOW, MT3DMS, PFLOTRAN, PHT3D, muFlowRT, Delft3D, HEC-RAS, EQuIS

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI/ML/Data science expertise

Applied AI/ML for hydrologic, geochemical, and subsurface systems using surrogate models, multivariate analysis, time-series forecasting (e.g., RNNs), geospatial statistics, classification, and dimensionality reduction (PCA/NMF) on complex environmental datasets. Integrates multimodal monitoring data with domain knowledge and regulatory expertise to support prediction, digital twins, and decision-focused model evaluation.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Environmental data management is a core service at Anchor QEA, supporting the full data lifecycle from collection to analysis and reporting. By combining early adoption with strong data management fundamentals, we implement cloud-based, scalable architectures to deliver high-density datasets with computing resources that scale with demand. To improve understanding and prediction of subsurface processes, our approach incorporates AI and ML to support data quality, integrate both structured and unstructured data, and provide timely, cost-effective insights for decision-makers.

Subject matter expertise

Anchor QEA evaluates surface water and groundwater systems across rivers, estuaries, reservoirs, aquifers, fractured bedrock, and karst. Our work spans groundwater-surface water interaction, geochemical interpretation, water quality, sediment, remediation, and reactive transport modeling. We apply mechanistic models and scientific analysis to support site understanding, restoration evaluation, and operationally relevant environmental decision-making. We work with datasets with long timeframes, large spatial extents, and numerous water quality/chemical constituents

Relevant Projects

Anchor QEA is leading development of a commercial software platform for groundwater and subsurface modeling that integrates flow, reactive chemical transport, and multiphase processes within a single interface. Developed through a long-term international research partnership, the tool streamlines model building, simulation, and visualization using industry-standard and advanced reactive transport codes. The platform has been applied across real-world projects and is transitioning into a scalable, commercially available product with near-term beta and release milestones. This effort demonstrates Anchor QEA’s ability to translate advanced scientific modeling into deployable digital tools aligned with Genesis priorities in subsurface systems, AI-enabled scientific workflows, and environmental decision-making.


Anchor QEA is developing AI-enabled digital twin capabilities for environmental and infrastructure systems. These dynamic, data-driven models integrate machine learning, cloud architectures, and domain-informed science to simulate real-world systems such as watersheds, ports, and energy assets. Prototypes developed with commercial technology partners demonstrate how digital twins assimilate heterogeneous data, support scenario analysis, and improve operational and resilience-focused decision-making. Anchor QEA grounds these tools in environmental physics and regulatory context to ensure real-world applicability. This work aligns with Genesis mission areas in AI-driven scientific workflows, water-energy systems, and infrastructure resilience.

Anchor QEA developed an identity-link generalized additive model (GAM) to decompose heterogeneous environmental monitoring history into smooth and parametric components that reflect the underlying sampling design and data-generating process.

Anchor QEA developed a novel feature engineering technique to address real-world geochemical complexity and data sparsity in an industrial groundwater monitoring dataset. This approach was used with GRUs to improve compliance forecasting by 5–10%.

Anchor QEA leads a large-scale remediation program integrating site characterization, modeling, and automated treatment systems for a complex upland and sediment site with heterogeneous contamination. The project combines high-resolution data collection, 3D subsurface/NAPL modeling, and groundwater–surface water interaction analysis to inform adaptive design. A key innovation is a fully automated hydraulic control system with real-time monitoring and dynamic response, supported by integrated data workflows. This work demonstrates data-driven, system-scale approaches aligned with Genesis priorities in subsurface systems, environmental resilience, and AI-enabled workflows.

Anchor QEA leads large-scale source control and monitoring for agricultural systems to reduce nitrate impacts to groundwater, integrating fate and transport analysis with field-scale data collection across regulated environments. The project applies system-scale evaluation of nutrient loading, groundwater response, and mitigation effectiveness. Key elements include continuous monitoring and adaptive management of engineered controls, generating long-term datasets to assess performance. This work demonstrates data-driven approaches to water quality and environmental systems, aligned with Genesis priorities in water-energy systems and AI-enabled environmental decision-making.

Project Idea Summary

Anchor QEA is seeking to partner on AI-enabled solutions for complex environmental systems, with a focus on Genesis challenge areas such as Predicting U.S. Water for Energy (15B), Unleashing Subsurface Strategic Energy Assets (17A), and AI for Scientific Reasoning (19B). Our project concept centers on developing AI-augmented workflows and digital representations (e.g., surrogate models or hybrid physics-AI systems) for groundwater–surface water interaction, contaminant fate and transport, hydrology, and water quality in real-world, data-limited settings.

We bring deep subject matter expertise in hydrology, hydrogeology, geochemistry, and remediation, along with experience working with complex, sparse, and long-horizon environmental datasets in regulatory and operational contexts. Our role is to ground AI approaches in defensible environmental physics, chemistry, and biology; monitoring realities; and decision-making needs, including regulatory requirements.
We are seeking a commercial AI or technology partner with advanced modeling capabilities (e.g., physics-informed ML, multimodal learning, explainable AI) and/or scalable software platforms. Ideal partners can help develop, operationalize, and deploy AI tools that integrate with domain-informed workflows, enabling robust prediction, uncertainty quantification, and actionable insights for water, energy, and environmental resilience challenges.
