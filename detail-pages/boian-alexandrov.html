    Introduction

    SLIC.AI is a next-generation deep-tech AI company founded by former Los Alamos National Laboratory scientists. Powered by award-winning SmartTensors AI technology (R&D 100, 2021), along with T-ELF, THOR, and the SLIC AI-suites — SLIC.AI delivers domain-specific, explainable intelligence and extraction of hidden features without hallucinations, performs anomaly detection, and delivers fast solutions of ultra-large partial differential equations and high-dimensional integrals with tensor networks.

    SLIC.AI employs deep analytics on extra-large (up to exabytes) or small, domain-specific data to uncover hidden patterns — swiftly classifying, providing insights, and forecasting outcomes at depths unattainable by existing methods. From biopharma and national security to manufacturing, intelligence, and finance, SLIC's unsupervised AI finds what others miss: patterns, risks, fraud, and answers grounded in real-time analytics.

    Unlike general-purpose LLMs, SLIC builds advanced Knowledge Graphs enabling computation of complex interrelationships — purpose-built for tasks where predictability, traceability, and tailored insights are non-negotiable. SLIC.AI effectiveness has been validated through field demonstrations and hundreds of scientific papers, including emerging trend discovery, cyber threat detection, power grid and nuclear emergency response modeling, data super-compression, and domain-specific generative AI with Structured RAGs, Knowledge Graphs, Vector Stores.     Relevant Projects

    1. NNSA Venture: Advanced Data Analytics for Proliferation Detection (ADAPD)
    Role: Tensor Lead

    ADAPD developed innovative methods for predicting nuclear proliferation by combining physics-based and expert models with experimental data through explainable unsupervised machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI).

    My role: I led the application of Nonnegative Tensor Factorization methods developed by my team to extract robust and explainable latent features. I also developed unsupervised exploratory AI tools for identifying multi-phenomenological signatures that characterize the state of an object of interest.

    Features extracted from diverse data sources—such as surveillance data, experimental measurements, model simulations, and unstructured (“soft”) data (e.g., text)—define a baseline state representing characteristic patterns of the object. These baseline patterns enable the detection of deviations indicative of anomalous or proliferative activity.

    SmartTensors AI technology was successfully applied within several  NNSA  Programs.

    2. DOE, LANL, LDRD DR: Tensor Networks for Big Data Analytics
    Role: Principal Investigator (PI)

    This project pioneered cutting-edge explainable machine learning approaches, exploring a new class of AI capable of learning and discovering patterns without human supervision.

    A key innovation was the use of Nonnegative Tensor and Matrix Factorization methods to extract essential, interpretable latent features from large-scale, high-dimensional, domain-specific datasets. These methods provide powerful tools for robust unsupervised learning, data mining, and anomaly detection, enabling the identification of interpretable hidden patterns with no direct analogs in existing approaches for ultra-large datasets.

    The project resulted in the development of SmartTensors AI technology, which won an R&D 100 Award (2021) in IT and a Bronze Medal in the Market Disruptor category.

    3. NNSA: Scientific Leadership Identification and Characterization (SLIC)
    Role: Principal Investigator (PI)

    SLIC, built on SmartTensors AI and applied to large-scale text corpora, developed a transformative capability for tracking scientific research and development with potential proliferation relevance.

    The system enables analysts to explore and query of vast focused scientific literature while simultaneously analyzing co-author networks, institutional affiliations, and the evolution of research topics. This integrated capability supports:

    - Identification of scientific and institutional leaders

    - Detection of emerging research trends

    - Monitoring of shifts in research priorities that may indicate strategic or technological developments

    - The project resulted in the development of SLIC AI suit.

    4. DOE, LANL, LDRD DR: Tensor Networks for Ultra-Fast Solving of Ultra-Large PDEs (2023–2025)
    Role: Principal Investigator (PI)

    This project developed innovative AI methods based on tensor networks for solving extremely large-scale partial differential equations (PDEs) and computing configurational integrals.

    The resulting suit, THOR AI, demonstrated breakthrough performance, achieving extreme compression while maintaining full computational access to tensor network representations. Specifically, the methods enabled:

    - Representation of yottabyte-scale transport operators using megabyte-scale tensor networks

    - Computation of configurational integrals in seconds instead of thousands of hours

    - No loss of accuracy despite the massive compression

    Project Idea Summary

    SLIC.AI proposes to contribute to collaborative projects through its advanced AI capabilities in:

    1. Domain-Specific Mini-AI for Scientific Reasoning SLIC.AI builds domain-specific Mini-AI models combining fine-tuned LLMs with curated knowledge graphs, aligning with the "composable, modular" paradigm of Topic 19: AI for Scientific Reasoning. This focus area emphasizes integrating base LLMs with specialized models for cross-domain reasoning. The Mini-AI framework is generalizable across any scientific domain.

    2. Cybersecurity and AI Robustness SLIC.AI builds upon demonstrated capabilities in cyber threat and anomaly detection, including identifying malicious activities in user source–destination data and detecting persistent attackers. Extending these to probing AI models for vulnerabilities is a natural progression, aligning with Topic 20: Cybersecurity for AI-Driven Science Workflows.

    3. Scalable AI for Materials Discovery SLIC.AI is scaling proven technology, not building from scratch. Its SLIC AI and THOR AI suites form a complete materials discovery pipeline. The SIGHT module predicts missing material–property associations for inverse design—validated by predicting superconductivity in NbSe₂ after all related papers were removed from the corpus. THOR AI computes thermodynamic properties via configurational integrals 400× faster than molecular dynamics. These close the autonomous discovery loop of Topic 12: Designing Materials with Predictable Functionality.
