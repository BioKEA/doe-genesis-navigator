Introduction

Altech Solutions is an SBA 8(a) certified small business specializing in AI-driven infrastructure intelligence for federal mission environments. Our work originates from the NASA AEGIS program, where we observed network engineers spending hours manually correlating syslog data to identify root causes — and built TLE (The Little Engine) to solve it.

TLE is now a three-module integrated platform: a physics-based digital twin and closed-loop simulation engine (Stream Service), an AI-driven anomaly detection and root cause analysis module, and a governance, risk and compliance layer. The platform generates high-fidelity, physics-constrained synthetic telemetry — including deterministic fault injection across thermal, kinetic, and network domains — producing the ground-truth training data autonomous AI agents require but cannot obtain from real-world operational logs.

Our alignment with the Genesis Mission is direct: DOE needs a closed-loop environment where AI agents can inject faults, observe physics-based consequences, and learn at scale before touching production infrastructure. That is what TLE was built to do.

We are pursuing Topic 10 (Data Centers) and Topic 21 (AFFECT) under Phase I. Our team includes two university partners and a Fortune 500 industry anchor in advanced discussions. We are actively seeking a DOE/NNSA laboratory connection to further strengthen the submission.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: NASA AEGIS — Network Intelligence & RCA Development

Client: NASA / Leidos (Prime) Period: Ongoing

Altech serves as a cybersecurity and network operations subcontractor on the NASA AEGIS program under Leidos. In this role, Altech engineers are embedded in mission-critical network operations supporting NASA data center infrastructure. A key outcome of this engagement was the observation that network engineers were spending hours manually correlating syslog data across disparate systems to identify root causes of infrastructure anomalies. This operational pain point became the conceptual foundation for TLE. Altech developed and demonstrated an initial correlation engine that ingested Syslog Server data alongside Spectrum simulated network data, routing the combined signal through an on-premises LLM to produce plain-English root cause analysis and remediation recommendations. This reduced triage time from hours to minutes and established the core RCA building block that underpins TLE's current architecture.

Project 2: NASA NEST — Digital Infrastructure Support

Client: NASA / Leidos (Prime) Period: Recent

Altech supported the NASA NEST program as a subcontractor providing digital infrastructure and IT modernization services. This engagement expanded Altech's operational footprint across NASA's enterprise IT environment and deepened the team's understanding of multi-system data flows, compliance requirements, and the operational complexity of managing federated government infrastructure at scale. Learnings from NEST directly informed the governance, risk and compliance (GRC) module of TLE, particularly the blast radius visualization capability that maps the downstream impact of a system failure or security event across dependent infrastructure components.

Project 3: TLE Platform Development — Internal R&D

Client: Internal / Altech Solutions Period: 2023–Present

Altech has conducted sustained internal R&D to develop TLE (The Little Engine), a proprietary AI intelligence layer for federal infrastructure environments. Building on AEGIS contract learnings, the team developed three integrated modules: a physics-based digital twin and closed-loop simulation engine (Stream Service), an AI-driven anomaly detection and root cause analysis module, and a governance, risk and compliance layer with blast radius visualization. The platform uses a composite signal model — baseline, physics, entropy, and seasonality — to generate high-fidelity synthetic telemetry across thousands of simultaneous infrastructure streams. A working prototype is live at demo.tle-lab.com. Co-development is underway with Louisiana Tech University, with academic expansion discussions ongoing with the University of Alabama Huntsville.

Project Idea Summary

Altech Solutions is pursuing Topic 10 (Securing U.S. Leadership in Data Centers) and Topic 21 (AFFECT — AI in Fluid Flow for Energy Components) under Phase I of DE-FOA-0003612.

Our project proposes TLE (The Little Engine) as a physics-based closed-loop simulation and AI intelligence platform for federal infrastructure environments. TLE addresses the Genesis Mission's core data gap: autonomous AI agents cannot learn to defend and optimize energy infrastructure from real-world operational logs alone — those datasets are overwhelmingly healthy baseline data. TLE's Stream Service generates high-fidelity, physics-constrained synthetic telemetry with deterministic fault injection across thermal, kinetic, and network domains, producing the ground-truth training data AI agents require.

Phase I scope: adapt TLE's existing data center physics models to energy sector components — Smart Solar Inverters, Battery Energy Storage Systems, substation networks — and validate fault cascade accuracy for cyber-physical scenarios. Platform is at TRL 4-5 with a working prototype and live demo.

We are exploring teaming with Louisiana Tech University and the University of Alabama Huntsville as academic partners, and are in advanced discussions with a Fortune 500 industry anchor.
