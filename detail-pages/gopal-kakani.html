Introduction

The University of Florida (UF), through the Global Food Systems Institute (GFSI) and UF/IFAS, brings nationally recognized expertise in AI-enabled agricultural and bioenergy systems, digital twins, and climate-smart production systems. Our team integrates multi-scale modeling (genotype × environment × management), remote sensing, soil–microbiome dynamics, and advanced computing (HiPerGator) to address complex challenges at the food–energy–water nexus.

In the context of the DOE Genesis Mission, UF is uniquely positioned to develop AI-driven, integrated digital twin platforms that couple biological, environmental, and engineered systems—bridging discovery to deployment in areas such as bioenergy feedstocks, soil carbon systems, water–energy interactions, and resilient bioeconomy pathways. Our ongoing collaborations with national and international partners, combined with strong capabilities in field-scale validation and translational research, enable us to contribute scalable, real-world solutions aligned with DOE priorities in AI-accelerated science and energy systems.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

The University of Florida provides world-class computing infrastructure through HiPerGator, one of the nation’s most powerful academic supercomputers, supporting large-scale AI, machine learning, and physics-informed modeling. The system enables high-throughput data processing, digital twin simulations, and integration of multi-modal datasets (genomics, imaging, remote sensing). Experimental or test facilities

The University of Florida provides integrated experimental and testbed capabilities spanning controlled environments, field-scale research farms, and advanced analytical facilities. UF/IFAS sites enable multi-location trials for crops, soil–water systems, and climate resilience studies. Core facilities include high-throughput phenotyping, remote sensing (UAV/IoT), and advanced imaging through ICBR. These are tightly coupled with HiPerGator HPC for AI-driven modeling, digital twins, and real-time data integration from field to lab scales.

Subject matter expertise

Dr. Vijaya Gopal Kakani is an expert in digital agriculture and bioenergy systems, interested in digital twins and multi-scale modeling of genotype × environment × management interactions. His work integrates crop physiology, soil–microbiome dynamics, remote sensing, and high-performance computing (HiPerGator) to optimize water, carbon, and biomass systems. He leads interdisciplinary efforts linking field-scale production to bioenergy, carbon sequestration, and circular bioeconomy pathways aligned with DOE’s Genesis Mission.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

We seek AI models, tools, and software for hybrid AI–physics modeling, digital twin development, and multi-modal data integration across crop, soil, water, and climate systems. Capabilities should include time-series forecasting, uncertainty quantification, and scalable ML frameworks, with support for workflow automation, model interoperability, and deployment on HPC/cloud platforms.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We seek expertise in advanced AI/ML and data science, including foundation models, hybrid AI–physics modeling, and multi-modal data integration (omics, remote sensing, environmental data). Capabilities in time-series prediction, uncertainty quantification, and scalable model training on HPC systems are essential, along with experience in autonomous workflows, data pipelines, and reproducible AI frameworks for scientific discovery.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We seek partners with datasets in AI-driven hydrologic and Earth system modeling, including coupled surface–groundwater systems and seasonal-to-multi-year prediction using hybrid AI–physics models. Multi-scale data sets of remote sensing, field, and model outputs.

Subject matter expertise

We seek subject matter expertise in crop physiology, soil–water dynamics, hydrology, and climate-resilient agricultural systems, with strengths in genotype × environment × management modeling and bioenergy crops. Expertise integrating these domains into AI-driven digital twins is essential to enable predictive, scalable water–energy–crop system optimization under variable conditions.

Project Idea Summary

AI-Driven Water–Energy–Crop Digital Twin for Resilient Systems
The AI-Driven Water–Energy–Crop Digital Twin will integrate crop, soil, hydrologic, and climate systems into a unified, predictive platform. Using hybrid AI–physics models and multi-modal data, it will enable real-time forecasting and optimization of water use, energy demand, and crop productivity, supporting resilient, climate-smart agricultural and bioenergy systems.
