Introduction

MDT Group, LLC is a woman-owned small business advisory firm focused on systems-level strategy at the intersection of energy infrastructure modernization, resilient power systems, leadership, workforce systems, advanced modeling, and AI-enabled decision support.

Our capabilities center on architecture-level advisory services for complex energy and infrastructure challenges, including hybrid load integration, grid resilience, protection and control strategy, deterministic screening methods, and secure, auditable AI-assisted engineering and R&D decision support. MDT supports utilities, infrastructure developers, public-sector initiatives, and institutional stakeholders in translating technical complexity into practical, scalable solutions.

We are particularly interested in Genesis Mission collaborations involving AI-enabled engineering and R&D workflows, resilient energy infrastructure, advanced grid systems, hybrid and distributed energy configurations, critical infrastructure applications, and cross-disciplinary partnerships that combine engineering, digital innovation, deployment strategy, workforce readiness, and stakeholder coordination.

MDT seeks collaborators in research translation, commercialization, demonstration, pilot deployment, and implementation-oriented partnerships where systems thinking, technical rigor, leadership, and strategic execution can accelerate mission impact.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

MDT offers architecture-level concepts and prototype approaches for secure, auditable AI-assisted engineering decision support, including deterministic screening concepts, modeling-informed advisory tools, and workflow concepts intended to support complex infrastructure planning and R&D decision processes. Focus is on bounded, explainable AI assistance integrated with engineering oversight, rather than black-box autonomous decision making.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Capabilities include application-oriented AI/ML expertise for engineering workflows, structured decision support, modeling integration, and analysis of complex infrastructure systems. Experience emphasizes practical use of AI to augment expert evaluation, support simulation-informed insights, and improve translation of technical complexity into scalable, mission-oriented solutions.

Subject matter expertise

Deep subject matter expertise includes resilient power systems, hybrid load integration, protection and control strategy, advanced grid modeling, deterministic screening methods, infrastructure risk evaluation, and strategic deployment planning. Expertise spans technical architecture, utility and infrastructure applications, and translation of complex engineering challenges into actionable pathways.

Other expertise

Additional expertise includes leadership strategy, workforce systems, stakeholder coordination, program formation, partnership development, and commercialization-oriented implementation planning. MDT contributes systems thinking that integrates technical, organizational, and deployment considerations needed to support interdisciplinary collaborations and mission-driven execution.

Relevant Projects

1. Systems and Methods of Circuit Protection Innovation Project (2021–2026)  
Led development of an innovation project resulting in U.S. Patent Application No. 17/684,621, Systems and Methods of Circuit Protection (filed March 18, 2026). Scope included development of methods addressing advanced circuit protection challenges for evolving power systems, including protection logic, evaluation approaches, and system-level application concepts relevant to modernized grid protection architectures. Achievements include filing of a nonprovisional U.S. utility patent application and creation of intellectual property aligned with resilient and adaptive power system operation.

2. DOE-Funded Microgrids AI Protection Support for Alaska Communities (2023–Present)  
Supporting a DOE-funded microgrids protection initiative with NLR involving resilient energy systems applications for Igiugig and Sitka, Alaska. Scope includes support for AI-assisted protection concepts, microgrid protection strategy, and technical contributions related to resilient operation of remote and community-serving energy systems. Achievements include contributions to project development, technical strategy support, and advancement of approaches linking protection engineering, resilience, and AI-enabled decision support for isolated and critical microgrid applications.

3. Deterministic Hybrid Screening Assistant and Hybrid Load Interconnection Strategy (2023–Present)  
Leading development of structured approaches for evaluating complex hybrid load interconnections involving large new loads, distributed generation, and battery energy storage, including the Deterministic Hybrid Screening Assistant concept. Scope includes deterministic screening methodologies, technical risk framing, and utility-facing workflows intended to improve consistency and speed of interconnection evaluation. Achievements include development of core screening methodology, regional utility engagement, and pilot-oriented concepts aligned with emerging infrastructure challenges.

Project Idea Summary

MDT Group seeks collaborators to advance resilient energy infrastructure and AI-enabled engineering approaches aligned with Genesis Mission challenge areas. Our immediate interests include deterministic screening methods for hybrid large-load interconnections (including data centers and distributed energy systems), AI-assisted protection and resilience applications for modern microgrids, and secure, auditable approaches supporting scientific and engineering workflows.

Current concepts include development and piloting of a Deterministic Hybrid Screening Assistant for complex interconnection evaluation, as well as collaborative work related to advanced protection methods being applied in microgrid projects, including remote and high-resilience applications.

We seek partners who can contribute complementary strengths in AI/ML methods, scientific computing, testbeds, advanced modeling, microgrid demonstrations, and translational R&D. We are particularly interested in joint proposal development, pilot demonstrations, and commercialization pathways that connect systems engineering, digital innovation, and deployment strategy to accelerate practical impact in critical infrastructure resilience.
