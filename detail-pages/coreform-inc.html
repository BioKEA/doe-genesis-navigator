Introduction

Recognized for advancing the areas of engineering analysis, Coreform is the first to develop a general-purpose, native isogeometric analysis (IGA) solver, called Coreform IGA, that up-levels speed and accuracy of the finite element method (FEM) by removing the need to defeature geometry to produce meshes suitable for complex nonlinear and multiphysics simulations. At Coreform, our mission is to serve the Model-Based Enterprise by consolidating geometries across their iterations into single sources of truth and subjecting them to truthful simulation.

Coreform's expertise in computational methods, isogeometric analysis, geometric modeling, and meshing workflows (we co-develop Cubit with Sandia National Labs) gives us a unique perspective and ability to integrate AI/ML technologies into engineering analysis workflows.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Coreform is developing AI-driven tools and software for computer-aided engineering, especially for finite element analysis, computational fluid dynamics, and isogeometric analysis.  Coreform Cubit has a ML-based capability for training & classifying geometric entities for rapid part identification within CAD models (e.g., STEP) that have been stripped of this information. It also has ML-based capabilities for defeaturing and reduced-order model generation.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Coreform has expertise in linear algebra, partial differential equations, and computer science and has experience applying these to AI/ML and data science applications.

Subject matter expertise

Coreform has subject matter expertise in:

* Computer-aided engineering workflows including geometric modeling, mesh generation, and input file setup & generation.
* Numerical methods for continuum mechanics, especially Galerkin methods such as the finite element method (FEM) and isogeometric analysis (IGA). 
* Efficient parallel algorithms for structured, unstructured, hierarchical, 1D-3D splines.

Other resources/tools

Coreform co-develops the Cubit mesh-generation software with Sandia National Labs, and provides Coreform Cubit for commercial and academic customers.  Coreform also develops Coreform IGA, a native isogeometric analysis solver that includes integrations with Abaqus/Standard, MOOSE, and EPIC. Both of these products are prime candidates for integration into AI-driven CAE workflows.

Relevant Projects

Advanced tire tread simulation through adaptive isogeometric analysis 

Demonstrated a proof-of-concept (PoC) steady-state rolling resistance simulation of realistic tire geometry within Coreform IGA. The PoC computes a nonlinear solid mechanics solution of a tire contacting the road, determines non-uniform volumetric heat generation in Endurica's Katana solver, and computes a steady-state heat transfer solution. Achievements include developing a heat transfer solver within Coreform's flex representation method (FRM) framework and loose-integration with Katana to support coupled-physics material properties and non-uniform load distributions. Critically, mesh generation is now trivial, requiring only a bounding annulus for the envelope domain to support fully-automated workflows. This verified that complex tire simulations, which typically require weeks of preparation, can be automated via FRM to provide a meshing-free workflow.

Integrating MFEM for Commercial IGA Simulation Acceleration 

Improved performance and scalability for spline-based isogeometric analysis (IGA) workflows through structured decomposition, efficient quadrature, and accelerated operator evaluation in MFEM. The project advanced methods to extract and handle structured subregions from unstructured spline data, improving serialization and parallelization while integrating decomposition routines into production workflows with automated handling of problematic mesh regions. In parallel, the project optimized quadrature-rule methods to reduce point counts while preserving compatibility with sum-factorization tensor-product structures. The project also advanced matrix-free/operator-application workflows, extending MFEM spline-patch support and demonstrating a PoC where CAD-derived structures from Coreform are solved within MFEM. GPU-enabling infrastructure and MLIR-based compilation pathways were also explored. Finally, the project executed large-deformation solid-mechanics studies, including Taylor bar impact and quasistatic DIW pad compression with frictional contact.

Enabling the Broader Use of MOOSE for Nuclear Energy and Other Simulation 

This project established an extraction-based interoperability pathway between Coreform's tools and MOOSE. A combined mesh and matrix format was collaboratively defined and integrated into MOOSE, enabling standard workflows to operate on data from Coreform’s IGA and FRM pipelines. Early demonstrations validated architectural compatibility via linear solid mechanics, while later efforts focused on benchmark testing and external use. By project end, engineers at a commercial nuclear organization independently executed simulations using the Coreform–MOOSE workflow. Simultaneously, effort was devoted to improving trimmed U-spline robustness for complex CAD geometries. A growing suite of nuclear-relevant models, compiled with stakeholders, drove extensive reliability improvements. These results include fallback capabilities that enhance reliability when underlying commercial CAD kernels fail.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

We're seeking partners who have "substrate" AI frameworks or tools that can enable Coreform's computer-aided engineering products and services to deploy AI workflows within high-assurance environments.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We're seeking partners who have expertise in AI/ML, especially when paired with subject matter expertise in relevant application areas, manufacturing processes, etc.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Coreform has several on-site servers, which are suitable for high-end CAE and software development, but not suitable for training or inference of leading AI models. Thus Coreform is interested in partners who are able to provide computing infrastructure for these (and related) purposes.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Coreform is seeking partners who, having unique subject matter expertise in relevant application areas, are able to provide representative (if not production or proprietary) datasets.

Experimental or test facilities

Coreform is seeking partners are able to provide experimental or test facilities and capabilities relevant to project application areas.

Subject matter expertise

Coreform is seeking partners with subject matter expertise in developing flagship DOE (including DOE/NNSA) HPC codes, application of CAE tools to solve engineering problems, advanced manufacturing processes, and nuclear energy physics & engineering.

Other expertise

We have extensive experience contracting with DOE & DOE/NNSA labs and manufacturing facilities and have extensive networks within the DOE and DOE/NNSA sites.

Project Idea Summary

We are interested in projects looking to port flagship DOE/NNSA codes to modern frameworks (e.g., MFEM) and simultaneously integrating Coreform's IGA technology as we've done with MFEM, Abaqus, MOOSE, and EPIC to enable meshing-free simulation workflows for complex, nonlinear, multiphysics applications.

We're also interested in developing AI or agentic tools that utilize Coreform's IGA technology and geometric-modeling expertise to enable trusted, verifiable, auditable simulation workflows.

We're also interested in providing meshing-free IGA and model-prep solutions for other projects with challenging simulation needs.
