Introduction

We are a multi-institutional research team from the University of Dayton and Wright State University, including Dr. Krishna B. Kidambi, Dr. Nicholas Stiffler, Dr. Raul Ordonez, Dr. Sean Banerjee, and Dr. Natasha Banerjee. Our team brings expertise in advanced control systems, decision-making under uncertainty, physics-informed machine learning, and immersive virtual and mixed-reality platforms for human-in-the-loop experimentation. We are working on Challenge Focus 1: Reenvisioning Advanced Manufacturing and Industrial Productivity – AI-Enabled Smart Manufacturing (AMMTO), aiming to develop agentic AI systems that enable the transition to Industry 5.0 manufacturing through real-time, immersive digital twins for human and robotic workforce training.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

We develop advanced AI models for complex dynamical systems, including physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) for learning system dynamics and control policies in uncertain, nonlinear environments. Our work integrates data-driven learning with first-principles physics to enable robust prediction, control, and decision-making.

Experimental or test facilities

We have developed and deployed immersive virtual and augmented reality (VR/AR) platforms for human-in-the-loop experimentation and validation of AI systems. This includes projects such as AI-driven augmented reality systems for mitigating musculoskeletal injuries and extended reality environments for simulating human-robot collaboration.

Subject matter expertise

Our team brings interdisciplinary expertise in control systems, physics-informed modeling, AI-driven manufacturing, and immersive simulation technologies. We have demonstrated experience in AI-enabled custom manufacturing systems, including NSF-supported work on intelligent metal recycling and production using AI, extended reality, and human-robot collaboration. This combination of expertise enables us to address complex challenges at the intersection of AI, physical systems, and industrial applications.

Project Idea Summary

Our team is working on Challenge Focus: 1 - Reenvisioning Advanced Manufacturing and Industrial Productivity: AI-Enabled Smart Manufacturing (AMMTO). As manufacturing operations move towards Industry 5.0 practices, our team proposes the development of agentic-AI-based systems that enable traditional manufacturing operations to move towards smart manufacturing that use real-time immersive digital twins for training of human and robotic workers. As part of our proposed framework, we will incorporate real-time states of robots, humans, and machinery through existing sensing found in typical industrial systems as well as wearables for human workers and operators. Our reasoning models will be developed to optimize allocation of human, machine, and automation resources based on current and predicted task needs with provisions for providing as-needed human-in-the-loop assistance. We are seeking partners who have access to existing manufacturing facilities that will be able and willing to provide access to floor plans, automation systems (e.g. mobile or fixed robots), equipment used, and personnel roles and actions.
