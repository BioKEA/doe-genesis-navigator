    Introduction

    Our research group focuses on creating sustainable engineered systems for environmental and energy applications by controlling molecular interactions of biomaterials. Our goal is to design innovative bionanomaterials, control their molecular interactions, and translate their nanoscale properties into scalable and sustainable systems. By leveraging unique programmability of biomaterials, we aim to develop environmentally benign technologies for biohydrometallurgy, molecular separations, water treatment, and energy conversion. Our current research focus is the development of sustainable systems for extracting rare earth elements (REEs), addressing the environmental limitations of conventional separation processes. We have developed novel M13 bacteriophage (phage)-based biosorbents, capable of inducing stimuli-responsive phase separations for REE recovery, eliminating the need for toxic extractants and organic solvents. Also, we are developing nanofibrous bacterial cellulose as bio scaffold for REEs separation. These programmable and smart biomaterials (phage and bacterial cellulose) exhibit excellent stability and recyclability, highlighting their strong potential for scalable and sustainable critical mineral separations.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    Experimental or test facilities

    We genetically and chemically modify biomaterials to develop advanced functional materials for sustainable applications. We engineer bacteriophages (phages) and bacterial cellulose for selective rare earth element (REE) recovery. Engineered phages incorporating REE-binding peptides show high selectivity and scalability. We also developed a thermo-responsive REE recovery system for selective lanthanide separation from complex sources and functionalized bacterial cellulose to enhance REE separation performance.

    Relevant Projects

    1. EFRI ELiS: Bacteria-Bacteriophage Hybrid System for Rare Earth Element (REEs) Biomining

    Scope of work: we genetically modified bacteriophage to develop stimuli-responsive REE adsorbent. 

    Achievement: we first time demonstrated virus-based selective REE separation through temperature modulation. This work was highlighted in UC Berkeley Engineering news: 

    https://engineering.berkeley.edu/news/2025/11/researchers-pioneer-greener-way-to-extract-rare-earth-elements/

    2. DARPA-SPREE: Separation and Purification of Rare Earth Elements. 

    Scope of work: we developed elastin-like peptide-based hydrogel to develop bulk-induced REE adsorption resin that can be used for ion exchange chromatography for individual REEs separation

    Achievement: we demonstrated porous protein hydrogel that can selectively separate REEs with high capacity, then demonstrated individual REEs separation. 

    3. Biomineralization: evolutionary approach to tissue engineering materials using phage

    Scope of work: we combined directed evolution and supramolecular assembly of phage to develop collagen-like tissue engineering materials

    Achievement: we could mineralize hydroxyapatite crystals on human dentin with fibrous phage structure.
