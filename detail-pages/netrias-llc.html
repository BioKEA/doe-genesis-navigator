Introduction

Netrias is a small business focused on curating and harmonizing heterogenous, multi-omics data to make it AI-ready for application to AI algorithms in domains such as biomarker discovery, predictive protein function, and automated biodesign/synthetic biology design, build test, and learn loops.

Netrias has built and deployed data harmonization and curation systems under programs funded by LLNL, DARPA, DTRA, ARPA-H, and NIH, processing diverse scientific datasets across biosurveillance, materials characterization, and clinical research. Netrias has developed fine-tuned models for scientific terminology standardization and domain-specific data validation, which will assist in the Genesis Mission's goals to integrate and apply AI to vast amounts of DOE data.

Netrias has experience collaborating with academic and government research teams to integrate tooling into real scientific workflows. This combination of technical depth, domain breadth across federal science programs, and a track record of collaborative deployment positions Netrias to deliver open-source tools that will benefit the Genesis Mission Consortium and larger DOE community.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Netrias builds and applies AI models to support automated curation and harmonization. Our core capabilities include: 

Metadata Harmonization. This capability operates on field names, schema structures, controlled vocabularies, and units of measurement rather than on the data values themselves, aligning them to ontologies and converting between different target formats. We include confidence scores and human-readable explanations for every mapping decision. 

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Predictive modeling. Development and application of novel embedding, tokenization, and AI algorithm architectures to predict and classify within domains of interest. Past work includes predicting the performance of ice modulating proteins, pathogenicity of bacteria, and optimization of synthetic biology design/build/test/learn loops through experiment combinatorial design prediction.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Data Curation. Statistical profiling to covering distributions, null rates, type consistency, cardinality, and inter-column correlations. Detect anomalies that deviate from distributional norms, including physically implausible measurements, unit-scale errors, and temporal inconsistencies. 

Subject matter expertise

Subject Matter Expertise includes data harmonization/curation, predictive modeling, metadata standards, ontology alignment, and experiment optimization.

Relevant Projects

DARPA OMNISCREEN - The goal of this effort was to transition, apply, and expand Netrias harmonization technology that performs harmonization of term metadata and predicts the pathogenic potential of bacteria from low-throughput plate-based phenotypic assays, as well as high-throughput low-dimensional droplet assays to high-dimensional multi-omics data.

The effort significantly enhanced the platform's capability to harmonize metadata from and model high-dimensional multi-omics assays of bacterial threats using data from the DARPA Friend or Foe OmniScreen project. The OmniScreen project collected multi-omics data across a wide array of bacterial threats. We transitioned low-dimensional models to the high-dimensionality that is present in multi-omics data, starting with proteomics and moving on to other ‘omics datasets that were collected. Given the high-dimensional nature of the data, we enhanced our predictive capabilities with the inclusion of prior knowledge (e.g. protein-protein interaction networks, protein structure) of bacterial threats. Data from the OmniScreen project enabled us to transition this concept to characterize a model’s performance using high-dimensional, dynamic prior knowledge of ‘omics data in publicly available sources with the high-dimensionality of the assays collected under OmniScreen.

DTRA CARES - The objective of this project was to advance the development, deployment, and operationalization of machine learning tools for biological pathogen detection using multi-omics data. Specifically, this included: (1) harmonizing and integrating diverse omics datasets through standardized metadata correction and provenance tracking; (2) developing and refining machine learning models to predict phenotypic outcomes such as virulence; (3) automating the extraction and training of high-performing models to reduce manual intervention; and (4) deploying models within a searchable, version-controlled framework that supports performance monitoring and re-training triggers. Together, these efforts created a scalable, reproducible, and interpretable modeling pipeline that supported DTRA’s mission to identify and assess emerging biological pathogens.

DARPA ICE - Curated one of the largest (>18k proteins) datasets of ice binding proteins across over 20 sources into a single corpus with consistent annotations. Developed a neural-network based toolkit that enables protein engineers to screen proteins based on functional relevance, expression, and activity, all of which achieved 90% accuracy from sequence and context. Released the toolkit for the larger community and it is actively being used to screen proteins of interest. Piloted a test harness to train, evaluate, and share the expression model in a consistent way to form a community around design of ice binding proteins
