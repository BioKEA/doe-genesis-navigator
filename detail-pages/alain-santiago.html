Introduction

Professor Santiago is a highly accomplished researcher and academic at Palm Beach Atlantic University, and an Early Career Advisor for Chem, a leading journal in Chemistry and related fields. With over twelve years of research experience in electrochemistry and (bio)nanomaterials, he has developed innovative solutions in electrochemical sensing, energy conversion, and energy storage.
He leads the Energy Nanomaterials Lab, a research group that is dedicated to bridging the gap between electrochemistry and artificial intelligence. By developing a specialized AI-driven pipeline, we aim to accelerate the discovery of bioinspired energy materials and redefine how catalytic reactions are optimized.
Previously, Professor Santiago worked as a Research Fellow in Prof. Goodenough's group (Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2019) and as a Postdoctoral Researcher in Prof. Echegoyen's group (Former President of the American Chemical Society), where he led pioneering projects on water-splitting reactions using innovative materials such as unique endohedral fullerene/fullertubes based low-dimensional heterostructure architectures. He has published 80 articles in top-ranked chemistry journals, including Journal of American Chemical Society (x7), Journal of Materials Chemistry A (x5), Angewandte Chemie (x4), Green Chemistry (x3), Chemical Society Reviews (x2), Chem (x2), Applied Catalysis B: Environment and Energy (x2), Advanced Energy Materials, Energy and Environmental Science, and ACS Catalysis.
