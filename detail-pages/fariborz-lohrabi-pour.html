Introduction

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte , specializing in the design of high-performance analog, mixed-signal, and RF integrated circuits. My research focuses on developing hardware accelerators for AI and RF applications.

As the Principal Investigator for the Cryo-Cortex initiative , I am leading a multi-institutional effort to develop Superconducting RF-Neural Accelerators for exascale edge-sensing. Our project aims to overcome the energy-density and thermal bottlenecks of conventional CMOS by utilizing superconducting logic and RFIC circuit motifs to perform neural inference directly at the cold-stage (<120K).

My technical expertise includes the design of novel RF-Neural Network (RF-NN) architectures for ultra-low-power neural weights and activations. We are seeking partners, specifically within DOE National Laboratories and the microelectronics industry who can provide access to cryogenic fabrication testbeds or assist in benchmarking and commercializing these next-generation edge-AI platforms.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

We are offering a Digital Twin framework of the Cryo-Cortex hardware. This tool enables researchers to simulate, optimize, and validate scientific AI workflows on the American Science Cloud before physical deployment in cryogenic environments.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our team provides expertise in RF-Neural Network (RF-NN) architectures and analog Multiply-Accumulate (MAC) units. We specialize in hardware-software co-design to achieve exascale AI advantage with significant energy and latency reductions.

Subject matter expertise

I provide expertise in cryogenic electronics and RFIC design. This includes high-fidelity modeling of superconducting interconnects and designing bias-stable analog neural primitives that account for extreme environment phenomena like the kink effect.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: Spin-Encoded RF Communications Using Topological Nano-Antennas

Scope: Investigating the use of spin-encoded RF communications for secure and compact wireless links.

Achievements: Successfully awarded $50,000 from the Commonwealth Cyber Initiative (CCI) Fellows Program and $45,000 from the Virginia Microelectronics Consortium (VMEC) to explore these beyond-CMOS communication primitives.

 

Project 2: RF-Neural Network (RF-NN) Architecture for Proactive Anomaly Detection

Scope: Developing analog implementations of Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN) in the RF domain to enable proactive anomaly detection in B5G wireless networks.

Achievements: Research accepted for presentation at the 2025 IEEE International Symposium on Circuits and Systems (ISCAS), providing the foundation for the RF-neural motifs used in the Cryo-Cortex initiative.

 

Project 3: Secure and Self-powered Wireless IoT Devices

Scope: Developing Physical Unclonable Functions (PUFs) and energy harvesting solutions to create secure, autonomous, and self-sustainable IoT devices.

Achievements: Awarded $40,000 by the Commonwealth Cyber Initiative (CCI) to investigate the integration of hardware-level security with sustainable power conversion.

Project Idea Summary

Our project, Cryo-Cortex, addresses the Recentering Microelectronics in America challenge by developing Superconducting RF-Neural Accelerators. We aim to bypass the thermal bottlenecks of conventional CMOS in cryogenic environments by performing neural inference directly at the cold-stage. We are seeking partners from DOE National Laboratories to gain access to mK-scale microelectronics testbeds for hardware validation. Additionally, we seek industry partners with expertise in edge-AI benchmarking to assist in commercialization and validating our digital twin framework.
