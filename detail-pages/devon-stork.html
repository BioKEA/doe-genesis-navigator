Introduction

Pioneer Labs is a nonprofit research organization focused on creating microorganisms for extreme environments. 

Our Genesis Mission-relevant platform is a scalable, AI-guided functional genomics system that characterizes genes from across the microbial tree of life, including unculturable organisms, by expressing them in diverse host strains under diverse stress conditions. The result is a high-dimensional phenotypic dataset linking sequence to function, directly enabling AI models for genotype-to-phenotype prediction, gene transfer success, and functional annotation of genetic dark matter.

At full scale, this proposal spans 1,000 donor genomes × 50 hosts × 100 conditions, yielding 5×10⁹ fitness measurements. A phased, de-risked scale-up strategy incorporating active learning reduces experimental costs by 10×, making this tractable within Genesis Mission Phase I and II funding horizons. The resulting AI models for protein variant effect prediction, gene transfer success, and functional annotation are precisely the predictive biological reasoning tools the Genesis Mission aims to accelerate.

We seek National Lab partners for a Phase I application under Topic 2, especially Focus Area 2B (Genotype to Phenotype).

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI/ML/Data science expertise

Pioneer Labs is developing three categories of AI models trained on functional genomics data: protein variant effect prediction, which leverages natural homology across donor genomes to predict fitness from sequence; gene transfer success prediction, which models the likelihood of functional expression across donor-host combinations; and functional class prediction, which assigns and predicts annotations for genes of unknown function, including from unculturable microbes. A closed-loop active learning framework guides experiment selection, reducing data collection costs by approximately 10×.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Our proposal is a large-scale functional genomics dataset linking genetic sequence to phenotypic function across the microbial tree of life. At Full Scale, yields 5×10⁹ fitness measurements on naturally-existing DNA fragments. Data is generated by expressing fragmented donor genomes in diverse microbial hosts under stressful conditions and measuring competitive fitness via barcode sequencing. All data and computational code will be publicly available via a REST API, with snapshots archived to third-party repositories. Preliminary data is already available on the Align Foundation platform.

Experimental or test facilities

Pioneer Labs operates a functional genomics wet lab platform for high-throughput overexpression screens, pooling fragmented donor genomes into barcoded libraries, conjugating those libraries into diverse microbial hosts, and conducting parallel competitive fitness selections across many environmental conditions. We envision full-scale data collection distributed across multiple partner facilities, with automation of growth-based selection, sampling, and NGS library preparation enabling thousands of samples per week per site, with site-to-site reproducibility as a key validation metric.

Subject matter expertise

Pioneer Labs brings expertise in microbial functional genomics, metagenomic sampling, and AI-based biological modeling. Our team has developed protocols for heterologous overexpression screens applicable to diverse, non-model host organisms across wide phylogenetic distances, building on methods including Boba-Seq and CIFR. We have particular expertise in identifying stress-tolerance genes relevant to harsh industrial and extraterrestrial environments, with direct relevance to DOE biomanufacturing and bioenergy applications.

Relevant Projects

Pioneer Labs has existed for less than 2 years, so we do not yet have many mature multi-year projects. Within that period, however, we have built several relevant engineering biology efforts in microbial adaptation, functional genomics, and biomanufacturing.

1. BioBloom: whole-genome barcoded saturation mutagenesis in E. coli 
Scope of work: Pioneer Labs developed BioBloom, a retron-based method for barcoded saturation mutagenesis across an entire bacterial genome. This included donor library design and synthesis, pooled library construction in E. coli, sequencing workflows, and analysis of selection experiments under antibiotic and salt stress. 
Achievements: We built a barcoded library with >99% projected sampling of all single-nucleotide polymorphisms in the E. coli genome. BioBloom identified beneficial mutations under rifampicin and salt selection, enabling rapid discovery of causal SNPs at nucleotide resolution. Compared with adaptive laboratory evolution and related methods, it improved speed, resolution, and sequencing efficiency. We also prepared an improved Biobloom-E.coli-2.0 library for broader community use that will be distributed through Addgene soon. 

2. Quantitative functional genomics for data-driven evolution 
Scope of work: Pioneer Labs built an experimental and computational workflow to generate genotype-to-fitness data from large pooled libraries of extremophile genomic fragments. The initial proof of concept used Halomonas elongata genomic DNA in E. coli under salt selection, combining long-read mapping of inserts to barcodes, short-read tracking over time, and statistical analysis of fitness effects. 
Achievements: This project established a scalable bioinformatics readout for evolution experiments, allowing us to measure fitness across ~10^6-member libraries rather than only isolate endpoint winners. We showed that the measurements were reproducible across technical replicates and improved further when aggregated at the gene level. The work identified statistically significant fitness-associated regions and created the foundation for larger phenomics datasets and future AI/ML-guided strain engineering.

3. Additive strain engineering and directed-evolution benchmarking 
Scope of work: Pioneer Labs built and tested an “engineering crank” for microbial improvement under Mars-relevant stress, using extremophile genomic DNA libraries delivered into host microbes through plasmid- and transposon-based approaches. We also compared adaptive evolution, additive engineering, and BioBloom-style SNP screening to understand where each method performs best. 
Achievements: This work showed that additive engineering can recover beneficial functions from extremophile donor DNA and complements native-genome optimization approaches. The comparison across methods produced a practical strategy for iterative strain engineering: use adaptive evolution for fast early gains, BioBloom for causal SNP discovery, and additive engineering to introduce new pathways when native adaptation plateaus. This project helped translate Pioneer’s Mars-biomanufacturing goals into an experimentally grounded roadmap.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We seek collaborators with experience in biological foundation models, large-scale fitness landscape modeling, and active learning for experimental design. National Lab groups with expertise applying machine learning to high-dimensional genomic or proteomic datasets would complement Pioneer Labs' experimental platform and accelerate development of deployment-ready predictive tools.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Our proposed experiments require substantial HPC infrastructure for processing paired PacBio and Illumina sequencing data, running Bayesian inference over millions of variants, and training active learning models that guide experiment selection between Pilot and Full Scale stages. We seek partners with large-scale biological data processing and model training capacity.

Experimental or test facilities

At Full Scale, data collection must be distributed across multiple partner facilities running standardized selection experiments. We seek partners able to execute library conjugation, competitive growth selection, and NGS sample preparation at scale, ideally with existing liquid handling automation and high-throughput sequencing access.

Project Idea Summary

Pioneer Labs is applying to Topic 2 (Scaling the Biotechnology Revolution), Focus Area 2B (Genotype to Phenotype) and potentially 2C (Predictive Engineering of Microbial Communities).

Our proposed project centers on a large-scale microbial functional genomics platform that generates AI-ready genotype-to-phenotype data by expressing genes from diverse donor organisms across multiple host strains and environmental conditions. At Full Scale, we envision 1,000 donor genomes × 50 hosts × 100 conditions, yielding 5×10⁹ fitness measurements. A closed-loop active learning framework reduces experimental costs by 10×. AI models trained on this data address protein variant effect prediction, gene transfer success, and functional annotation of genetic dark matter from unculturable microbes. Full details are available in our public proposal: doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.17990299

We are seeking partners able to contribute one or more of the following: HPC infrastructure for large-scale sequencing data processing and model training; high-throughput sequencing capacity and automated selection experiment execution at scale; and AI/ML expertise in biological foundation models or large-scale fitness landscape modeling.

Pioneer Labs is a nonprofit. We are open to a National Lab serving as lead institution if preferred.
