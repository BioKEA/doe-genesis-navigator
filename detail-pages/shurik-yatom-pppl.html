Introduction

I am a plasma diagnostics specialist at Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory with deep expertise in laser scattering, laser spectroscopy and emission spectroscopy plasmas. I also specialize in plasma sources and applications, plasma-induced chemistry, plasma-assisted synthesis of materials. My recent work (Yatom et al., Plasma Sources Sci. Technol. 35 035007, 2026) demonstrated a fast feed-forward neural network that extracts absolute Ar metastable densities directly from standard broadband OES. This capability directly supports Genesis Mission Topic 11.C by enabling the valorization of routine, high-throughput diagnostics into rich datasets that reveal unexpected correlations and phenomena not predicted by current models. I am interested in developing a closed-loop AI framework that uses standard diagnostics to autonomously identify such discrepancies, generate plausible new mechanistic hypotheses, and test them in real time-  precisely matching the high-priority goal of AI-enabled hypothesis generation and scientific discovery.

We have a PPPL team and an academic partner. We are interested in an industrial partner to co-develop and validate this platform in real manufacturing environments, particularly for plasma-mediated processes in microelectronics and advanced materials synthesis. Such a collaboration would give the partner early access to a cutting-edge AI-driven plasma insight and process optimization tool.

Project Idea Summary

We are interested in developing a closed-loop AI framework under Genesis Mission Topic 11.C (AI-Accelerated Science: Correlation to Understanding) that valorizes standard, low-cost online diagnostics (broadband OES, fast imaging, FTIR, I/V) into a powerful engine for autonomous hypothesis generation and real-time plasma process control. Built on our recent work (Yatom et al., Plasma Sources Sci. Technol. 35 035007, 2026) - a fast feed-forward neural network that extracts absolute Ar 1s₅ metastable densities from routine OES in real time - and the PINN architecture from Lin et al. (2024), the system will discover unexpected correlations not predicted by current models, autonomously generate plausible new mechanistic hypotheses, and test them in closed loop.

The primary challenge focus is plasma-mediated chemistry for nanomaterial synthesis (carbon onions, nanotubes, nanodiamonds), methane and CO₂ decomposition, and hydrogen generation, with additional relevance to microelectronics plasma processes. The AI-driven workflow will accelerate scientific discovery while enabling high-precision, repeatable plasma synthesis techniques across these applications.
