Introduction

Denovicon Therapeutics is a computational drug discovery company that has built a generative molecular design platform producing QUBO (Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization) formulations natively from machine-learned multi-objective optimization landscapes. The platform searches trillion-molecule chemical spaces on single-GPU infrastructure, generates 99.9% novel structures with improved synthesizability, and deploys without reformulation to photonic Ising machines, quantum annealers, and classical solvers. Our lead compound achieved single-digit nanomolar potency with >2,000× selectivity over off-target proteins — designed in under one year.

We have an active 18-month CINT (Center for Integrated Nanotechnologies) collaboration with Sandia and Los Alamos National Laboratories to validate the machine learning-to-QUBO pipeline on photonic Ising hardware. The underlying architecture is domain-agnostic — the same optimization framework that designs therapeutics applies to energy-relevant molecular design problems including electrolytes, catalysts, and advanced materials.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: CINT Photonic Ising Hardware Validation (2025-present)
Scope: 18-month collaboration with the Center for Integrated Nanotechnologies (Sandia/Los Alamos National Laboratories) to validate Denovicon's machine learning-to-QUBO molecular design pipeline on photonic Ising hardware. The project benchmarks QUBO-formulated molecular optimization problems against classical solvers (GPU-based simulated annealing) to evaluate whether photonic hardware provides computational advantages for real-world chemistry applications. Denovicon provides the ML pipeline and QUBO formulations; CINT provides photonic hardware access and expertise.
Achievement: First collaboration to deploy a working generative molecular design QUBO formulation on photonic Ising hardware at a DOE national laboratory. Work is ongoing with initial benchmarking underway.

Project 2: DNT-1569 PARP7 Inhibitor Discovery (2023-present)
Scope: Internal drug discovery program using Denovicon's generative platform to design a highly selective PARP7 inhibitor for immuno-oncology. The platform was tasked with generating novel scaffolds optimized simultaneously for on-target potency, off-target selectivity, and drug-like properties.
Achievement: Discovered DNT-1569 in under one year — PARP7 = 4 nM, with >2,000x selectivity over PARP1 and >2,500x over PARP2. The clinical benchmark (RBN-2397) achieves only ~12x selectivity over PARP1. DNT-1569 represents a novel scaffold with a dramatically improved selectivity profile. Immune biomarker assays on next-generation compounds are in progress.

Project 3: Materials Science Consulting for a Major Technology Company (2020-2025)
Scope: Five-year consulting engagement applying computational chemistry, physics-based modeling, and AI/ML expertise to advanced materials challenges for a major technology company. Work involved molecular-level modeling and optimization of material properties relevant to next-generation product development. Engagement subject to indefinite confidentiality obligations.
Achievement: Demonstrated extensibility of computational molecular optimization approaches beyond therapeutics into materials science applications, establishing domain-agnostic platform capabilities that span drug discovery and energy-relevant molecular design.
