    Introduction

    I am a theoretical plasma physicist whose work focuses on self-organized states and reduced theoretical descriptions of complex physical systems. My research emphasizes identifying physically interpretable structure in nonlinear, high-dimensional dynamics and using that structure to build reduced models of complex behavior. As it pertains to the Genesis Mission, I am interested in collaborations where such reduced physical structure can inform interpretable AI models, physics-constrained surrogates, digital twins, or reduced state representations for complex-system prediction, classification, or control. My likely role in a Genesis team would be in the theoretical and modeling layer, working with partners who provide application-specific data, simulations, experimental platforms, or AI implementation.

    Relevant Projects

    Project 1: Formation of multiscale structures in a self-gravitating dusty plasma with matter current

    Developed a theoretical model for multiscale magnetic-field structure formation in a rotating, self-gravitating dusty plasma with matter current. The work formulated a three-component fluid description with electrons, ions, and charged dust grains, including gravitomagnetic effects generated by mass currents. A major outcome was the derivation of a triple-curl Beltrami equilibrium that supports three distinct spatial scales determined by a cubic eigenvalue problem. The analysis showed how rotation, self-gravitation, and electromagnetic interactions jointly produce organized plasma states, and identified parameter regimes in which dust-mediated gravitational effects become comparable to electromagnetic forces, significantly altering equilibrium field and flow structure. Achievements include a peer-reviewed publication in Physical Review E (2025) and support through NSF RUI Award No. 2310066. This project is relevant to the Genesis Mission because it demonstrates experience in constructing reduced, physically interpretable descriptions of complex nonlinear systems, with potential application to physics-informed surrogate modeling, reduced state representations, and interpretable AI workflows for complex physical systems.

    Project 2: Beltrami-Bernoulli equilibria in weakly rotating self-gravitating fluid 
    Developed a theoretical study of equilibrium flow organization in a weakly rotating, self-gravitating fluid subject to a stationary gravitomagnetic field. The project reformulated the problem in terms of a generalized vorticity that combines ordinary flow vorticity with gravitomagnetic contributions, leading to a Beltrami-Bernoulli framework for organized steady states. A key result was the derivation of a double-curl equilibrium equation showing that rotation and self-gravity can support both large- and small-scale flow structures, analogous in mathematical form to double-Beltrami states in plasma, while retaining distinct physical behavior because gravity remains attractive. The work also clarified the role of helicity and energy in classifying equilibrium states and interpreting the associated Lagrange multiplier. The main achievement was a peer-reviewed publication in Journal of Plasma Physics (2022). This project is relevant to the Genesis Mission because it highlights my experience in constructing reduced, physically interpretable descriptions of nonlinear systems and in identifying structured state representations that may be useful in theory-guided surrogate modeling and interpretable AI workflows for complex physical systems.

    Project 3: Hybrid electrostatic waves in linearized gravity

    Developed an analytical study of electrostatic wave propagation in plasma subject to weak gravitational and gravitomagnetic fields. The work derived modified upper-hybrid and lower-hybrid dispersion relations in a coupled electromagnetic-gravitational setting and identified clear physical signatures of field interaction, including cancellation effects and frequency shifts. The project emphasized reduced theoretical modeling, systematic derivation, and interpretation of how added physical structure changes wave behavior in a complex system.  This project is relevant to the Genesis Mission, particularly AI for Scientific Reasoning, because it reflects experience in theory-guided analysis, and structured reasoning of complex systems.
