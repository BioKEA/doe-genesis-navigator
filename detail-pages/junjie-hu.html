    Introduction

    I'm an assistant professor of Computer Science at UW-Madison. My research centers on building robust, adaptive, and human-aligned AI agents for real-world deployment. I focus on the algorithmic foundations and understanding of NLP and machine learning models, especially large language models (LLMs), with an emphasis on safety, efficiency, and generalization. Specifically, I have extensive experiences on machine translation, code generation, multimodal machine learning, foundation models, and neuro-symbolic reasoning. 

    My research is relevant to the following topics in DoE's ASCR office:

    - 18-A, B, C: HPC Code Curation, Translation, and Development

    - 19-A, B, C: AI for Science Reasoning 

    - 20-A, B, C: Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows

    Relevant Projects

    1. Related to Formal Verification, Code Generation, Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning

    We develop a multi-agent chain-of-thought reasoning framework for formal theorem proving. The idea is to use the expressive power and language bias in LLMs to improve the long CoT reasoning in formal languages (e.g., LEAN in theorem proving). The techniques can be used to translate natural language to codes.

    Project Idea Summary

    My project focuses on building LLM-based systems for scientific reasoning in domains that require integrating and verifying multimodal evidence (e.g., text, tables, figures, and experimental data). The specific challenge I aim to address is how to ensure that foundation models produce scientifically valid and verifiable outputs, especially in settings where reasoning involves multiple steps, heterogeneous data sources, and strict consistency with established scientific principles.

    Concretely, I am interested in developing methods for:

    -Multimodal retrieval and reasoning, where models can iteratively gather and synthesize evidence from diverse scientific artifacts (papers, charts, datasets).

    -Systematic verification and alignment, including mechanisms to check intermediate reasoning steps against known constraints (e.g., physical laws, statistical consistency, or domain knowledge).

    -Efficient agentic workflows, where models can plan, query, and validate their reasoning while maintaining computational and token efficiency.

    -Problem to code generation, where models can follow natural language to write code. 

    For a potential partner, I am seeking:

    -Domain expertise and/or access to high-quality scientific datasets (e.g., biomedical, chemistry, materials science, or climate science), especially those involving multimodal data.

    -Collaboration on evaluation and validation, including defining meaningful benchmarks for scientific correctness and reliability.
