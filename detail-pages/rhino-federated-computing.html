Introduction

Federated computing architectures enable organizations or teams of organizations to analyze large, multi-source data (‘federated analytics’) or to train machine learning models (‘federated learning’) on such data without compromising the privacy of individual datasets therein, or, if needed, of the model or calculation itself. Rhino Federated Computing provides a fully managed federated computing platform, eliminating the need for enterprise or research customers to build this infrastructure from scratch. Our platform and full-service model bridge the gap between other available options: complex, expertise-demanding open-source frameworks for the do-it-yourself approach; on the other extreme, rigid, one-size-fits-all solutions which lack flexibility for unique applications. 
In every technical challenge area of the Genesis Mission, AI training and deployment meet challenges of data sovereignty and security. For example, development and performance monitoring of models for robotic process automation, sampling and real-time characterization are needed in mining, manufacturing and energy-generation processes -- but the data associated with these processes are highly sensitive. Federation allows for real, commercially valuable data (such as those held by industry partners) or national-security-critical data (such as those held by national labs) to be safely incorporated into model training and then to be accessed by trained or recurrent models for real-time intervention.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

We provide tools which can support the development and deployment of AI models. In addition to the Rhino federated computing platform (FCP), our main infrastructure offering, these include functionalities for data harmonization, quality control, analytics and cataloging. The Rhino FCP includes native integration of TensorBoard, providing views of model training progress over the federated network. 

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

We provide cloud-agnostic infrastructure for privacy-preserving federated computing. Users include partnerships, consortia, or large multi-part entities with internal privacy constraints. These users or user groups come with their code (models, statistical analyses, etc.) and data, and our platform enables code to be executed on data while keeping the data on its original source (no data exchange, "sharing" or exposure to the internet) and behind its original firewalls. Code objects themselves can also be kept private from data holders or other code contributors.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

We do not currently develop AI models in-house. If any party has an AI model or other code/software that could be deployed in a federated manner (e.g., federated learning to develop or fine-tune the AI model), our infrastructure can enable this without any further work on the part of the model developers. 

Datasets or data infrastructure

We do not own any data and only provide infrastructure for federated computing to be done on data held by others. If other parties (e.g., companies or national labs) have data to contribute to a project effort which is either sensitive in nature or too large to "share", we can enable compute to be sent to that data over a secure federated network. 

Subject matter expertise

We are open to working with subject matter experts (i.e., scientists and engineers) on any of the challenge areas.
