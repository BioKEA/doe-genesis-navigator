Introduction

Dr. Wang is a professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Clemson University, South Carolina. His research focuses on advanced radio-frequency (RF) sensing technologies integrated with artificial intelligence for rapid, label-free characterization of complex biological and chemical systems.

His group develops AI-driven dielectric spectroscopic flow cytometry (DiSC-Omics) platforms for sensing, classification, and separation of single cells and microorganisms. These systems enable high-throughput measurements of mammalian and microbial cells across a broad frequency range from DC to 50 GHz, with ongoing efforts to extend toward the terahertz regime. The resulting multimodal RF datasets support AI-based electromagnetic signature learning for real-time identification and decision-making. The platforms also enable selective detection of biochemical liquids, supporting near–real-time, sample-to-answer workflows for biomedical diagnostics and environmental monitoring.

In addition, Dr. Wang has developed tunable electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR) spectrometers for sensitive radical detection, providing complementary data for chemical and plasma systems. His earlier work includes Raman free-electron lasers and relativistic traveling-wave amplifiers based on plasma physics and pulsed power systems.

His work aligns with DOE Genesis priorities by enabling integrated sensing, data generation, and AI-driven discovery for complex, multiscale systems.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

We have develped machine learning algorithms and models to analyze and classify microorganisms based on cell images and RF spectra.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We have produced approximately 7 TB data (cell image and RF spectra) of microorganisms and Chinese hamster ovary cells. 

Experimental or test facilities

We have built multiple RF systems (from micro- nano-fabricated broadband sensing devices, high sensitivity sensing circuits, to single cell measurement platforms) to measure single cells and liquids at high throughput. 

Subject matter expertise

1. Micro- nano-fabrication: We have been long-term, active users at the Cornell NanoScale Facility and the Center for Nanoscale Material, Argonne National Lab.

2. RF sensing and characterization of materials (molecules, cells, and radicals): We have developed and demonstrated various dielectric spectroscopic flow cytometry systems and ESR/EPR systems.

3.Machine learning for cell type/state classification: We developed and demonstrated ML for accurate classification of single cells

4. Pulsed power systems, relativistic electron beams and related diagnostic techniques.

Other expertise

RF/Microwave science and technologies, including CMOS RFIC design and testing.

Fluidic memristors and testing
