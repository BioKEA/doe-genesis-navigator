Introduction

OpenStar is focused on developing a practical nuclear fusion reactor to generate clean, abundant, carbon-free electricity by fusing hydrogen isotopes at extremely high temperatures. Rather than pursuing the conventional and capital-intensive tokamak or stellarator approaches, OpenStar technology is based on the levitated dipole concept. This design uses a single powerful high-temperature superconducting magnet that levitates within a vacuum chamber, naturally confining hot plasma. Inspired by planetary magnetic fields, the approach is grounded in a solid physics basis and offers fundamental simplicity and robustness.

OpenStar core technological focus is the development of a large, isolated, radiation-resistant high-temperature superconducting magnet designed for extremely hostile environments, alongside established plasma control, exhaust, fueling and heating systems common to other magnetic fusion concepts. Because of its simplicity and strong physics foundations, the levitated dipole provides a rapid and cost-effective pathway toward a volumetric neutron source, and ultimately commercial electricity production.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
Experimental or test facilities

Junior is a levitated dipole fusion experiment developed by OpenStar Technologies designed to test commercial fusion energy using a 5.6 T high-temperature superconducting magnet. It achieves plasma confinement within a 5.2 m vacuum chamber, with initial plasma successes reported in late 2024 and full levitation experiments planned for 2025.

Project Idea Summary

We are seeking strategic partners to collaborate on the development of an AI-driven, integrated modeling platform for the levitated dipole concept, with the goal of accelerating its path to a commercially viable fusion power plant. This platform will unify multi-physics simulations—spanning plasma behavior, magnetic confinement, materials performance, and system-level engineering—within a cohesive, data-driven framework. By leveraging advanced AI techniques for model coupling, optimization, and real-time predictive control, we aim to significantly enhance design iteration speed, reduce uncertainty, and unlock new performance regimes unique to dipole configurations. We are particularly interested in partners with expertise in high-performance computing, machine learning, plasma physics, and digital twin technologies who share our ambition to transform the levitated dipole into a scalable, reliable source of clean energy.
