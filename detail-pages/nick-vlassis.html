Introduction

Nick N. Vlassis received his bachelor’s degree in civil engineering and a master’s degree in structural design and analysis of structures from the National Technical University of Athens, Greece, in 2017. He received his PhD degree from Columbia University in 2021, conducting research on machine learning and data-driven methods for solid mechanics. After graduation, he worked as a postdoctoral research scientist at Columbia University. 

He has been an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering at Rutgers University since the fall of 2023. His group, Mech[Ai] integrates computational solid mechanics with interpretable machine learning, graph learning, and generative AI, with particular emphasis on multiscale modeling.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

We are developing AI models, software tools, and computational simulation capabilities for digital twins of physical systems. We are also developing AI agents and knowledge bases to support these systems.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We have expertise in AI and data science, including the development of generative AI methods, graph learning approaches, agentic AI systems, and foundation vision models for engineering applications.

Subject matter expertise

Our group focuses on computational solid mechanics, with an emphasis on modeling and inverse design of multiscale and multifunctional material systems.

Project Idea Summary

We are developing an end-to-end framework for the digitalization of industrial processes that integrates foundation vision models, agentic AI, physics-based simulation, and human-in-the-loop interaction. The methodology is designed to be process-agnostic and adaptable across application domains, enabling the construction of digital twins, automated process understanding and control, and reusable knowledge layers from raw observations through system-level decision-making.
