    Introduction

    I am Forough Ghahramani, Vice President for Research and Innovation at New Jersey Edge (Edge), a  Research and Education Network (REN) for New Jersey. I lead initiatives that advance research capacity, innovation ecosystems, and workforce development across institutions in New Jersey and beyond, with a focus on enabling equitable access to AI, quantum, and data-intensive technologies. NJEdge (Edge) 

    I play a central role in enabling collaborations across academia, national laboratories, industry, and government by convening stakeholders, aligning priorities,and building scalable frameworks that connect institutions to resources and expertise.

    My work focuses on shared and federated research infrastructure, workforce development in AI and advanced computing, and ecosystem-building to support innovation. As Principal Investigator on NSF-funded initiatives including the CC* award # 2346648 and an NSF EAGER NAIRR award #2505400 I lead efforts to deploy regional cyberinfrastructure and develop AI workforce pathways through programs such as Campus Champions, while contributing to national research computing communities.

    Edge provides advanced technology services, infrastructure, and community coordination to support research, education, healthcare, and public service. At its core, Edge operates an extensive optical fiber network that serves as the backbone for connectivity across the state. This infrastructure connects 50+ member institutions and extends to national labs and networks.

    Relevant Projects

    1. NSF CC CRISPIE (Award #2346648) – Regional Cyberinfrastructure and Connectivity*
    Role: Principal Investigator
    Scope: Led a multi-institutional initiative to design and deploy regional research cyberinfrastructure across a diverse set of New Jersey institutions, including minority-serving and under-resourced organizations. The project integrates advanced networking (Science DMZ), Data Transfer Nodes (DTNs), performance monitoring, and identity-enabled access to support high-throughput, data-intensive science. A key component includes workforce development and community engagement to ensure adoption and sustainability.
    Achievements: Established a scalable regional model for shared infrastructure and collaboration; expanded institutional participation in national research ecosystems; improved data transfer capabilities and access to advanced computing resources; and developed training programs that increased researcher readiness to utilize HPC, AI, and data platforms. The project serves as a replicable model for enabling distributed scientific collaboration.

     

    2. NSF EAGER: NAIRR Expansion – AI Workforce Development and Access
    Role: Principal Investigator (Award #2505400)
    Scope: Led a national effort focused on expanding access to AI resources through facilitation and workforce development. The project leverages the Campus Champions model to connect researchers—particularly from under-resourced institutions, to advanced computing, data, and AI infrastructure, including resources aligned with the National AI Research Resource (NAIRR). Activities include training, mentorship, conference engagement, and development of reusable educational materials.
    Achievements: Strengthened the role of research computing facilitators as critical connectors to national AI resources; expanded participation in AI-enabled research; developed scalable training and engagement models; and contributed to a growing national ecosystem supporting AI workforce development. The project advances inclusive pathways for researchers to engage in AI for scientific discovery.

     

    3. NSF CCRI Planning-C: AI-Enabled Cybersecurity Infrastructure (Award #2213794)
    Role: Co-Principal Investigator
    Scope: Contributed to the planning and development of a community research infrastructure for AI-enabled cybersecurity, focusing on integrating large-scale data, advanced analytics, and collaborative research environments. The project included convening national stakeholders, conducting workshops, and developing a strategic framework for infrastructure that supports AI-driven cybersecurity research.
    Achievements: Produced a nationally recognized workshop report outlining infrastructure and research priorities for AI-enabled cybersecurity; advanced models for collaborative, multi-institutional research environments; and informed future investments in secure, AI-driven data and computing platforms. This work aligns with national priorities in secure computing and data environments.

    Project Idea Summary

    This project focuses on addressing the challenge of enabling scalable, federated research ecosystems that integrate AI and Quantum, advanced tools, compute, testbeds, and data resources to support mission-driven scientific discovery. Access to these capabilities remains fragmented, limiting collaboration, slowing innovation, and creating barriers for all types of institutions to fully participate in national research efforts.

    To address this challenge, I seek partners that can provide complementary capabilities across tools and resources, compute infrastructure, testbeds and facilities, and data and data infrastructure. The goal is to co-design and implement a federated, interoperable model that enables seamless access to distributed resources and supports collaborative, multi-institutional research aligned with DOE priorities.

    The partnership model will combine partner-provided infrastructure and platforms with my expertise in education, workforce development, and ecosystem coordination. I will lead efforts to engage and train researchers, develop facilitation models to connect users to resources, and help design shared governance and operational frameworks that ensure scalability and sustainability.

    This approach will enable broader participation in advanced computing and data-driven science, strengthen workforce readiness in AI and emerging technologies, and create a replicable model for integrating infrastructure, people, and partnerships to accelerate scientific discovery.
