Introduction

Internet2 is a nonprofit organization serving the U.S. research and education community. We own and operate the nation’s largest and fastest research and education network and access management infrastructure, connecting hundreds of universities, national laboratories, and government agencies. Our network and trust federation is purpose-built for science: engineered to move massive datasets and support the complex, multi-facility workflows that cutting-edge research demands. Internet2 recently designed and built a hyperscale AI research network — purpose-built infrastructure that spans 3,400 km and exceeds 138 Tbps of dedicated capacity, among the highest ever built for AI research. Internet2 also operates InCommon, the nation's largest federated identity and access management service, trusted by over 1,000 US-based organizations to manage secure, privacy-preserving access to research resources for multi-facility workflows. Our Insight Console allows provisioning of Layer 2 and 3 circuits across the Internet2 footprint, including to commercial cloud providers, and provides visibility into network performance, traffic, and anomalies. We have also demonstrated post-quantum cryptography running live on the Internet2 national backbone. Internet2 is seeking partners — research teams, national laboratories, and technology organizations — who need high-performance, quantum-ready, AI-capable network infrastructure and operational expertise to advance their Genesis Mission proposals.

Other expertise

Internet2 brings deep operational expertise in high-performance, long-distance research networking — including traffic engineering, network telemetry, anomaly detection, and AI-driven network operations (AIOps) for complex, multi-facility scientific workflows. We also offer specialized knowledge in federated identity and access management for research through InCommon, enabling secure, privacy-preserving, science-aware access control across institutional boundaries — a critical capability for protecting sensitive research data in distributed DOE environments and enabling access to it. 

Other resources/tools

Internet2 offers access to three flagship infrastructure assets directly relevant to Genesis Mission teams: our hyperscale AI research network, engineered for distributed AI training at 138+ Tbps; our national R&E backbone, connecting hundreds of universities, laboratories, and agencies with purpose-built, high-capacity research connectivity; and InCommon, our federated single sign-on platform serving 1,000+ organizations with secure, standards-based identity and access management. 

Relevant Projects

1. Hyperscale AI Research Network (2025–present)
Internet2 designed and built a dedicated hyperscale network for AI research in partnership with an industry member. The first phase established 57.6 Tbps of connectivity between two data centers 300 km apart — one of the highest-capacity dedicated connections built for AI research. The network now spans 3,400 km and exceeds 138 Tbps, interconnecting compute resources across five U.S. data center locations. This project demonstrates Internet2's ability to architect infrastructure for large-scale, distributed AI workloads.

2. Post-Quantum Cryptography on the National Backbone (2026)
Internet2, working with Ciena and its partner Purism, demonstrated post-quantum cryptography for data in transit on the Internet2 national backbone, using a FIPS 203-compliant solution for both quantum-safe encryption and quantum-safe key exchange. Testing was validated by cryptographers at a third-party organization in the R&E community. This establishes Internet2 as an operational testbed for quantum-safe networking at national scale.

3. Next Generation Infrastructure (NGI) — Fifth-Generation Backbone (2021–2022)
Internet2 completed the transition to its fifth-generation backbone, interconnecting 37 state and regional networks across every U.S. state. The network's optical layer supports up to 32 terabits per segment, with a new packet layer providing 8–16 petabits per second of port capacity per location — delivered with a 70% reduction in power consumption. NGI established the software-driven, fully automated infrastructure foundation underlying all current Internet2 services.

4. Insight Console — Network Observability and Automation Platform (2022–present)
Internet2 built the Insight Console to create software development and service operation capacity focused specifically on network-related automation and orchestration, as well as network telemetry collection, analysis, and reporting. The platform enables network engineers and cloud architects to visualize, manage, and troubleshoot Internet2 network services in one place and serves as the foundation for Internet2's ongoing AIOps development.

5. InCommon Federation — Federated Identity at National Scale (ongoing) 
InCommon connects over 1,000 universities, research institutions, and federal agency partners through a single, policy-governed identity federation. Researchers access multi-facility workflows and sensitive datasets using existing home institution credentials: Relying Parties leverage the security including MFA and identity proofing of a researcher’s institutional credentials and the policy and accountability structure of the Federation. InCommon’s trust framework is purpose-built for the cross-institutional, multi-stakeholder environment that large-scale science demands.

6. InCommon Federation — Identity Assurance Rollout with NIH (2025–present) 
InCommon and NIH are actively working together to roll out identity assurance capabilities across the U.S. research and education community to enable researchers to access NIH's Controlled-Access Data Repositories using campus credentials that NIH consider as comparable to FISMA-Moderate requirements (NIST SP 800-63-3 IAL2). This partnership is driving broader institutional adoption of federated MFA, identity proofing, and contributor attributes, establishing a replicable federal agency model that DOE and others can apply to their own controlled-access research environments.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

We are seeking partners who have developed or can develop AI/ML models for network traffic analysis, anomaly detection, and predictive performance management — particularly models that can ingest multi-modal telemetry streams and generate real-time, actionable insights for network operators. Experience with models that can be fine-tuned on domain-specific operational data, and that support explainable outputs suitable for operational decision-making in high-stakes research network environments, is especially valuable.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We seek partners with hands-on expertise in applying machine learning to time-series telemetry, network flow data, and operational event logs — including experience with anomaly detection, root-cause analysis, and predictive modeling at scale. Familiarity with the data characteristics of high-performance research networks, or with AIOps platforms in comparable large-scale infrastructure environments, would significantly strengthen a proposed collaboration.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Internet2’s Cloud Connect service offers dynamic creation of direct connections to four cloud service providers: Amazon Web Services (Direct Connect), Google Cloud (Google Cloud Partner Interconnect), Microsoft Azure (Expressroute) and Oracle Cloud (Fastconnect). Using Insight Console or its API, virtual switch (layer 2) or cloud router (layer 3) connections can be created or deleted within seconds. Internet2 also supports private dedicated connections to any cloud service provider with a presence at any of Internet2’s points of presence through the Rapid Private Interconnect service.

Experimental or test facilities

We seek partners who can provide access to active, instrumented multi-facility science workflows that span both the Internet2 backbone and ESnet — serving as live testbeds for developing, training, and validating AIOps models under real operational conditions. Access to network operations centers, monitoring infrastructure, or sandbox environments at DOE National Laboratories or major research universities would be particularly valuable for iterative model development and demonstration.

Subject matter expertise

We are seeking domain scientists and research computing professionals who operate or depend on multi-facility DOE workflows — for example, teams conducting experiments at light sources, accelerators, or large-scale climate and simulation platforms that involve university researchers accessing national laboratory facilities. Their workflow requirements, performance expectations, and pain points will ground the AIOps solution in real operational needs and ensure the resulting capability is relevant across DOE's Genesis Mission portfolio.

Project Idea Summary

AI-Driven Network Operations for Multi-Facility Workflows
Internet2 seeks research partners — either university research groups or DOE Laboratories — to develop and demonstrate an AIOps capability tailored to the performance demands of high-priority DOE science workflows.

The core idea: leverage Internet2's national backbone telemetry and operational infrastructure as a real-world testbed for AI models that predict network bottlenecks, detect and diagnose anomalous behavior, and proactively optimize connectivity for multi-facility science workflows — exactly the capabilities described in Challenge Area 11, Focus Area B. Many of DOE's highest-priority workflows involve university researchers accessing national laboratory facilities such as light sources, particle accelerators, and climate modeling platforms — traversing both the Internet2 backbone and ESnet. These cross-network, multi-institutional workflows are ideal candidates for AI-driven performance optimization.

Internet2 would contribute network telemetry data, operational expertise, testbed access, and integration with InCommon's identity fabric for science-aware, privacy-preserving data tagging. A lead research partner would drive AI/ML model development and validation. DOE Laboratory partners would provide domain science context and real workflow requirements.

The goal is a deployable AIOps capability that generalizes across the DOE and university research network environment.
