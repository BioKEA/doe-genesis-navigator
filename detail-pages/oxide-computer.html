Introduction

Oxide Computer Company designs and manufactures rack-scale cloud computers in the United States for organizations that require the performance and economics of owned infrastructure with the operational simplicity of the public cloud. Our systems integrate compute, storage, networking, and a hardware root of trust into a single, API-driven platform that operates with zero external dependencies, including in fully air-gapped environments.

Oxide's architecture is purpose-built for sustained, high-utilization workloads including AI model training, large-scale simulation, and autonomous lab orchestration. Every rack ships with a secure boot chain rooted in hardware, no third-party hypervisor licensing, and a unified control plane that runs entirely on-premises. This makes Oxide infrastructure well-suited for environments handling CUI, ITAR-controlled data, or other sensitive research outputs.

We are interested in contributing to the Genesis Mission through the High-Performance Computing & Cloud Infrastructure working group. Oxide can provide sovereign, scalable compute infrastructure to support AI-driven discovery workflows at National Laboratories and partner institutions, enabling federated research environments that meet the security and data governance requirements of mission-critical programs. Our systems are manufactured domestically with a documented, auditable supply chain and are currently deployed across defense, aerospace, and scientific research customers.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Oxide manufactures rack-scale cloud computers in the U.S. Each system boots from a hardware root of trust with measured firmware verification at every stage. The entire software stack is open source and publicly auditable, delivering attestable integrity from power-on through workload execution. AMD EPYC compute with high-density NVMe, integrated networking, and a fully on-rack control plane enables air-gapped operation with zero external dependencies. API-driven cloud operations on sovereign, customer-owned infrastructure purpose-built for sustained AI training, simulation & R&D

Relevant Projects

Project 1: Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (LLNL) -- Cloud and HPC Convergence (2024-present)

Deployed Oxide Cloud Computer at the Livermore Computing HPC Center to bring on-premises cloud capabilities alongside NNSA high-performance computing workloads. Scope included secure multi-tenant VM provisioning via self-service APIs, integration with LLNL's Flux resource manager, and enabling cloud-like IaaS for persistent services (databases, Jupyter notebooks, Kubernetes clusters) running alongside HPC batch jobs. Researchers from Los Alamos National Laboratory and Sandia National Laboratories also participate in activities on the Oxide rack. LLNL plans additional Oxide deployments focused on scale-out and disaster recovery.

Project 2: Idaho National Laboratory (INL) -- Sovereign Cloud Infrastructure (2023-present)

INL was Oxide's first DOE customer, deploying the Oxide Cloud Computer to support nuclear energy research and advanced modeling and simulation workloads. The deployment provides INL researchers with API-driven, self-service compute provisioning on sovereign, U.S.-manufactured infrastructure with hardware root of trust and fully open source software stack. The system operates with zero external dependencies, supporting INL's requirements for secure, on-premises compute that complements its existing HPC resources.

Project 3: Defense and Aerospace Deployments -- Sovereign Compute for National Security Missions (2024-present)

Multiple Oxide rack deployments across defense technology and aerospace organizations supporting classified and sensitive workloads including AI/ML training, autonomous systems data processing, and ground segment infrastructure. All deployments leverage Oxide's hardware root of trust, attestable open source software, and air-gap-capable architecture. Systems are U.S. manufactured (CAGE 9BZ23) and configured for NIST 800-171 and CUI compliance. These deployments demonstrate Oxide's ability to deliver cloud-operational compute in environments where data sovereignty, supply chain integrity, and zero external dependencies are non-negotiable.

Project Idea Summary

Oxide proposes contributing sovereign compute infrastructure to the Genesis Mission's High-Performance Computing & Cloud Infrastructure working group, with a focus on extending the American Science Cloud to sensitive research environments that cannot operate on shared public cloud platforms.

Oxide Cloud is deployed at DOE/NNSA national laboratories including Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory and Idaho National Laboratory, where they provide self-service cloud capabilities alongside HPC workloads. Researchers from Los Alamos and Sandia National Laboratories also participate in activities on the LLNL deployment. These systems are U.S. manufactured, built on a hardware root of trust with attestable open source software, and operate with zero external dependencies.

We are seeking partners across three areas: (1) AI model developers building foundation models for Genesis Mission challenge areas - materials science, nuclear energy, advanced manufacturing, semiconductors -- who need sovereign, high-integrity compute for training and inference; (2) National laboratories and universities generating large-scale scientific datasets who require secure data infrastructure with no third-party access to the control plane; (3) Robotics and automation teams building autonomous experimental platforms who need co-located, low-latency compute for closed-loop AI-driven workflows. We welcome partnerships that pair our  capabilities with domain-specific AI expertise to accelerate discovery.
