Introduction

Morton Labs is building the integration and orchestration layer that enables AI to operate on real fusion energy workflows. Today, the most advanced simulation tools remain fragmented, difficult to couple, and not designed for AI-native execution. This limits the ability to move from concept to validated engineering decisions at the speed required for commercial fusion. Morton Labs transforms research-grade codes into secure, standardized, and API-accessible systems that can be orchestrated by AI across the full engineering lifecycle. Our platform enables closed-loop workflows that connect modeling, simulation, analysis, and decision-making, allowing teams to iterate faster, reduce uncertainty, and move toward integrated, model-based system engineering for fusion devices.

Morton is enabling secure, export-control-aware collaboration across the fusion ecosystem. Rather than centralizing sensitive code or data, Morton Labs supports site-local execution with governed interfaces, allowing partners to retain full control over their intellectual property, restricted datasets, and regulated workflows while still participating in broader AI-driven systems. This enables the integration of global MODSIM capabilities into a unified but federated environment aligned with DOE and NNSA requirements. We are seeking collaborators who can contribute simulation workflows, experimental data, HPC resources, and domain expertise to bring fusion energy to the grid faster, safer and more reliably.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Morton Labs develops software systems that transform research-grade fusion simulation codes into AI-operable, production-ready workflows. Our platform provides standardized APIs, workflow orchestration, and execution layers that enable AI models and agents to interact directly with complex multi-physics simulations. Rather than replacing existing codes, we wrap and integrate them into modular, composable systems that support automation, closed-loop optimization, and reproducible analysis. This lets AI plan, execute, and refine simulation-driven engineering tasks across the fusion lifecycle.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Morton Labs brings applied expertise in deploying AI/ML techniques within physics-based engineering environments, with a focus on integrating data-driven methods with first-principles simulation. We support the development and deployment of physics-informed surrogate models, optimization frameworks, and AI-driven analysis tools that operate within governed workflows. Our approach emphasizes human-in-the-loop decision support, uncertainty quantification, and model validation, ensuring that AI outputs are interpretable, auditable, and aligned with scientific and engineering constraints. 

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

We provide infrastructure orchestration across heterogeneous computing environments, including cloud platforms, on-premise HPC systems, and secure or air-gapped environments. Our platform can operate seamlessly across these environments, enabling distributed execution of simulation workflows while maintaining consistent interfaces and governance controls. We do not replace existing HPC infrastructure but instead enable unified access and coordination across compute resources, allowing users and AI systems to execute workflows without needing to manage underlying infrastructure complexities.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Our platform standardizes data formats, captures full provenance and metadata, and supports version-controlled workflows that ensure traceability from inputs to outputs. We enable the creation of curated benchmark datasets and derived feature sets that can be used for training and validating AI models, while maintaining strict governance over sensitive or export-controlled data. This infrastructure is essential for enabling reliable AI-driven workflows and for supporting validation, verification, and regulatory requirements in fusion energy systems.

Subject matter expertise

Morton Labs operates at the intersection of computational physics, fusion energy systems, and modern software engineering. Our team and collaborators bring deep experience in multi-physics simulation workflows, including neutronics, plasma physics, CFD, materials, and system-level engineering. We focus on the practical challenges of coupling disparate codes, managing complex data flows, and translating scientific models into engineering decisions. This domain expertise allows us to define meaningful benchmark problems, design integrated workflows, and meet the needs of fusion researchers.

Other resources/tools

Morton Labs provides additional capabilities in workflow orchestration, secure execution environments, and policy-aware governance systems. Our platform enables site-local execution with controlled interfaces, allowing institutions to retain full ownership of their code, data, and intellectual property while participating in broader collaborative workflows. We embed access controls, auditability, and compliance mechanisms directly into the workflow layer, supporting export control and data governance requirements- enabling federated collaboration across export controlled codebases. 

Relevant Publications

Energy-Aware Kubernetes: Monitoring and Analyzing Fusion Simulation Power Consumption with Kepler

Energy usage is important in infrastructure for Fusion companies who want to measure and optimize cost and sustainability when running simulation models. The Kepler (Kubernetes-based Efficient Power Level Exporter) project provides a mechanism to measure power consumption metrics for Kubernetes workloads by leveraging hardware counters, kernel statistics, and machine learning–based models. In this presentation, we demonstrated how to deploy and configure Kepler in a Kubernetes cluster, integrate it with observability stacks such as Prometheus and Grafana, and interpret the resulting energy metrics at the node, pod, and container levels.

Relevant Projects

1. Fusion Virtual Test Bed (FVTB) Development with ORNL

Morton Labs partnered with Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL), including collaboration with Cami Collins and the FREDA SciDAC framework, to develop early components of a Fusion Virtual Test Bed (FVTB) for integrated multi-physics fusion workflows. The scope focused on transforming research-grade simulation tools into structured, reproducible workflows that could serve as reference benchmarks for industry and future regulatory use. Morton Labs contributed to workflow architecture, code integration strategy, and packaging of complex simulation chains—such as plasma-material interactions, thermal loads, and engineering analysis—into documented, reusable pipelines. Key achievements include defining standardized interfaces between coupled codes, establishing reproducibility and documentation practices, and contributing to a monoblock plasma-facing component reference workflow. This work demonstrated how fragmented simulation tools can be elevated into validated, shareable engineering systems, directly aligning with Genesis priorities around AI-enabled scientific infrastructure, closed-loop workflows, and verification, validation, and uncertainty quantification (VVUQ).

2. Hydrocode Hardening and Commercialization Strategy with First Light Fusion

Morton Labs worked with First Light Fusion to support the hardening, packaging, and commercialization strategy of their Prometheus hydrocode, a simulation tool for inertial fusion target and driver modeling. The scope included identifying the technical and operational requirements needed to transition the code from a research-grade internal tool into a scalable, externally usable software product for U.S. customers. Morton Labs contributed to defining API-enabled access, standardized input/output interfaces, automated validation and regression testing, and improved documentation and onboarding workflows. The work also addressed secure deployment models to ensure export control compliance and intellectual property protection while enabling broader collaboration. Key achievements include delivering a clear roadmap for productionizing the code, improving reproducibility and usability, and identifying pathways for integration into multi-physics workflows. This effort supports Genesis objectives by enabling legacy simulation tools to become AI-compatible, production-ready systems that can be integrated into larger, automated scientific and engineering environments.

3. Materials Damage and Multi-Physics Workflow Development with Inertia

Morton Labs collaborated with Inertia to design and prototype integrated multi-physics workflows focused on materials damage and performance under extreme fusion-relevant conditions. The scope included coupling simulation tools across domains such as radiation damage, thermal loading, and structural response to create a more comprehensive understanding of material behavior in fusion systems. Morton Labs led workflow architecture design, integration strategy, and the development of structured pipelines that reduce the need for manual “glue code” and enable more efficient analysis. This included outlining approaches for parameter sweeps, sensitivity analysis, and future incorporation of surrogate modeling and optimization. Key achievements include establishing a reproducible and extensible workflow framework that improves engineering efficiency and enables systematic exploration of material performance.

Project Idea Summary

Morton Labs is focused on enabling AI-native fusion engineering workflows, specifically within fusion energy system design, simulation, and analysis. Our project addresses the challenge of transforming fragmented, research-grade simulation codes into integrated, AI-operable workflows that support closed-loop design, optimization, and decision-making. Today, fusion modeling efforts remain siloed across institutions and tools, limiting the ability to fully leverage AI for system-level engineering. We aim to develop secure, standardized workflow infrastructure that allows AI systems to orchestrate simulation, analyze results, and iterate on designs across heterogeneous environments, including HPC systems and experimental facilities.

A key focus of this effort is enabling secure, export-control-aware collaboration across national laboratories, universities, and industry. We are seeking partners who can contribute advanced simulation codes, experimental datasets, and domain expertise in fusion physics and engineering, as well as collaborators with experience in AI/ML, HPC infrastructure, and secure computing environments. Industry partners who can provide real-world system requirements and validation use cases are especially valuable. Together, these collaborations will enable the development of validated, reusable workflows that serve as foundational infrastructure for the Genesis platform and accelerate the transition from scientific discovery to commercialized fusion energy.
