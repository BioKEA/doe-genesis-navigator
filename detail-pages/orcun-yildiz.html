Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

My AI workflow experience spans continual learning workflows for coherent diffraction imaging and neural architecture search workflows. We have built ChatVis, an LLM-based agent and benchmark for automatically generating scientific visualizations from natural language prompts. I currently develop LLM-driven approaches for automating key workflow aspects, including task code generation, workflow configuration, and execution management (RFA Topic 18.B, Automated Scientific Problem-to-Code Generation).

AI/ML/Data science expertise

I am also actively working on AI for scientific reasoning, collaborating with materials science experts from CNM on hypothesis generation through automated LAMMPS simulation code generation from natural language problem descriptions (RFA Topic 19.B, Hypothesis Generation from Multi-Modal Data).

Relevant Projects

ChatVis is both an agent for automatically generating scientific visualizations from natural language prompts and a benchmark for evaluating synthetically-generated visualizations, whether generated by ChatVis or other tools.

 

Continual learning workflow in coherent diffraction imaging: Deep neural networks (DNNs) have been used to enhance the speed and accuracy of defect identification in materials science; however, DNNs rely on extensive training datasets with well-balanced distribution of sample types, requiring significant computational and manual effort. The full pipeline for building defect identification models involves the integration of various tasks, including crystal data generation, atomistic and diffraction simulations, and validation of AI models. To this end, in collaboration with scientists from MCS, CNM and APS, I developed an automated workflow for defect identification that dynamically generates training data on demand as a neural network is being trained. To minimize the amount of data needed to train the neural network, we employed a continual learning approach, which can incrementally train the model when the data are observed sequentially rather than in bulk. We also developed a smart data generation mechanism that generates data with highest potential benefit based on the uncertainty of the deep learning models.
