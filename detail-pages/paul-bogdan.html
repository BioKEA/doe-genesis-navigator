    Introduction

    Paul Bogdan’s research in Artificial Intelligence centers on developing fundamentally new mathematical and computational frameworks for learning, modeling, and controlling complex systems from incomplete, heterogeneous, and multiscale data. His work advances a data-first, geometry-aware AI paradigm that departs from traditional model-driven approaches by extracting intrinsic structure—such as multifractality, topology, and long-range dependencies—directly from data to construct compact, interpretable, and highly generalizable models. These innovations enable AI systems to capture non-Gaussian, non-Markovian dynamics and operate robustly under uncertainty and “unknown unknowns.”

    A central contribution is the development of operator-learning and geometry-based AI methods that unify learning, prediction, and control across domains. His frameworks, including CyAMMs and neural operator learning, allow AI to infer governing laws of complex systems, forecast their evolution, and design efficient control strategies with minimal data and intervention. This approach significantly improves out-of-distribution generalization, uncertainty quantification, and decision-making reliability.

    Bogdan’s research also pioneers biologically inspired AI, including NeuroAI and nature-inspired general intelligence, where principles of brain networks, memory, and self-organization inform new distributed and energy-efficient learning architectures.     Project Idea Summary

    Paul Bogdan’s research projects focus on advancing artificial intelligence through mathematically grounded, geometry-aware frameworks that learn from incomplete, heterogeneous, and multiscale data. A central theme is the development of Cyber-Physical Artificial Intelligence Models (CyAMMs), which infer hidden structures and non-Markovian dynamics in complex systems to enable accurate prediction, efficient control, and robust decision-making under uncertainty.

    His projects span several interconnected domains. In biomedical AI, he leads efforts on Biological Geometry-Aware AI (BGAI), creating models that analyze the spatiotemporal dynamics of physiological signals to detect diseases early, model brain activity, and accelerate drug discovery. These systems leverage multifractal analysis and network-based representations to capture biological complexity and variability.

    In parallel, his work on neural operator learning develops AI systems capable of discovering governing equations of complex processes and forecasting their evolution across scales. This enables applications in autonomous systems, robotics, and cyber-physical systems, where real-time learning and control are critical.

    Another major research direction involves trustworthy and distributed AI, including NeuroAI-inspired architectures and leader-follower neural networks that mimic biological intelligence for energy-efficient, decentralized learning.
