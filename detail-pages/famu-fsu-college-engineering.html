Introduction

We are a research team in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the FAMU-FSU College of Engineering, working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, microelectronics, and quantum-related hardware/system design. Our current Genesis Mission interest is in AI-enabled approaches for stable and scalable circuit-based emulation of quantum qubits, with future directions including scaling, error-correction readiness, and portable implementations. We are seeking collaborators whose expertise can strengthen the technical foundations, evaluation, and transition potential of this effort.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

We can contribute AI-enabled modeling, surrogate prediction, closed-loop optimization, and performance evaluation tools for quantum-related hardware/system workflows. Our current interest includes software and algorithmic tools for circuit-based emulation of quantum qubits, adaptive control/calibration, design-space exploration, and tradeoff analysis across stability, scalability, and implementation constraints.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our team has expertise in AI/ML, data-driven modeling, real-time optimization, and quantitative system evaluation. We are particularly interested in applying AI to modeling, control, calibration, and scalable performance analysis for emerging hardware platforms, including quantum-related systems and circuit-based qubit emulation. We can contribute methods for surrogate modeling, closed-loop learning, and multi-objective tradeoff analysis.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We can contribute data generation, curation, and analysis workflows for AI-driven hardware/system studies, including simulation-generated and experimentally informed datasets for model development and evaluation. Our current interest includes building structured datasets for circuit-based qubit emulation, operating-condition variation, control/calibration behavior, and scaling/error-correction-related performance analysis.

Project Idea Summary

Our project idea is centered on AI-enabled circuit-based emulation of quantum qubits as a pathway toward more stable, scalable, and implementation-friendly quantum-system development. The core motivation is to address key limitations of physical quantum qubits, including instability and stringent environmental constraints, by exploring whether classical/traditional circuit approaches can emulate important qubit behaviors while supporting future extensions such as scaling, error-correction readiness, and portable deployment.

Our primary focus is Topic 8, especially the quantum computing/networking direction, with Topic 9 serving as a supporting theme for hardware/system realization and scalable implementation. From the AI side, we are particularly interested in data-driven modeling, closed-loop control/calibration, and quantitative tradeoff analysis for evaluating stability, scalability, and design choices in circuit-based qubit emulation.

In a potential partner, we are specifically seeking expertise and capabilities in quantum computing/networking, quantum control, error correction, and scalable benchmarking/evaluation. We are also interested in partners who can contribute relevant hardware/system validation perspectives, experimental or benchmarking environments, or non-sensitive datasets and technical input that can strengthen the technical foundation and evaluation strategy of this effort.
