    Introduction

    I am a tenure-track Assistant Professor at UT Austin. My research is in computational mathematics and scientific machine learning. I design and analyze efficient learning algorithms that are tailor-made for heterogeneous, noisy, incomplete, and limited scientific data. Specific topics include operator learning for partial differential equations (PDEs), statistical and stochastic inverse problems, generative modeling, approximate Bayesian inference/uncertainty quantification, experimental design, and data assimilation for dynamical systems. Application areas include medical imaging, weather forecasting, and Earth systems modeling.

    Recent thrusts relevant to the DOE Genesis Mission include scientific foundation models, optimal data selection, and surrogate modeling for high dimensional PDEs from fusion energy science and many-body quantum systems.

    Before joining UT Austin, I was a Klarman Fellow in the Department of Mathematics at Cornell University and an NSF Postdoc in the Department of Mathematics at MIT. I earned my Ph.D. from Caltech in 2024, where my doctoral dissertation on operator learning was awarded the W. P. Carey & Co. Prize in Applied Mathematics and the Centennial Prize for the Best Thesis in MCE. My work was recognized with a SIGEST award from the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics in 2024.

    Project Idea Summary

    This project targets topic “19 – AI for Scientific Reasoning,” focus areas 19A and 19C. To address these challenges, the technical approach is to interpret scientific foundation models from a new operator learning perspective. This unlocks novel model capabilities that will certify AI advantage over other existing approaches for scientific reasoning.

    I am seeking potential partners with experience leading large sponsored projects in ASCR-adjacent research areas.

    In addition to the project described above, I am open and interested in collaborating as a partner on projects centered on topic areas 19(B), 18(E), 21(B,C), 5(G), 7(C), 11(A,C), 15(A,B,C). I anticipate my theoretical and algorithmic contributions to be in digital twins, AI-based surrogate modeling, probabilistic inversion, uncertainty quantification, and data assimilation.
