Introduction

Source Cooperative (source.coop) is a vendor-neutral data publishing utility operated by Radiant Earth, a US 501(c)(3). Built on cloud object storage, Source hosts 5.6 petabytes of scientific data across 664 million objects, averaging ~500 million data requests per month and exceeding 1 billion in March 2026. Datasets relevant to DOE priorities include Catalyst Cooperative's Public Utility Data (US energy and utility data), Dynamical.org's NOAA GFS and MRMS precipitation archives, Google AlphaEarth Foundations Earth observation embeddings (2017–present), Harvard Library Innovation Lab's archive of Data.gov, and Vantor's Open Data analysis-ready satellite imagery. Publishers get S3-compatible access, a discovery catalog, and DOI/ORCID/ROR identifier support without standing up their own infrastructure. We work in cloud-native open formats (e.g. STAC, Cloud-Optimized GeoTIFF, GeoParquet, Zarr) and are interested in contributing to Genesis Mission Data Integration & Standards work as a neutral hosting layer for AI-ready scientific data.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Source Cooperative operates a multi-cloud, S3-compatible publishing layer running on AWS S3 (primary, us-west-2) with regional replicas, fronted by a Rust data proxy on Cloudflare. The architecture serves ~500M API requests/month and supports federated access to public scientific data without requiring publishers to manage their own buckets, billing, or access controls. We can stand up storage and access for participating Genesis teams using the same architecture and open formats. GitHub code search returns over 1,000 examples of code that reference Source Cooperative.

Subject matter expertise

Cloud-native open formats and scientific data architecture. Radiant Earth's team includes contributors with a decade of experience operating petabyte-scale public data infrastructure, including via the AWS Open Data Program (Jed Sundwall, ED, was previously Head of Open Data at AWS). We work daily in STAC, Cloud-Optimized GeoTIFF, GeoParquet, and Zarr — formats developed and refined within the Cloud-Native Geospatial Forum community we convene (cloudnativegeo.org). We're available to teams working on dataset structure, access patterns, and federation across cloud regions and providers.

Relevant Projects

1. Global Fields of The World (FTW) — Taylor Geospatial Institute (2024–present)

Source published and distributes the Fields of The World dataset, sponsored by Taylor Geospatial — the first global-scale estimates of agricultural field boundaries for 2024–2025. Inputs are Sentinel-2-derived median composites in COG and Zarr v3 formats; outputs are Zarr, GeoParquet, and PMTiles. Available at source.coop/ftw/global-data. Achievement: first-of-its-kind global crop field dataset at planetary scale, enabling agricultural monitoring, food security analysis, and AI training data for land-use models. Announced by Taylor Geospatial as a foundational dataset for the AI-era geospatial stack.

2. Dynamical.org — Cloud-Native Federal Weather Data (2024–present)

Source hosts Dynamical.org's archive of NOAA GFS forecasts, MRMS hourly precipitation analysis, and ASOS airport weather (1940–present) — all transformed into Zarr cloud-native format so analysts can read only the chunks they need without downloading full files. Available at source.coop/dynamical. Achievement: within one month of launch, the platform served 31 million data requests to 13,000 unique visitors; Dynamical secured collaboration support from NOAA and Amazon. Demonstrates the cloud-native publishing model for AI-ready federal weather data — directly relevant to Genesis Mission water and grid challenges.

3. Earth Genome — Earth Index Embeddings (2023–present)

Source hosts Earth Genome's Earth Index project: 60 TB of cloud-optimized satellite imagery and 3.5 billion AI embeddings, served via S3-compatible APIs to researchers and partner organizations. Available at source.coop/earthgenome. Achievement: one of the largest publicly available AI-embedding archives for Earth observation. Establishes the publishing pattern for the next generation of foundation-model training assets at planetary scale.

Capabilities Seeking
Datasets or data infrastructure

We are seeking high-value, authoritative scientific datasets that benefit from durable, vendor-neutral, cloud-native publication. Priority domains include federal energy and grid data, atmospheric and hydrological observations, Earth observation imagery and embeddings for AI training, scientific data archives at risk of loss, and outputs from autonomous-laboratory and simulation workflows. Source scales to petabyte volumes and accommodates heterogeneous formats. Highest-priority publishers are DOE National Labs, NSF-funded centers, federal partners, universities, and research nonprofits.

Subject matter expertise

We are seeking domain experts who can identify high-value datasets in their fields, validate metadata and structure for AI readiness, and help Source extend cloud-native publishing into domains we don't currently specialize in – particularly nuclear physics, materials science, biotechnology, fusion, high-energy physics, and federal scientific archives that have not yet been made AI-ready. DOE National Lab data scientists, federal data stewards, and university PIs with established data products are the partners we'd most like to hear from.

Project Idea Summary

Source Cooperative (source.coop), operated by Radiant Earth (a US-based 501c3), is a data publishing utility hosting 5.6 PB across 664M objects, serving ~500M data requests/month (1B+ in March 2026). We are seeking two kinds of partners.

(1) Authoritative data publishers — DOE national labs, universities, federal partners, and research nonprofits with high-value scientific data who want it durably accessible in cloud-native, AI-ready formats (STAC, COG, GeoParquet, Zarr) without standing up their own infrastructure. Existing publishing partnerships on Source include Catalyst Cooperative's PUDL (US energy/utility data), Dynamical.org's NOAA GFS and MRMS in Zarr, Google's AlphaEarth Foundations embeddings, Harvard LIL's Data.gov archive, and Vantor's Open Data analysis-ready satellite imagery.

(2) Genesis Mission proposal teams — DOE labs, academic PIs, and industry partners forming applications against Genesis RFAs (initially DE-FOA-0003612) who need an open, vendor-neutral hosting layer for the data their AI workflows depend on. Source can serve as a subcontracted publishing utility within proposal teams, with hosting budget scoped to the data volume and access patterns of the project.

We are particularly interested in challenges related to Predicting U.S. Water for Energy, Scaling the Grid to Power the American Economy, and AI for Scientific Reasoning — where existing Source datasets and cloud-native expertise are immediately applicable.
