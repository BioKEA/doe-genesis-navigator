Relevant Projects

- Over the past 10 years I have been a core engineer behind the Mochi project, which aims to provide a collection of software components and a methodology to rapidly develop HPC data services. In this context I have developed many of the components and services and supports 100+ international users of our technology. This project received an R&D100 award in 2021. I am currently the tech lead of this project, in charge of maintaining and improving our collection of components, including benchmarking them on the most recent machines and setting up automated (and more recently AI-driven) CI to support this effort. Mochi technology is still at the core of many of our projects today.

- For the Diaspora "resilient event fabric" project, I have developed Mofka, a Kafka equivalent for HPC. Mofka makes use of Mochi technology, including efficient threading and RDMA, to implement a distributed event streaming service for HPC. I have evaluated Mofka on multiple supercomputers (OLCF's Frontier, and ALCF's Polaris and Improv) with real scientific workflows and benchmarks, and compared its performance against Kafka and Redpanda.

- For the "HEP on HPC" project I have developed HEPnOS, a distributed object store tailored to the needs of high-energy physics applications. HEPnOS was used in real HEP workflows and was the focus of autotuning work using AI.
