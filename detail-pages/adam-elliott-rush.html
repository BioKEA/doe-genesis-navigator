Introduction

isScience, Inc. is developing a content-level cryptographic provenance and disclosure-governance platform for AI-assisted research. A US provisional patent application covering the core architecture was filed in March 2026, and the platform itself has been in development for one year.

The platform addresses a structural gap in AI-driven science: the absence of evidentiary-grade records of AI participation in research, including content access, exclusions, recipient-specific disclosure history, and unforgeable human-versus-AI attribution. These properties are foundational for trustworthy AI in research under federal evidentiary, classification, technology-transfer, and export-control regimes.

isScience operates as a governance and provenance layer beneath existing AI tools, HPC pipelines, instruments, and data infrastructure, integrating without rip-and-replace through standardized interfaces, governed artifact packages, and external verification endpoints. Format-agnostic across text, source code, structured datasets, audio/video, images, and 3D models. Complementary to action-governance, policy-enforcement, and AI-integration approaches, filling the content-level evidentiary gap.

Aligned with Topic 20 (Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows), Topic 13 (AI-Driven Autonomous Laboratories), and AI for Scientific Reasoning.

Project Idea Summary

isScience proposes to develop and demonstrate a content-level cryptographic provenance and governance layer for AI-driven scientific workflows—aligned primarily with Topic 20 (Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows), with secondary fit to Topic 13 (AI-Driven Autonomous Laboratories) and AI for Scientific Reasoning.

The work addresses a structural gap: existing approaches (action-governance, workflow-lineage, AI-integration) do not produce evidentiary-grade records of which content elements an AI model accessed, what was excluded, who received which disclosure, and whether contributions were human- or AI-generated. Foundational for trustworthy AI under federal evidentiary, classification, technology-transfer, and export-control requirements.

Proposed work extends isScience's already-implemented core with spec-defined modules in design: policy-aware AI inference, controlled-agent governance, federated evaluation across institutions, and integration interfaces for existing AI tools and HPC pipelines.

Ideal consortium: (1) a DOE/NNSA national lab partner providing a classified or controlled research workflow as validation environment; (2) an academic or non-profit partner with complementary expertise (e.g., human-AI teaming, attribution standards); (3) industry partners with scientific computing or AI-tool integration capabilities. Designed to wrap existing tools, not replace them.
