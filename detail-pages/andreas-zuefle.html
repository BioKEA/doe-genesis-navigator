    Introduction

    I'm an Associate Professor in Computer Science at Emory University. I specialize in Spatial Computing and the development of Spatial Foundation Models designed to unify disparate spatio-temporal data. My work moves beyond "black-box" AI to create agentic, urban-scale simulations that are Scalable, Interpretable, and Resilient. By integrating human mobility trajectories with agent-based modeling, I build AI-driven platforms capable of autonomous decision-support, predicting complex social interactions and "black swan" edge cases (e.g., extreme weather impacts) where traditional models fail. I am seeking to partner with National Labs and industry leaders to scale these foundation models for the next generation of resilient, autonomous GIS.

    Project Idea Summary

    While Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in processing and generating natural language, their performance on spatio-temporal data remains limited. Spatial-temporal processes are highly complex as they depend on (1) Where?: the location type and proximity to other events, and (2) When?: the time, weather, and current events like school holidays. Both spatial and temporal datasets exist, but building a unified model remains challenging because both dimensions are inherently non-linear coining the phrase “spatial is special”.

    The goal of this project is to create a spatio-temporal foundation model capable of digesting multi-dimensional information, including: (1) past energy usage time-series, (2) population information, (3) social and cultural data such as religious holidays, (4) event calendars for major sports, and (5) weather information. Building upon this foundation model, we will investigate a spatio-temporal agent that uses this model as a tool to predict future states. The resulting modular AI system can be easily plugged into other systems to answer spatio-temporal questions, such as predicting energy usage during a winter storm, and ground those answers in the data for verifiable reasoning. 

    We're looking for experts in industrly and at national labs with an interesting in understanding spatial and spatio-temporal data and we're looking for compute infrastructure to train our envisioned models.
