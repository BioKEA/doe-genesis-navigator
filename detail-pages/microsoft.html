Introduction

Microsoft is committed to partnering with DOE laboratories as a long‑term science and engineering collaborator. We bring leadership in advanced AI—securely enabling access to frontier and open models with strong governance, auditing, and responsible use. We support discovery science with dedicated engineers and researchers who help translate scientific questions into reproducible, operational workflows. For high‑performance computing, we collaborate to extend and integrate supercomputing‑class capabilities across hybrid environments, including scheduler‑based and time‑sensitive workloads. We are advancing practical quantum pathways for scientific computing through co‑development and research collaboration. Security is foundational: zero‑trust architectures, identity and policy controls, and deep observability designed for sensitive research environments. Finally, we have deep experience supporting national security and nuclear missions, including work in classified and highly regulated contexts.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Microsoft enables labs to access frontier and open AI models (11,000+ available) through a governed interface with authentication, audit logs, policy enforcement, and usage controls. 
Our AI toolchain supports model selection, evaluation, fine‑tuning, deployment, and continuous monitoring, plus agent frameworks that connect models to data and lab workflows for reproducible results.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Microsoft provides hands-on AI/ML and data-science expertise to take mission use cases from concept to repeatable delivery: data curation and feature engineering; scalable model training and inference; evaluation and benchmarking; and end-to-end workflow automation. Our teams include ML engineers, data engineers, and solution architects who design secure, compliant pipelines, support responsible AI practices, and deliver enablement through office hours, hackathons, and training.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Microsoft cloud (commercial, government, and hybrid) provides scalable compute, networking, and storage for HPC and AI workloads—plus AI and Copilot experiences grounded in work data for automation, summarization, and agent‑driven workflows with enterprise‑grade trust and centralized management.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Azure enables secure, scalable data foundations for science: a lake/lakehouse approach for storing and sharing datasets, automated ingestion and curation pipelines to make data AI‑ready, and a governance layer for cataloging, metadata, lineage, and policy‑based access across hybrid and multicloud environments.

Experimental or test facilities

Azure supports experimental and test facilities by securely connecting instruments, edge systems, and lab networks into cloud workflows—enabling API-based integration, outbound-only connectivity patterns, hybrid operation, and orchestration for closed-loop experimentation (plan → run → collect → analyze → iterate) with centralized governance and observability

Subject matter expertise

Microsoft brings a full bench of experts—AI engineers and solution architects, Microsoft Research scientists, and Microsoft Discovery & Quantum specialists—who can move from ideas to validated workflows. We pair that with cybersecurity experts to embed Zero Trust and data governance, and data center/energy professionals to plan capacity and reliability so mission workloads run safely and at scale.

Project Idea Summary

Microsoft seeks to collaborate in the Genesis Mission as a long-term, execution-ready partner—working with national labs, universities, and industry to shorten the cycle from hypothesis to validated results. We bring multidisciplinary experts (engineering and research) to co-design measurable pilot outcomes, then scale what works through repeatable operating rhythms: shared intake, delivery cadence, and program-level reporting. We focus on interoperability so partners can integrate their models, data, instruments, and workflows without lock-in, while establishing governance and validation practices that make results trusted and reusable. Across AI, quantum, nuclear, energy, and cybersecurity, our aim is to pair scientific ambition with disciplined delivery: secure collaboration, responsible use of data, continuous monitoring, and capacity planning so critical workloads run reliably as adoption grows.
