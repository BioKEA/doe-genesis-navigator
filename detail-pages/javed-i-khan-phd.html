Introduction

Kent State University specializes in the verification, validation (V&V), and security of high-consequence AI systems, specifically addressing "epistemic exploitation" in probabilistic models. Our team is pioneering the VECTOR7 framework and VECTOR7.GUARD, a standalone AI Output Security Gateway designed to function as an "epistemic firewall" at the trust boundary. This technology intercepts AI-generated technical results and code to detect fabricated procedures and ensure the reliability of scientific artifacts, directly supporting the Genesis Mission’s objectives for Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows (Topic 20) and Trustworthy AI (Topic 18). By providing an independent, evaluative layer for the American Science Cloud and ModCon initiatives, we aim to demonstrate a quantifiable "AI advantage" through a 9-month Phase I prototype that secures R&D workflows against logic-embedded vulnerabilities .

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

We offer VECTOR7.GUARD, a standalone AI Output Security Gateway designed as an "epistemic firewall" to intercept and verify probabilistic AI outputs at the trust boundary. Unlike internal model guardrails, the Guard operates independently to detect fabricated technical procedures, hallucinations, and logic-embedded vulnerabilities. It is built upon the VECTOR7 framework for AI Verification and Validation (V&V), providing quantitative epistemic risk scores to ensure the integrity of AI-generated scientific artifacts before they are integrated into sensitive R&D HPC workflows.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our expertise focuses on the security and reliability of Large Language Models (LLMs) and AI-as-a-Service (AIaaS) systems. We specialize in characterizing the reliability-throughput trade-off in high-consequence inference systems and developing adversarial robustness protocols. Our team excels in identifying "epistemic exploitation"—vulnerabilities where AI generates authoritative but incorrect content. We apply advanced data science techniques to map AI competencies to workforce roles and develop automated verification pipelines that detect subtle defects in complex AI outputs.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

As an academic institution, we have access to limited HPC environments optimized for AI security testing and model evaluation. Our infrastructure supports the deployment of middleware like VECTOR7.GUARD for real-time monitoring of AI-driven workflows. We are equipped to simulate small scale "stress" conditions on AI artifacts to measure integrity degradation under varying throughput loads. This environment is ideal for benchmarking the performance overhead of security gateways and validating their effectiveness within the American Science Cloud or ModCon ecosystem. But certainly we need more.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We provide highly specialized adversarial probing datasets and epistemic risk benchmarks designed to stress-test the limits of AI-generated technical content. These datasets include curated examples of fabricated technical procedures and "devilish" LLM claim deceptions—analogous to phishing—that challenge standard verification methods. Our infrastructure also includes structured data for mapping AI competencies and V&V metrics, facilitating the creation of a "Gold Standard" for scientific integrity in AI workflows, essential for the Genesis Mission's goals of transformative discovery.

Subject matter expertise

Our team brings deep subject matter expertise in cybersecurity analysis (utilizing the HARM 66 framework), AI ethics, and the preservation of artifact integrity. We specialize in the conceptualization of "provable cyber assurance" for autonomous systems, particularly in nuclear energy and advanced manufacturing contexts. Our expertise extends to the development of ethical frameworks for AI deployment, ensuring that scientific discovery workflows are not only accelerated but also secured against reputational, social, and legal harms arising from unverified machine-generated conclusions.

Other expertise

We offer significant experience in managing successful federal research projects and academic infrastructure, including leading proposals for the National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cybersecurity (NCAE-R). Our background in ABET accreditation coordination ensures a rigorous approach to workload, compensation, and institutional justification for complex multi-partner initiatives. This administrative expertise is vital for navigating the teaming requirements of the Genesis Mission, allowing us to serve as a reliable lead or key partner in bridging academic, lab, and industry categories.

Other resources/tools

We provide specialized curriculum development frameworks and assessment strategies for AI verification, originally developed for NCAE cybersecurity training. These tools can be repurposed to train DOE researchers and lab personnel on the VECTOR7 V&V methodology, ensuring that the "AI Advantage" is understood and maintained across the workforce. Our resources include detailed module titles, competency mappings, and assessment rubrics for AI security, providing a roadmap for the cultural and technical shift required to integrate trustworthy AI into national energy and science labs.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources We seek access to DOE High-Performance Computing (HPC) environments or the American Science Cloud to benchmark the performance and latency of the VECTOR7.GUARD middleware. We are looking for partners with curated, domain-specific scientific datasets (e.g., from Advanced Manufacturing or Nuclear Energy workflows) to serve as a ground-truth baseline. Access to datasets containing known edge cases or historical anomalies is critical for validating the Guard's ability to detect "epistemic exploitation" and model hallucinations. We aim to test our adversarial probing techniques against data that reflects the complexity of actual DOE scientific artifacts.

Experimental or test facilities

We seek collaboration with Scientific User Facilities to test VECTOR7.GUARD in an operational R&D setting. Our goal is to demonstrate the tool's effectiveness in protecting active scientific workflows, ensuring that AI-generated procedures or code meet the rigorous safety and integrity standards required for DOE facility operations. Partnering with a facility allows us to validate the tool as a "production-ready" security layer for the broader ModCon and AmSC ecosystems.

Subject matter expertise

We are looking for domain experts in DOE challenge areas—such as nuclear engineering, materials science, or power systems—to provide expert human-in-the-loop verification of AI outputs. This collaboration is essential to confirm that our quantitative epistemic risk scores accurately reflect the technical "correctness" and "trustworthiness" of AI-generated results. Expert validation will help refine our evaluative logic to align with specific scientific standards.

Project Idea Summary

This project addresses a critical security gap in AI-driven research through VECTOR7.GUARD, a standalone AI Security Gateway. As LLMs integrate into DOE workflows, they introduce "epistemic exploitation"—vulnerabilities where models produce authoritative but fabricated technical procedures or incorrect logic. Unlike internal guardrails, the Guard acts as an independent "epistemic firewall" at the trust boundary. It intercepts probabilistic AI outputs during inference for real-time inspection, ensuring the integrity of scientific artifacts before they cross into high-consequence R&D environments.

We focus on Topic 20: Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows (Focus Area C).  We seek a Lab or Industry partner to help identify and provide compelling, specific use cases—such as Nuclear Energy or Advanced Manufacturing—to serve as a validation testbed. A successful Phase I/II can also be a high-performance hardware vision to achieve the line-rate performance needed for the American Science Cloud. We seek an Lab and/or industry partner to co-design the Guard using hardware acceleration (e.g., FPGAs or SmartNICs) to provide real-time, low-latency verification. Simultaneously,This collaboration will demonstrate a quantifiable "AI Advantage" by securing the trajectory of transformative scientific discovery.
