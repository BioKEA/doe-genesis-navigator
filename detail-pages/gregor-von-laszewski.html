Introduction

Dr. Gregor von Laszewski is a Research Professor at the University of Virginia and an expert in High-Performance Computing (HPC), Cloud Computing, and AI. Within the context of the Genesis Mission his work focuses on the intersection of automated code generation and AI-driven orchestration.

As a long-standing contributor to the DOE ecosystem (including over a decade at Argonne National Laboratory), his role in the Genesis Mission and the Transformational AI Models Consortium (ModCon) centers on current efforts in:

Automated Code Optimization: Developing AI-driven tools that automate code generation to improve performance while strictly adhering to energy constraints.

Knowledge-Driven AI: Utilizing ontology-based frameworks to provide "focus" for application performance improvement, enabling models to be retrained on newly generated code to create a self-improving, relevant codebase.

Benchmarking and Portability: Leveraging his leadership in MLCommons Science Benchmarks to ensure that AI foundation models remain portable and efficient across diverse hardware, including NVIDIA DGX systems.

His current efforts bridge the gap between high-level scientific research and the practical, automated management of next-generation AI models for national-scale supercomputing infrastructure.

Relevant Projects

1. MLCommons Science Benchmarks (CloudMask)

Scope: As a lead contributor to the MLCommons Science working group, Dr. von Laszewski spearheaded the development of an automated benchmarking framework for any AI based application. This was tested on CloudMask, a deep learning application designed to identify cloud formations in satellite imagery. The project focused on creating a reproducible, high-performance workflow across diverse supercomputing environments, including ORNL’s Summit and various NVIDIA DGX systems. Achievements: He successfully automated the deployment and performance measurement of large-scale U-Net models. By utilizing his Cloudmesh toolkit, he enabled seamless execution across hybrid-cloud and HPC infrastructures. This work established rigorous standards for evaluating AI model performance on scientific datasets, directly contributing to the global "MLPerf" standards used by industry and national labs to validate AI hardware efficiency.

2. High-Performance Orchestration for DGX Systems

Scope: Dr. von Laszewski managed the technical orchestration of NVIDIA DGX Station A100 nodes to support large-scale AI research and education. The scope involved configuring complex GPU partitioning (MIG), Slurm scheduling, and containerized environments (Docker/Ollama) to facilitate the parallel execution of Large Language Models and scientific simulations. Achievements: He developed a robust Python-based CLI ecosystem that automated the management of multi-GPU workloads. By implementing strict GPU indexing, he optimized resource allocation for intensive AI training tasks. 

3. Cyberinfrastructure for Autonomous AI Research (Cloudmesh)

Scope: Over the last five years, he evolved the Cloudmesh ecosystem into a specialized toolset for "AI-First" research. This involved creating plugins to automate the generation of code and the management of distributed data across multi-cloud environments (this included AWS, Azure, and On-Premise to clouds, but also to HPC resources and LLM services). Achievements: He successfully demonstrated the ability to deploy complex virtual clusters and scientific workflows using simple CLI commands, significantly reducing the "time-to-science" for researchers. This project achieved a high level of portability. Users can transition workloads between cloud and HPC resources without manual code rewrites. His work in this area directly informs the current "self-improving" and "automated code generation" goals of the ModCon initiative.
