Introduction

ORNL is leveraging AI across its broad scientific portfolio to drive the nation’s energy, security, and competitiveness. 

ORNL researchers are embedding AI directly into scientific workflows — developing explainable methods that enable near-real-time insights into a range of phenomena including quantum materials, nuclear energy, and cybersecurity. These efforts move beyond data analysis toward autonomous science, where AI systems guide experiments, modeling, and decision-making and, by extension, greatly reduce time to solution. 

These advancements are only possible thanks to ORNL’s exascale computing resources, which are enabling more efficient approaches to training large-scale AI models while reducing energy demands. At the same time, researchers are designing secure-by-design architectures with built-in guardrails to protect against misuse and adversarial threats.[DB1] [JS2] 

In addition to the existing exascale-class system, Frontier, two new computing systems at Oak Ridge National Laboratory – Discovery and Lux – will advance the Genesis Mission. ORNL will chart new pathways for AI-driven science, accelerating innovation and developing the American Science Cloud (AmSC), a secure, connected and integrated, science-optimized research platform that links DOE’s computing and experimental facilities.

By advancing Genesis, ORNL is ensuring that AI remains a secure and reliable partner in scientific discovery and that America continues to lead scientifically and economically.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

ORNL develops and deploys advanced AI models, tools, and software—from LLMs augmented with scientific literature to autonomous “self-driving” experimental platforms—that generate hypotheses, run experiments, and correlate complex data to accelerate discovery in materials and manufacturing. ORNL also builds AI foundation platforms such as ORBIT, converting global weather forecasts into sub-kilometer environmental intelligence for real-time water and energy decisions. ORNL has applied AI to accelerate fusion power plant design and, with Atomic Canyon, speed nuclear licensing workflows.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

ORNL brings expertise and leadership in advanced computing and AI, with multidisciplinary teams spanning national security, energy, manufacturing, and bioengineering, to build scalable, mission-ready analytics and agentic workflows. ORNL applies these capabilities from AI-accelerated nuclear reactor safety analysis to autonomous manufacturing optimization using ML, reinforcement learning control, and AI-enabled imaging, and delivers real-time systems including agentic AI on Frontier for autonomous plant phenotyping and AI for experiment steering and anomaly detection at neutron facilities.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

ORNL provides world-class computing infrastructure and cloud resources, coupled with efficient computing R&D that advances semiconductor devices and architectures and explores new paradigms such as neuromorphic and analog computing. ORNL also applies AI and data science to optimize data center operations and improve coordination between data centers and the electric grid to reduce energy use and increase reliability. These capabilities extend to developing AI-enabled approaches that support a more resilient and secure power grid.

Datasets or data infrastructure

ORNL delivers robust data sets and data infrastructure for efficient access, organization, retrieval, sharing, and analysis of big data at scale. Through CADES, ORNL integrates HPC systems, scalable storage, and analytics tools to help user facilities and teams manage and process large data volumes. ORNL also has a long history of AI-enabled digital twins, including reactor and infrastructure twins and the R&D 100 Award–winning ExaDigiT framework for near-real-time digital twins of supercomputing data centers to optimize workloads, power, and cooling.

Experimental or test facilities

ORNL offers a unique ecosystem of experimental and test capabilities, combining automated laboratories that streamline and accelerate research with high-performance computing. Frontier provides exascale computing for AI-driven simulation and data analytics, and next-generation systems such as Lux and Discovery will further expand DOE’s AI capacity. ORNL user facilities include world-leading neutron scattering (Spallation Neutron Source), materials and nanoscale research (Center for Nanophase Materials Sciences), and additive manufacturing (Manufacturing Demonstration Facility).

Subject matter expertise

ORNL’s strength is collaboration across divisions and disciplines to advance cross-cutting science. More than 300 ORNL staff members are engaged in cutting-edge fusion technology. More than 600 staff members work in projects associated with national security. ORNL employees harden the energy grid, improving resilience, security, and adaptability to evolving U.S. demand, and ORNL researchers work in areas related to evolving quantum science, including computing, materials, and communications. Additionally, ORNL researchers drive biotechnology innovation, including work on OPAL and GPTgp.

Other expertise

ORNL delivers transdisciplinary computational science and analytics at scale to accelerate discovery across physical sciences, engineering, and health: advancing OPAL autonomous labs, enzyme design, fusion materials analysis, and AI-driven plant productivity. ORNL also leads in quantum information science, developing methods, applications, and data assets, including AI for quantum materials discovery, and partnering with industry and labs to strengthen critical materials supply chains.

Other resources/tools

The National Center for Computational Sciences (NCCS), the parent organization of the OLCF, provides state-of-the-art computational and data science infrastructure and support for technical and scientific professionals. To accelerate scientific discovery and engineering advances across a broad range of disciplines, the NCCS offers leadership-class computing facilities—including the exascale system, Frontier—to users across the DOE complex.

Relevant Projects

INTERSECT: Advanced research instruments in laboratories and at user facilities produce data at ever-increasing rates, and automation and robotics allow measuring large systematic datasets without constant human attention and intervention. Combining diverse research tools makes ambitious multi-domain and/or multi-modal experiments possible that result in complex datasets, while high performance computing facilitates the simulation and modeling of large systems with a fidelity previously thought impossible.

The INTERSECT initiative worked to build interconnected “Smart Labs of the Future” that would enable self-driving autonomous experiments that leverage advanced compute systems, scientific instruments and facilities, with real-time data analysis and feedback optimizations using AI/ML and edge computing. These Smart Labs consist of a human-AI-machine interface, experiment management software, autonomous science applications, and “Big Data” analysis and management as well as data-driven feedback. INTERSECT delivers a common One-ORNL computational infrastructure and the currently missing common, seamless and secure infrastructure required to scale across facilities and science domains, minimizing duplication of effort and enabling collaboration in the spirit of co-design to develop transferable building blocks for autonomous workflows.

Enabling adaptively controlled additive manufacturing through automation: ORNL is developing an autonomous additive manufacturing lab, accelerating the ability to print complex metal components that can be used with confidence in safety-critical applications such as aircraft and energy generation. Researchers are creating an autonomous system to adaptively control the additive manufacturing printing process using state-of-the art robotics, in-situ characterization, simulations, and applied math methods. 

HydraGNN: With experiments generating petabytes and even exabytes of data over time, tracing links between hundreds, thousands or even millions of variables in processes such as drug discovery and materials development can be a Herculean task. With the advent of AI, researchers can rely on graph neural networks (GNNs) to map the connections and unravel their relationships, greatly expediting time to solution. Researchers are evolving GNNs to scale on the nation’s most powerful computational resources, illustrating how to scale GNNs on DOE’s leadership-class computing systems. 
The team of ORNL and Lawrence Berkeley researchers demonstrated the scaling of HydraGNN on the Perlmutter system as well as the Summit and Frontier supercomputers. HydraGNN is an ORNL-branded implementation of GNN architectures that aims to produce fast and accurate predictions of material properties by abstracting the lattice structure of a solid material as a graph, where atoms are represented by nodes and metallic bonds by edges, eliminating the need for computationally expensive data pre-processing.

ORBIT: ORBIT is a unified AI foundation platform that transforms global weather forecasts into sub-kilometer environmental intelligence. ORBIT will train on DOE exascale supercomputers and deploy anywhere to deliver real-time predictions, enabling real-time water forecasting for energy systems, strengthening grid reliability and infrastructure resilience, and democratizing access to high-resolution prediction. ORBIT delivers global prediction at 0.9 km resolution with orders-of-magnitude speedup—up to 99% accuracy for global prediction.

Project Idea Summary

ORNL is uniquely positioned to address the science and technology challenges in the Genesis Mission by leveraging leadership in AI, high-performance computing (HPC), and large-scale experimental science. ORNL can integrate AI with exascale computing to tackle complex, data-intensive problems such as advanced materials discovery, grid modernization, and nuclear energy innovation. 

With the Oak Ridge Leadership Computing Facility and upcoming systems such as Discovery and Lux, ORNL enables the large-scale simulations and AI model training needed to “double the productivity and impact” of U.S. research—capabilities critical for scaling the electric grid, accelerating fusion and fission technologies, and optimizing energy systems. In national security, ORNL applies AI-driven modeling, cybersecurity tools, and advanced data analytics to protect critical infrastructure and prevent misuse of nuclear materials, supported by secure computing and threat detection expertise. ORNL also bridges fundamental discovery and real-world deployment by combining neutron science, materials characterization, and AI-enabled autonomous laboratories to accelerate the transition from breakthroughs to commercial technologies. Finally, ORNL’s collaborative ecosystem across national labs, universities, and industry aligns with Genesis goals to unify U.S. scientific infrastructure and datasets.
