Introduction

Dr. Tahir Ekin is the Fields Endowed Chair in Business Analytics and Professor at Texas State University, as well as the founding Director of the Center for Analytics and Data Science (CADS). His research focuses on probabilistic modeling, statistical learning, adversarial AI, and decision-making under uncertainty, with applications in high-stakes domains such as risk analytics, healthcare, and national security . His work emphasizes translating advanced AI methodologies into real-world decision systems, aligning closely with the Genesis Mission’s goal of accelerating scientific discovery through robust, deployable AI frameworks.

Texas State University contributes strong interdisciplinary capabilities in data science and applied AI through CADS and related initiatives, supporting research that integrates AI, data systems, and domain applications. This aligns with the Genesis Mission Consortium’s objective to unite academic, industry, and national lab expertise to develop scalable AI-driven solutions for energy, manufacturing, and national security challenges .

Together, Dr. Ekin and Texas State University bring expertise in AI robustness, decision intelligence, and applied analytics, positioning them to contribute to key Genesis Mission priorities such as AI model development and validation, data-driven decision systems, and resilient, real-world AI applications across energy and industrial domains.

Relevant Projects

1. Adversarial Robustness in Transformer Models (LLMs)
Scope: Investigated robustness of transformer architectures (BERT, LLaMA, FLAN-T5) under word-, sentence-, and semantic-level adversarial attacks.
Achievements: Demonstrated architecture-dependent resilience patterns and robustness–accuracy tradeoffs; implemented QLoRA-based fine-tuning and evaluation pipelines; established benchmarking framework for adversarial testing across models, supporting development of trustworthy AI systems.

2. AI-Driven Fraud Detection and Anomaly Analytics
Scope: Developed probabilistic and machine learning models (Bayesian networks, topic modeling, classifiers) for large-scale healthcare fraud detection and anomaly identification.
Achievements: Delivered interpretable, high-performing detection systems; advanced methods for temporal anomaly detection and decision support; results published in leading journals (e.g., ESWA, JRSS), demonstrating real-world impact in high-stakes environments.

3. Decision Intelligence and Probabilistic Modeling for Complex Systems
Scope: Designed statistical learning and simulation frameworks for decision-making under uncertainty in domains such as healthcare, operations, and risk analytics.
Achievements: Developed scalable models for forecasting and optimization; contributed to methods for uncertainty quantification and sequential decision processes; enabled actionable insights for resource allocation and system-level decision support.

Project Idea Summary

Focus Area: Scaling the Grid to Power the American Economy (Challenge Area 16)

This project develops AI-enabled infrastructure intelligence systems for real-time monitoring, forecasting, and decision support in energy and grid systems. The approach integrates distributed sensing, physics-based modeling, and probabilistic AI to enable dynamic state estimation and resilience under uncertainty and multi-hazard conditions.

We seek partners with access to energy infrastructure data, HPC and cloud resources, and testbed environments (e.g., grid, power systems). Ideal collaborators include DOE national labs and industry partners for large-scale validation, deployment, and integration into operational energy systems.
