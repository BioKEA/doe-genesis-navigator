Introduction

Mavros is a technology innovation company building and deploying AI, robotics, and cybersecure IT/OT systems across critical infrastructure sectors — including power and electricity, oil and gas, water utilities, and the federal government. We do not just advise on technology; we design, engineer, and operate it. Our teams are embedded in live industrial environments, developing intelligent systems that bridge the physical and digital worlds to solve real operational challenges at scale.

Mavros and our partners bring complementary robotics and autonomous sensing capabilities, extending our combined platform into new industrial deployment contexts, including sustainable e-waste recovery and resource recovery operations.

Together, we are applying to DE-FOA-0003612 (Topic 1-D: Digitalization of Industrial Processes) to develop AI-driven digital twins powered by robotic sensing, secured by proven IT/OT architecture, and deployed within real energy-sector facilities we already operate in. Our goal is to demonstrate quantifiable AI advantage — moving critical infrastructure from reactive to predictive intelligence. We are seeking a National Laboratory partner with AI modeling and HPC expertise to complete our Phase I team.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Mavros designs and deploys custom AI models for industrial operations, including predictive maintenance systems, anomaly detection engines, and autonomous process control tools. Our AI solutions are built for real-world deployment in critical infrastructure — not lab environments. Combined with robotic platforms, we deliver end-to-end AI-powered systems that collect, process, and act on operational data in power, oil & gas, water, and federal facilities.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our team applies machine learning, computer vision, time-series analysis, and physics-informed modeling to industrial datasets. We specialize in developing AI workflows that extract actionable intelligence from sensor-rich environments — including SCADA systems, ICS networks, and robotic platforms. We design AI pipelines from raw data ingestion through model training, inference, and closed-loop feedback into operational systems, with expertise in edge AI deployment for latency-sensitive industrial contexts.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Mavros designs and manages secure computing infrastructure for government and industrial clients, including hybrid cloud architectures, edge computing deployments, and hardened IT/OT networks. We build the secure data pipelines that move operational technology data safely to AI inference systems — a critical capability for Genesis Mission workflows that require real industrial data without compromising critical infrastructure security. We support FedRAMP-aligned and CMMC-compliant environments.

Subject matter expertise

Extensive expertise in power and electricity, oil and gas, water utilities, traffic engineering, and federal government systems. Our teams understand the operational realities of energy-intensive industrial environments — not just the technology layer, but also the regulatory, safety, and security constraints that guide the responsible deployment of AI. This provides us with direct access to live industrial testbeds and trusted relationships with facility operators, which are essential for deploying and validating AI systems at scale.

Other expertise

Mavros brings specialized expertise in autonomous robotics, IT/OT cybersecurity, and critical infrastructure protection. Our robotics capabilities enable autonomous data collection in industrial environments — thermal, acoustic, visual, and process telemetry — that traditional instrumentation cannot provide. Our cybersecurity practice secures AI-driven science workflows against threats specific to operational technology environments, including SCADA, ICS, and industrial control systems, protecting U.S. energy infrastructure.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

Seeking access to DOE-developed scientific foundation models, simulation frameworks, and open-source AI tools built on years of federally funded research — including physics-based digital twin architectures, molecular dynamics models, and high-fidelity simulation environments not available commercially. These models provide the scientific baseline our industrial AI systems will build upon.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Seeking research scientists with peer-reviewed expertise in scientific AI — including physics-informed machine learning, uncertainty quantification, and multi-scale modeling. This academic rigor validates our AI outputs against established scientific principles, satisfies DOE's standards for reproducibility, and positions results for publication and integration into the Genesis Mission platform.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Seeking direct access to DOE Leadership Computing Facilities — Aurora, Frontier, or Perlmutter — for large-scale AI model training that exceeds commercial cloud capabilities. We also seek integration pathways into the American Science Cloud for model hosting, dataset sharing, and interoperability with other Genesis Mission teams.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Seeking access to DOE-curated scientific datasets, national laboratory experimental data repositories, and federated data infrastructure built on years of federally funded research — including energy systems performance data, materials characterization datasets, and environmental monitoring records. These validated, high-quality datasets provide the training foundation our AI models require and cannot be replicated through commercial or operational data collection alone.

Experimental or test facilities

Seeking access to DOE national user facilities — including manufacturing demonstration facilities, scientific user facilities, and specialized testbeds such as Oak Ridge's Manufacturing Demonstration Facility or Argonne's robotics and materials labs. These world-class environments allow our AI models and robotic systems to be validated under controlled scientific conditions, stress-tested at scale, and benchmarked against established performance standards before deployment in live industrial settings

Subject matter expertise

Seeking federally credentialed domain scientists whose research aligns with DOE mission areas — energy systems, environmental management, nuclear, or critical materials. Their scientific authority strengthens proposal credibility, satisfies peer review standards, and ensures our industrial AI work advances DOE's broader research agenda beyond commercial application.

Other expertise

Nice to have: Seeking an institution with established DOE relationships, existing security clearances, prior Genesis Mission involvement, and experience navigating federal award compliance — including data management plans, open-source licensing requirements, and IP frameworks. This institutional infrastructure accelerates award execution and ensures seamless integration into the Genesis Mission ecosystem. Mavros has cleared personnel in Texas and DC and the ability to utilize cleared personnel in Tennessee. 

Project Idea Summary

Mavros Technology and partners are applying to DE-FOA-0003612 under Topic 1-D (Digitalization of Industrial Processes) and related challenge areas including grid modernization, water, oil and gas, cybersecurity, and critical minerals recovery. We are targeting FY26 Phase I with an April 28 deadline and are seeking a committed partner now.

Our team brings full-stack capability in AI development, robotics, autonomous sensing, IT/OT integration, and cybersecure critical infrastructure deployment. Based in Dallas, TX with presence in Nashville, TN.

Our project develops AI-driven digital twins powered by autonomous robotic sensing, secured by proven OT cybersecurity architecture, and validated against real industrial process data. The goal is to demonstrate a measurable AI advantage — transforming critical infrastructure from reactive to predictive.

We are seeking a National Laboratory or academic institution to contribute: DOE-curated scientific datasets; HPC computing access for AI model training; experimental testbed facilities for validation; scientific AI expertise; and integration pathways with the Genesis Mission platform.
