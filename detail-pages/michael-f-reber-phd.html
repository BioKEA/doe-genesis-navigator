Introduction

Michael F. Reber and the HITs AI Workforce Development Platform

I am Michael F. Reber, a U.S. citizen based in Japan and Fellow of The Schumacher Institute. In collaboration with IBM Japan, I developed the Holarchical Innovation Teams (HITs) AI Workforce Development Platform concept to address the need for adaptive, equitable team formation in AI- and robotics-augmented environments.

HITs AI matches individuals to optimal roles in milliseconds, enabling real-time team assembly without traditional screening tools. At its core is the Simulated Experience Function (SimX), a foresight engine that stress-tests organizational logic to detect misalignment before it occurs—preserving moral architecture while accelerating operational rhythm.

This work aligns with the DOE's Genesis Mission, particularly its focus on AI-accelerated science, anticipatory systems, and equitable workforce orchestration. I am currently seeking consortium partners—national labs, mission-aligned companies, and public sector collaborators—to co-develop, pilot, and case-study the HITs AI Platform. I also welcome discussions on monetization pathways, including a DOE-aligned spin-out company integrating both the technology and a holarchical governance model.

I look forward to exploring how HITs AI can contribute meaningfully to the Genesis Mission and to building adaptive, just, and resilient workforce systems for the AI-accelerated future.

Relevant Projects

Holarchical Innovation Teams (HITs) AI Workforce Development Platform Concept

From 2020 to 2023, I spearheaded a concept development project in collaboration with IBM Japan to explore the feasibility of a Holarchical Innovation Teams (HITs) AI Workforce Development Platform. The project aimed to address the inefficiencies of traditional hiring and team formation by designing a system that could match individuals to optimal roles and teams in real time—without resumes, job searches, or interviews.

Our scope included defining the platform’s core logic, identifying key profile and team-matching variables, and outlining a multi-phase development roadmap. We focused on how holarchical design principles could inform adaptive, equitable workforce systems, particularly in AI- and robotics-augmented environments. The result was the conceptual framework presented in a HITs AI Deck, which articulates the platform’s vision, mission, user value, and potential market impact.

While we did not produce a validated architecture or deployable prototype, the project laid critical groundwork for future development. It also informed the publication of four papers on HITs and one on the Corporate Estate (CE) model in Economia Aziendale Online. Future work will explore the Simulated Experience Function (SimX), a foresight mechanism designed to anticipate misalignment and preserve institutional coherence in complex systems.

Project Idea Summary

Project Summary: HITs AI Workforce Development Platform

The purpose of the project is to develop, pilot, and case-study the HITs AI Workforce Development Platform—a regenerative framework designed to ensure continuous, meaningful employment for billions of people worldwide. During the COVID-19 pandemic, I collaborated with IBM Japan to develop the HITs AI concept and shape its foundational architecture. The platform remains in the concept development phase and is now ready for co-development and piloting with mission-aligned partners.

HITs AI addresses a critical challenge in AI-accelerated science and distributed teams: matching individuals to optimal roles in milliseconds, eliminating traditional screening tools, and enabling precise, real-time team formation. At its core is the Simulated Experience Function (SimX), a computational foresight engine that stress-tests organizational logic to detect role distortion, entitlement drift, and profile compression before they manifest.

I seek partners to co-develop and pilot the platform in real-world environments and contribute to case studies. Ideal collaborators bring expertise in AI/ML, data infrastructure, cloud computing, or workforce development.
