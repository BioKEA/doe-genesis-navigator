    Introduction

    I run the Materials by Design Lab at the University of Missouri-Kansas City. We investigate new, unusual, and complex materials for next-generation device technologies such as detectors, optical/electrical switches, and integrated circuit components. We are focused on developing methods and understanding mechanisms of thin-film deposition processes, understanding and optimizing the relationship between atomic/electronic structure and functional properties such as optical, electrical, thermal, and mechanical behavior, and bringing this knowledge together to ultimately design and tune materials to meet the requirements for device integration. We have focused especially on boron-rich solids, amorphous materials, semi-insulators and dielectrics. We are interested in developing AI/ML pipelines for materials design, specifically focusing on generating and curating meaningful experimental data sets (including handling messy data for messy materials), and understanding which chemical/physical relationships and descriptors are most useful for augmenting AI/ML models.

    Relevant Projects

    Boron Carbon Nitride (BCN) as a Thermally Conductive Low-k Dielectric (ONR (sub-project), 2023-present)

    Objective: Investigate the potential of a-BCN as a low-dielectric constant (low-k), high-thermal-conductivity (κ) interlayer dielectric for thermal management in high-performance Si CMOS and 3D integration. Map process–property relationships and optimize films to achieve properties superior to SiO2 (k < 4; κ > 1 W/m·K; and adequate electrical and mechanical properties).

    Accomplishments: Forthcoming.

    Area-Selective Deposition: Novel Crosslinkable SAM Blocking Layers & Alternative ALD/MLD Chemistries for Enhanced Selectivities (Intel Corp, 2022-2025)

    Objective: Expand palette of ASD dielectric on dielectric high-k and low-k processes as well as demonstrate improved selectivities and processing ranges (e.g., temperature) by implementing novel ASD molecular layer deposition (MLD) processes combined with novel self-assembled monolayer blocking layers.

    Accomplishment: Demonstrated new SAM blocking layers with improvement over SOTA.

    DMREF Collaborative Research: Predictive Modeling of Polymer-Derived Ceramics: Discovering Methods for the Design and Fabrication of Complex Disordered Solids (NSF, 2017-2021)

    Objective: Develop a general methodology which uses experimental growth and characterization data to drive the creation and verification of structural models. Use machine learning to establish a predictive link between structure/property simulation and synthesis parameters. Investigate the application of topological constraint theory to the optimization of disordered solids.

    Accomplishments: We fabricated and characterized families of a-SiCN:H and a-BC:H thin films from custom and commercial precursors, quantified medium-range order via 4D-STEM, and advanced reactive MD and hybrid reverse Monte Carlo modeling platforms (including ML-derived interatomic potentials and contributions to the public HRMC code) to link synthesis conditions with the atomic structure of complex disordered solids.
