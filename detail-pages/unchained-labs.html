    Introduction

    Unchained Labs has developed Stuntman, a native AI-driven, fundamentally flexible automation platform designed to accelerate discovery, development, and scale-up of next-generation energy materials. With natural-language AI at its core, Stuntman translates research intent into intelligent experiment design, adaptive optimization, and closed-loop workflows—shortening the path from hypothesis to validated results. 

    https://www.unchainedlabs.com/stuntman/

    Its modular architecture enables rapid deployment of custom workflows across synthesis, processing, characterization, and data feedback, creating a unified system for batteries, catalysis, and advanced functional materials.

    Unchained Labs is a U.S.-based company committed to strengthening domestic innovation and manufacturing. We actively collaborate with DOE national labs, demonstrating our ability to deliver automation in complex, mission-driven environments.

    Stuntman aligns strongly with the Genesis Mission’s goals to accelerate materials innovation and U.S. competitiveness. Key applications include AI-driven materials discovery, rapid iteration of new chemistries, closed-loop experimentation, and bridging lab-scale research to scalable manufacturing.

    We welcome the opportunity to support Genesis Mission objectives and contribute to DOE-led energy materials initiatives.

    Relevant Projects

    1. PNNL Energy Storage Materials Initiative (ESMI). Material Innovation through Robotics & AI Laboratory (MIRAL) uses Unchained Labs automated systems

    https://www.pnnl.gov/projects/energy-storage-materials-initiative-esmi/capabilities

    Combining artificial intelligence and lab automation for high-throughput experimentation

    The Energy Storage researchers at PNNL have turbocharged their materials discovery research with the Material Innovation through Robotics & AI Laboratory (MIRAL). Anchored by two AI-guided High-Throughput Experimentation (HTE) platforms, capable of handling both aqueous and non-aqueous electrolyte studies, MIRAL offers automated materials synthesis, screening, formulation, characterization, and optimization capabilities for large scale data generation.

    Part robot, part workstation, part intelligent database, MIRAL functions autonomously using integrated machine learning algorithms and advanced software control, enabling researchers to accomplish tasks within a day that used to take weeks or even months. ESMI’s researchers can now perform hundreds of experiments per day all while maintaining a bird’s-eye view of results by using physics-informed data sets to improve experimental design and eliminate errors.

     

    2. ANL RAPID-200: Autonomous Lab for Materials and Chemistry uses Unchained Labs equipment

    https://www.anl.gov/autonomous-discovery/rapid200-autonomous-lab-for-materials-and-chemistry

    The RAPID-200: Autonomous Lab for Materials and Chemistry at Argonne National Laboratory is a cutting-edge facility designed to transform how scientists approach chemistry and materials research. By combining robotics, artificial intelligence (AI) and advanced experimental tools, this lab enables researchers to solve complex problems faster and more efficiently than ever before. 

    Imagine a robotic arm seamlessly connecting experimental stations, synthesizing new materials and analyzing their properties — all while AI systems learn and adapt to optimize the process. This approach accelerates discovery, reduces costs and opens the door to breakthroughs that would be impossible with traditional methods.

     

    3. ANL Accelerated Discovery Laboratory uses Unchained Labs automated systems

    https://www.anl.gov/cse/accelerated-discovery-laboratory

    Argonne’s Accelerated Discovery Laboratory (ADL) utilizes highly automated and parallel approaches for applying high-throughput and combinatorial experimental methodologies to accelerate the development of new materials.

    Argonne’s ADL allows scientists to synthesize and screen large numbers of compounds, as well as optimize reaction or process conditions much more rapidly than possible with conventional approaches. This ultimately reduces the time and cost involved in materials development and optimization.
