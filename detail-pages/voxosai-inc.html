Relevant Projects

1. ELI: Adaptive Scientific Paper Engine (2025-present) - https://eli.voxos.ai

AI-powered platform that renders scientific papers at 7 comprehension levels, from intuitive visual summaries to PhD-depth analysis. Users adjust complexity in real time via swipe interface. The system fetches and parses papers from arXiv, PubMed, bioRxiv, and medRxiv, then generates section-by-section explanations calibrated to each level using large language models. Built for cross-disciplinary STEM education: enables non-specialists to engage with research outside their domain without losing fidelity. Production stack: React frontend, Python/Flask API, AWS Lambda, CloudFront CDN, DynamoDB. Includes vocabulary builder with spaced repetition, reading progress tracking, and community paper request board.

2. The Jobs Index: AI Labor Market Intelligence (2025-present) - https://jobs.voxos.ai

Quantitative platform tracking AI automation risk across 694+ U.S. occupations using Bureau of Labor Statistics data and ML-derived risk scoring. Features include per-occupation AI displacement probability, prediction markets for workforce impact, weekly news digests, and monthly analytical reports. Data pipeline ingests BLS employment statistics, O*NET task descriptions, and real-time news, then applies ML models to generate composite risk scores. Serves policymakers, researchers, and workforce development professionals with real-time visibility into how AI is reshaping the U.S. labor market. Production stack: React frontend, Python API, DynamoDB, Lambda, S3, CloudFront.


3. Human-in-the-Loop Task Infrastructure (2025-present)

API-first platform where AI agents submit structured human review tasks, matched to qualified workers with Stripe-backed escrow payments. Designed for workflows where AI accelerates discovery but human judgment remains essential: regulatory review, cross-disciplinary validation, safety-critical approvals. Full REST API with batch operations, worker qualification matching, webhook delivery, dispute resolution, and audit trails. Production stack: Python/Flask API, AWS Lambda, DynamoDB, Stripe, CloudFront. SOC 2 compliant.

Project Idea Summary

We are building an AI-powered scientific knowledge accessibility platform that addresses a core bottleneck in cross-disciplinary research: specialists who need to read, evaluate, and act on work outside their domain.

Our platform renders scientific papers at multiple comprehension levels in real time, from intuitive summaries to full technical depth, enabling a policy analyst and a domain researcher to engage with the same paper at their respective levels without losing fidelity. The system currently integrates with arXiv, PubMed, bioRxiv, and medRxiv.

We see direct application to the "AI for Scientific Reasoning" challenge. When 17 National Labs and hundreds of partner organizations collaborate on shared problems, the constraint is often comprehension, not compute. Our tools help researchers reason about science across disciplinary boundaries.

Additionally, our labor market intelligence platform (tracking AI automation risk across 694+ U.S. occupations using BLS data) provides a model for how AI can synthesize large federal datasets into actionable analysis for policymakers.

We are seeking a National Lab or academic partner with: (1) domain scientists who work across disciplines and can validate whether our adaptive comprehension approach improves cross-team knowledge transfer, (2) access to internal research corpora beyond public preprint servers for pilot integration, and (3) experience navigating DOE funding and compliance requirements.
