    Introduction

    Yunzhu Li is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Columbia University. His research lies at the intersection of robotics, computer vision, and machine learning. He focuses on robot learning, with the goal of significantly expanding robots’ perception and physical interaction capabilities. His work is organized around three main directions: foundation robotics models, structured world models and digital twins, and multimodal sensing.

    Before joining Columbia, he was an Assistant Professor at UIUC CS and spent time as a Postdoc at Stanford, collaborating with Fei-Fei Li and Jiajun Wu. Yunzhu earned his PhD from MIT under the guidance of Antonio Torralba and Russ Tedrake.

    His work has been recognized with the Best Paper Award at ICRA, the Best Systems Paper Award, and as a Finalist for the Best Paper Award at CoRL. He is also a recipient of the AAAI New Faculty Highlights, the Sony Faculty Innovation Award, the Amazon Research Award, the Adobe Research Fellowship, and the First Place Ernst A. Guillemin Master’s Thesis Award in AI and Decision Making at MIT. His research has been published in top journals and conferences, including Nature and Science, and featured by major media outlets such as CNN, BBC, and The Wall Street Journal.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    AI models, tools, or software

    We develop foundation models for robotics, including action-conditioned world models, multimodal policies, and simulation platforms for long-horizon prediction and control. Our systems enable scalable policy learning, evaluation, and deployment via real-to-sim-to-real pipelines, supporting tasks such as dexterous manipulation, contact-rich interaction, and embodied reasoning.

    AI/ML/Data science expertise

    Expertise in robot learning, computer vision, and machine learning, with a focus on structured world models, imitation learning, and multimodal representation learning. We design learning systems that integrate physical priors with data-driven models to enable robust perception, planning, and control under distribution shift, particularly for long-horizon and contact-rich tasks.

    Subject matter expertise

    Domain expertise in robotic manipulation, embodied AI, and physical interaction. We study how robots perceive, model, and interact with complex environments, including deformable objects and human-centered settings. Our work emphasizes scalable evaluation, sim-to-real transfer, and system-level integration for real-world deployment.

    Other expertise

    Digital twins and simulation for scalable evaluation; visuo-tactile sensing systems; real-time video prediction; shared autonomy and human-in-the-loop learning; benchmarking and validation of robotic systems.

    Other resources/tools

    Custom robotic platforms for manipulation (single-arm and bimanual), tactile sensing systems, large-scale multimodal datasets (vision + action + touch), and GPU-based infrastructure for training and simulation.

    Relevant Projects

    1. Interactive World Simulator for Scalable Robot Learning

    Developed an action-conditioned world model enabling long-horizon video prediction (10+ minutes at ~15 Hz) from real-world interaction data. The system supports policy training and evaluation in a simulated environment with strong correlation to real-world performance. Demonstrated that policies trained on simulator-generated data can match those trained on real data, enabling scalable evaluation and reducing reliance on costly real-world trials.

    2. Visuo-Tactile Sensing for Fine-Grained Manipulation

    Designed scalable tactile sensing systems integrated with vision for precise manipulation. Developed learning frameworks that fuse multimodal sensory inputs to enable tasks requiring fine contact understanding (e.g., alignment, insertion). Demonstrated improved robustness and precision in real-world manipulation, with applications to deformable objects and human-interactive environments.

    3. Real-to-Sim-to-Real Learning and Digital Twins

    Built pipelines for reconstructing real-world environments into digital twins for scalable policy evaluation and training. Focused on maintaining visual and physical consistency to enable reliable sim-to-real transfer. Applied to industrial and logistics scenarios, enabling systematic benchmarking across large configuration spaces and accelerating iteration cycles for robotic systems.

    Project Idea Summary

    We aim to develop general-purpose embodied AI systems that integrate perception, reasoning, and physical interaction across key domains, including advanced manufacturing, biotechnology, data centers, and autonomous laboratories.

    A central challenge is enabling AI systems to operate robustly in complex, real-world environments involving long-horizon processes, partial observability, and safety constraints. Our approach combines structured world models (digital twins), multimodal sensing, and foundation models for decision-making, enabling scalable training and evaluation through real-to-sim-to-real pipelines.

    In manufacturing, this enables adaptive automation and improved productivity; in biotechnology, scalable experimentation and lab automation; in data centers, intelligent monitoring and optimization; and in autonomous labs, closed-loop scientific discovery.

    We are seeking partners with complementary strengths in (1) domain-specific systems and datasets, (2) large-scale compute and infrastructure, and (3) real-world testbeds for deployment and validation. Collaborations that bridge AI advances with operational environments will be critical for unlocking reliable, scalable, and high-impact intelligent systems.
