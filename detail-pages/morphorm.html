Introduction

Morphorm LLC is an Albuquerque-based deep-tech company developing physics-informed AI software for the simulation and optimization of energy and defense-critical devices. Built by a team of scientists and engineers whose careers span Sandia National Laboratories, Morphorm combines HPC-native device simulation with structure-preserving ML to compress design timelines from years to months.

Morphorm's GPU-accelerated simulation framework enables large-scale, optimization-driven design under coupled multiphysics (carrier transport, electrical, thermal, and mechanical), directly aligned with Genesis Mission's pillars of Accelerating Advanced Manufacturing and Discovering Mission-Ready Materials. Designed for Genesis Mission's integrated supercomputing infrastructure, the framework unlocks capabilities beyond the reach of conventional commercial tools.

Built on this simulation engine, Morphorm's structure-preserving ML framework constructs surrogates that preserve physical laws to machine precision, enabling design-space exploration orders of magnitude faster than full simulation under uncertain inputs, with risk-tolerant device designs as the outcome. Unlike conventional data-driven models, these surrogates encode governing physics directly, making AI a reliable reasoning partner, not a black box.

Supported by a DOE SBIR Phase II award and a CRADA with Sandia National Laboratories, Morphorm is ready to deliver physics-grounded AI tools built for the speed of national need.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Morphorm develops two tightly integrated software frameworks. The first is a GPU-accelerated, HPC-native device simulator built for large-scale, PDE-constrained optimization under coupled multiphysics. The second is a structure-preserving ML framework that constructs physics-informed surrogates guaranteed to preserve mathematical structure and physical laws to machine precision. Together, they enable design-space exploration orders of magnitude faster than full simulation under uncertain inputs, delivering risk-tolerant device designs without sacrificing physical accuracy.

Subject matter expertise

Morphorm's subject matter expertise spans computational physics, PDE-constrained optimization, and physics-informed machine learning. The team brings deep experience in high-performance computing, multiphysics device simulation, topology and shape optimization, design under uncertainty, and surrogate modeling. This expertise is applied to the co-design of coupled electrical, thermal, and mechanical systems in energy and defense-critical devices, with a foundation built across careers at Sandia National Laboratories and sustained through active partnerships with DOE, DARPA, and DoW.

Relevant Projects

DOE SBIR Phase II: Optimization-Based Design Ecosystem for Photovoltaic Modules (2024-present, $1,200,000, Department of Energy)

Scope of Work: Development of a physics-informed, optimization-based design ecosystem targeting performance, reliability, and stability of photovoltaic modules. The platform combines GPU-accelerated multiphysics simulation with structure-preserving AI surrogates to enable large-scale design-space exploration under uncertain material properties, loading conditions, and operating environments. The framework couples electrical, thermal, and mechanical physics within a unified PDE-constrained optimization architecture, enabling risk-tolerant module designs that account for real-world variability.

Achievements: Delivered a GPU-accelerated simulation framework for coupled multiphysics analysis of photovoltaic devices. Demonstrated optimization-driven design workflows that compress design exploration timelines significantly compared to conventional simulation approaches. Established the technical foundation for structure-preserving AI surrogate integration, currently under active development as the next phase of the platform.

Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA): Multi-Physics Semiconductor Device Simulator (2024-2026, Sandia National Laboratories)

Scope of Work: Joint development of commercial simulation software for semiconductor device design in partnership with Sandia National Laboratories, built on Sandia's open-source Plato platform. Morphorm is bridging the gap between advanced national laboratory R&D and practical industrial application, developing HPC-native tools for coupled multiphysics optimization of semiconductor devices targeting solar energy and defense applications.

Achievements: Established an active partnership with Sandia, integrating Morphorm simulation capabilities with national laboratory expertise and computational resources. Software trial version is on track for release by end of 2026, ahead of original schedule. Recognized by Sandia as a CRADA success story, with the partnership featured on the Sandia National Laboratories website as a model for technology transfer from national laboratory to commercial industry.

Technology Readiness Gross Receipts (TRGR) Project: Simulation-Driven Design Algorithms for Semiconductor Devices (2024-2025, $150,000, New Mexico Economic Development Department)

Scope of Work: Collaborative project with Sandia National Laboratories to advance the commercial maturity of Morphorm's simulation software, expanding capabilities for the semiconductor, sustainable energy, and defense industries. The project focused on developing and validating high-fidelity 3D multiphysics simulation capabilities for semiconductor device design.

Achievements: Delivered coupled electrical, thermal, and mechanical multiphysics simulation capability in three dimensions. Completed a specialized semiconductor simulation solver for characterizing the physical behavior of solar cells and modules. Currently validating the software with real-world data from a solar cell manufacturer, and formalizing a follow-on project with a U.S. original equipment manufacturer in the defense industry. Project outcomes featured in the TRGR FY2025 Progress Report.

Morphorm seeks access to cloud HPC, GPU clusters, and DOE supercomputing resources such as Frontier and Aurora to advance two priority objectives. Morphorm seeks partnership to access device characterization and experimental validation data, as well as simulation datasets from national laboratory campaigns, for mission-critical semiconductor problems spanning energy and defense applications. These datasets serve two purposes: validating and benchmarking Morphorm's coupled multiphysics simulation framework against real-world device behavior, and training structure-preserving AI surrogates on high-fidelity physics data. Access to national laboratory device data would accelerate both the simulation and AI layers of Morphorm's platform.

Project Idea Summary

Challenge Focus Area: Risk-Tolerant Design of Energy and Defense-Critical Semiconductor Materials via Simulation-Driven Design Optimization

Designing next-generation semiconductor devices, including wide-bandgap materials such as GaN and Ga2O3, for energy and defense requires navigating a coupled design space spanning electrical, thermal, and mechanical physics. Conventional approaches produce components optimized for nominal conditions, leaving devices vulnerable under operational conditions where reliability requirements for energy systems and defense platforms are most demanding.

Morphorm is developing a physics-informed platform combining GPU-accelerated, HPC-native multiphysics device simulation with PDE-constrained optimization to accelerate discovery and design of mission-ready semiconductor materials. The platform explores design space under uncertain inputs, yielding risk-tolerant designs grounded in real-world variability, directly addressing Genesis Mission priorities in Accelerating Advanced Manufacturing and Discovering Mission-Ready Materials.

We seek collaboration with national laboratories possessing device physics experimental capability and industry partners with real-world device validation data. Partners gain access to Morphorm's simulation and optimization platform and physics-grounded AI design tools, while contributing device characterization datasets and experimental results that validate our framework and accelerate AI surrogate integration.
