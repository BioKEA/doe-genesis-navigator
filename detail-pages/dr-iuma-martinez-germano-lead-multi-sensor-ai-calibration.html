Introduction

Strategic Technical Lead and Renaissance Philanthropy Fellow bridging the gap between sophisticated AI architectures and subsurface reality. I am currently managing a portfolio of U.S.-based research tracks including NASA Ames (Advanced Geosensing), Texas Tech (Geologic Hydrogen), and the Battelle-Tsodilo AI collaboration.

I specialize in Multi-Fidelity Modeling and Bottleneck Diagnosis, leveraging high-fidelity 'truth sets' (100km+ of drill core and 30k+ assays) to ensure AI-driven mineral targeting remains physically meaningful and operationally defensible under real-world geological uncertainty. My goal for the Genesis Mission is to serve as a Strategic Subject Matter Expert (SME) for National Lab-led consortiums seeking to ground foundation models in geological and mineralogical reality.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI/ML/Data science expertise

Strategic Advisory in Physics-Informed AI. I provide high-level guidance on designing "Strategic Guides" for foundation models to ensure AI architectures are constrained by geological reality. My focus is collaborating on the design of Cascaded Calibration—using high-fidelity ground truth to validate models that interpret lower-fidelity data. This advisory role supports bridging raw sensing data to decision-ready outputs in collaboration with Battelle and USC.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Technical Liaison for High-Fidelity Subsurface Datasets. I facilitate technical interchange using over 100,000m of diamond drill core and 30,000+ geochemical assays (via Tsodilo Resources). My role is to provide the strategic context for how these datasets can be used to calibrate "Geologist-in-the-Loop" scientific AI models for Critical Minerals (CMM) and REE recovery.

Subject matter expertise

Mineral Systems Integration and Subsurface Targeting. I provide deep domain expertise in the mineralogical and geochemical signatures of unconventional mineral sources, including mine tailings. I collaborate in an advisory capacity with NASA Ames (Advanced Geosensing) and support research tracks in Geologic Hydrogen (Texas Tech). My focus is identifying the "bottlenecks" in subsurface interpretation and advising on the multi-sensor workflows to clear them.

Other expertise

Technical Strategy for Multi-Sensor Integration. Advisory expertise in the credible adaptation of multistatic ultra-wideband GPR (mUWB-GPR) and hyperspectrally calibrated modular microsensing systems (MMS). I focus on the discipline of calibration logic and "fitness-for-purpose" output design, ensuring sensing architectures remain trustworthy for downstream decisions in "no-entry" or high-constraint environments. These approaches facilitate scalability, surveying, and targeting cost reduction while supporting the path toward automation across the mining cycle.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: Geologic Hydrogen Strategic Exploration & ARPA-E Liaison:

Scope: Served as Principal Investigator for Rio Tinto on the ARPA-E Geologic Hydrogen project (DE-FOA-0002784). Prioritized global operations for H₂ prospectivity and provided North American pilot sites/samples for stimulation experiments. Coordinated a technical network including Texas Tech, NREL, and LBNL.

Achievements: Facilitated strategy to secure $1.6M in ARPA-E funding. Identified key lithologies (ultramafics, BIFs) and developed a correlation model using FeO, Fe₂O₃, and TiO₂ to estimate missing Fe²⁺ values, expanding the targeting database by orders of magnitude. Managed the distribution of 200+ kg of drill core across seven international labs, ensuring program success and bridging fundamental science with industrial engagement.

 

Project 2: Global Mining Land Use & Deforestation Impact Mapping:

Scope: Conducted a global geospatial project using Descartes Labs AI/ML software and Sentinel-2 FBEC products to quantify the land-use footprint of large-scale mining relative to global agriculture.

Achievements: Co-developed an automated Python methodology that accelerated mapping to 2–3 months (tasks typically taking years). Mapped barren land proximal to mining using continental drill hole and activity data for Australia and the Americas. Findings highlighted that large-scale mining has minimal impact on global deforestation compared to animal-derived food agriculture, providing critical data for ESG strategy.

 

Project 3: Cost-Effective Satellite-Based Stockpile Volumetric Monitoring:

Scope: Developed a non-traditional, cost-effective volumetric change detection method for mine stockpiles without ground control. Identified MAXAR WorldView as the optimal solution for a POC across five Pilbara mines.

Achievements: Demonstrated that a mono-optical geometric approach using 30cm MAXAR imagery approximates volumes within 5–15% of LiDAR benchmarks at 75–90% lower cost. Established a calibration baseline by coordinating synchronous data capture (UAV/LiDAR) and illustrated how linking volumetric changes to market prices serves as an early indicator of demand shifts.

Strategic Tech Transfer:
Building on these industrial tracks, my current research focuses on transferring planetary subsurface sensing technologies to terrestrial mining, specifically to solve challenges in tailings safety and operational resource mapping.

 

Project 4: Multistatic UAV-GPR for Tailings Characterization:

Scope: Lead Researcher (Self-Directed/Advisory, 2024–2026) developing calibration logic for a multistatic ultra-wideband GPR (mUWB-GPR) system for high-resolution characterization of tailings storage facilities (TSFs).

Achievements: Authored technical strategy for 3D volumetric models of internal TSF structures to detect failure precursors like moisture saturation. Demonstrated this logic could identify re-minable material—revealing $100M+ in latent value—while providing a scalable pathway toward autonomous, zero-entry monitoring.

 

Project 5: Modular Micro-Sensor (MMS) for Mineral Mapping:

Scope: Lead Researcher (Self-Directed/Advisory, 2024–2026) designing the Modular Micro-Sensor (MMS) framework for hyperspectrally calibrated mineral mapping.

Achievements: Developed logic showing MMS replicates key hyperspectral insights at ~8–34% of traditional costs. Established a scalable pathway for real-time ore discrimination during excavation, providing the "Strategic Guide" to integrate low-cost sensors with AI workflows.

Project Idea Summary

Challenge Focus Area:
This project addresses the Critical Minerals and Materials (CMM) challenge, specifically the CM²US initiative for unlocking mineral supply from mine tailings. The primary bottleneck is the inability of current sensing to differentiate mineralogy in complex subsurface environments at scale.

The Approach:
I am proposing a Multi-Fidelity AI framework that anchors sophisticated architectures (NVIDIA, OpenAI) in geological reality. By utilizing an exclusive, high-density industrial "truth-set" (100km+ curated drill core and 30k+ assays), we can train models using Cascaded Calibration. This integrates planetary technology transfer—specifically multistatic UWB-GPR and modular microsensing (MMS)—to bridge the gap between raw geophysical signals and predictive mineralogical maps.

Partnership Request:
I am seeking National Lab leads and Industry AI partners (e.g., OpenAI, NVIDIA, KNIME) to co-develop a Phase I proposal for the April 28, 2026, deadline. Specifically, I am looking for expertise in AI Foundation Models, Workflow Orchestration, and Computing Infrastructure.

Note: If this specific project idea does not fully resonate with your current mission, I still encourage you to reach out. I am currently leading the strategic AI development for two additional Critical Minerals initiatives (BiTS and Tsodilo AI) that require similar partnerships and offer unique industrial data access.
