Introduction

Infologic is a small consultancy and integration firm powered by AI, dedicated to transforming how organizations discover, develop, and commercialize innovations. We provide comprehensive AI integration services across all Technology Readiness Levels (TRL 1–9) through four key service areas: strategic AI consultancy, technical integration, workforce development, and knowledge-sharing at our specialized AI for R&D conferences. 

Founded by Has Patel, who has an extensive background with Bell Laboratories, GlaxoSmithKline Pharmaceuticals, and BP Chemicals, Infologic benefits from over 15 years of experience supporting U.S. Defense RDT&E organizations. This diverse expertise allows us to bring unique cross-sector knowledge to our AI integration efforts. Our proprietary AI4R&D framework merges AI, innovation, and value-creation strategies to effectively bridge the "Valley of Death" and expedite the transition from foundational research to commercialized solutions.

We are looking to partner with National Laboratories, Academic Institutions, and leading industry applicants under the Genesis Mission.

AI4R&D Framework

The framework integrates AI, innovation, and value-creation strategies to bridge the "Valley of Death" and accelerate the transition from Research (TRL 1) to Commercialization (TRL 9). Examples:

1. Discovery Science: Utilizing AI to automate research workflows and manage information overload in early TRLs (1-3).

2. Advanced Manufacturing: Aligning AI integration with Manufacturing Readiness Levels (MRL 1-10) for a more efficient "design-to-production loop."

3. Biotechnology: Employing the ERAF-AI and BRLs framework to mitigate risks in drug delivery and formulation innovation.

Subject matter expertise

Technology Readiness Levels and Manufacturing Readiness Levels (TRLs / MRLs) Consultancy and Training:

We have successfully developed hybrid courses, including one titled "Successfully Transitioning R&D Projects to Commercial Products." In addition, we have provided TRLs/MRLs training for two U.S. Navy NAVWAR S&T programs based in San Diego, CA. Our team has also created and facilitated TRL/MRL workshops. Furthermore, we have conducted research to enhance the maturity of TRLs/MRLs frameworks and have published articles and papers in various forums and conferences.

Other resources/tools

Multimodal Innovation Model

We should shift from traditional Technology Management, which focuses on product innovation and commercialization, to a multimodal Innovation Management approach. This model includes R&D for tangible products, services, processes, and business models, with innovation categorized as radical, disruptive, evolutionary, or integrative.

Value Creation Model

Value creation in AI4R&D requires a multi-faceted approach to ensure R&D is scientifically valid and commercially viable. Key Performance Indicators include Market Share, ROI, and operational effectiveness.

Relevant Projects

 

Project 1: Workforce Transformation: Scaling Dual-Competency Training for AI-Driven R&D 

Scope of Work: Developed a proprietary AI for R&D training curriculum specifically designed to upskill scientific and engineering personnel in high-stakes, regulated environments. The project addressed the "Dual Competency" gap by combining domain-specific science with AI literacy. It included creating a hybrid course titled "Successfully Transitioning R&D Projects to Commercial Products," focusing on Technology Readiness Level (TRLs) and Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRLs) maturity assessments (stages 4–7). This initiative provided a roadmap for accelerating the transition from lab-scale discovery to industrial-scale engineering.
 

Achievements & Impact:  
•    Federal Impact: Successfully upskilled science and technology personnel at two U.S. Navy NAVWAR S&T programs in San Diego, CA, enhancing the transition of defense RDT&E to commercialization.  
•    Entrepreneurial Support: Delivered TRL/MRL acceleration workshops for the University of California, Irvine (UCI) Incubators, directly supporting over a dozen high-tech startups.

 

Project 2: Federal AI Standards & Manufacturing Ecosystem Integration  


Scope of Work: Served as a Subject Matter Expert (SME) and strategic volunteering contributor to national working groups focused on AI safety, manufacturing readiness, and industrial scaling. This project involved deep collaboration with the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Generative AI Public Working Group (NIST GAI-PWG) and the University of Southern California-managed Advanced Manufacturing Partnership for Southern California (AMP SoCal).

Achievements & Impact:  
•    Standards Development: Contributed to the NIST GAI-PWG, helping to shape national guidelines for the responsible deployment of generative AI in engineering and research sectors.  
•    Regional Competitiveness: Published articles and supported the USC-led Advanced Manufacturing Partnership for Southern California (AMP SoCal) network to enhance regional industrial growth.

 

Project 3: The AI4R&D™ Framework: Bridging the TRL 4–7 "Valley of Death"  
 

Scope of Work: Conducted comprehensive methodology development and sector-specific research to address the global R&D productivity crisis, often referred to as Eroom’s Law. This project codified the AI4R&D™ Methodology, a framework that leverages multimodal AI orchestration to reduce time-to-market for breakthroughs in Biotech, Defense, and Semiconductors. The scope involved mapping AI capabilities across the TRL 1–9 spectrum, with a specific focus on the "Valley of Death" (TRL 4–6), where most academic and laboratory research struggles to achieve commercial validation.

Achievements & Impact:  
•    Strategic Blueprint: Published a high-level roadmap for 2026 outlining how AI-integrated research and development (R&D) can unlock $560 billion in global economic value.  
•    Validated Case Studies: Presented case studies on AI4R&D in biotech at the IEEE Global Emerging Technologies Conference in 2025, and in defense at the Aerospace Defense Forum in 2025, demonstrating significant reductions in research-to-validation cycles.  
•    Entrepreneurial Support: Mentored students and evaluated their business plans at academic incubators using the AI4R&D methodology. Additionally, presented a paper titled "Accelerating Innovation from Lab to Market" at the Pismo Global Startup Symposium in 2024.

Project Idea Summary

 

Project Idea: The AI4R&D Consortium (AIRC)

The project is a dual-track initiative designed to bridge the TRL 4–6 "Valley of Death" while closing the national "Dual Competency" talent gap. The project uses a proprietary AI orchestration framework (AI4R&D) to automate maturity assessments and translate lab discoveries into engineering reality. In parallel, the workforce track deploys a scalable, hybrid curriculum to train scientists and engineers in AI-integrated R&D workflows. 

Challenge Focus Area:

Accelerating AI-driven discovery into engineering reality (TRL 4–6) and mobilizing a "Dual Competency" workforce. The project addresses the systemic decline in R&D productivity by standardizing AI Engineering disciplines within high-stakes, regulated sectors.

What is Specifically Sought in a Potential Partner:

Infologic is a small business seeking to join a team planning to develop a similar or complementary project. In addition, we seek Academic & Lab Partners (TRL 1–3) for AI-for-Science expertise; Educational Partners (Universities/Community Colleges) to jointly develop and scale the "Dual Competency" curriculum; and Industrial Partners (TRL 7–9) for real-world validation.
