    Introduction

    The Statistics Online Computational Resource (SOCR) designs, validates and freely disseminates knowledge. SOCR invents, implements, confirms, and shares cutting-edge tools and end-to-end computational protocols for study designs, mathematical modeling, probability inference, statistical computing, and artificial intelligence.

    Specifically, SOCR develops a novel theoretical foundation to extend the notion of time to the complex plane. This approach lifts the concept of time from a positive real number, representing event ordering, to a 2D complex-time (kime) field comprising a pair of coordinates, time (t) and phase (φ). This enables powerful data-driven analytical strategies for representation, modeling, simulation, and AI analytics of large-dimensional repeated measurement longitudinal data. The 5D spacekime analytics bridge between theoretical mathematical-physics, computational statistical inference, and experimental generative AI modeling (GAIM).

    Kime representation of classical observable time-processes as kime-surfaces (manifolds) provides mechanisms to model, sample from, and learn the latent space, which is proxy-measured by the observed time-series data. Space-kime analytics supports AI prediction, model-free inference, and uncertainty-quantification based on the information-rich kime-surface models, reconstructed by kime-phase tomography. See www.spacekime.org.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    AI models, tools, or software

    SOCR foundational and generative AI models (GAIMs) provide examples of designing, validating, deploying and servicing artificial and augmented intelligence models, see https://socr.umich.edu/GAIM/. 

    AI/ML/Data science expertise

    The SOCR DSPA2 framework (https://dspa2.predictive.space) includes a wide range of end-to-end data wrangling, modeling, harmonization, aggregation, pre-processing, visualization and analytical protocols supporting open study-designs, reproducible discoveries, and collaborative scientific analytics.

    Subject matter expertise

    SOCR has developed advanced techniques for computationally-tractable mathematical representations of high-dimensional, time-varying, incomplete, heterogeneous, and incongruent data-sources. One example includes complex-time (kime) representation of repeated-measurement longitudinal time-curves (observed time-series) as information-rich manifolds (kime-surfaces). The latter support advanced AI modeling and analytics which decompose the observed variability into domain-space (intrinsic kime-phase) variation and an independent range-space (extrinsic, classical) dispersion.

    Statistical consulting.

    Other expertise

    SOCR has designed, validated, deployed and openly-shared the first AI Statistical Consulting Agent (https://sda.statisticalcomputing.org/AIConsultant), which provides on-demand support on a wide range of study designs, model-based statistical analysis, model-free AI classification, regression, and forecasting.

    SOCR provides advanced consulting on study-design, mathematical modeling, statistical computing, data science, scientific visualization, and program evaluation.

    Relevant Projects

     (1) SOCR Generative AI Models (GAIMs)

    Foundational Model Development & Ecosystem: Development of SOCR’s foundational generative AI models, leveraging transfer learning and transformer architectures with parameters ranging from 1 to 100 billion. The focus was on building reliable, scalable, and trustworthy AI predictors and classifiers to support responsible decision-making across various domains.Successfully helped establish a core suite of applications, including the Augmented Intelligence Agent (AIA) and the Michigan Intelligent Teaching Assistant (MITA), demonstrating the versatility of the foundational models in enhancing both analytics and educational experiences.

    Healthcare & Synthetic Data Applications: Focused on the application of GAIMs within healthcare, involving the development of the Virtual Hospital platform and the EHR-Sim Digital Twin Simulator. Work included implementing the DataSifter technology for synthetic patient generation, statistical obfuscation, and data augmentation to balance privacy risks with data utility. Successfully deployed the Healthcare & Nursing AI Trainer (NAIT) and the Synthetic Brain Generator, creating specialized tools for clinical education and medical imaging analysis. The work enabled the generation of complex, realistic datasets for training predictive models like tumor detection without compromising patient confidentiality.

    Interactive Tools & Educational Platforms: Designed and integrated AI-powered educational tools, including the Interactive Learning Platform (ILP) and specialized AI tutors for university courses (UMich HS650, ENG255-499). This involved translating natural language commands into analytical code and providing interactive learning experiences. Launched the SOCR AI Bot, which leverages GAIMs to translate natural language into R code, perform data analysis, and generate reports. This project successfully bridged the gap between advanced AI capabilities and practical, accessible educational and analytical tools for students and researchers.

    (2) SOCR Complex-time (kime) Representation and the Induced Space-Kime Analytics

    Theoretical Framework & Methodology Development: Foundational research and development of the complex-time (kime) representation, a novel mathematical framework for analyzing repeatedly sampled time-series data. This involved formalizing the transformation of conventional time-series into complex-valued surfaces to enable more sophisticated analytics. Introduced the theoretical foundation of space-kime analytics, establishing a new paradigm for longitudinal data analysis. This work provided a rigorous mathematical basis for subsequent tool development, demonstrating how complex-time representations can reveal hidden structures not easily captured by traditional time-domain methods.

    (3) SOCR Statistical AI Consulting Agent

    Advanced Consult Mode Architecture & Implementation: Spearheaded the design and implementation of the "Advanced Consult" mode for the SOCR Statistical AI Consulting Agent. This involved architecting an 8-section structured response format to emulate a professional biostatistical consultation, with specialized knowledge in clinical trials, epidemiology, bioinformatics, and regulatory compliance. Successfully deployed the Advanced Consult mode, which automatically configures generation parameters (temperature: 0.5, max tokens: 2048) to optimize for precision. Transformed the agent from a general AI into a specialized, expert-level biostatistician.

    Project Idea Summary

    SOCR is looking for National Lab experimental team, theoretical physicists partners, and groups interested in advanced complex0time representation of observable processes that can be used to motivate, validate or falsify kime-phase tomography (KPT) methods for recovering the unobservable phase law and the kime-surface reconstruction from observed time-course data.

    In addition, SOCR can provide data-science expertise to manage heterogeneous, incongruent, time-varying measurements, support advanced scientific visualization needs, and mathematical-statistics consulting pertaining to AI system design, training, and deployment.
