Introduction

Stratos Group develops SAIF, a runtime governance system that enables autonomous Al systems to operate safely and reliably in real-world environments. SAIF governs system behavior at runtime through deterministic safety constraints while generating fully auditable execution traces, enabling safe deployment of Al in high-risk, high-consequence environments.

SAIF governs system behavior at the execution layer, not just individual action. Rather than post-hoc monitoring or documentation-based compliance, SAIF actively governs system behavior during operation through integrated runtime systems for auditability, risk enforcement, deterministic recovery, and resource control.

The system has been operating in live autonomous production environments for over 200 days with zero safety incidents across multiple concurrent tenants. SAIF is designed to integrate directly into Al-driven research and experimental worktlows as a modular control plane, providing oversight, validation, and constraint enforcement across distributed systems without requiring modification to underlying models or workflows.

This aligns directly with efforts to enable Al-driven autonomous laboratories and secure, auditable scientific workflows. We are seeking teams developing Al-enabled systems where governance, safety, and controllability are required for deployment at scale. SAIF serves as the runtime governance system that ensures autonomous systems operate within defined, verifiable boundaries over time.

Relevant Projects

1. SAIF Autonomous System Deployment (Production Environment)

Deployed SAIF as a real-time control and governance layer operating directly in the execution path of live autonomous systems. The system enforces deterministic safety constraints, enables real-time intervention, and generates fully auditable traces for all actions.

Over 200+ days of continuous operation, SAIF has maintained zero safety incidents while supporting reliable autonomous execution across concurrent workloads. This demonstrates readiness for integration into high-risk, AI-driven research and experimental environments where control and auditability are critical.

This deployment model is directly applicable to AI-driven scientific infrastructure, including autonomous laboratories, HPC-integrated workflows, and experimental systems requiring deterministic control and verifiable execution.

2. Distributed AI Workflow Oversight and Control

Implemented SAIF across distributed AI-driven workflows as a runtime control layer governing multi-step and multi-agent systems. SAIF monitors execution state, validates actions, enforces constraints, and enables real-time intervention to maintain alignment with safety and operational parameters.

This allows complex autonomous workflows to operate reliably in dynamic environments while preserving control authority, traceability, and system integrity at scale.

This approach aligns with AI-enabled scientific workflows where multi-agent systems must operate under strict safety, validation, and reproducibility constraints in both simulated and real-world environments.

3. Modular AI Governance Layer Integration

Designed SAIF as a modular execution-layer control system that integrates into existing AI pipelines without requiring system redesign. The architecture operates within the execution loop, enabling real-time intervention, constraint enforcement, deterministic recovery, and full auditability across diverse AI applications.

This enables organizations to deploy advanced autonomous capabilities while retaining continuous control, compliance, and operational visibility in production environments.

The modular design supports integration into research, experimental, and HPC-based systems, providing a control layer that enables the transition from experimental AI workflows to reliable, production-grade autonomous infrastructure.

Project Idea Summary

We are seeking to partner with teams developing or operating AI-driven autonomous laboratories, experimental pipelines, or complex scientific workflows where systems are expected to operate with limited or no human intervention.

As these systems move from experimentation to real-world operation, the core challenge is not model performance but control. Most AI systems today lack a runtime layer that can enforce deterministic constraints, maintain system integrity, and provide full auditability during execution.

Stratos Group develops SAIF, a real-time control and governance layer that operates directly in the execution path of AI-driven systems. Rather than relying on policy guidance or post-hoc monitoring, SAIF actively governs system behavior at runtime by enforcing constraints, validating actions, and generating auditable execution traces.

SAIF is already operating in live autonomous production environments, where it has run continuously for over 200 days with zero safety incidents. It integrates with existing AI systems and experimental workflows without requiring changes to underlying models.

We are looking to collaborate with teams building AI-enabled research and operational systems where reliability, safety, and controllability are required for deployment at scale. SAIF serves as the runtime control layer within these systems, governing execution in real time and ensuring autonomous workflows operate within defined, verifiable constraints.
