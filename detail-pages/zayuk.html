Introduction

Zayuk is a San Diego-based, PE-led technology company building AI-native inspection, asset management, and contract verification platforms for critical infrastructure. We are applying to DE-FOA-0003612 under Focus Area 16-B (Grid Operations Optimization) with 11-C as a secondary focus.

Our production platform combines drone photogrammetry and 3D point cloud analysis with multimodal AI to autonomously detect infrastructure defects, generate work orders, and trigger smart contract-based vendor payments upon verified completion. This closed-loop pipeline — 

drone captures asset → AI classifies anomaly → immutable ledger records findings → smart contract releases payment on verified milestone 

— is deployed today in property and facility management and is directly portable to grid infrastructure inspection and maintenance workflows.

We bring three differentiated capabilities to a Genesis Mission team: 

(1) a working multimodal AI vision pipeline trained on real inspection data

(2) an immutable ledger with cryptographic proof-of-work for maintenance attestation and supply chain verification

(3) a California PE (#24471, Electrical Engineering – Power) leading the technical program

Project Idea Summary

Zayuk is building an AI-driven grid infrastructure inspection and operations optimization platform targeting Focus Area 16-B (Grid Operations Optimization), with 11-C (AI-Driven Autonomous Laboratories) as a secondary focus, under DE-FOA-0003612.

Our approach: drone-captured grid asset imagery feeds a multimodal AI pipeline (deployed on AWS Bedrock) that autonomously detects anomalies, classifies defect severity, generates work orders, and 
triggers smart contract-based vendor payments upon verified completion — creating a closed-loop, auditable grid maintenance workflow with full data sovereignty retained by the asset owner.

We are seeking two partners:

1. ELECTRICITY SECTOR PARTNER (utility, RTO, or grid operator): We need access to real operational grid data — inspection records, outage history, asset condition logs, or sensor telemetry — to train and validate our AI anomaly detection models on actual grid infrastructure. The partner does not need to provide funding.

2. DOE NATIONAL LAB: We are seeking a lab with grid AI, HPC, or energy systems expertise to serve as our required lab team member. PNNL, NLR, or Argonne would be natural fits given their existing Genesis Mission grid work.

April 28, 2026 deadline. We are moving fast and need partners who can commit to a Letter of Commitment within 10 days.
