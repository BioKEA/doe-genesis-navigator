Introduction

I'm an Assistant Professor at the University of Utah seeking collaborators for AI, digital twin, and critical infrastructure projects aligned with the Genesis Mission. My research focuses on trustworthy AI for cyber-physical systems, including anomaly detection, operator decision support, cyber-physical risk analysis, and semantically grounded AI methods for industrial environments. My group has expertise in OT cybersecurity, industrial control systems, digital twins, and human-in-the-loop evaluation.

We also bring access to the SPHERE CPS Enclave, a reconfigurable research and demonstration environment for water, energy, and industrial process scenarios. SPHERE supports realistic validation using live control components, sensors, actuators, and high-fidelity digital twin workflows. In addition to resilient monitoring and decision support, we are developing agentic approaches for asset mapping and threat mapping, where a partner’s system can be mapped to a SPHERE “surrogate” to support analysis, experimentation, and reusable libraries of threats, tools, and operational scenarios across physical domains.

For Genesis, I am very interested in partnerships related to grid operations optimization and industrial process digitalization, including anomaly detection, situational awareness, operator-transparent recommendations, and secure AI for critical infrastructure. I  welcome collaboration with utilities, industrial operators, national labs, and technology partners in this space.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

We develop AI-enabled tools for cyber-physical monitoring, anomaly detection, operator decision support, and agentic scenario generation in industrial environments. Our software stack includes semantically grounded reasoning workflows, digital-twin-backed analysis, backend-agnostic CPS interfaces, and reusable attack/defense and validation pipelines for industrial control settings.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our expertise includes trustworthy AI for cyber-physical systems, anomaly detection, retrieval-augmented and agentic reasoning, human-in-the-loop evaluation, and physics-aware/semantically grounded AI methods for industrial data and operational contexts. We are particularly interested in AI that supports situational awareness, interpretable recommendations, and resilient operations in safety-critical infrastructure.

Experimental or test facilities

We offer access to the SPHERE CPS Enclave, a reconfigurable testbed for experimentation across water, energy, and industrial process scenarios. SPHERE supports live PLCs, sensors, actuators, power and water use cases, backend mappings, evidence capture, and reproducible validation workflows, making it suitable for development, testing, and demonstration of AI-enabled operational technologies.

Subject matter expertise

We bring subject matter expertise in industrial control systems, OT cybersecurity, cyber-physical resilience, digital twins, and security evaluation for safety-critical infrastructure. Our work spans formal reasoning about CPS behavior, security retrofits for legacy/closed-source systems, and cross-domain reasoning across water, power, and industrial processes.

Other expertise

Additional expertise includes agentic asset mapping, cross-domain threat mapping, surrogate-based experimentation, and operator-centered evaluation. We are developing approaches to map partner systems to SPHERE surrogate environments and associate them with reusable threat, tool, and scenario libraries for analysis, experimentation, and future operational decision support.

Relevant Projects

1. SPHERE – Security and Privacy Heterogeneous Environment for Reproducible Experimentation (NSF Mid-scale RI-1, Co-PI, 2024–present)
Scope of work: Development of a reconfigurable, high-fidelity CPS research and demonstration infrastructure spanning water, energy, and industrial process environments. The platform supports live PLCs, sensors, actuators, backend mappings, evidence capture, and reproducible experimentation for AI, cybersecurity, and resilience research.
Achievements: Helped establish a cross-domain experimentation platform for water, power, and industrial use cases, and advanced capabilities for partner onboarding, surrogate mapping, and agentic asset/threat mapping to support future utility and industrial collaborations. 

2. Formal Verification and Digital Twin Methods for Programmable Logic Controllers (NSF FMitF, PI, 2022–present)
Scope of work: Led research on formal verification, translation, and digital-twin-based reasoning for programmable logic controllers and industrial control systems, with a focus on trustworthy operation, safety, and security.
Achievements: Produced methods and publications including HyPLC and HyTwin, helping connect industrial control software, formal semantics, and digital-twin-based security interventions relevant to trustworthy AI and provable cyber assurance for critical infrastructure. 

3. Cross-Domain Threat Inference and Agentic CPS Security Workflows (Utah-led research efforts, 2024–present)
Scope of work: Developing semantically grounded approaches for mapping assets, threats, and scenarios across industrial domains, with emerging agentic workflows for situational awareness, operator support, and cyber-physical exercise generation on live infrastructure.
Achievements: Demonstrated early results in cross-physical-domain threat inference for ICS defense adaptation and established foundations for agent-based orchestration and surrogate-based asset/threat mapping on SPHERE.

Project Idea Summary

We are seeking partners for projects aligned with Scaling the Grid to Power the American Economy, especially Grid Operations Optimization, with openness to related industrial digitalization efforts. Our project idea is to develop trustworthy, operator-facing AI for critical infrastructure that combines physical process data, OT context, and digital twin models to improve anomaly detection, situational awareness, and operational decision support. Our group brings expertise in cyber-physical systems, OT cybersecurity, digital twins, semantically grounded AI, and high-fidelity validation through the SPHERE CPS Enclave. We are particularly interested in partners who can provide operationally relevant data, grid or industrial domain expertise, and validation or demonstration pathways, as well as collaborators with complementary AI tools or deployment experience.
