Introduction

David I. Inouye | Assistant Professor of ECE, Purdue University

I direct research on Robust Machine Learning, building AI systems that withstand imperfect, real-world conditions to make them safe, reliable, and explainable, particularly in new out-of-distribution (OOD) scenarios.

Core Research Focus:
* Spurious Correlations: Ensuring models are invariant to unstable spurious signals and uncover the stable  causal mechanisms. 
* Imbalanced & Unrepresentative Data: Building resilient algorithms that perform robustly across diverse, edge-case, and high-stakes scenarios.
* Distribution Shifts: Maintaining reliable performance when deployed data drifts from training environments.

Genesis Mission Alignment:
I am actively seeking partnerships with domain experts across the Genesis Mission Consortium. My goal is to apply causally-grounded, robust ML techniques to your specific scientific and energy applications, ensuring our AI-driven breakthroughs are as dependable as they are transformative.

I seek physical science experts facing out-of-distribution (OOD) failures in Scientific ML, particularly cases where measurement is challenging. I need collaborators with datasets exhibiting distribution shifts, spurious correlations, or unrepresentative/biased data. For example:

Fusion plasma disruption across different tokamaks.

Materials spectroscopy with inter-lab reproducibility issues.

Climate edge automation dealing with degrading sensors.

We require access to these domains to apply robust ML methods to isolate true physical processes from localized measurement noise.

Project Idea Summary

Our project addresses the critical challenge of out-of-distribution (OOD) robustness in Scientific Machine Learning (SciML). Standard AI models often latch onto spurious correlations and measurement artifacts, causing them to fail unpredictably when deployed across degrading sensors, different laboratories, or novel physical regimes. To solve this, we are developing a scalable, noise-tolerant framework that addresses this problem in a data-driven way. Our approach systematically forces the model to ignore environmental biases and learn only the universal, underlying physical system. 

We are specifically seeking partners that are rooted in the scientific ML domain who believe out-of-distribution robustness or ability to handle spurious correlations and imbalanced datasets is critical for their application.
