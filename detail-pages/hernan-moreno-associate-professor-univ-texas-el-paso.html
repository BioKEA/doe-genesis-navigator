Introduction

I am a faculty member at the University of Texas at El Paso working at the intersection of Earth system science, geospatial technologies, and artificial intelligence. My research focuses on developing interpretable and physics-informed AI methods to analyze and model complex environmental systems, including hydrology, atmospheric processes, and remote sensing observations.

In the context of the Genesis mission, my work aims to advance AI for scientific reasoning by integrating data, models, and domain knowledge to discover governing relationships, improve prediction, and enable scalable, trustworthy Earth system modeling.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

I have expertise in machine learning, deep learning, and data science applied to large-scale, complex datasets. I develop models such as neural networks, LSTMs, and transformers for spatio-temporal prediction and pattern recognition. I work with heterogeneous data (remote sensing, in situ, and model outputs), focusing on data fusion, feature engineering, and scalable workflows. My experience includes model evaluation, uncertainty analysis, and interpretable AI for understanding relationships and variable importance.

Datasets or data infrastructure

I work with large-scale, multi-source geospatial and environmental datasets, including remote sensing (satellite imagery, microwave/optical), in situ observations, and reanalysis/model outputs. I develop data pipelines for ingestion, cleaning, harmonization, and fusion across spatial and temporal scales. My experience includes handling high-performance computing environments, cloud-based workflows, and building structured datasets for AI/ML applications, supporting scalable analysis and model development for Earth system science.

Subject matter expertise

My subject matter expertise is in Earth system science, with a focus on hydrology, atmospheric processes, and remote sensing. I specialize in soil moisture dynamics, land–atmosphere interactions, and environmental monitoring using satellite and in situ data. My work also includes geospatial analysis, GIS, and GeoAI applications across water resources, climate, and environmental systems, with an emphasis on integrating data and models for improved understanding and prediction.

Project Idea Summary

I am interested in developing AI approaches that enhance reasoning, discovery, and decision-making in complex scientific and engineering systems. The focus is on moving beyond prediction toward methods that can better integrate data, models, and domain knowledge in a structured and interpretable way.

The core challenge aligns with advancing AI for Scientific Reasoning, including improving how AI systems learn from diverse data sources, support model development, and contribute to insight generation.

I am seeking partners with expertise in:

(1) AI/ML methods and frameworks,

(2) interpretable or knowledge-driven approaches, and

(3) domain applications supported by relevant datasets and computational infrastructure.

The goal is to collaboratively develop robust and scalable AI solutions that support understanding and informed decision-making across a range of applications.
