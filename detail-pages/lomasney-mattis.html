Introduction

Pacific Northwest National Laboratory is a different kind of national lab. PNNL advances the frontiers of knowledge, taking on some of the world’s greatest science and technology challenges. Distinctive strengths in chemistry, Earth sciences, biology, and data science are central to our scientific discovery mission. Our research lays a foundation for innovations that advance energy resilience through grid modernization and energy storage. It also enhances national security through nuclear materials and threat analyses. PNNL collaborates with academia in fundamental research and with industry to transition technologies to market.

We are a national lab with Pacific Northwest roots and global reach. Whether our researchers are accelerating scientific discovery and strengthening national security—advancing AI-driven research, reinforcing grid resilience, and reducing nuclear threats—or positioning the U.S. for global leadership in the bioeconomy, we accept great challenges for one purpose: to create a world that is more prosperous and secure.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

PNNL has a mature, research-focused cloud computing capability to support scientific research and discovery. Characteristics include parity of support for Amazon, Microsoft, and Google clouds; established Authority to Operate for up to FISMA Moderate data (CUI) in Commercial Regions, and CUI/NOFORN in GovCloud Regions; highly automated and monitored self-service provisioning capability allowing near instant dispensing of cloud accounts; 100% charge-back billing of all cloud usage to direct-funded projects; support for external Collaborators with configurable role-based access to cloud accounts

Experimental or test facilities

PNNL provides partners with access to its premier scientific user facilities, including the Environmental Molecular Sciences Laboratory (EMSL) and the Grid Storage Launchpad (GSL). Through EMSL, partners can leverage world-class capabilities in molecular-level characterization, multi-omics biology, high-resolution imaging, and data-rich experimental science to understand complex chemical and biological systems. Through GSL, partners gain access to grid-scale battery testing, validation, and performance analytics to accelerate the development and deployment of advanced energy storage tech.

Subject matter expertise

PNNL offers partners access to deep expertise across advanced chemistry and materials science; data science, artificial intelligence, and advanced computing; Earth, environmental, and climate systems; and biological and life sciences. These capabilities support collaborative solutions in clean and resilient energy systems, nuclear security and nonproliferation, environmental sustainability and remediation, and integrated national security technologies, enabling partners to translate scientific discovery into real-world impact.
