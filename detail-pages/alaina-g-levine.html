Introduction

I specialize in STEM workforce and career development for large-scale scientific ecosystems, helping scientists, engineers, and students translate technical expertise into high-impact roles across academia, industry, and government. My work focuses on building and activating workforce pipelines, enabling cross-sector collaboration, and preparing talent to contribute effectively to AI-enabled scientific initiatives - aligning individual capabilities with national research priorities and collaborative funding opportunities.

With a focus on building the human infrastructure that enables scientific collaboration, accelerates innovation, and ensures that talent can effectively contribute to complex, interdisciplinary missions, I bring ~30 years experience in STEM workforce development and science communication.

I have delivered 1000+ programs across 5 continents, partnering with organizations like the US Dept of Energy, LBNL, BNL, PNNL, Caltech, Princeton, Brown, U Chicago, UC Berkeley, UT Austin, and NASA, who said about me “her work is worth its weight in gold.” And I have written 500+ articles about career development issues in pubs such as Science, Nature, SciAm, and World Economic Forum, including my 3 year column, Your Unicorn Career, in Science Magazine, 2 books on STEM career development, 2 courses on STEM career development with Oxford University Press, and a recent paper in Nature Biotechnology about philanthropy and STEM career development.

Project Idea Summary

My project focuses on strengthening the STEM workforce pipeline for AI-enabled scientific discovery by addressing the gap between advanced technical training and the ability to operate effectively in cross-sector, mission-driven environments.

Despite major investments in AI and scientific infrastructure, many STEM professionals are not equipped to translate their expertise into applied impact, navigate interdisciplinary collaboration, or access opportunities within ecosystems like the Genesis Mission Consortium.

This project will develop a scalable workforce framework that prepares participants to:

Translate technical skills into mission-aligned contributions

Navigate career pathways across academia, industry, and government

Participate effectively in collaborative research and funded initiatives

Access non-obvious opportunities within AI-enabled science

Challenge Focus Area: Workforce Development and Career Development for AI-Enabled, Interdisciplinary Science

Partner Needs: Access to target audiences (students, researchers), alignment with active projects or working groups, collaboration on pilot programs (workshops/cohorts), and insight into workforce needs tied to AI and applied science. Ideal partners include labs, universities, industry R&D, and scientific societies.
