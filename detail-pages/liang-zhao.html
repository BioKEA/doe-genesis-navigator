Introduction

Liang Zhao’s expertise and potential contribution to this proposal span graph-based scientific foundation models, decision-centric world models, and trustworthy agentic AI for complex scientific reasoning. He develops foundation models for structured scientific domains, including grid foundation models that learn transferable, feasibility-aware representations across grid topologies and optimization tasks such as ACOPF and SCUC, as well as molecule graph foundation models for controllable analog generation and geospatial foundation models for spatial and cognitive reasoning. He also brings expertise in dynamic, nonstationary graph learning and future distribution shift, including dynamic graph prediction and optimization, event forecasting, spatial multitask learning, and temporal domain generalization for adapting to unseen future tasks and regimes without retraining. On world models, he works on decision-conditioned models that predict how complex scientific systems evolve under actions or interventions, with explicit treatment of uncertainty, counterfactuals, and reliability, aligning well with scenario generation, intervention screening, and selective high-fidelity simulation. He also develops knowledge-augmented and agentic LLM systems that connect base LLMs with specialized agents, tools, and domain foundation models to support grounded, auditable, and modular reasoning. Project Idea Summary

This project investigates whether AI world models can provide a useful new layer of decision support for power-grid operations under stressed conditions. Unlike conventional predictive models that estimate a single next state or map an operating point to a single outcome, a world model is designed to represent system dynamics under intervention: it learns how the grid state is likely to evolve over time as different actions are applied, enabling rollout-based comparison of alternative response strategies. In this setting, the approach supports faster and more structured screening of candidate corrective actions using simulation and operational data, with uncertainty-aware ranking before engineering verification. If successful, this work could support better scenario exploration, prioritization, and interpretation of recovery options after disturbances.
