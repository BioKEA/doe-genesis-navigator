Introduction

SmithGroup is an integrated architecture and engineering firm with more than 1,600 professionals in 20 offices, specializing in mission critical, AI and high performance computing (HPC) data centers for government, research, and commercial clients. We design end to end digital and physical infrastructure—encompassing power, cooling, structural, and controls systems—to support extreme rack densities, liquid cooling, and multi megawatt IT loads, with a focus on reliability, scalability, and cybersecurity informed layouts.

In addition to architecture and engineering, SmithGroup is actively engaged in the intersection of AI and energy, collaborating with the U.S. Department of Energy and industry partners to push the envelope on high density computing, ultra-high efficiency cooling, and grid aware facility design. Our portfolio includes pioneering work on DOE’s Energy Systems Integration Facility at NLR, where we integrated liquid cooled HPC with building and campus heating loads, as well as numerous data center and mission critical projects that serve as testbeds for AI enabled controls, digital twins, and energy responsive operations. This experience positions SmithGroup as a valuable industry partner for Genesis Mission teams that need facility scale modeling, thermal and energy systems integration, and infrastructure concepts that align AI workloads with clean energy production, grid stability, and waste heat utilization.

Relevant Projects

National Laboratory of the Rockies:

The NLR HPC project, part of the Energy Systems Integration Facility in Golden, Colorado, integrates an advanced waste heat recovery system that significantly enhances energy efficiency. Waste heat from the HPC Data Center (HPCDC) is repurposed to directly heat office and conference spaces, supplement laboratory heating, and provide all campus heating during summer months. This system operates alongside geothermal storage and an ambient water loop, enabling year-round heat storage and extraction during winter, reducing heating energy use. These innovations contribute to an extremely low energy usage intensity (EUI) of 23.0 kBTU/sf/yr—74% below the national office building average—and a world-class data center power usage effectiveness (PUE) of 1.04. The facility’s sustainable design supports a future net-zero energy campus.

 

Argonne National Laboratory Waste Heat Recovery Master Plan:

The Argonne National Laboratory Waste Heat Recovery Master Plan focuses on capturing and repurposing waste heat from the Advanced Photon Source (APS) and Aurora HPC facility to enhance energy efficiency, sustainability, and resiliency. The APS ring, consuming 127,000 MWh annually, generates waste heat exceeding its heating load. SmithGroup designed a system using waste heat recovery chillers to capture this heat and redirect it to the APS building complex, offsetting campus steam heating and reducing energy consumption and emissions. This system can meet up to 55% of the annual heating load and cut life cycle costs by 49%.

Additionally, the Aurora HPC facility's waste heat is being studied for integration into the campus's "Energy Quad" to further reduce energy use and emissions. The Genesis AI Facility master site planning incorporates on-site power generation and energy strategies, aligning with Argonne's goals for energy efficiency and sustainability.
