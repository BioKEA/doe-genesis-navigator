Introduction

I am a Research Scientist and Principal Investigator at the University of California, San Diego, leading research on plasma boundary physics, plasma–material interactions, and real-time control in magnetic confinement fusion devices. My work integrates experiments on major tokamak facilities (including DIII-D and KSTAR), advanced diagnostics, physics-based modeling, and AI/ML to enable predictive, reactor-relevant plasma operation.

My research focuses on critical risks to stable fusion operation arising from plasma–surface interactions, impurity transport, transient material injection events, and coupled core–edge dynamics under three-dimensional magnetic fields. These processes govern confinement, stability, heat-flux handling, and material lifetime, and are central to disruption avoidance in future fusion pilot plants (FPPs).

I lead the Orlov Lab at UC San Diego (https://dmorlov.com/), working at the interface of plasma science and fusion engineering with strong national laboratory and private-sector collaborations. We develop physics-informed, data-driven frameworks that fuse multimodal diagnostics with modeling and AI/ML to infer unobservable plasma-facing states, detect off-normal events, and enable real-time monitoring, early warning, and control.

Relevant Projects

1. AI-enabled plasma boundary reconstruction under diagnostic constraints

Led development of a neural-network-based framework to reconstruct the DIII-D plasma boundary using a reduced set of diagnostics, targeting reactor-relevant conditions where full diagnostic coverage may not be available. The work demonstrated accurate, fast plasma shape inference suitable for real-time applications and control-oriented workflows. This project established a pathway toward robust AI-enabled equilibrium awareness for future fusion devices and resulted in a 2025 Journal of Plasma Physics publication.

2. Plasma confinement state classification using FPP-relevant microwave diagnostics

Led a project developing AI/ML tools to identify plasma confinement state using limited, fusion-pilot-plant-relevant microwave diagnostics rather than a full experimental diagnostic suite. The approach demonstrated that confinement regime classification can be achieved under sparse, deployment-relevant measurement conditions, directly supporting the needs of future fusion pilot plants. The work advances interpretable, real-time plasma state awareness for control and monitoring and resulted in a 2026 Plasma Physics and Controlled Fusion publication.

3. Optimization and control of transport and edge instabilities with 3D magnetic fields

Led and contributed to integrated experimental and modeling studies on DIII-D and KSTAR using externally applied 3D magnetic fields to control transport, suppress edge instabilities, and modify plasma boundary conditions. This work addressed plasma response, ELM suppression, edge transport, and scenario optimization with direct relevance to stable, high-performance operation in future fusion pilot plants. The project linked physics understanding with control-oriented strategies and contributed to recent Nuclear Fusion publications on KSTAR scenario optimization and DIII-D edge control.

Project Idea Summary

Develop a coordinated effort to enable AI-driven, real-time monitoring, prediction, and control of fusion plasmas for reactor-relevant operation. The focus is on integrating physics-based modeling, reduced-order representations, and data-driven approaches to provide fast, reliable estimates of plasma state, transport, and boundary conditions.

Key directions include the development of surrogate modeling frameworks for rapid exploration of operational space, real-time detection and tracking of transient events (e.g., fueling, impurity influx, material injection), and AI-enabled interpretation of diagnostic signals under sparse, reactor-relevant conditions. Additional efforts target predictive modeling of edge transport, heat fluxes, and plasma–surface interaction dynamics, as well as coupling transport prediction with control-oriented decision frameworks.

The goal is to establish a unified approach that bridges simulation, diagnostics, and operation, enabling validation, scalability, and deployment in fusion pilot plant environments.

Seeking collaborators across academia, national labs, and industry to co-develop and validate these capabilities, including partners with expertise in diagnostics, modeling, experimental platforms, and scalable data and compute infrastructure.
