Introduction

Akamai Technologies  operates the world's most distributed compute platform, spanning 4,400+ edge locations across 135+ countries. For over 25 years, Akamai has secured the most demanding digital missions for federal civilian agencies, DoD components, and the intelligence community.

For the Genesis Mission, Akamai contributes production-grade security infrastructure purpose-built for the threats Topic 20 addresses. Processing 30% of all daily web traffic and more than 13 trillion DNS queries per day, Akamai's global telemetry provides real-time visibility into command-and-control signatures, newly observed malicious domains, and data exfiltration patterns.

Akamai secures research environments through Zero Trust Network Access (EAA), AI-powered microsegmentation (Guardicore Segmentation), Firewall for AI, and API Security governing machine-to-machine workflows across hybrid, cloud, and Kubernetes environments. Akamai Inference Cloud, the first global-scale implementation of the NVIDIA AI Grid, delivers distributed AI compute at up to 2.5x lower latency and up to 86% lower cost than centralized hyperscalers.

Akamai's Cloud for Government achieved FedRAMP High Ready status in December 2025, with active pursuit of FedRAMP High and DoD IL5, positioning Akamai as a credentialed partner for the most security-sensitive Genesis programs.

Project Idea Summary

AI inference pipelines are an underdefended and high-value attack surface. Prompt injection, model abuse, and data exfiltration at inference speed represent a threat class that conventional security tooling was not designed to detect, and that cannot be studied without production-scale adversarial data. That is the gap Akamai fills.

Akamai proposes to contribute its production threat detection infrastructure as the core research asset for a Topic 20-C application: Firewall for AI, DNS threat intelligence (13 trillion queries/day), and API Security behavioral monitoring, instrumented against adversarial AI inference scenarios in a national laboratory environment. Working with the lab PI, Akamai will co-develop a validated detection methodology covering prompt injection, network-layer threat signatures, and anomalous agentic behavior at inference speed. The output is a validated detection model and labeled threat dataset, documented for reproducibility.

Detection is the research contribution. Mitigation is the proof it works. Validated detections feed into Guardicore policy enforcement and edge-level blocking, demonstrating an end-to-end detect-and-contain pipeline for distributed DOE research environments.

We seek partners who bring: a PI and research leadership; adversarial AI and inference attack expertise; a real AI workload environment for instrumentation; and dataset curation capacity for Topic 20-B data provenance.
