Introduction

My name is Alexander Leder, and I am an experimental physicist working on the LEGEND Experiment, an international effort searching for Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay (0vbb), at Los Alamos National Laboratory. My research experience mostly focuses on detector hardware development for ultra-low background instrumentation for rare-event searches as well as development of analysis/simulation tools.

My research background encompasses a broad portfolio of experiments probing physics beyond the Standard Model, including searches for WIMP Dark Matter, measurements of CeVens at nuclear reactors, validation of Nuclear Matrix Elements, and Axion searches and most recently 0vbb. All these efforts require precision detector design, precise understanding of low-background materials and robust analysis frameworks.

In terms of development of AI-tools, I have experience with analysis techniques including Markov Chain Monte Carlo, Boosted Decision Trees, and emerging AI-agentic workflows for experiment optimization and automated analysis on the above efforts.

My current work focuses on AI-assisted neutron monitoring for nuclear reactors and underground laboratories, as well as the design of large-scale ultra-low radio-background components for next-generation rare-event detectors. I am seeking industry and university collaborators interested in applying AI to radiation detection technologies and low-background instrumentation to challenges in neutron monitoring and rare-event searches

Project Idea Summary

We have two main ideas:

1) Development and testing of a AI-powered neutron monitoring framework for use in dynamic neutron environments. The framework would use AI to accelerate the simulation of the expected neutron environment in order to determine an optimal layout of detector systems in order to be able to reconstruct a room flux with a 24 hours turnaround time between data collection and generation of a room flux

2) We are working on building a 6 meter tall 2 meter diameter 1.5 mm thin at it thinnest metal tube to be installed as part of the LEGEND experiment. However; the metals to be employed must meet extremely strict radio-purity requirements and be able to function long term in a cryogenic environment. We are looking to optimize the design of this tube using AI-accelerated simulation software in order to quickly scan through the large design parameter space in order to find a design that is both mechanically feasible while still meeting the strict radioactive background requirements of the LEGEND experiment.
