    Introduction

    I am Haojian Jin, an Assistant Professor at UC San Diego's Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute, with an affiliate appointment in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering. My research sits at the intersection of human-computer interaction, privacy and security, and AI systems.

    We focus on enhancing human-in/on-the-loop interaction for agentic AI, developing frameworks that keep humans meaningfully in control as AI systems take on greater autonomy.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    AI models, tools, or software

    Our lab develops human-centered AI safety tools and frameworks designed to make agentic AI systems safer to build and deploy. This includes systems that enforce human-specified behavioral policies on generative AI models — enabling fine-grained control over model behavior without retraining — as well as interaction frameworks that keep humans meaningfully in control as AI agents operate with increasing autonomy. We also build privacy analysis platforms that automate compliance review for AI-powered systems.

    Relevant Projects

    Project 1: Moderator — Behavioral Policy Enforcement for Generative AI Developed an open-source system enabling fine-grained, context-aware content moderation for text-to-image diffusion models without requiring model retraining. The system allows operators and users to define custom behavioral policies that are enforced at inference time, addressing a critical gap between AI capability and human oversight. The project demonstrated that human-specified policies can be translated into scalable, enforceable system-level constraints across diverse generative AI deployments. Published at ACM CCS 2024, where it received the Distinguished Paper Award.

    Project 2: Human-on-the-Loop Interaction for Agentic AI Designed and evaluated interaction frameworks that keep humans meaningfully in control as AI agents operate with increasing autonomy. This work introduced mechanisms for humans to monitor, intervene, and redirect agentic AI behavior without disrupting task execution — addressing key safety challenges as AI systems take on more proactive roles. Complementary work developed GameArena and lmgame-Bench, live evaluation platforms for assessing LLM reasoning and decision-making in dynamic environments. Published at CHI 2026 (Best Paper Award), ICLR 2025, and ICLR 2026.

    Project 3: Peekaboo — Privacy Transparency for Smart Home AI Systems Built a hub-based middleware system that intercepts and mediates data flows between smart home devices and third-party cloud services, giving users and developers fine-grained visibility into what data is collected, by whom, and for what purpose. Peekaboo introduced a modular privacy flow architecture that enforces data minimization at the system level, without requiring changes to individual apps. The project directly influenced subsequent privacy framework design in IoT contexts. Published at IEEE S&P 2022, and covered by ACM TechNews and the RSA Conference Podcast.

    Project Idea Summary

    As AI agents become increasingly capable of autonomous action, ensuring that humans remain meaningfully in control is one of the most pressing challenges in responsible AI deployment. In high-stakes scientific environments — such as those found across DOE national laboratories — the consequences of unchecked agentic behavior can be significant, making human oversight not just desirable but essential. 

     

    Our research focuses on human-on-the-loop interaction: designing systems where AI agents can operate with autonomy while humans retain the ability to monitor, intervene, and redirect behavior at critical decision points. We are interested in translating these frameworks into real-world DOE workflows, where agentic AI could accelerate scientific discovery while remaining aligned with researcher intent and institutional safety requirements. Key challenges we aim to address include how to surface the right information to human operators at the right time, how to design intervention mechanisms that do not bottleneck productivity, and how to audit agent decisions in complex, multi-step scientific pipelines. We are seeking partners from DOE national laboratories or affiliated research institutions who are actively exploring AI-assisted workflows and are interested in co-designing human-centered oversight mechanisms grounded in real operational contexts.
