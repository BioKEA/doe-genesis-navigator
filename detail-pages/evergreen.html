Introduction

Evergreen is building the infrastructure to capture, govern, and verify reality across critical environments. We develop high-integrity systems for provenance, data governance, workflow control, and auditability, designed for sectors where trust cannot be assumed and failure carries real consequences. Our focus is the technical layer that brings accountability, traceability, and durable coordination to complex operations, enabling institutions to work from a more secure, verifiable, and shared foundation of truth.

Evergreen is aligned with the need for trusted AI-driven science. Our role is centered on the data and workflow integrity layer beneath advanced scientific computing, autonomous research, and machine-assisted discovery. We are particularly focused on provenance-aware architectures, tamper-evident records, and governed scientific workflows that help ensure research inputs, transformations, and outputs remain attributable, auditable, and defensible across teams, systems, and institutional boundaries.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Evergreen is developing software for provenance-aware data management, workflow governance, and integrity verification in AI-driven scientific environments. Our work includes schema-aware ingestion, policy-based workflow controls, traceable event logging, and auditable linkage between source data, transformations, analytical steps, and downstream outputs.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Evergreen’s AI/ML expertise is focused on the data and systems layer surrounding model use in scientific workflows. We work on provenance-aware pipelines, metadata discipline, machine-actionable policy logic, and traceability structures that improve confidence in model inputs, transformations, derived outputs, and reproducibility across multi-step environments.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Data infrastructure for high-integrity workflows. We are developing architecture that preserves lineage, enforces metadata structure, supports tamper-evident event histories, and enables auditable reconstruction of how data moved through a process across teams, tools, and institutional boundaries, including distributed ledger approaches where appropriate for shared trust and verifiable record integrity.

Subject matter expertise

Data provenance, integrity assurance, governed workflow design, and trust architecture for complex technical environments. Our focus is on systems where scientific outputs require stronger guarantees around source authenticity, transformation history, access control, and evidentiary defensibility.

Other expertise

Provenance architecture, canonical data models, semantic control structures, policy enforcement, and distributed trust frameworks, including blockchain-informed approaches to tamper-evident records, cross-entity coordination, and auditable workflow state management.

Other resources/tools

Architectural design logic, framework concepts, and technical approaches for provenance-backed workflow infrastructure. This includes governed records, evidentiary linkage of process steps, tamper-evident histories, auditability across heterogeneous systems, and integrity-verification patterns relevant to AI-driven science workflows.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

Seeking partners with mature tools for scientific workflow orchestration, provenance capture, data integrity validation, model governance, anomaly detection, and secure pipeline monitoring. Priority is software that can operate in research or HPC environments, integrate with heterogeneous systems, and support verifiable handling of inputs, transformations, intermediate states, and outputs without requiring a full rip-and-replace of existing infrastructure.

Looking for collaborators with deep experience in AI-driven scientific workflows, ML pipeline design, data curation, uncertainty handling, and evaluation of model reliability in research settings. Strong fit includes teams that understand how weak metadata, poor lineage, or unverified transformations degrade downstream scientific reasoning, reproducibility, and automation, and who can help translate those risks into measurable technical requirements.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Seeking access to secure compute environments suitable for testing provenance-aware controls in realistic scientific workflows. This includes HPC, hybrid cloud, or other research-computing infrastructure capable of supporting data-intensive pipelines, logging and verification overhead, controlled access patterns, and repeatable validation exercises. Environments with strong security, compliance, and multi-user coordination are especially relevant.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Looking for partners with datasets, reference architectures, or operational data environments that can support work on lineage preservation, metadata rigor, workflow traceability, and integrity verification. High-value collaborators include those managing scientific, technical, or mission-relevant data across multiple tools or institutions, where provenance, version control, access discipline, and auditability are essential to trusted downstream use.

Experimental or test facilities

Seeking access to laboratory, testbed, or simulated research environments where provenance and integrity controls can be evaluated in practice. Ideal settings include autonomous or semi-autonomous workflows, instrumented experimental pipelines, and facilities where multiple systems or teams interact with shared data. The goal is to validate controls under realistic operating conditions rather than in a purely theoretical software context.

Subject matter expertise

Looking to engage domain experts in scientific computing, laboratory operations, cybersecurity, data governance, and research integrity. The strongest contributors will understand how real scientific workflows break in practice, where trust assumptions currently fail, and what technical, operational, or institutional constraints must be respected for a provenance and integrity layer to be adopted in serious research environments.

Other expertise

Interested in collaborators with expertise in secure systems architecture, distributed trust, workflow assurance, ontology and semantic design, policy enforcement, and cross-organizational coordination. This includes teams that can help shape interoperable approaches for maintaining trusted state, attribution, evidentiary traceability, and control consistency across diverse tools, institutions, and scientific operating environments.

Project Idea Summary

Evergreen’s project idea is to build and validate a provenance-backed trust and integrity layer for AI-driven scientific workflows, based on framework design and system logic we have already developed. Centered on Cybersecurity for AI-driven Science Workflows, it addresses a core weakness in the scientific landscape: AI systems, autonomous research tools, and HPC pipelines are advancing rapidly, while the data and workflow environments beneath them often remain fragmented, weakly governed, and difficult to verify. Our focus is the control layer that makes scientific data, transformations, workflow events, and outputs traceable, tamper-evident, policy-governed, and auditable across complex computational and experimental environments.

In practice, this means applying Evergreen’s existing framework concepts for provenance capture, integrity verification, governed workflow state, and durable scientific records across teams, tools, institutions, and machine-executed processes. We are seeking partners who can ground this work in real settings through scientific computing environments, AI-enabled research workflows, laboratory or testbed infrastructure, relevant datasets, and domain expertise in scientific computing, cybersecurity, autonomous labs, or workflow orchestration. The ideal consortium pairs Evergreen’s architecture with partners who can validate, refine, and demonstrate it under realistic operating conditions.
