Introduction

Contextualize helps research organizations and industrial operators leverage their existing infrastructure investments — without the disruption or adoption friction of wholesale platform migrations or workflow overhauls. Our Carta platform meets teams where they are, integrating incrementally into established processes and enabling knowledge to flow naturally from individual contributors outward to teams, partners, and external stakeholders.

Our flexibility-first approach reduces implementation time, lowers adoption friction, and protects prior investments in tools, methods, and infrastructure — whether physical or computational in nature and whether on-premise, cloud, or hybrid. By reducing data silos and enabling cross-organizational collaboration, Carta helps institutions scale their research and operational capabilities without scaling their complexity.

The Contextualize team brings decades of demonstrated success in cross-cutting materials science domains — including polymers, alloy development, magnetic materials, additive manufacturing, and energy storage — as well as applied AI/ML, data science, and next-generation automated laboratory systems.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

The Contextualize Carta platform proxies direct contributions from domain experts and developers. Hosting their own solutions leaves resource owners in complete control while providing centralized access to decentralized analytical, empirical, and AI models, tools, and other software.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

The Contextualize Carta platform is an integration layer that connects heterogeneous, geographically distributed resources — compute, data storage, physical research equipment, and simulation and modeling capabilities. Platform functionality is extended and refined through direct contributions from domain experts and developers, enabling Carta to adapt to the specific workflows, methods, and infrastructure of each deployment context.

Subject matter expertise

The Contextualize team has experience at the intersection of AI/ML, data science, scientific software development and many areas within physical and computational R&D in materials science: polymers, metallurgy and alloy development, energy materials, simulation and modeling (DFT, MD, MC), metaheuristics and optimization, X-ray characterization (XRD, X-ray CT), electron microscopy, spectroscopy, and thermal analysis.

Other resources/tools

Contextualize's expertise in materials R&D, in data science and scientific software development, and Carta's syncretic approach to multi-institution data infrastructure uniquely positions Contextualize to work across a broad set of Genesis Mission program topics.

Relevant Projects

High Throughput Materials under Extreme Conditions (HTMDEC, Army DEVCOM) Under Army Research Laboratory Cooperative Agreement W911NF-22-2-0128, Contextualize built data infrastructure for multi-organizational, high-throughput materials research with HTMDEC Seedling awardees and the BIRDSHOT Center. An initial survey across six organizations revealed researchers averaged eight separate storage locations, fewer than half had automated data collection, and 25% of data was inaccessible beyond the originator — findings that directly shaped the architecture. Contextualize integrated the BIRDSHOT team's Google Drive with Carta via a Service Account-backed Dispatcher, formalized their data topology through a schema-driven folder structure generator, and developed 29 JSON Schema metadata schemas across two experimental campaigns spanning DED synthesis, tensile testing, nanoindentation, XRD, and LIPIT. The birdshot Python library and CLI exposed this infrastructure through graph-based bidirectional data flow, enabling automated feature extraction triggered on upload. Approximately 140 Tools and Workflows were developed on Seven Bridges using CWL, with Dispatchers providing API-stable, authentication-controlled access for downstream applications.

AI Manufacturing Pilot Facility (Georgia Tech, EDA) Since early 2024, Contextualize has served as data infrastructure and software development partner for Georgia Tech's AIMPF under a multi-phase engagement now exceeding $3M. Beginning with system architecture design and Carta platform deployment, successive phases delivered custom data schemas, a version-controlled forms database, digital sample tracking, a self-service schema builder, SSO authentication, and instrument-specific CLIs. The aimpf Python library provides researchers programmatic access to the full AIMPF resource stack. Beyond infrastructure, Contextualize contributed direct research support across refractory alloy development, Al-MMC characterization, DSC analysis, and signal processing. The 2025 engagement culminated in demonstration of an automated research workflow at the AIMPF facility opening. The current phase expands into production operations, adding DEXR, a Workflow Manager, a SCADA wallboard, and LLM-integrated dispatchers covering statistical, ML/AI, graph, and data transformation operations.

Extracting Constitutive Relationships from Large Manufacturing Datasets (NSF SBIR 2116383) Contextualize pursued two goals: developing Argos, a framework to map between neural network weights and analytical function coefficients, and building Carta as supporting cloud infrastructure. Argos was rigorously explored across number theory, functional analysis, and probabilistic approaches but did not reduce to a tractable solution — the ReLU activation function's non-invertibility precluded the guarantees required for clean series-based mapping. Carta proved the more impactful deliverable. Deployed on AWS, the platform provides federated data integration across heterogeneous sources (SQL, CSV, HDF5, HyperThought) via a common graph representation, a granular permissions system, a Unix-inspired atomic operations framework, and pycarta — enabling scientists to deploy custom Python functions to the cloud with a single decorator. Carta was demonstrated against 270,000+ measurements from a multi-instrument manufacturing dataset. The project engaged data from over 100 researchers across eight institutions.

Project Idea Summary

Incorporating AI required bridging the gap between experimental, analytical, and empirical science and the rapidly evolving world of AI. Rather than forcing scientists and engineers to reshape their work around rigid software, Contextualize proposes an accommodation layer — adapting AI capabilities to fit naturally into existing scientific workflows, data ecosystems, and domain expertise.

The platform connects an organization's instruments, datasets, analytical tools, and subject-matter experts into a coherent, extensible ecosystem. Gateway authenticates all callers — human or machine — and isolates AI models from enterprise data, while Carta Permissions resolves complex, multi-institution access relationships across teams, services, and resources. Collections and Form Builder provide structured data capture so domain scientists can formalize knowledge without writing code. CartaFS brokers secure file access across client-controlled storage, keeping sensitive research data where it belongs. Workflow Builder offers visual automation so researchers can orchestrate multi-step analytical pipelines, and Service Manager handles discovery and routing for registered services — whether invoked by a person, another service, or an AI agent via Model Context Protocol (MCP).

Carta doesn't replace trusted laboratory, simulation, or data tools. Instead, it creates the adaptive layer that drastically reduce the friction that typically separates experimental discovery from AI-driven analyses.
