Introduction

Nebius is a global AI infrastructure company building full-stack cloud platforms purpose-built for the age of artificial intelligence. Headquartered in Amsterdam and operating globally, Nebius provides high-performance, AI-native cloud infrastructure - including large-scale GPU clusters, data center solutions, and developer tools - that enable organizations to build, train, and deploy advanced AI systems at scale.

As part of the Genesis Mission, Nebius brings deep expertise in scalable compute, AI deployment, and ecosystem enablement, supporting startups, enterprises, and public sector initiatives in accelerating innovation. The company is committed to democratizing access to advanced AI infrastructure and fostering cross-sector collaboration to drive technological progress and real-world impact.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Nebius provides a full-stack, AI-native cloud platform for developing, training, and deploying advanced AI models at scale. It offers high-performance GPU infrastructure optimized for large-scale training and inference, alongside integrated software tools that streamline the AI lifecycle. Capabilities include managed environments for foundation models, scalable inference, and tools for orchestration, monitoring, and optimization. Nebius supports open-source frameworks and works with startups, enterprises, and public sector partners to accelerate AI adoption.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Nebius provides end-to-end AI/ML and data science expertise, supporting the full lifecycle from data engineering and model development to large-scale training and deployment. The company enables advanced workloads including deep learning, generative AI, and scientific computing, leveraging high-performance GPU infrastructure and distributed training. Nebius also supports MLOps best practices, including model optimization, monitoring, and scaling, helping organizations operationalize AI solutions efficiently and at scale.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Nebius provides AI-native computing infrastructure and cloud resources optimized for large-scale AI workloads. This includes high-performance GPU clusters, distributed training environments, and scalable storage and networking designed for demanding model training and inference. The platform supports elastic provisioning, workload orchestration, and high-throughput data pipelines, enabling efficient, reliable, and cost-effective execution of AI/ML and scientific computing workloads across research, enterprise, and public sector use cases.

 

Datasets or data infrastructure

Nebius provides data infrastructure to support large-scale AI and ML workloads, including high-throughput storage, data ingestion pipelines, and distributed data processing environments. The platform enables efficient handling of structured and unstructured datasets for training and inference, with support for data versioning, secure access controls, and integration with common ML frameworks. It is designed to ensure reliable, scalable, and performant data workflows for AI development and deployment.

Experimental or test facilities

Nebius provides experimental environments for AI development and testing through isolated, scalable cloud-based compute sandboxes. These environments support rapid prototyping, benchmarking, and validation of AI models at scale using high-performance GPU infrastructure. Users can run controlled experiments for training, fine-tuning, and inference, with support for reproducibility, monitoring, and workload isolation. This enables safe, efficient testing of advanced AI systems before production deployment.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: Large-Scale AI Model Training Infrastructure
Nebius supported enterprise and AI-native customers in training large foundation models by providing high-performance GPU cloud infrastructure and distributed compute environments. Scope included provisioning multi-node GPU clusters, optimizing networking for high-throughput workloads, and enabling scalable storage for massive datasets. Achievements included significant reductions in training time, improved cost efficiency for large-scale workloads, and successful deployment of production-grade AI models across multiple industries, including generative AI and scientific computing.

Project 2: AI Cloud Platform for Startups and Research Labs 
Nebius delivered an AI-native cloud platform enabling startups and research institutions to build, train, and deploy ML models at scale. The project included managed ML environments, orchestration tools, and MLOps capabilities such as monitoring and model versioning. Achievements included accelerated time-to-production for AI applications, improved experimentation cycles, and enabling access to enterprise-grade GPU infrastructure for early-stage innovators and academic researchers.

Project 3: High-Performance Computing for Data-Intensive AI Workloads 
Nebius provided scalable compute infrastructure for data-intensive AI and analytics workloads in domains such as energy, simulation, and large-scale data processing. Scope included building resilient GPU clusters, optimizing distributed training pipelines, and ensuring secure, high-performance data handling. Achievements included enabling complex AI simulations, improving workload efficiency, and supporting mission-critical AI experimentation for enterprise and public sector clients.

Project Idea Summary

Nebius proposes collaboration under the Securing U.S. Leadership in Data Centers challenge, focused on enabling next-generation AI infrastructure for large-scale model training, inference, and scientific computing workloads.

The objective is to advance scalable, secure, and energy-efficient AI data center systems through improved workload orchestration, resource optimization, and distributed systems performance across GPU-based infrastructure, while increasing efficiency and reliability under extreme computational demand.

Nebius seeks partnership with DOE national laboratories and research institutions to access experimental testbeds and co-develop validation environments for AI-optimized data center operations. This includes digital twins of compute infrastructure, benchmarking frameworks for frontier AI workloads, and evaluation of energy-aware scheduling, dynamic resource allocation, and thermal-aware workload distribution.

Additional areas include resilience under peak load conditions, fault tolerance in large-scale distributed clusters, and interoperability across heterogeneous compute environments. We also aim to explore methods to improve latency, throughput, and predictability of AI workloads in production-scale systems.

Nebius contributes expertise in AI-native cloud infrastructure and HPC platforms, supporting joint development of scalable and sustainable solutions. The partnership aims to strengthen U.S. leadership in secure, efficient, high-performance data centers.
