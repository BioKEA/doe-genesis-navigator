Introduction

Flower Labs develops the leading open-source federated learning framework and platform, enabling collaborative training of AI models across distributed, sensitive, and institutionally siloed data. It allows organizations to build a single model across multiple institutions and data environments while keeping data in place.

Flower is widely used across both research and industry and is actively deployed across multiple U.S. Department of Energy laboratories. Flower supports all major machine learning frameworks, from classical ML to deep learning, foundation models, and agent-based systems, enabling seamless collaboration across national labs, industry partners, and experimental facilities while preserving data locality, security, and governance constraints.

In the context of the Genesis Mission, Flower enables AI-driven scientific workflows that span heterogeneous, multi-institution environments. By unlocking access to otherwise inaccessible data and enabling cross-site learning, Flower helps achieve measurable AI advantage through improved model generalization, faster iteration cycles, and tighter integration of experimental and computational systems.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Flower provides an open-source framework and platform for federated learning, enabling collaborative training, fine-tuning, and evaluation of AI models across distributed data. Flower supports all major machine learning frameworks (e.g., PyTorch, TensorFlow, JAX, scikit-learn) and is used across research and production environments. It enables development of models ranging from classical ML to deep learning, foundation models, and agent-based systems in federated settings.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Flower Labs has deep expertise in federated learning, distributed optimization, confidential computing, and AI governance.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Flower SuperGrid is a hosted platform for running federated learning experiments across distributed environments. It enables organizations to deploy, manage, and monitor training workflows without building custom infrastructure. SuperGrid supports on-premise, HPC, and cloud environments while preserving data locality, security, and governance, and provides orchestration and visibility for multi-institution AI workflows.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

We seek collaborators developing domain-specific AI models (e.g., materials, energy, biology, scientific simulation) that can benefit from training across distributed datasets. Flower enables these models to be trained, fine-tuned, and evaluated across institutions without centralizing data, improving generalization and enabling access to otherwise siloed information.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We seek partners with applied AI/ML expertise in scientific domains to co-develop models and workflows. This includes teams working on simulation-driven ML, experimental data modeling, and scientific AI systems. Flower enables joint development across institutions, allowing teams to collaborate on models without sharing underlying data.

Experimental or test facilities

We are interested in working with partners operating experimental or test facilities (e.g., national labs, pilot plants, instrumented systems) where AI models can be trained on distributed data and deployed in closed-loop workflows for real-time analysis, optimization, and scientific discovery.

Subject matter expertise

We seek domain experts in areas such as materials science, advanced manufacturing, energy systems, biotechnology, and scientific computing. These collaborations enable the development of AI-driven workflows that integrate distributed data and models to accelerate discovery and improve predictive capabilities.

Project Idea Summary

We are exploring project opportunities across Genesis Mission challenge areas and are open to co-developing proposals with partners. We are particularly interested in working with partners who bring domain expertise, datasets, models, or experimental facilities where distributed AI can accelerate discovery and improve performance. We welcome collaboration across all focus areas where data fragmentation or institutional boundaries limit traditional AI approaches.
