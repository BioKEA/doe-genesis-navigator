Introduction

I am a physics professor at the University of California, Merced. My group works on applying AI and quantum computing to solve physics problems. I have applied machine learning to many-body physics in curved space and quantum systems influenced by classical randomness. Moreover, my group is investigating molecular states and thermal transport with assistance from AI tools. My group also translates physics problems, such as topological and geometric phases, molecular spectrum, and spin systems, into quantum circuits. By using optimization software to compactify the quantum circuits, we are able to fit selected problems into currently available NISQ hardware and extract quantitative results. While discovering and improving computations on NISQ hardware, my group also explore quantum error mitigation and error correction schemes to further improve the accuracy and reliability of quantum computing. Recently I started seeking collaborations with computer-scientists and industrial partners to apply AI to quantum computing with the goals of optimizing the circuits and mitigating or correcting possible errors in an automatic and efficient fashion. I also explore possibilities of replacing some of the modules in AI by quantum computing to seek quantum advantages. I would like to contribute my background in atomic and molecular physics and condensed matter physics to integrate AI into my research and make new discoveries.

Relevant Projects

1. AI for atomic and molecular physics: Used LLM to transform wavefunctions and scattering lengths to molecular potentials, produce codes for numerical simulations, and evaluate the energy spectrum. Currently using supervised machine learning to differentiate different kinds of bound states and bulk states. Produced two publications and expect one honor undergraduate thesis.

2. AI for many-body physics: Used supervised and unsupervised learning to identify and track vortices of a spin model on a spherical sphere. Determined the Kosterlitz-Thouless transition temperature and traced vortex-antivortex annihilation in curved space. Used supervised learning to investigate the localization of quantum walks induced by classical randomness and found systematic deviations from neural-network based methods. Published two papers and currently work on two more papers.

3. AI for quantum computing: Used machine-learning assisted optimization software to compactify quantum circuits for demonstrating topological phase transitions on IBM quantum computers. Demonstrated clear improvements from software optimization and hardware upgrade on cloud-based quantum computers. Currently testing LLMs for quantum circuit generations and analyses. Published one paper and working on two more.

Project Idea Summary

The proposed research aims to use AI to improve both accuracy and scalability in mapping out many-body physics energy spectra and monitoring quantum dynamics. Currently, quantum computers can only resolve very few energy levels or follow a few steps faithfully in quantum dynamics in systems of interest due to errors and noise from the NISQ hardware. The classical calculations and quantum circuits both require resources exploding rapidly with the system size and correlation, thereby calling for approximate methods at different stages to achieve practical extractions of physical information relevant to the real world. While my group has experiences on classical and quantum simulations of many-body quantum systems and their dynamics, I look for partners knowing synergetic ways for integrating them and reach highly efficient and accurate classical-quantum hybrid calculations of the energy spectrum and dynamics of complex quantum systems. I also look for partners with AI tools or software for optimizing classical, quantum, or hybrid computations. Moreover, partners from the industry or national labs looking for a channel to obtain highly motivated graduate and undergraduate students are welcome since my group produces well-trained and ambitious students.
