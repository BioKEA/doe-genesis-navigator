Introduction

Sero Yang is a Senior Nuclear Engineer at Blue Energy. He is a strategic leader in advanced energy systems and nuclear innovation, focused on accelerating the deployment of next-generation nuclear technologies through cross-cutting collaboration and digital integration using state-of-the-art AI agents. His work aligns with the objectives of the Genesis Mission Partnership to advance AI-enabled scientific discovery by connecting industry, academia, and national laboratories, drawing on experience across all three sectors. 

Sero holds a Ph.D. in Nuclear Engineering with a specialization in thermal hydraulics from Texas A&M University. He previously served as a Principal Nuclear Engineer at Argonne National Laboratory, where he supported the USHPRR program, including work on the MIT research reactor HEU-to-LEU conversion project. His work focused on software development for safety analysis and system integration, in addition to performing safety analyses and supporting system-level integration. 

During his time at Texas A&M, he contributed to DOE NEUP projects in reactor thermal hydraulics. Sero and his team, under Professor Yassin Hassan’s guidance, conducted research on asymptomatic virus transmission during COVID-19, showing that sneeze could spread viruses without symptoms. The work used DOE NEUP-funded facilities and was featured as a NEUP success story.

Sero aims to contribute to an ecosystem that bridges scientific discovery with scalable, real world energy solutions.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: USHPRR — MIT Research Reactor HEU-to-LEU Conversion (HALEU) Argonne National Laboratory | 2021–2024

Supported the U.S. High-Performance Research Reactor (USHPRR) program for the conversion of MIT's research reactor from HEU to LEU using HALEU (U-10Mo) fuel. The project required retrofitting a new fuel element design into the existing reactor form factor without modifying the core geometry, demanding rigorous safety analysis to support NRC licensing. Contributed to the development and validation of thermal-hydraulics framework codes — STAT7 and PLTEMP/ANL — used to demonstrate safety margins under the new fuel configuration. Authored and co-authored multiple verification and validation reports and user guides accepted by the NRC review process. This work established direct experience in licensing non-standard fuel designs within constrained form factors, directly informing the accelerated licensing methodology pursued under RAPID-NPP.

Project 2: STAT7 and PLTEMP/ANL Code Development Argonne National Laboratory | 2021–2024

Led development and validation of STAT7, a best-estimate-plus-uncertainty thermal-hydraulics code applying Monte Carlo methods to predict onset of nucleate boiling and onset of flow instability in plate-fueled research reactors. Simultaneously contributed to verification and validation of PLTEMP/ANL across two volumes. Both codes are used in NRC-accepted safety analysis frameworks for research and test reactors. Deliverables included user guides, V&V reports, and peer-reviewed publications. This software development experience in a nuclear safety context directly supports RAPID-NPP's objective of building AI-augmented, NQA-1-compliant computational workflows that compress licensing timelines without compromising analytical rigor.

Project 3: High-Temperature Latent Heat Storage System for Microreactors (Patent) U.S. Patent Application No. 18/489,645 | 2024

Co-invented a high-temperature latent heat storage system utilizing transportable heat pipes for versatile integration with emerging microreactors. The invention addresses a critical challenge in microreactor deployment: decoupling heat generation from end-use demand through a transportable thermal storage medium, enabling flexible siting and load-following capability without reactor power transients. The system is designed for compatibility with a range of microreactor configurations, supporting both remote and grid-connected applications. This patent directly reflects the RAPID-NPP theme of project-financeable innovation — developing modular, deployable energy solutions that reduce site-specific infrastructure requirements and broaden the commercial applicability of advanced nuclear technologies.

Project 4: Port of Victoria SMR Deployment Blue Energy | 2025–Present

Currently serving as a senior nuclear engineer on Blue Energy's Port of Victoria plant development project, with a focused mandate to accelerate SMR deployment timelines well beyond conventional large-scale reactor benchmarks. The project is already significantly de-risked at an early stage: Blue Energy has secured a site and executed a Power Purchase Agreement with Crusoe, an AI data center operator, establishing a clear demand signal and revenue anchor ahead of construction. Responsibilities span nuclear plant engineering, supply chain development, and deployment methodology, with active engagement in balance-of-plant modularization strategies and OEM-based approaches to manufacturing sequencing.

Project Idea Summary

RAPID-NPP (Reactor-deployment Acceleration with Project-financeable Innovation Driving Nuclear Power Plant Development) is a Blue Energy's initiative to develop an AI-enabled, high-performance and cloud computing-aided framework that accelerates SMR deployment by adopting an OEM approach centered on mature, proven reactor technologies, transforming traditionally sequential design, licensing, and manufacturing processes into concurrent, intelligent workflows.

Core innovations are the modularization and resequencing of balance-of-plant manufacturing, prioritizing systems that do not require nuclear safety-related licensing upfront, through super- or hyper-modules preassembled at shipyard docks and shipped directly to a port near the project site, dramatically reducing on-site construction time and schedule risk.
We are targeting challenge topics 4-A, 4-C, and 1-E, with secondary threads in 16-A/B and 4-H.
Blue Energy leads as technology integrator and grounding the research in real SMR deployment constraints rather than theoretical frameworks.

We are specifically seeking partners in three areas:

DOE/NNSA national laboratories with HPC infrastructure, or existing NRC licensing tool and AI capabilities
Academic institutions with expertise in probabilistic analysis, ML/AI or cloud-based engineering simulation
 

Industry partners with shipyard modularization, OEM reactor experience, or nuclear-grade manufacturing capabilities interested in co-developing NQA-1 compliant AI workflows.
