Introduction

Bolt Threads is a biotechnology company that develops high-performance protein-based materials inspired by nature and enabled by modern biomanufacturing. Our R&D capabilities span protein design, microbial expression and fermentation, downstream processing, materials characterization, formulation, and scale-up. Over more than a decade, Bolt has built an interdisciplinary platform for turning biological inspiration into manufacturable products, including engineered silk-based materials and ingredients. Importantly, we focus not only on invention, but on translation: developing processes capable of producing protein materials at tonne scale and at price points that can support real commercial adoption.

 

This capability set aligns strongly with the Genesis Mission’s focus on accelerating discovery and R&D through AI-enabled workflows, advanced computation, and tighter integration between data, models, experiments, and manufacturing. Bolt can contribute as an industry partner with deep expertise in biologically derived materials, design-build-test-learn cycles, and the challenges of moving from laboratory insight to scalable, cost-accessible manufacturing. We are especially interested in collaborations that combine AI, modeling, and experimental infrastructure to speed the discovery, optimization, and scale-up of next-generation biomaterials and biomanufacturing processes relevant to U.S. scientific leadership, industrial competitiveness, and resilient domestic manufacturing.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI/ML/Data science expertise

Bolt Threads applies AI/ML and data science to biologically derived materials R&D, including sequence-structure-function analysis, experimental design, process optimization, and design-build-test-learn workflows. Our team brings practical experience using computational tools to connect protein design, fermentation performance, downstream processing, and material properties, with a focus on accelerating development of scalable, cost-accessible protein materials.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Bolt has generated internal experimental data across protein sequence/design, expression and fermentation performance, downstream processing, analytical characterization, formulation, and material/end-use testing. These data support structure-property-process-performance understanding in protein materials and can inform AI-enabled design-build-test-learn workflows, model development, and data-driven optimization for scalable biomaterials manufacturing.

Experimental or test facilities

Bolt offers integrated biomaterials R&D capabilities spanning protein design, microbial expression and fermentation, downstream processing, analytical characterization, formulation, and performance testing. Our experimental workflows support iterative development from lab concept through process optimization and scale-up, enabling evaluation of both manufacturability and end-use performance for engineered protein materials.

Subject matter expertise

Bolt has deep subject matter expertise in protein-based materials, especially engineered silk-inspired systems, and in translating biological inspiration into manufacturable products. Our team brings experience in protein engineering, biomolecular materials, fermentation-based production, downstream purification, formulation, and structure-property-performance relationships relevant to advanced materials and biotechnology applications.

Other expertise

Bolt’s additional strength is translational industrial R&D: connecting discovery to scalable manufacturing and commercial deployment. We understand how to develop protein materials not only for technical performance, but also for robustness, manufacturability, and cost. This includes experience in process scale-up, productization, and producing tonne-scale protein materials at commercially relevant price points.

Other resources/tools

Bolt can contribute established design-build-test-learn workflows, standardized analytical and performance assays, historical biomaterials and process-development know-how, and practical frameworks for evaluating scalability and cost-in-use. These resources support faster iteration across protein design, bioprocess development, purification, formulation, and end-use validation for next-generation biomaterials.

Relevant Projects

 

DARPA AI BTO / AIxBTO Project – AI-Native Protein Polymer Discovery

Scope: Bolt Threads received and completed a DARPA AI BTO project focused on using AI to transform fragmented scientific literature on protein polymers into a structured discovery engine. The work involved scanning the literature, extracting historical data on sequences, motifs, processing, assembly, and properties, organizing those data into a searchable database, and training models to help design new protein-based materials.

Achievements: The project established a foundation for AI-enabled design-build-test-learn workflows for protein materials and advanced Bolt’s capability to connect literature-derived knowledge with practical materials invention and R&D prioritization.

DARPA ICE Project with Ginkgo Bioworks – Ice-Modulating Protein Materials

Scope: Bolt is part of the DARPA ICE effort with Ginkgo Bioworks to develop biologically sourced or inspired molecules that control ice crystallization, propagation, and adhesion in extreme cold environments. Bolt’s role is focused on translating candidate ice-binding and ice-modulating proteins into usable materials through formulation, stress testing, and application-relevant performance evaluation.

Achievements: This project extends Bolt’s protein-materials platform beyond consumer applications into high-performance functional biomaterials for demanding environments, demonstrating expertise in turning novel proteins into deployable materials systems.

Recombinant Protein Materials Platform – From Molecular Design to Commercial Translation

Scope: Bolt has built an end-to-end platform for recombinant protein materials spanning protein design, microbial expression, fermentation, downstream processing, analytical characterization, formulation, and scale-up. This platform has supported silk-inspired materials and ingredients for personal care and broader advanced-material applications.

Achievements: Bolt translated protein-materials R&D into commercial ingredients and products while building expertise in producing tonne-scale protein materials at commercially relevant price points. The work demonstrates practical capability in linking molecular design to manufacturable materials, with emphasis on scalability, cost, and end-use performance rather than laboratory novelty alone.

Mylo™ Mycelium Leather Platform – Material Development, Performance, and Scale-Up

Scope: Bolt developed Mylo™, a mycelium-based leather alternative produced through controlled growth and processing of mycelial biomass into soft, supple sheet materials. The work spanned process development, materials engineering, finishing, drape and hand-feel optimization, and translation into brand-ready applications.

Achievements: Bolt advanced Mylo into high-visibility product collaborations, developed substantial know-how around mycelium material performance and manufacturability, and built a meaningful patent estate covering mycelium materials, composite structures, property enhancement, and production methods. This work demonstrated Bolt’s ability to develop non-animal biomaterials with tunable aesthetics and functional performance.

Project Idea Summary

Bolt Threads is interested in a project at the intersection of 12-C | Biomolecular Materials and 11-C | AI-Accelerated Science: Correlation to Understanding to create an AI-enabled discovery platform for next-generation protein materials. The goal is to build models that move beyond pattern recognition to uncover mechanistic relationships linking protein sequence, structure, processing, assembly, and material performance. We envision an integrated design-build-test-learn workflow combining literature-derived knowledge, experimental data, molecular and mesoscale modeling, and iterative validation to design protein-based films, fibers, coatings, and hierarchical materials with targeted mechanical, barrier, interfacial, and environmental-response properties.

Bolt would contribute expertise in protein materials, fermentation-based production, downstream processing, formulation, analytical characterization, and translation toward scalable manufacturing. We are seeking partners with strengths in AI/ML, multimodal data integration, scientific reasoning systems, inverse design, HPC and simulation, autonomous or high-throughput experimentation, and advanced structural/material characterization.
