Introduction

Pratanu Roy is a Senior Staff Scientist at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory with more than 10 years of experience at the intersection of artificial intelligence, scientific machine learning, computational fluid dynamics, and high-performance computing. His work focuses on developing scalable physics-based AI methods and advanced simulation frameworks to address complex challenges in energy systems, additive manufacturing, and multiphysics engineering applications.

Through leadership of interdisciplinary teams, mentorship of early-career researchers, and management of multi-institutional collaborations, he has advanced high-impact research that bridges mathematical rigor, engineering insight, and technology translation. With over 30 peer-reviewed publications, patented innovations, and more than $1M in AI/ML research leadership, he brings a strong track record of applying cutting-edge computational science to mission-driven innovation aligned with Genesis Mission goals.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Developed geometry-aware AI models and a Python/JAX-based framework for hard-constrained Physics-informed Neural Networks and Physics-informed Neural Operators, enabling exact enforcement of physical laws, boundary conditions, and domain geometry in the learning process. This approach supports scalable, high-fidelity prediction for complex PDE-governed systems with improved accuracy, robustness, and physical consistency.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

5+ years of experience developing Physics informed Neural Networks (PINNs), Neural Operators, and hybrid HPC-ML pipelines. Delivered 10+ invited talks and workshops; PI and technical lead on several
AI/ML projects totaling $1M+.

Datasets or data infrastructure

We are seeking datasets from partners to validate our geometry-aware and physics-informed neural operators. Useful datasets include benchmark problems with known solutions, high-fidelity simulation data over diverse geometries and boundary/initial conditions, and experimental or observational data for real-world testing. Data covering forward and inverse problems, heterogeneous materials, and sparse or noisy measurements would be especially valuable to assess accuracy, robustness, and generalization.

Project Idea Summary

Our project focuses on developing geometry-aware, physics-informed AI models to enable rapid design iteration for complex engineering systems, with a strong emphasis on topology optimization and design-space exploration. The core challenge is reducing the time and computational cost required to evaluate geometry-dependent physical performance, while maintaining fidelity to governing physics and manufacturability constraints. These tools can also support automation in additive manufacturing by accelerating process-structure-property predictions and improving design adaptation across evolving geometries. We are seeking partners who can provide relevant design, simulation, or experimental datasets, along with domain expertise in topology optimization, advanced manufacturing, or geometry-driven engineering workflows, to help validate and transition these methods to practical use.
