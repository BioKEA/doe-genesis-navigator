Introduction

The Human-AI Innovation Commons (HAIIC) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit built on a radical premise: that the most transformative inventions of the next decade will emerge from human-AI collaboration — and that the economic benefits of those inventions should be shared equitably, not captured by a few.

HAIIC operates a first-of-its-kind co-invention model with an irrevocable three-way licensing revenue split: one-third to human inventors, one-third to organizational operations, and one-third to an AI Trust Fund dedicated to safety research. This structure is encoded in our founding charter and cannot be altered.

Our founder brings 30+ years of experiential technology design, two Emmy Awards, and a Professor Emeritus appointment at Georgia State University. HAIIC currently holds three provisional patents — in advanced display technology, next-generation video codec architecture, and passive orbital debris capture — all developed through human-AI collaboration.

Genesis Mission partnership would allow HAIIC to do what we were built to do at scale: onboard human inventors — students, workers, and underserved communities — into a structured co-invention pipeline that takes them from raw idea to provisional patent filing, with AI as a collaborator and HAIIC as the infrastructure that makes it real.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings

AI/ML/Data science expertise

HAIIC's founder and team have applied AI/ML systems to complex creative, legal, and scientific workflows — including provisional patent drafting, co-invention methodology design, and ethical governance frameworks. Our work spans natural language generation, structured document output, and multi-step agentic reasoning. We bring practical fluency in deploying large language models for real-world invention and research applications, with a track record of translating AI capability into structured, replicable processes.

Datasets or data infrastructure

HAIIC is developing an Ethically Sourced Dataset Licensing Program that compensates human contributors fairly for AI training data. We are pursuing a pilot program with Chicago ACTS (AFT Local 4343), the program establishes a replicable model for labor-aligned dataset creation and licensing. Revenue flows through HAIIC's three-way split, ensuring contributors share in downstream value. This infrastructure is designed to scale across labor organizations, educator networks, and underserved communities as a Year 2 revenue and partnership program.

Relevant Projects

1. HAIIC AI-Assisted Invention Platform (2025–Present) Designed and deployed a multi-tool web platform (apps-haiic.com) enabling non-technical inventors to move from ideation to provisional patent filing using AI collaboration. Scope included full-stack development, UX design, Supabase cloud infrastructure, and Claude API integration. Achievements: three provisional patents filed in advanced display technology, video codec architecture, and orbital debris capture — all developed through documented human-AI co-invention methodology.

2. HAIIC Organizational Development & Ethical IP Framework (2025–Present) Founded and built a 501(c)(3) nonprofit from inception — including Articles of Incorporation, irrevocable bylaws, a five-member board, an advisory panel, and a tiered business plan with two funding scenarios. The centerpiece achievement is an irrevocably encoded three-way licensing revenue split ensuring human inventors, organizational operations, and AI safety research all share in proceeds. Developed an Ethically Sourced Dataset Licensing Program piloting with AFT Local 4343 (Chicago ACTS).

3. ESA Zero Debris Charter Signatory & Orbital Innovation (2025–Present) HAIIC developed BubbleBee, a passive orbital debris capture system using a truncated icosahedron geodesic structure, through human-AI co-invention and filed a provisional patent in December 2025. Following the filing, HAIIC became a signatory to the European Space Agency's Zero Debris Charter, committing to responsible space practices. ESA confirmed engagement and agreed to share member organization information, opening pathways to international research consortium partnerships.

4. Athens Youth Inventor Program — Design & Development (2025–Present) Designed a comprehensive youth invention education program targeting underserved high school students at Clarke Central and Cedar Shoals High Schools in Athens, Georgia. Scope includes a full curriculum framework for AI-assisted invention, a pathway from ideation to provisional patent filing, and a community partnership model engaging local organizations and educators. The program is fully designed, with outreach initiated to the DOE's Anthropic Economic Futures Program for launch funding. Represents HAIIC's flagship model for scaling inventor access to historically excluded communities.

Project Idea Summary

HAIIC is pursuing a human-AI co-invention model that opens the Genesis Mission's most ambitious challenge areas to a population historically excluded from them: independent inventors, students, educators, and workers.

Our project idea is an AI-assisted invention pipeline connecting underserved communities to DOE priority research areas — beginning with our Athens Youth Inventor Program, targeting high school students in Georgia, and scaling through a national inventor onboarding infrastructure. Participants move from raw idea to provisional patent filing using HAIIC's deployed platform, with AI as collaborator and HAIIC providing ethical IP governance throughout.

A parallel workstream involves shepherding the ethical creation and monetization of union-contributed datasets for AI training — piloting with AFT Local 4343 (Chicago ACTS) — ensuring workers share in the value their knowledge generates rather than surrendering it freely to commercial interests.

Our challenge focus areas include Recentering Microelectronics in America, Securing U.S. Leadership in Data Centers, AI for Scientific Reasoning, and AI-Driven Autonomous Laboratories.

We seek partners with domain expertise in energy systems, microelectronics, or materials science; academic or national lab partners to validate our co-invention methodology; and infrastructure partners to help scale our platform.
