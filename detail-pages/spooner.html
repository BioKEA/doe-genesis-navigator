Project Idea Summary

Green Horizon Innovation proposes an AI-accelerated discovery platform for graphene quantum dots (GQDs) synthesized from domestic Alaska coal feedstock, addressing Topic 3B: AI-Enabled Materials Discovery under Securing America's Critical Minerals Supply.The challenge: GQD synthesis from coal involves a high-dimensional parameter space, where traditional one-factor-at-a-time experimentation is prohibitively slow. U.S. dependence on foreign-sourced advanced carbon nanomaterials creates supply chain vulnerability for quantum computing,energy storage, and defense applications.Our approach deploys QM2ARL (Quantum Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning), co-developed with xAI, as a closed-loop experimental design engine. QM2ARL agents autonomously navigate the synthesis parameter space using Bayesian optimization, ENGRAM-ENGN reasoning layer prunes non-viable pathways through eliminative induction, reducing required experiments by an estimated order of magnitude. The system ingests characterization data, each synthesis cycle and updates its predictive model, targeting optimal quantum yield, emission tunability, and single-photon emission for quantum computing applications.  We seek partners with: (1) national laboratory synthesis and characterization facilities (2) coal-derived nanocarbon chemistry and GQD optical properties, (3) quantum device physicists for single-photon emitter integration, and (4) materials informatics experience for AI experiment partners, DOE, Universities.
