Introduction

Hong Fang is an assistant professor in the Department of Physics of Rutgers University in New Jersey. He has worked in various fields at the intersection of condensed-matter physics, materials science, neutron physics, and physical chemistry. His current research focuses on the theory and modeling of solid-state materials and biopolymers, aiming to gain a fundamental understanding of their structure, dynamics, transport, and reactivity behaviors, with applications in energy, sustainability, and other functional areas. 

He is interested in AI-enabled materials discovery, development, and qualification, especially studies related to energy storage materials. He is working on machine-learning atomic potentials for large-scale molecular dynamics simulations and building machine-learning models for high-entropy materials design and high-throughput materials screening.

Hong’s work has appeared in leading scientific journals, including PNAS, Nature Communications, Angew. Chem., Joule, Nature Materials, PRX Energy, and Reports on Progress in Physics. He serves as an Invited Reviewer for the Vehicle Technologies Office of the U.S. Department of Energy. He is an affiliate of the Rutgers Climate and Energy Institute.
