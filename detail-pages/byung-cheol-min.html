    Introduction

    I am Byung-Cheol (“B.C.”) Min, Professor with a joint appointment in the Department of Computer Science and the Department of Intelligent Systems Engineering at the Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering, Indiana University Bloomington, where I direct the SMART Lab. My research lies at the intersection of robotics, AI, and intelligent autonomous systems, with a focus on planning, control, learning, and multi-agent coordination for real-world scientific and engineering challenges.

    My current research includes human-robot interaction, robot learning, multi-robot systems, reinforcement learning, learning from demonstration, generative AI, swarm robotics, and distributed control. I am particularly interested in how AI-driven autonomous systems can reason, adapt, and collaborate in complex environments, and how digital and physical systems can be coupled to accelerate experimentation, decision-making, and deployment.

    Before joining Indiana University in Fall 2025, I served on the faculty at Purdue University from 2015 to 2025 and was Director of the Purdue Applied AI Research Center from 2024 to 2025. My research has been supported by agencies including the NSF, and I am a recipient of the NSF CAREER Award. Through this background, I bring expertise in AI, robotics, and intelligent systems that aligns well with the Genesis Mission’s goals in advanced manufacturing, intelligent automation, and AI-enabled scientific workflows.

    Relevant Projects

    1. NSF Project: Multi-Robot Systems and Human-Robot Interaction (PI)

    This NSF-funded project focused on developing learning-based methods for coordination and decision-making in multi-robot systems operating in dynamic, human-populated environments. The work addressed challenges in distributed control, adaptive planning, and human-aware interaction. Key achievements include the development of scalable multi-agent coordination algorithms, integration of learning-based decision-making into real-time robotic systems, and validation in simulation and physical robot platforms. The project produced multiple peer-reviewed publications and contributed to advancing the state-of-the-art in multi-robot autonomy and human-robot teaming.

    2. Generative AI and Learning-Based Robotics Systems (Multiple Projects)

    Dr. Min has led multiple research efforts exploring the integration of generative AI, reinforcement learning, and learning from demonstration for robotic systems. These projects investigate how AI can enable robots to adapt to new tasks, environments, and human preferences. Achievements include the development of frameworks for task planning, multi-agent coordination, and adaptive behavior generation, as well as the use of large language models to support high-level reasoning in robotic systems. These efforts demonstrate the feasibility of combining generative AI with physical systems for real-world applications.

    3. AI-Driven Applied Research Center Leadership (Director, Purdue Applied AI Research Center)

    As Director of the Purdue Applied AI Research Center (2024–2025), Dr. Min led interdisciplinary efforts to apply AI technologies to real-world challenges across domains such as manufacturing, robotics, and intelligent systems. The center coordinated collaborations among faculty, industry partners, and external stakeholders, supporting applied research projects and fostering innovation in AI-driven solutions. This role provided experience in managing large-scale, interdisciplinary initiatives and translating AI research into practical applications.

    Project Idea Summary

    We are seeking collaboration with DOE National Laboratories and industry partners to develop an AI-driven smart manufacturing framework under Focus Area 1.E (AI-Enabled Smart Manufacturing).

    Our project proposes a generative, agentic AI system integrated with a hyper-realistic digital twin to accelerate the transition from laboratory-scale discovery to scalable manufacturing. The system will autonomously generate, evaluate, and refine manufacturing workflows within a closed-loop simulation environment, reducing reliance on costly physical experiments and improving robustness under real-world variability.

    We are particularly interested in partners who can provide:

    - Industrial datasets (e.g., process data, sensor streams, equipment logs)

    - Experimental or pilot-scale testbeds for validation and sim-to-real transfer

    - HPC or cloud resources for large-scale simulation and model training

    - Domain expertise in manufacturing processes, materials, or industrial systems

    DOE National Labs are valuable for high-fidelity simulation and integration with broader AI/data ecosystems, while industry partners are key to ensuring real-world relevance and deployment pathways.

    This effort aims to demonstrate measurable AI Advantage by accelerating process development, reducing experimental cost, and improving scalability and resilience in advanced manufacturing systems.
