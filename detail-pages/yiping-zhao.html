    Introduction

    Yiping Zhao is a Distinguished Research Professor of Physics at the University of Georgia and a leading researcher in nanotechnology, spectroscopy, and AI-enabled biosensing. His research focuses on nanofabrication of plasmonic structures, surface-enhanced Raman spectroscopy (SERS), and machine learning methods for rapid biological and chemical detection. He is widely recognized for pioneering work in glancing angle deposition (GLAD) nanostructures and their applications in biosensing, environmental monitoring, nanophotonics, hydrogen storage and production, photocatalysts, and nanomotors. 

    Dr. Zhao has authored more than 360 peer-reviewed publications with over 24,000 citations and holds multiple U.S. patents. His research has been supported by federal agencies including NSF, DOE, USDA, NIH, and DOD. 

    At UGA, Dr. Zhao leads interdisciplinary efforts that integrate nanomaterials, spectroscopy, and artificial intelligence to develop next-generation diagnostic technologies. His group has developed AI-assisted Raman and SERS platforms for detecting pathogens, viruses, and environmental contaminants, as well as open science infrastructure such as SpectraGuru, an open-source ecosystem for spectroscopic data analysis and sharing. These efforts align closely with the Genesis Mission’s vision of accelerating biotechnology discovery through advanced sensing technologies, AI-driven data integration, and collaborative innovation across academia, national laboratories, and industry.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    AI models, tools, or software

    Our group develops AI-enabled tools for analyzing spectroscopic data, particularly Raman and SERS, for rapid molecular and biological detection. We build machine learning and deep learning models for spectral classification, regression, and spectral unmixing. We also developed SpectraGuru, an open-source platform for spectral preprocessing, visualization, and AI-assisted analysis with standardized, reproducible workflows. These tools support large-scale spectral databases, AI-driven sensing systems, and portable diagnostics for biological, environmental, and materials applications.

    AI/ML/Data science expertise

    We develop machine learning and deep learning approaches for analyzing complex spectroscopic datasets, particularly Raman and SERS. Expertise includes spectral preprocessing, feature extraction, classification, regression, spectral unmixing, and data augmentation. We use AI methods such as CNNs, transformer models, and statistical learning to enable rapid identification of biomolecules, pathogens, and environmental contaminants. We also integrate AI with experimental sensing systems and develop reproducible data workflows to support large-scale spectral analytics and data-driven discovery.

    Datasets or data infrastructure

    We develop curated spectroscopic datasets and open data infrastructure for Raman and SERS. Our work includes building structured spectral libraries linking spectra with experimental metadata, molecular identity, and measurement conditions. Through the SpectraGuru platform, we provide tools for standardized data ingestion, preprocessing, visualization, and sharing. These datasets support machine learning model development, cross-instrument data harmonization, and reproducible research for applications in biosensing, pathogen detection, environmental monitoring, and materials characterization.

    Experimental or test facilities

    Our laboratory provides facilities for nanomaterial fabrication, spectroscopy, and biosensing experiments. Capabilities include nanostructure fabrication using glancing angle deposition (GLAD), preparation of plasmonic nanostructures and SERS substrates, Raman and SERS spectroscopic measurement systems, and optical characterization tools. These facilities support the development and testing of sensing platforms for biological, environmental, and chemical detection, as well as the generation of high-quality spectroscopic datasets for AI-driven analysis and diagnostic applications.

    Relevant Projects

    1. AI-Driven SERS Platform for Rapid Virus Detection

    Scope: This project developed a rapid diagnostic platform integrating SERS with machine learning (ML) for detecting viral pathogens in biological samples. The work focused on designing highly reproducible plasmonic nanostructures (silver nanorod arrays fabricated by glancing angle deposition) and developing deep learning models capable of analyzing complex spectral signatures from viruses in clinical specimens.

    Achievements: The platform demonstrated rapid detection and classification of multiple respiratory viruses, including SARS-CoV-2 and its variants, directly from biological samples such as nasal swabs. Machine learning models enabled automated interpretation of complex spectral fingerprints and quantitative estimation of viral load. The work produced multiple peer-reviewed publications and contributed to ongoing collaborations with clinical researchers and industry partners to translate the technology into portable diagnostic systems. The project established a framework for AI-assisted spectroscopic pathogen detection with potential applications in infectious disease surveillance and rapid diagnostics.

     

    2. SpectraGuru: Open-Source AI Ecosystem for Spectroscopy

    Scope: SpectraGuru is an open-source cyberinfrastructure project designed to support reproducible analysis, data sharing, and machine learning research in Raman and SERS spectroscopy. The project integrates tools for spectral preprocessing, visualization, metadata management, and AI-assisted analysis within a browser-based platform.

    Achievements: The platform provides standardized workflows for spectral data processing, enabling researchers to perform baseline correction, denoising, normalization, and ML analysis through an accessible interface. SpectraGuru supports curated spectral datasets and promotes FAIR data principles for spectroscopy research. The system has been used globally for spectral analysis and serves as a foundation for developing large-scale spectral libraries and AI models. Supported by the NSF POSE program, the project aims to build a community-driven ecosystem for spectroscopic data infrastructure and AI-enabled scientific discovery.

     

    3. AI-Enhanced SERS Detection of Environmental Contaminants

    Scope: This project focuses on combining Raman spectroscopy, SERS, density functional theory, and ML to detect environmental contaminants such as per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) and other chemical pollutants. 

    Achievements: The project successfully demonstrated sensitive detection and identification of multiple PFAS compounds using Raman and SERS spectroscopy. ML methods were developed to interpret subtle spectral differences and improve chemical classification accuracy. The work provided experimental spectral libraries and computational insights into vibrational modes of PFAS molecules. The results contribute to the development of rapid environmental sensing technologies and AI-enabled chemical monitoring systems, supporting applications in environmental protection, water quality monitoring, and chemical risk assessment.

In particular, we are interested in foundation models capable of interpreting scientific literature, figures, and tables; tools for automated data mining and digitization; AI systems for multimodal reasoning across spectral, textual, and metadata sources; and scalable machine-learning frameworks for building and benchmarking predictive models using large spectroscopic datasets.

Expertise in explainable AI, uncertainty-aware modeling, and scientific knowledge graphs will support the development of the OpenSpectra infrastructure.

    Project Idea Summary

    Modern spectroscopic techniques such as Raman, IR, and XPS generate vast amounts of chemical and biological information. However, most spectral data remain fragmented across publications, proprietary databases, and individual laboratories. This limits reproducibility, cross-study comparison, and the development of reliable ML models.

    Our project aims to develop OpenSpectra, an open, AI-enabled, FAIR-aligned, machine-readable spectral ecosystem for large-scale spectroscopic data integration and analysis. The platform will integrate literature-derived spectra, laboratory datasets, and computational spectra with standardized metadata and provenance tracking. The project will focus on AI-driven spectral analytics, automated spectral interpretation, machine-learning model development, and LLM-enabled knowledge extraction and reasoning, enabling heterogeneous spectroscopic data to be transformed into actionable insights for scientific discovery.

    We seek partners with complementary capabilities in AI/ML for scientific data analytics and multimodal reasoning, high-throughput spectroscopy or automated laboratory platforms for generating large datasets, cloud computing infrastructure for scalable data management, and organizations maintaining spectroscopic databases/libraries. The collaboration will help build a sustainable, community-driven infrastructure that accelerates spectroscopic discovery in chemistry, materials science, environmental monitoring, and biotechnology.
