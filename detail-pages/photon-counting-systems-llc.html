Introduction

We are developing ultra high speed imaging cameras to support megapixel Single Photon Avalanche photoDiode (SPAD) arrays.  Our camera can support over 20 GB/sec continuous recording from SPAD or other imaging sensors.  We also provide FPGA/GPU resources inside the camera for machine learning or HPC on the raw data stream.  Our camera will enable "AI at the Edge" in an off the shelf product.  We also provide deep vacuum cooling of the sensor for the ultimate sensitivity.  Quantum Imaging, Optical qubit reading, and advanced microscopy techniques will all be greatly enhanced.  We look forward to working with you.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
Other expertise

We are experts at image sensor control, characterization, and scientific camera design.  With our DoE SBIR Phase II grant, we are building a camera to enable megapixel single photon imaging at picosecond timescale resolution.  This includes embedded FPGA and GPU resources for "AI at the Edge" or HPC.  We envision providing the camera hardware and performing embedded mapping of partner supplied AI models on the hardware.  This includes both internal and external FPGA/GPU resources.  Continuous recording at 20 GB/sec is an excellent source of training data. 

Relevant Projects

Currently executing DoE SBIR Phase II grant entitled "Accelerating Quantum Imaging with an Ultra High Speed Imaging Platform with End User Programmable FPGA and GPU".  In this effort, we are supporting two different one megapixel Single Photon Avalanche photoDiode (SPAD) image sensors.  We are designing the platform from the component level including a vacuum chamber for deep cooling to reduce dark count rate.  Note the platform can support new sensors as they become available within months.  Our camera can continuously record raw data at 20 GBytes/sec for TBytes.  This enables the acquisition of model training data in an actual instrument focal plane.  Also not the data acquisition electronics can be used with our high speed data sources, not just image sensors.  The one megapixel Pi Imaging SPAD alpha sensor can operate at 76KHz framerate, generating 10 GBytes/sec.  The camera is designed to record raw data at this rate as well as provide real time processing on the data stream.  it is highly anticipated that numerous applications will greatly benefit from, or be enabled by, this camera platform.  It is also expected that this camera, combined with AI models embedded in the camera or nearby external resources, will enable many new applications, instruments, and fundamental scientific discoveries.  

Previously we designed and built a prototype system to operate the 512x512 time gated SwissSPAD2 device continuously at full speed (3.2 GBytes/sec).

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

Our camera can capture TBs of continuous raw data from single photon sensitive megapixel sensors, or other sensors, at 20 GB/sec continuously.  To fully unleash the capabilities of our camera and these emerging high speed, high sensitivity sensors, real time processing or ml inference is required.  Currently our expertise does not include AI model development or training and we seek a partner which such capability.   We want a partner to train an AI model with raw data and then map the model onto the internal and/or external FPGA/GPU hardware for real time processing.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

We would like to partner with groups to develop ultra high speed image processing in real time with AI/ML models using embedded FPGA/GPU resources.  This can include quantum imaging, entangled pair coincidence detection, ghost imaging, and numerous advanced microscopy techniques with quantum or classical light.  Optically read fluorescent qubits, for example cold atom or nitrogen vacancy systems, are additional application domains for our camera.  Massively parallel inference models can greatly enhance the speed of such qubit systems.

Experimental or test facilities

We want to integrate our imaging camera into any imaging or spectroscopy focal planes that will benefit from high time resolution, zero read noise, single photon sensitivity,   The SPAD devices are silicon and support 400nm to 1um imaging.  It is possible to integrate other devices in our camera, i.e. InP SPADs, etc. for IR work.  

For example, entangled photon quantum microscopy systems or trapped cold atom fluorescence qubit reading in quantum computing applications, will benefit from our camera and SPAD sensors.  

Project Idea Summary

"AI at the edge" promises unprecedented real time imaging analysis across numerous scientific imaging modalities.  Wide field single photon sensitivity, at picosecond timescale resolution, provides an unprecedented window on single molecule interactions across entire samples concurrently.  The enormous data rate of megapixel SPAD sensors presents a data processing and storage challenge, with current devices exceeding 10 GBytes/sec of data production.  Integrating AI models into our camera system's FPGA or GPU resources would enable real time processing, unleashing a new window on single molecule activity.  

During a Phase II project timeline, wide field event streaming SPAD devices are likely to become available, supported by our camera.  These devices timestamp and continuously stream the arrival of single photons across the sensor, perfect for entangled photon coincident measurement, enabling quantum light microscopy unprecedented performance with ultra low illumination levels, eliminating photobleaching and phototoxicity.  Concurrent widefield imaging of both Raman signatures and fluorescence will be possible, providing unparalleled mapping and tracking of non-labeled and labeled molecules alike.

We seek partners with advanced microscopy modalities that recognize the potential of wide field single photon sensitivity and picosecond time resolution coupled with massively parallel inference models embedded in a camera system to enable scientific imaging "AI at the edge".
