    Introduction

    Exabyte Inc., creator of Mat3ra.com, builds open, cloud-native digital infrastructure for materials R&D. Mat3ra.com provides an interoperable environment that connects physics-based simulation, structured data, and AI-ready workflows so teams can move quickly from computation to insight to experiment. Our focus is on openness, reproducibility, and rapid execution: lowering barriers to advanced modeling, organizing data for trustworthy AI/ML, and enabling theory-in-the-loop and autonomous experimentation across DOE-relevant research environments. 

    Exabyte Inc. has a proven federal track record, with 8 SBIR/STTR awards totaling $3.89M across DOE, DoD, and NIST, including DOE-sponsored projects to develop a FAIR digital ecosystem for DOE materials modeling, support wide-scale adoption through visually enhanced HPC codes for semiconductor R&D, and create an accessible open-source software framework for multi-scale modeling. 

    Mat3ra.com supports and accelerates DOE research, including Mission Genesis initiatives, by helping researchers share methods, generate trusted datasets faster, and deploy scalable digital workflows across labs, facilities, and partner organizations.

    Offerings & Experience

    Offerings
    AI models, tools, or software

    Mat3ra.com is an open, cloud-native software platform for materials R&D that connects physics-based simulation, structured data, and AI-ready workflows. It supports automated execution of DFT, molecular dynamics, and related modeling workflows, organizes outputs for downstream AI/ML use, and enables theory-in-the-loop workflows for autonomous experimentation. The platform is built to accelerate DOE-relevant digital research and collaboration.

    AI/ML/Data science expertise

    Exabyte Inc. applies AI/ML in materials R&D through workflow automation, ML-ready data generation, and integration of learned surrogate approaches such as neural-network force fields. Our work includes organizing simulation and process data for model development, enabling uncertainty-aware campaigns, and supporting digital workflows that connect prediction, validation, and iteration across DOE-relevant research programs.

    Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

    Mat3ra.com develops and deploys secure, multi-tenant, cloud-native computing environments for materials modeling through major cloud providers, such as Google, AWS, Azure, Oracle, and others. It integrates high-performance computing with browser-accessible workflows, APIs, and automation to help users run advanced simulations more quickly and reproducibly. Prior work includes cloud infrastructure, distributed job orchestration, and support for scalable workflows aligned with DOE HPC environments.

    Datasets or data infrastructure

    Mat3ra.com provides data infrastructure for capturing, structuring, and exchanging materials modeling data with provenance. Exabyte Inc.'s prior work includes DOE- and NIST-sponsored efforts on FAIR materials data, modular data standards, and repeatable workflow metadata for semiconductor R&D. These capabilities support trusted dataset generation, reproducibility, and data sharing across computational and experimental teams.

    Subject matter expertise

    Exabyte Inc.'s technical work spans computational materials science, semiconductor and microelectronics R&D, multiscale modeling, and digital materials workflows. Exabyte Inc.'s teams have built and applied capabilities around DFT, molecular dynamics, quantum and atomistic simulation, workflow orchestration, and data standards to support federal R&D programs across DOE, DoD, and NIST.

    Other expertise

    Exabyte Inc. brings experience in open and interoperable workflow design, FAIR data practices, and research software deployment for collaborative science. Prior DOE-sponsored efforts include developing a FAIR digital ecosystem for DOE materials modeling, WAVE-HPC workflows for semiconductor R&D, and an open-source software framework for multi-scale modeling.

    Other resources/tools

    Mat3ra.com also provides APIs, browser-based user workflows, and collaborative data exchange capabilities that help reduce barriers to advanced modeling. These tools are designed to make complex simulation workflows easier to access, repeat, and share across distributed teams and partner organizations working on DOE-relevant research.

    Relevant Projects

    Exabyte Inc. has executed federal R&D programs as a Small Business prime across DOE, DoD, and NIST, including 8 SBIR/STTR awards. Representative projects from the past 5 years include:

    1. USAF Phase II (FA8650-22-C-5018, FA8649-20-C-0261), "Developing a digital ecosystem for Air Force materials modeling." Exabyte Inc. developed and deployed a cloud-native digital materials R&D environment to support multiscale modeling, high-throughput simulation, and AI/ML-enabled workflows. Achievements included secure cloud-based infrastructure, workflow automation for DFT, AIMD, QM/MM, and photonic and microelectronics use cases, plus support for digital-thread and virtual prototyping workflows across AFRL research areas.

    2. NIST Phase II (70NANB24H205), "Developing Data Standards for Accelerated Digital R&D of Semiconductor Materials from Nanoscale." Exabyte Inc. developed modular data standards for semiconductor R&D, including process recipes, provenance, deposition and thin-film growth metadata, and simulation context. Key achievements included reducing data heterogeneity, improving repeatability and traceability, and enabling downstream analytics and AI/ML on manufacturing-relevant data.

    3. DOE Phase I (DE-SC0021514), "Developing a FAIR digital ecosystem for DOE materials modeling." Exabyte Inc. developed a FAIR-oriented digital environment for DOE materials modeling workflows, with emphasis on structured data, provenance, and reproducible execution. Achievements included strengthening interpretability and interoperability across simulations and datasets, improving traceability, and establishing a reusable digital foundation for collaborative computational materials research.

    4. DOE Phase I (DE-SC0023566), "Wide-scale Adoption through Visually-Enhanced HPC codes (WAVE-HPC) for semiconductor R&D." Exabyte Inc. developed workflows using DOE-relevant software stacks for accelerated semiconductor materials R&D. Achievements included benchmarking and optimization on heterogeneous CPU/GPU systems, visually enhanced analysis of HPC outputs, and improved accessibility of advanced HPC workflows for materials researchers.

    5. DOE Phase I (DE-SC0022390), "Accessible Open-Source Software Framework for Multi-Scale Modeling." Exabyte Inc. developed an open-source, cross-platform framework connecting first-principles modeling, atomistic simulation, and downstream analysis in one interoperable workflow. Achievements included Python-based orchestration, consistent machine-readable data representations, and faster integration of multi-domain modeling tools in HPC research environments.

    Project Idea Summary

    Exabyte Inc. seeks to apply Mat3ra.com to DOE-relevant materials challenges where physics-based modeling, structured data, and AI/ML can accelerate discovery, screening, and decision-making for real-world applications. The focus area is an open, reproducible digital workflow for materials R&D that connects simulation, data management, and theory-in-the-loop analysis to support faster iteration between computation and experiment.

    We are seeking partners with strong materials use cases and experimental validation capabilities, including experimentalists and applied R&D groups at DOE labs and in industry. We are also seeking compute infrastructure providers that can support shared cloud or on-premise HPC deployments, and AI/HPC code developers whose tools can be integrated into collaborative, production-oriented materials workflows on Mat3ra.com.
