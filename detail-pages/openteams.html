Introduction

OpenTeams brings deep private sector expertise in the application of open-source technologies to machine learning and artificial intelligence. We participated in the creation, maintenance and development of foundational scientific and AI open source software, from scipy to numpy to pytorch. We are eager to partner with National Laboratories, industry partners, and academic institutions to form multi-institutional teams. Here are the specific capabilities we bring:

OpenTeams AI Ecosystem: We provide comprehensive open-source technology solutions, offering training, deployment, and engineering services across more than 680 open-source technologies. We bring deep, specialized expertise in building secure AI infrastructure.

Our Genesis Mission Vision: We are focused on developing "Applied AI Engineers"—scientific translators who can bridge the gap between raw data and actionable insights to resolve capability bottlenecks in critical Department of Energy research.

Open Source Advocacy: We strongly believe the Genesis Mission should focus on open-source platforms. We believe this approach drastically reduces financial barriers for students and academic institutions, and guarantees technical independence for American scientists.

Workforce Pipeline: We are founding partners of the Applied AI Society, a national community of early-career professionals and emerging AI practitioners. This connects students to hands-on applied AI experiences through a decentralized network of chapters.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Our open source Nebari platform represents our engineers’ decades of expertise providing modular, open source software for scientific computing, data science, and AI/ML.  Nebari lays a secure, consistent abstraction layer over any major CSP or on-premise cluster.  We install hardened, integrated “software packs”  offering SOTA tooling for building AI capabilities.  Capabilities built on Nebari can be redeployed across heterogeneous compute environments, including air-gapped.  The Department of War and NASA  use Nebari to conduct AI/ML workloads in up to IL5

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our expertise in open source AI/ML/DS is unrivaled, we are embedded deeply in its lingua franca - Python.  NumPy, SciPy, and JupyterLab are a few of the industry standard tools that we helped create.  We have made more contributions to PyTorch (combined with our sister PBC, Quansight) than any organization except Meta and Intel.  Some of the world’s leading AI technology firms including Meta and Google hire our engineers to optimize their AI/ML stacks.  Several of the nation’s top five banks and hedge funds contract with us for optimizing and securing algorithmic tools.

Subject matter expertise

Our engineers are from science and engineering fields backed by strong software engineering discipline. This makes us a solid partner to research organizations who require stronger software engineering domain expertise needed to move research into practical production scenarios. For example,  we provide specialized technical infrastructure and consulting for the Earth System Sciences, focusing on scaling the PyData stack to handle multi-terabyte geophysical datasets. Other expertise

As stewards of the open-source AI/ML/DS ecosystem, OpenTeams provides direct access to core maintainers of key open-source projects. Our upstream influence gives customers priority insight into roadmaps, the ability to shape features to mission needs, faster resolution of critical issues, and optimized performance across diverse hardware. This ensures reduced risk, faster innovation cycles, and long-term sustainability without vendor lock-in, enabling DOE systems to evolve with the ecosystem rather than react to it.

Other resources/tools

Engineers at OpenTeams sit on steering committees and hold leadership positions in various open source ecosystems and standards committees, including the Python Software Foundation, Consortium for Python Data API Standards, Conda-Forge, PyPi, HoloViz and many foundational AI and Scientific open source communities.

Relevant Projects

Customer: DoW Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Officer (CDAO)

For the Joint AI Test and Evaluation (T&E) Infrastructure Capability (JATIC) program, OpenTeams supports AI T&E through development of a modular, MOSA-aligned reference implementation (“Checkmaite”) in collaboration with MIT Lincoln Laboratory. The platform integrates datasets, models, metrics, and augmentation pipelines into reproducible workflows and wraps both open-source and proprietary vendor tools into a unified evaluation framework. Deployments span AWS GovCloud and on-premises infrastructure up to IL5 via Nebari. This enables consistent, portable evaluation of AI systems in degraded, disconnected, and operational settings, supporting scalable and extensible validation of mission-critical capabilities.


Customer: Google

Across multiple contracts, OpenTeams has supported core AI/ML framework development and performance optimization. For PyTorch/XLA/TPU, we are entering our third year contributing to compiler and execution performance, including work on GPU backends and the transition to a new pytorch-tpu library enabling modern, out-of-tree accelerator support. In the JAX ecosystem, we provide engineering support on Flax, contributing features, bug fixes, and developer tooling. We have also been engaged to support model training performance and feature engineering for internal deep learning workloads. These efforts reflect deep expertise in optimizing AI systems across hardware architectures and contributing directly to upstream open-source frameworks.

Customer: Sandia National Laboratories

OpenTeams served as prime contractor to enable AI/ML workflows in secure, air-gapped environments. We enhanced the open-source conda-mirror tool (previously defunct), adding capabilities to create and maintain private package repositories that could be transferred via physical media (e.g., DVD) into disconnected systems. We also developed mechanisms for incremental updates, allowing environments to be patched and extended without full re-deployment. This work enabled Sandia to manage scientific Python and AI software stacks in high-security settings. OpenTeams continues to provide support for Conda and related open-source ecosystems in air-gapped environments, ensuring sustainability and operational continuity.

We partner with leading cloud providers, but large-scale AI and HPC workloads remain cost-intensive. High-quality, well-governed datasets are the primary constraint in AI/ML success. We seek partners with access to mission-relevant data and robust data infrastructure, including pipelines, labeling, and governance frameworks. Collaboration ensures data readiness, interoperability, and reproducibility, enabling scalable and trustworthy AI solutions for Genesis use cases.

Subject matter expertise

While OpenTeams brings deep AI/ML and open-source expertise, we seek partners with domain knowledge across the energy ecosystem. Combining our technical capabilities with mission-specific expertise enables more accurate problem framing, better model performance, and operational relevance. Together, we deliver solutions that are both technically advanced and grounded in real-world mission impact.

Project Idea Summary

We propose a portable AI intelligence hub for the Genesis Mission: an “on-prem HuggingFace” for DOE, enabling end-to-end AI/ML workflows to be developed once and deployed seamlessly across national labs, user facilities, instruments, and secure or air-gapped environments while ensuring data and model provenance and secure role based access. The core challenge is enabling reproducible, interoperable AI across fragmented compute ecosystems spanning cloud, HPC, edge, and disconnected systems.

OpenTeams has already built key components of this vision. Nebari enables consistent deployment of full AI platforms across infrastructure. Our engineers, including core maintainers of foundational open-source libraries, have optimized AI workloads across diverse hardware architectures. We have also operationalized workflows to package and move mixed language AI software environments into secure, air-gapped environments used in national lab contexts.

We seek partners with access to DOE facilities, experimental data pipelines, advanced compute (including novel and emerging architectures), and domain expertise in energy systems. Together, we aim to create a unified, portable AI layer that accelerates scientific discovery, enables cross-facility collaboration, and ensures AI systems can operate wherever the mission requires.
