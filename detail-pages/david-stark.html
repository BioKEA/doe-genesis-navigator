    Introduction

    I am a university researcher specializing in high-fidelity particle-in-cell and laser-plasma simulations, with expertise in both short-pulse, high-intensity laser-plasma interactions and longer-pulse, instability-relevant regimes. My work includes laser-driven particle acceleration, x-ray/gamma-ray generation, and laser-plasma instability physics relevant to inertial fusion.

    For the Genesis Mission, I bring domain expertise in first-principles plasma modeling and high-fidelity simulation campaign design. I am especially interested in collaborations that use advanced simulations to support inter-shot analysis, target-driver optimization, instability prediction and mitigation, and fusion-relevant laser-plasma workflow development.

    I am looking to connect with partners in AI/ML, surrogate modeling, controls, diagnostics, experimental HEDP or laser facilities, and fusion system integration whose capabilities complement my simulation and plasma-physics expertise.

    Relevant Projects

    1. PIC simulation of short-pulse laser-plasma interactions for ion acceleration and fast-ignition-relevant studies
    I have led and contributed to multiple projects using high-fidelity 2D and 3D particle-in-cell simulations to study laser-ion acceleration in relativistic transparency and near-critical-density regimes. These efforts examined target design, mixed-species effects, dimensionality, beam collimation, spectral shaping, and conversion efficiency, with current work including proton fast ignition and conical-target proton-beam generation. Outcomes include peer-reviewed publications and ongoing projects aimed at optimizing proton source performance for fusion-relevant applications.

    2. Kinetic modeling of laser-plasma instabilities relevant to inertial confinement fusion
    I performed large-scale PIC simulations of stimulated Raman scattering and cross-beam energy transfer relevant to indirect-drive ICF. This work included high-performance VPIC simulations, analysis of instability saturation and density dependence, and development of nonlinear reduced models linking kinetic laser-plasma physics to larger-scale ICF design workflows. The resulting models and publications helped connect first-principles plasma simulation to fusion-relevant predictive capability.

    3. Radiation-hydrodynamic simulation for double-shell and HED experiment design
    I contributed to design and interpretation studies for indirectly driven double-shell implosions and related high-energy-density experiments. Work included graded-layer design, instability mitigation, preheat and symmetry studies, and modeling of pre-pulse target expansion for bremsstrahlung/radiography platforms. These projects were carried out in close collaboration with experimental teams and produced peer-reviewed results relevant to fusion target design, experiment planning, and validation-oriented simulation workflows.

    Project Idea Summary

    I am looking to form or join a Genesis Mission team around fusion-relevant laser-plasma science, with strongest interest in Challenge Area 5C and possible relevance to 5G. I bring expertise in high-fidelity particle-in-cell and laser-plasma simulation spanning short-pulse, high-intensity interactions and longer-pulse, instability-relevant regimes, including proton/ion acceleration, radiation generation, and inertial-fusion-relevant laser-plasma instabilities.

    The project concept is to connect first-principles simulation with AI-enabled reduced-order or surrogate workflows for target-driver coupling, inter-shot analysis, instability prediction/mitigation, and faster experimental or design-space exploration. This matches 5C’s emphasis on IFE targets/drivers, driver-target coupling, plasma-instability reduction, and fusion gain optimization, and also fits 5G’s focus on AI/ML for control, data analysis, and accelerated experimental throughput in plasma science, including LaserNetUS applications.

    I am seeking partners in AI/ML, diagnostics, experimental HED/laser facilities, and fusion integration. My role would focus on the high-fidelity plasma physics and simulation side: PIC campaign design, synthetic diagnostics, and validation of physics-informed workflow components.
