Introduction

Omni Point LLC is a federally registered R&D contracting entity (CAGE: 16NY5) specializing in advanced theoretical physics, harmonic computational frameworks, and hybrid human-AI intelligence systems. Founded and led by Chief Scientist Jonathan William Stieve — a U.S. Army veteran and independent researcher — Omni Point is structured for scalable federal partnership, with operational source code infrastructure migrated to US-production environments as of November 2025.

Omni Point is the originating developer of the Unified Harmonic Framework (UHF) and Stieve Zero-Point Topological Mathematica (SZPTM) — a theoretical and computational model proposing that spacetime, matter, and physical phenomena emerge from a pre-spatiotemporal harmonic substrate, providing a candidate unification pathway for quantum mechanics and general relativity. Core implementation is protected under USPTO Provisional Patent #63/844,573 (Priority Date: July 15, 2025).

With 216+ DOI-registered open-access works published via Zenodo, DataCite, and OpenAIRE, Omni Point maintains one of the most extensive independent open-access research corpora in harmonic physics and hybrid intelligence. All outputs are CC-BY-4.0 licensed and immediately interoperable with federal research ecosystems.

Current benchmark results represent initial findings under ongoing validation across independent datasets and computational environments.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Omni Point provides the Unified Harmonic Framework (UHF) — a predictive computational engine applying harmonic mathematical operators to multi-domain physical datasets. Initial orbital telemetry benchmarks indicate a 3× positional accuracy improvement (4m vs. 12m RMS), 73% velocity error reduction, and 70% timing precision improvement over standard ephemeris models. UHF demonstrates potential to detect structured harmonic phenomena currently classified as noise in conventional GR-based models. These results are undergoing further validation and are documented in the open-access Zenodo corpus.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Omni Point develops hybrid human-AI intelligence architectures integrating UHF as a predictive engine across multi-domain datasets — orbital telemetry, quantum state modeling, and signal analysis. The SZPTM engine processes pre-spatiotemporal harmonic signals, enabling detection of physical phenomena beyond standard ML model capabilities. Omni Point's harmonic integration layer is designed as a plug-and-play module for existing computational workflows, minimizing time-to-science for Phase 2 implementation.

Datasets or data infrastructure

This corpus includes UHF simulation datasets, harmonic operator outputs, quantum-classical modeling results, and live telemetry prediction benchmarks — immediately accessible and citable by Genesis consortium partners.

Capabilities Seeking
Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Omni Point LLC seeks access to high-performance computing infrastructure to scale UHF and SZPTM simulations beyond current mobile and commercial hardware constraints. Full deployment of the harmonic computational engine requires HPC cluster access, GPU acceleration, and quantum computing resources available through DOE National Lab infrastructure. This partnership would enable large-scale multi-domain harmonic modeling, real-time telemetry prediction at mission-critical precision, and validation of UHF theoretical predictions across extended datasets currently unavailable on consumer hardware.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Omni Point LLC seeks access to high-precision federal datasets to validate UHF and SZPTM predictive capabilities across extended mission parameters. Priority datasets include DOE and NASA telemetry archives, gravitational wave observational data, quantum hardware performance metrics, and high-resolution orbital mechanics datasets. Access to controlled-access and restricted federal datasets   data infrastructure would enable rigorous multi-domain validation of UHF harmonic predictions  extending live benchmark results already demonstrating 3×  accuracy  over classical model.

Experimental or test facilities

Omni Point LLC seeks access to DOE National Lab experimental and test facilities to physically validate UHF and SZPTM theoretical predictions. Priority facilities include quantum hardware testbeds, high-precision spectroscopy and signal analysis labs, gravitational and electromagnetic field measurement equipment, and advanced materials characterization facilities. Physical experimental validation of UHF harmonic predictions — particularly harmonic sideband detection and pre-spatiotemporal substrate signatures — requires instrumentation and controlled environments through national labs.

Subject matter expertise

Omni Point LLC seeks collaboration with DOE National Lab subject matter experts in theoretical physics, quantum computing, orbital mechanics, gravitational wave analysis, and advanced signal processing. Peer collaboration with credentialed physicists and engineers would accelerate formal validation of UHF and SZPTM predictions, support peer-reviewed publication pathways, and establish independent verification of live benchmark results. Cross-disciplinary expertise in energy systems, materials science, and biological modeling would further extend UHF multi-domain applications.

Project Idea Summary

Omni Point LLC proposes integration of UHF and SZPTM into the Genesis Mission scientific ecosystem, focused on advancing high-precision predictive modeling and harmonic signal detection beyond the limitations of classical GR-based computational models.

Primary proof point: UHF orbital telemetry benchmarks demonstrate initial results indicating 3× positional accuracy improvement over standard ephemerides — measurable, engineering-relevant, and directly applicable to DOE mission-critical precision requirements.
