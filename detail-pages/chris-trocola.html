Introduction

AICT (Assurance | Intelligence | Certification | Trust) is an independent enterprise risk management firm providing compliance frameworks, organizational readiness assessments, and certification services for AI deployment in regulated industries.
Our research-based methodology addresses the documented 70-80% AI project failure rate by evaluating governance maturity, cultural readiness, and compliance architecture across 68+ regulatory standards—including NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, ISO 42001, EEOC guidelines, and sector-specific requirements spanning 12 industries.
Active Research Data program publishing frameworks, white papers, and industry analysis on AI governance convergence, organizational failure patterns, and multi-jurisdictional compliance architecture.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: AICT & Verified Safe AI Certification

Scope of Work: As CEO, I led the development of the Verified Safe AI certification, a proprietary audit framework mapped to NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, and UL standards. I built the AICT Master Source Library, an empirical dataset tracking global AI litigation and failures to provide real-world risk benchmarks. This data is currently being used to unify a coalition of organizations to establish a standardized AI Deployment framework and a centralized enforcement body for the United States.

Achievements: Successfully launched the Certified Shadow AI Practitioner (CSAP) designation. The framework has seen rapid international traction, with non-US companies adopting AICT standards for cross-border compliance. We established strategic pilot programs for AI risk audits with global insurance leaders, successfully transitioning AI governance from theoretical policy into a technical, insurable business process. Currently in negotiations to expand operations with a dedicated Asia chapter to harmonize global AI safety standards.

Project 2: Mentis Technologies – Solar Industry Compliance & Reform

Scope of Work: Founder and IP Architect of a high-compliance infrastructure for the renewable energy sector. I collaborated with government agencies and state legislators (2023–2025) to develop AI-driven onboarding, automated KYC, and contract validation systems. This project aimed to solve systemic consumer protection failures and fraudulent sales tactics within the $100B+ solar industry.

Achievements: Featured in TIME Magazine and national news for exposing regulatory gaps. Successfully launched 28 retail storefronts in Walmart using standardized, protected contract structures. Within 90 days, the framework secured nearly $500M in contracts. This work directly influenced state-level consumer protection laws and proved that technical governance can stabilize volatile, high-stakes industries.

Project 3: AI Is Eating Your Business Summit (Arizona Tech Week)

Scope of Work: I am the primary organizer of the first solutions-focused AI Safety Summit, held during Arizona Tech Week (April 8, 2026). The project scope includes orchestrating a high-level assembly of 200+ enterprise leaders, including CTOs, compliance officers, and legal teams. I designed the summit’s technical curriculum to move beyond industry "hype," focusing instead on actionable frameworks for securing AI infrastructure, mitigating "Shadow AI" proliferation, and reconciling state-level regulations with federal mandates.

Achievements: Successfully secured a speaking roster of industry veterans from AICT, Silicon Oasis, and Dell Technologies. The summit established the "Network Path" to AI compliance—a streamlined 3-6 month roadmap for enterprise readiness. By prohibiting "stage-selling" and focusing on peer-vetted, educational content, the project has created a defensible model for AI governance that has already reached its 200-person capacity, signaling a massive market demand for the Verified Safe AI standards.

Project Idea Summary

The "Agent Visibility Black Hole" is a critical security gap caused by the rapid penetration of frameworks like OpenClaw. Unlike human users, autonomous agents currently lack standardized identity, ownership data, or operational boundaries, despite having system-level permissions. This creates pervasive "Shadow AI" vulnerabilities across government and private sectors. Our focus is Agentic Accountability, establishing a verifiable identity for every autonomous entity interacting with critical infrastructure.

We are building the National AI Agent Registry via our proprietary IDEN Framework. This "Digital License Plate" system uses Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP) to verify an agent’s safety profile and ownership without exposing sensitive code. Integrated with our Verified Safe AI™ certification, this platform moves beyond passive observation into Active Enforcement. We seek a consortium of DOE National Labs for exascale computing to scale ZKP protocols, Academic partners for mathematical validation, and National Security SMEs to refine enforcement for high-consequence sectors like Energy and Defense.
