Introduction

 

VitalX LLC

 (UEI: HVF6RHEXS6R8) is a West Virginia-based small business redeveloping 2,800+ acres of legacy coal mining infrastructure in McDowell County, Appalachia into an integrated energy, critical minerals, and data campus. Our site hosts confirmed light rare earth element (LREE) concentrations of 362 ppm — exceeding DOE's 300 ppm economic threshold — along with co-located critical minerals including Cobalt, Vanadium, Barium, Zirconium, and Niobium, verified by XRF analysis through the Appalachian Critical Mineral Institute.

VitalX brings a uniquely asset-rich platform: three AMLIS-confirmed AML inventory sites anchoring a reclamation-first development strategy, a 41-MW closed-loop pumped storage hydropower facility leveraging a 600-foot natural elevation differential, mine-water geothermal cooling infrastructure, and hyperscale data center capacity. We hold an active relationship with NETL and a signed Letter of Collaboration with Dr. Bruce S. Kang, Ph.D. at West Virginia University for Mechano-Chemical Beneficiation (MCB)-enhanced REE extraction.

Our mission aligns directly with the Genesis objective: transforming domestic legacy energy liabilities into sovereign-grade critical mineral and clean energy assets. VitalX is seeking research, technology, and commercialization partners to accelerate extraction process validation, site characterization, and integrated campus development in one of Appalachia's most economically distressed community's

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
Experimental or test facilities

VitalX operates a 2,800+ acre legacy coal mining site in McDowell County, WV serving as an active field-scale test facility for critical mineral extraction, subsurface energy characterization, and integrated infrastructure development. The site features confirmed LREE concentrations (362 ppm, XRF-verified), co-located critical minerals (Co, V, Ba, Zr, Nb), a 600-ft natural elevation differential suitable for pumped storage hydropower, and mine-water thermal resources. Available for in-situ process validation, pilot extraction, and sensor/instrumentation deployment.

Subject matter expertise

Principal Investigator Jerry Salyers brings 15+ years of Appalachian coal mining operations experience (2010–2018 active operations), deep knowledge of SMCRA regulatory frameworks, AML reclamation strategy, and legacy mine infrastructure redevelopment. VitalX holds three AMLIS-confirmed AML inventory records and has navigated WVDEP permitting, federal grant formation (NETL, DOE, AMLER), and site characterization in one of the nation's most complex legacy mining environments. Active NETL relationship and WVU research partnership (Dr. Bruce S. Kang, MCB-enhanced REE extraction)

Other expertise

VitalX offers integrated site development expertise spanning reclamation-to-revenue conversion of legacy energy infrastructure — including AML liability quantification, critical mineral resource assessment, federal funding strategy (DOE FOAs, AMLER, USDA), and multi-stakeholder land and mineral rights coordination. The company provides a replicable Appalachian redevelopment model pairing ESG reclamation narrative with commercial-grade energy and mineral asset monetization, directly applicable to other legacy coal regions across the eastern U.S.

Relevant Projects

1 VitalX Integrated Energy & Critical Minerals Campus — McDowell County, WV (2023–Present)

Scope: Redevelopment of 2,800+ acres of legacy coal infrastructure into an integrated campus combining REE/critical mineral extraction, pumped storage hydropower, mine-water geothermal cooling, and hyperscale data center infrastructure. Achievements include: XRF-confirmed LREE concentration of 362 ppm exceeding DOE economic threshold; identification of co-located Co, V, Ba, Zr, and Nb via Appalachian Critical Mineral Institute analysis; signed Letter of Collaboration with Dr. Bruce S. Kang (WVU) for MCB-enhanced REE extraction; active submissions to NETL DE-FOA-0003583, DOE DE-FOA-0003600, and FY2026 WVDEP AMLER ($8M request); and engagement with Rep. Carol D. Miller (WV-1) for congressional support.

2. Sandy River Innovations Highwall Mining & REE Recovery — McDowell County, WV (2023–2024)

Scope: Development of a surface mine permit application (SOAP) for a proposed highwall mining operation on approximately 16 acres with projected output of 175,000 tons/year. Project integrated incidental metallurgical coal extraction under SMCRA 30 CFR Part 707 with REE recovery framing, establishing a regulatory pathway for dual-commodity recovery from legacy refuse and highwall features. Achievements include completed SOAP documentation, AML highwall characterization (AMLIS WV003378, Priority 2), and establishment of the reclamation-cost/commercial-activity separation framework required for AMLER eligibility.

3. Fourpole Creek Watershed REE & Reclamation Project — Mingo County, WV (2024–2025)

Scope: AMLER grant application development for highwall bench reclamation paired with an onsite REE separation facility in the Fourpole Creek watershed. Work involved AML cost segregation analysis distinguishing AMLER-eligible reclamation expenditures from commercial REE processing infrastructure, leverage ratio modeling incorporating private investment and projected REE/coal revenue, and coordination with WVDEP on permitting boundaries. Achievements include completed federal grant application package, financial pro forma, and replicable cost-allocation framework for future combined reclamation/extraction projects.

Capabilities Seeking
AI models, tools, or software

VitalX seeks AI/ML tools capable of processing multi-modal geospatial, geochemical, and sensor data to optimize critical mineral targeting and extraction sequencing across a 2,800+ acre legacy mine site. Specific needs include: predictive ore body delineation from XRF/borehole datasets, AI-assisted process parameter optimization for mechanochemical beneficiation (MCB) of REE-bearing material, and autonomous laboratory workflow integration to accelerate bench-to-pilot scale validation of novel extraction protocols.

Datasets or data infrastructure

VitalX seeks access to national critical mineral geochemical reference datasets, DOE/NETL legacy mine characterization databases, and USGS mineral resource data layers for cross-validation of site-specific XRF and geospatial findings. Data infrastructure support for integrating field sensor streams, mine-water chemistry monitoring, and drone-based topographic surveys into a unified site digital twin would directly accelerate process design and federal reporting for active grant submissions.

Other expertise

Seeking subject matter expertise in rare earth element hydrometallurgy, mechanochemical processing scale-up, and mine-water treatment for critical mineral recovery. Partners with demonstrated experience transitioning bench-scale REE extraction processes to pilot or commercial scale — particularly from unconventional feedstocks such as coal refuse, acid mine drainage, and legacy tailings — are strongly preferred. Regulatory and environmental permitting expertise for Appalachian legacy mine sites is also valued.

Other resources/tools

VitalX seeks pilot-scale processing equipment, mobile or deployable laboratory infrastructure, and access to DOE user facilities for advanced materials characterization (e.g., XRD, ICP-MS, SEM-EDS) to complement existing XRF site data. Access to techno-economic modeling tools and life cycle assessment frameworks applicable to domestic REE supply chain development would support both federal reporting requirements and commercial offtake negotiations currently under early development

Project Idea Summary


 VitalX LLC is developing an integrated critical minerals and data infrastructure campus on 2,800+ acres of legacy coal mining land in McDowell County, WV — purpose-built to support sovereign U.S. data center capacity anchored by domestic clean energy and critical mineral supply chains.
The campus design pairs hyperscale data center infrastructure with on-site power generation (41-MW closed-loop pumped storage hydropower, 600-ft elevation differential) and mine-water geothermal cooling — eliminating two of the primary constraints facing U.S. data center siting: energy availability and thermal management. Co-located REE and critical mineral recovery (confirmed 362 ppm LREE; Co, V, Ba, Zr, Nb) provides a direct domestic supply pathway for the hardware supply chain serving those same facilities.
VitalX targets the "Securing U.S. Leadership in Data Centers," "Securing America's Critical Minerals Supply," and "Unleashing Subsurface Strategic Energy Assets" challenge areas. We bring the field site, AML reclamation framework, active NETL and DOE grant infrastructure, and an existing WVU research partnership for MCB-enhanced REE extraction.
Seeking partners with expertise in: data center energy optimization and siting; AI-driven subsurface energy asset characterization; and critical mineral process scale-up. National lab and industry partners with HPC or hyperscale infrastructure interest are strongly encouraged.
