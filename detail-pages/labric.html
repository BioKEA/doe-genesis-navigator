Introduction

Labric is a data infrastructure company currently deployed at multiple DOE national laboratories. We connect directly to lab instruments, automatically parsing and structuring experimental data as it's produced, so scientists can fully leverage every experiment they run without changing how they work. 

We believe that how research data is captured and shared is just as important to scientific progress as the discoveries themselves, and we share the Genesis Mission's conviction that realizing its vision means stronger support for scientific software and better incentives for full data use.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

Labric provides each lab with a scientific agent that has access to high-context structured lab data, protocols, instrument data, and lab-specific metrics and scripts. It's customizable and supports any LLM compatible with the OpenAI API format, including Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT (with zero-day data retention options), open-source models hosted privately on our platform VPC, or a lab’s privately hosted models. 

We also help labs apply off-the-shelf AI models to their particular scientific needs, such as fine-tuning computer vision models like SAM3 and YOLO for image analysis tasks.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Labric helps labs implement domain-specific ML models by first ensuring their data is complete, well-structured, and designed for ML goals. We've designed schemas and figures of merit for many labs, and built infrastructure to accommodate a wide variety of scientific workflows.

Our founding team brings computer science degrees from Stanford, experience on Google Search, and leadership of data systems at a climatetech solar company. Our broader team is composed of top-tier software engineering talent across infrastructure, machine learning, and data systems.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Labric provisions best-practice infrastructure on Google Cloud, handling databases, app servers, object storage, vector embeddings, AI model deployments, and HPC clusters out of the box. Scientists interact with the platform like any other application, while the underlying infrastructure is fully managed. We also support cross-cloud and on-premises data ecosystems, so labs can bring their own components or have our platform integrate with their existing resources.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Labric provides end-to-end data infrastructure for research labs, connecting directly to lab instruments to automatically parse, ingest, and structure experimental data in real time. Labs get a read-only relational database they can query like any standalone database, a Python API that lets on- and off-platform jobs read from and write to their data, and the ability to link external structured datasets and databases alongside their lab-specific data using OpenAPI or SQL. The resulting data is clean, queryable, provenance-tracked, and AI-ready from the moment it’s processed.

Subject matter expertise

Our team combines deep expertise in databases, data infrastructure, and modern software design with hands-on experience working in research labs across materials science, solar energy, biotechnology, and chemical engineering. This allows us to think across disciplines in ways that are rare in scientific software. We understand the tension between lab-specific data workflows and broader interoperability, and know how to resolve it in ways that make science scalable.

Relevant Projects

1. National lab data ingestion and collaboration
Labric is currently deployed at multiple DOE national laboratories as a paid vendor. To reach this point, we completed the full federal procurement process, including SAM.gov registration, and met the security and compliance requirements of each institution. We are actively working with research teams at these labs.
At each lab, we designed custom relational database schemas tailored to their specific instruments, experiments, and data workflows. We wrote parsers for their instrument data formats so that raw output is automatically structured and loaded into the database as it's produced. We also deployed our desktop application, Labric Sync, on instrument host computers inside the labs, enabling automated real-time data collection directly from the source.
This work has established Labric as operational infrastructure within the national lab ecosystem. We are a working product already integrated into active research environments.

 

2. Automatic defect detection and classification for quality control
We built a computer vision and machine learning pipeline to automatically detect, classify, and quantify defects in high-resolution microscopy images of material samples. The dataset included thousands of .tif files with associated metadata, replacing a manual review process that was bottlenecking research progress.
We worked directly with domain scientists to define defect categories, establish labeling conventions, and build preprocessing steps that accentuated defect features on sample surfaces. We then trained models to detect, categorize, and quantify defects, ultimately producing quantitative measurements like percent area coverage per defect type, across a wide range of defect morphologies and variable image quality.
The resulting pipeline saved thousands of scientist-hours of manual analysis and enabled investigations at a scale that wasn't previously practical.


3. Scientific metric design for multiscale characterization
We automated a lengthy, manual material characterization workflow by developing algorithms and a quantitative framework. Previously, scientists visually inspected measurement curves at multiple scales, manually marked transition points and features, and combined these into an overall quality assessment by judgment. We replaced each of these steps with reproducible and validated scripts.
We worked with scientists to pinpoint the exact features they were looking for in the curves, then designed algorithms to identify those features automatically. We validated against a set of scientist-provided manual labels, iterating on approach until we achieved extremely high accuracy across a range of data quality (including noisy, discontinuous, and irregular measurements).
We then developed a combined figure-of-merit that distilled multiscale information into a single quantity useful for meta-analysis across samples and conditions, along with an error metric that flagged poor fits for scientist review.

Capabilities Seeking
Datasets or data infrastructure

We're looking to partner with labs that are interested in centralizing and structuring their experimental data, whether to streamline data acquisition, enable cross-lab collaboration, or lay the groundwork for AI in their workflows. We would also like to work with labs that are producing large or unique datasets.

Experimental or test facilities

We work well with labs that generate high volumes of instrument data and are looking for better ways to capture, organize, and use it. We would be especially excited to work with labs that have multiple facilities and labs that share instruments or data with other groups.

Subject matter expertise

We're looking for scientists who are curious about deploying AI in their research, whether that means scientific AI agents, automated analysis pipelines, or working toward closed-loop self-driving labs. Labric provides the tools and API to make this possible.

Project Idea Summary

We're looking to partner with experimental research labs and DOE national laboratories that generate significant instrument data and want to make it complete, structured, and ready for AI. We see a natural fit with the American Science Cloud: as AmSC builds the federated platform for AI-driven science, the bottleneck will increasingly be data readiness at the lab level. Labric is built to solve exactly this problem.

We provide data infrastructure that connects directly to lab instruments, automatically parsing and structuring experimental data as it's produced. Scientists don't need to change how they work. Data is organized, queryable, and ready for analysis and model training by the time it leaves the instrument.

We want to be the software partner that helps labs get their data in order. We're excited to work with teams thinking about making their data interoperable across facilities, deploying scientific AI agents, or simply getting a handle on the data they're already producing.
