Introduction

My group develops foundations and system-level methods for humanoid and legged robotics in dynamic, uncertain, and contact-rich environments. We combine mechanics, control, optimization, and learning to enable robots to move, manipulate, and interact robustly in complex real-world settings. Our recent work focuses on whole-body autonomy, contact-rich tool use, and physical human-robot interaction, with an emphasis on reliability, adaptability, and deployment in challenging operational environments.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

My research group works on generative models for robot perception and decision making required for performing physically complex tasks, including foundation models (LLM/VLM/VLA) and world models.

Experimental or test facilities

My lab houses four humanoid robots, including Agility Robotics' Digit, Unitree's G1, and Booster Robotics' T1, along with multiple quadrupedal robots and robot arms. We also have a 15-camera Vicon motion caption system that can support human demonstration data collection, along with an instrumented treadmill that can emulate dynamic, changing environments.

Subject matter expertise

My group has deep expertise in robot perception and decision making for complex embodied systems, especially humanoids. We develop multimodal sensing, state estimation, world modeling, learning, and control methods for robust whole-body manipulation and locomotion in dynamic, uncertain, and contact-rich environments.

Relevant Projects

1. Vision-Language-Action for Humanoid Loco-Manipulation in Manufacturing Environments
Our newer work focuses on VLA-enabled humanoid loco-manipulation for manufacturing and industrial environments, where robots must combine semantic understanding, whole-body mobility, manipulation, and safety-aware decision making. The scope of this effort is to develop embodied AI methods that connect high-level task understanding with low-level action generation for contact-rich industrial workflows such as reaching, handling, navigation in clutter, and coordinated manipulation near people and equipment. A key emphasis is improving action representation, multimodal perception, and modular safety guardrails so that foundation-model-enabled robots can operate more reliably in real-world settings. This effort is relevant to DOE because advanced manufacturing environments require adaptable robotic systems that can work across variable layouts, task conditions, and human-centered constraints without extensive task-specific reprogramming. The project builds on our broader expertise in humanoid autonomy, action chunking, multimodal sensing, and safety-aware embodied intelligence for real deployment.

2. Athletic AI for Humanoid Whole-Body Autonomy
Our group has developed learning-based methods for athletic humanoid robots that tightly couple perception, prediction, and whole-body control under fast, dynamic task demands. Using humanoid table tennis as a testbed, we built an end-to-end reinforcement learning framework that jointly coordinates locomotion and upper-body striking, rather than treating mobility and manipulation as separate modules. The project introduced future-state prediction and physics-based predictive rewards to improve proactive decision making and task execution. This work is relevant to DOE because it addresses a core challenge in next-generation autonomous systems: achieving fast, coordinated, contact-rich behavior in dynamic settings where the robot must make decisions under uncertainty and act with whole-body agility. Outcomes include an accepted ICRA 2026 paper, open-source release, and a transferable framework for high-speed embodied decision making in complex physical tasks.

3. Robotics in Non-Inertial and Dynamically Disturbed Environments
Our group has established a research program on perception, estimation, and control for legged and mobile robots operating in non-inertial environments, including moving and dynamically disturbed surfaces. This work addresses a critical autonomy problem for field robotics: maintaining reliable mobility and state awareness when the environment itself is not stationary. We developed provably grounded control and invariant filtering methods for quadrupeds, humanoids, and ground robots under dynamic platform motion, and we complemented theory with experimental evaluation on commercial quadruped systems. This project is relevant to DOE because many mission-relevant environments involve vibration, motion, uncertainty, and limited observability, all of which challenge standard robotics pipelines. Outcomes include multiple peer-reviewed publications in IEEE/ASME Transactions on Mechatronics and IEEE Transactions on Robotics, along with a broader systems-level understanding of robust robot operation under real-world disturbances.

Project Idea Summary

Our group is interested in partnerships around two DOE FY2026 focus areas: Advanced Robotics for Dynamic Laboratory Environments (ASCR) and AI-Enabled Diagnostics and Remote Handling (FES). We develop perception, decision making, learning, and whole-body control methods for complex robotic systems, especially humanoids, operating in dynamic, uncertain, and contact-rich environments. Our recent work spans multimodal perception, vision-language-action and foundation-model-enabled robot policies, safety guardrails, locomotion, manipulation, and robust operation under environmental disturbances.

For ASCR, we are interested in robot autonomy for dynamic laboratory and industrial environments, including multimodal perception, manipulation under uncertainty, embodied foundation models, and digital-twin-enabled training for robots that interact with instruments, materials, and complex workflows. For FES, we are interested in AI-enabled remote handling and inspection, including autonomous or shared-autonomy manipulation in hazardous, cluttered, or hard-to-access environments.

In a potential partner, we are specifically seeking DOE-relevant application insight, representative datasets, digital twins or simulation assets, compute resources for large-scale model training, and/ access to realistic test environments or facilities. We would especially value collaborators who can help identify high-impact use cases and define meaningful performance, safety, and reliability requirements.
