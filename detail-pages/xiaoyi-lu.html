Introduction

Prof. Xiaoyi Lu' Parallel and Distributed Systems Laboratory (https://padsys.org/) at UF develops next-generation AI-native infrastructure to enable scalable, autonomous scientific discovery.

We build heterogeneity-aware platforms that seamlessly integrate GPUs, DPUs, quantum computing, and emerging accelerators to support large foundation and reasoning models for science (e.g., HPC-R1), enabling efficient training, inference, and system-level optimization at scale. Our work advances AI-driven digital twins and closed-loop scientific workflows that couple simulation, real-world data, and learning systems to continuously refine models and accelerate discovery.

We are designing self-evolving scientific workflows, where AI agents and code generation techniques dynamically adapt pipelines, optimize performance, and orchestrate complex multi-stage computations. In parallel, we develop secure and resilient AI infrastructure, including differential privacy and system-level protections (e.g., DPAR), to enable trustworthy, large-scale collaborative science.

Our work aligns with the Genesis Mission vision of AI-enabled science platforms. We serve as an HPC and AI systems backbone for multidisciplinary teams across academia, national labs, and industry, enabling transformative advances in science and energy. We welcome collaborators in large reasoning models for science (e.g., HPC-R1), agentic AI designs, automated code generation, digital twins, quantum computing, and AI-driven workflows.

Relevant Projects

HCCL: Heterogeneity-Enriched Collective Communication Library (https://hccl.padsys.org/): 
This is my NSF CAREER project. We aim to develop a next-generation collective communication framework for heterogeneous systems integrating GPUs, DPUs, and multi-rail accelerators. HCCL enables scalable and efficient data movement for large-scale AI training and scientific workloads, improving performance and adaptability across diverse HPC environments. Results have been published in top-tier HPC venues and validated on modern accelerator platforms.

 

OpenDOTA: Open-Source Data Offloading and Transfer Architecture for DPUs (https://www.opendota.io/): 
Designed and implemented an open-source framework for DPU-accelerated data movement and near-data processing. OpenDOTA enables efficient orchestration of data-intensive workflows by offloading communication and data operations to SmartNICs/DPUs, significantly improving throughput and system efficiency in HPC and AI pipelines. This is my NSF-funded OAC Core project.

 

SR-APPFL: Scalable and Resilient Advanced Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning (https://sr-appfl.padsys.org/): 
Developed a scalable federated learning simulation framework integrating privacy-preserving techniques (e.g., differential privacy) with resilient data management. SR-APPFL supports large-scale, distributed AI simulation across multiple institutions, enabling secure collaboration while maintaining performance and robustness for scientific and real-world applications. This project is supported by funding from the DOE DRS program.
