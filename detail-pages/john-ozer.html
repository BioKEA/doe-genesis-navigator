Introduction

John Ugur Ozer is an Project/Programme Management, AI governance and workforce strategy executive with 20+ years of experience in financial services, risk management, and enterprise AI governance. He advises organizations on designing and scaling AI systems in regulated, high-stakes environments, with a focus on governance, model risk, and cross-institution collaboration.

Through Aeon AI Risk Management, he supports public- and private-sector initiatives in building governance frameworks for AI platforms operating across shared datasets, models, and stakeholders.

Relevant to the Genesis Mission, his work focuses on:

Governance architecture for multi-institution AI-for-science platforms

Model and data-sharing controls across organizations

Auditability, reproducibility, and risk management of AI outputs

Workforce readiness and scaling strategies for AI adoption

He is currently leading the development of U.S. AI Workforce Vulnerability and Readiness Index frameworks, integrating policy, labor data, and credentialing ecosystems to support responsible workforce transition.

Aeon AI Risk Management is positioned to contribute as a subawardee or advisory partner, providing capabilities that strengthen proposal competitiveness and long-term program scalability.

Established governance processes for AI lifecycle management, including model validation, monitoring, and auditability.

Achievements:

Implemented governance controls across a large portfolio of AI/ML use cases

Enabled alignment with emerging regulatory frameworks (e.g., NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act principles)

2. AI Risk & Model Governance Advisory (Financial Services / Consulting)

Scope of Work:

Advised multiple organizations on AI risk management, including model validation frameworks, explainability standards, and governance structures for deploying AI systems in regulated environments. Focused on integrating AI governance into existing enterprise risk frameworks.

Achievements:

Developed reusable AI governance frameworks adaptable across institutions

Improved model transparency, auditability, and risk controls

Supported organizations in operationalizing responsible AI practices

3. U.S. AI Workforce Vulnerability & Readiness Index (Ongoing – Blueprint Phase)

Scope of Work:

Leading the design of a dual-index framework assessing workforce exposure to AI-driven transformation (Vulnerability Index) and preparedness across policy, education, and industry adoption (Readiness Index). Includes methodology development, data sourcing strategy, and pilot design for U.S. states.

Achievements:

Developed structured framework integrating labor data (ONET), policy indicators, and credentialing ecosystems

Defined scalable methodology for national-level workforce assessment

Established collaboration with workforce and credentialing stakeholders

Project Idea Summary

I am seeking to contribute to Genesis Mission proposals focused on AI platform integration and Discovery Science, where multi-institution collaboration, shared datasets, and reusable models are core to the solution.
My proposed contribution is a dedicated workstream on governance, workforce architecture, and scaling design for AI-for-science platforms. This includes:
Governance frameworks for cross-institution AI systems
Model and data-sharing controls across partners
Auditability and reproducibility of AI-generated outputs
Workforce pipeline design to support long-term program sustainability
These components are critical given the Genesis Mission’s emphasis on shared infrastructure, reusable artifacts, and coordination across national labs, universities, and industry.
What I am seeking in a partner:
I am looking to collaborate with U.S.-based lead applicants (national labs, universities, or federal integrators) developing proposals in AI for scientific discovery, energy systems, or platform-level AI capabilities.
The ideal partner would be:
Leading or contributing to a Genesis Mission proposal
Focused on technical AI model, data, or infrastructure development
Looking to strengthen governance, scalability, and workforce components of their submission
I am positioned to participate as a subawardee or advisory partner, providing capabilities that enhance proposal competitiveness and long-term viability.
