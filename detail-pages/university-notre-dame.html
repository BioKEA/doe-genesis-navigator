Introduction

University of Notre Dame: AI-Enabled Science & Energy Capabilities

The University of Notre Dame is a research university with deep capabilities at the intersection of AI, high-performance computing, quantum information science, cybersecurity, and domain sciences including nuclear physics, materials, environmental systems, and fluid dynamics.

Strategic Research Domains: Notre Dame brings expertise in areas prioritized in the Genesis Mission:

AI/ML: AI-guided Materials Discovery; Automated Experimentation; Advanced Manufacturing (Digital twins and AI-driven workflows); Cross-cutting infrastructure

Quantum Information Science (QIS): Home to an on-campus quantum computing lab, a strong quantum matter center, quantum devices and quantum sensing research, and AI-driven quantum devices and nanomanufacturing labs. ND is a part of the NSF IUCRC Center for Quantum Technologies, focusing on quantum sensing and AI-driven nanomanufacturing.

Critical Materials

Cybersecurity: Expertise through the Cyber SMART IUCRC, focusing on the security of AI agents and distributed research networks.

Domain Sciences: Deep expertise in nuclear physics, fluid dynamics, materials science, and nuclear energy 

Established Partnerships: ND has established pipelines to Oak Ridge, Argonne, Sandia, Fermilab, and other National Labs.

AI models, tools, or software

AI/ML pipelines for genomics, materials discovery, and scientific computing; knowledge graph tools; LLM fine-tuning for domain science applications; conformal prediction and uncertainty quantification methods.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Faculty across CS, applied math, chemistry, physics, and engineering with expertise in physics-informed ML, reinforcement learning, generative AI, and agentic workflows; Scientific AI Initiative

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Cyberinfrastructure available through the on-premise Center for Research Computing (CRC) resources:  AI-optimized infrastructure, storage, CUI-compliant datacenter; experience with DOE-compliant data governance; support for access to cloud resources, including AWS, Azure, Google Cloud and Oracle Cloud Infrastructure.

Project Idea Summary

The University of Notre Dame brings a uniquely broad and deep portfolio to the Genesis Mission anchored by the DOE-operated Notre Dame Radiation Laboratory, the Nuclear Science Laboratory accelerator complex, the nation's top academic fluid dynamics laboratory (FlowPAC), and the CHIPS Act Silicon Crossroads Microelectronics Commons Hub. Our Scientific AI Initiative and Data, AI & Computing Initiative provide cross-cutting AI-for-science infrastructure applicable across all Genesis themes. Active EFRC partnerships with PNNL (IDREAM) and Brookhaven (MSEE) demonstrate Notre Dame's proven capacity to contribute to multi-institutional DOE center-scale research. 

We seek national laboratory partners who bring experimental facilities, proprietary operational datasets, and domain depth to complement our AI methods, HPC infrastructure, and domain science faculty. We are also interested in industry partnerships that lend the capability to augment datasets to integrate innovations, scale, and pace into Notre Dame-led projects. Notre Dame is prepared to lead submissions in microelectronics, particle and nuclear physics, and AI for scientific reasoning, and to participate as a strong academic partner in nuclear energy, fusion, materials, and manufacturing topics where national labs hold unique data and facilities.
