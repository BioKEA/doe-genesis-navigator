Introduction

Every Genesis proposal faces the same hidden problem: scientific data that has never been prepared for AI. Instrument outputs, simulation logs, experimental records are valuable, domain-rich, and almost entirely unusable for training or evaluating AI without significant curation work. That gap between raw scientific data and AI-ready assets is where most proposals quietly stall.

Mercor closes that gap. We are an AI data and evaluation company seeking to join Genesis proposals as an industry co-PI.

Independent Performance Measurement. We design benchmarks that answer the question every DOE reviewer will ask: not whether the AI ran, but whether it worked. We are not building your model — we are measuring it, which is what makes the results credible.

Autonomous Task Execution. We build agentic task environments, including our open-source APEX-Agents framework, which runs AI through long-horizon scientific workflows and produces measurable, reproducible performance evidence.

Scientific Data Structuring. We transform raw scientific data into structured assets that models can learn from, drawing on production experience curating expert-generated information into high-signal training and evaluation sets.

Expert Human Network. Mercor matches the world's top technical talent with leading AI labs. That network of vetted scientists and domain specialists is a direct asset for proposals requiring expert annotation, evaluation, or human-in-the-loop validation.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

APEX-Agents is a production AI evaluation framework: 480 structured tasks across 33 simulation environments for measuring autonomous AI performance. The tooling includes task scaffolding, ground-truth scoring, and reproducible benchmarking pipelines applicable to scientific AI workflows.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Mercor's AI/ML expertise centers on evaluation methodology for agentic systems: designing task environments, establishing ground-truth performance baselines, and measuring AI reliability across novel conditions. We have applied this to complex professional domains where AI failure carries high stakes — financial analysis, legal reasoning, strategic consulting. The same evaluation discipline applies directly to scientific AI: does the model generalize, how do you score it, and what does reliable performance actually look like at deployment scale.

Subject matter expertise

Mercor brings deep expertise in AI benchmark design and agentic system evaluation — specifically, how to measure whether AI performs reliably on complex, multi-step tasks. This methodology is directly applicable to autonomous laboratory systems, AI-driven scientific reasoning, and deployment-stage validation of AI models in high-stakes research settings.

Relevant Projects

1. APEX-Agents Benchmark (2025–2026) Mercor designed and released APEX-Agents, an open-source benchmark evaluating frontier AI agents on long-horizon professional tasks. Scope: 480 tasks across 33 simulated real-world environments spanning investment banking, corporate law, and strategic consulting — each requiring multi-step reasoning, file navigation, and cross-application execution. Key finding: frontier models complete fewer than 25% of tasks on first attempt, and fewer than 40% with 8 attempts — establishing the performance ceiling for current agentic AI. Dataset released on Hugging Face (CC-BY); infrastructure open-sourced via Archipelago. Published on arXiv (2601.14242).

2. Expert Data for Model Training (2025–2026) In collaboration with Applied Compute, Mercor demonstrated that expert-quality labeled data produces step-change improvements in AI model performance. Scope: 874 expert-labeled agentic tasks used to fine-tune a frontier model. Achievement: Pass@1 on the corporate law sub-benchmark tripled — from 4.4% to 16.3% — with under 1,000 training examples and one epoch. The fine-tuned model achieved first place on the APEX corporate law leaderboard. Demonstrated that domain-expert-generated task data is the binding constraint on AI performance in high-complexity settings.

3. APEX-SWE (March 2026, co-developed with Cognition) Mercor co-developed APEX-SWE, a software engineering benchmark measuring real production AI capability — shipping systems, diagnosing failures, implementing fixes — rather than coding puzzles. Scope: 200 tasks across integration challenges (live containerized services, AWS emulators, CRM/ticketing systems) and observability challenges (Grafana, Prometheus, real open-source bug diagnosis). Top score at launch: 41.5% Pass@1 (GPT-5.3 Codex), revealing significant gaps between benchmark-optimized and production-capable AI. Open-source dev set on Hugging Face; full leaderboard held-out.

4. Frontier AI Lab Work - Mercor is the preferred data partner for frontier models to help them evaluate and improve their capabilities. We cannot speak about specific client projects, but we are trusted across the five major AI labs, neo labs, and more.

Capabilities Seeking
Datasets or data infrastructure

We are seeking partners with access to domain-specific scientific datasets (e.g. experimental results, simulation outputs, or instrument-generated data) that can serve as ground truth for AI performance evaluation. Mercor designs evaluation tasks against real data. The richer and more domain-specific the dataset, the more rigorous and credible the benchmark we can build. Partners with existing DOE data assets or national lab data access are especially valuable.

Experimental or test facilities

We are seeking partners with access to experimental workflows where AI is making or informing decisions: autonomous labs, synthesis pipelines, characterization instruments, or simulation environments. These are the settings where AI performance evaluation is most critical and most difficult to design well. Mercor's APEX-Agents framework was built for exactly this structure: controlled task environments with ground-truth scoring. We want to apply that to real scientific workflows.

Subject matter expertise

We are seeking domain scientists with active research programs in AI-accelerated discovery: chemistry, materials science, nuclear engineering, advanced manufacturing, or autonomous experimentation. The ideal partner is a PI or research group that has identified a Genesis challenge area, understands the scientific problem deeply, and needs an industry co-PI to contribute AI evaluation methodology and deployment-stage validation. We bring the benchmarking infrastructure; you bring the science.

Project Idea Summary

Mercor is seeking national lab and university research teams to join as the industry co-PI on a Genesis Mission Phase I proposal. We bring AI evaluation infrastructure (APEX-Agents: 480 tasks, 33 simulation environments) and the methodology to measure whether AI systems perform reliably on complex scientific workflows. We are the independent validation layer that strengthens any proposal's credibility with DOE reviewers.

We are prioritizing proposals in: AI-Driven Autonomous Laboratories, AI for Scientific Reasoning, and HPC Code Curation. The ideal partner has strong domain science, an active or forming proposal, and needs a credible industry co-PI who can contribute evaluation design and deployment-stage performance validation, not just a logo.
