Introduction

SeguEN Technologies provides a multidisciplinary approach to subsurface energy challenges, combining over two decades of reservoir engineering expertise with advanced Data Science and Physics-Informed Machine Learning (PINNs). Our team specializes in bridging the gap between traditional engineering/geoscience workflows and modern AI to accelerate the development of critical mineral resources. Our leadership has an established track record of working with multiple operators in oil and gas, mining and lithium brine, delivering advanced physics-informed optimizations for prediction of production.  

Core Capabilities:

Physics-Informed & Geochemical ML: Expert application of Machine Learning models validated against rigorous geochemical simulations (PHREEQC) to predict mineral behavior and scaling indices using USGS data.

Reservoir Characterization & Engineering: We have pioneered the transition from traditional petroleum engineering to the critical mineral sector, applying proven subsurface methodologies to accelerate the evaluation and extraction of resources like lithium brine.

Predictive Analytics & Spatial Mapping: Proficiency in developing AI models with high predictive accuracy for rapid evaluation of geolocations and identification of regional trends with high production potential.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

SeguEN offers a proprietary framework integrating Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and geochemical and reservoir modeling to accelerate critical mineral recovery. Unlike black-box AI, our models honor fundamental laws to predict reservoir behavior using geospatial and open-source data. We provide high-fidelity productivity scenarios that bypass lengthy characterization. Our tools transform raw field data into actionable geospatial scaling and resource and productivity scenarios. 

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our expertise lies in hybrid modeling: merging classical engineering and geological workflows with AI to solve subsurface challenges. We utilize PINNs to ensure AI outputs remain physically consistent with reservoir laws. Our team excels in processing heterogeneous USGS/operator datasets through rigorous EDA, outlier detection, and geospatial risk mapping. This approach provides a scalable platform for evaluating critical mineral availability and optimizing recovery.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: Physics-Informed ML for Permian Basin Scaling Prediction (2024–2025)

Scope of Work: Developed an integrated geochemical and machine learning workflow to predict mineral scaling potential (calcite) in Permian Basin produced waters. The project involved conducting over 7,200 PHREEQC simulations to train a machine learning model capable of estimating saturation indices using field data.

Achievements: Successfully validated a predictive model achieving high accuracy and effectively replicating complex geochemical software capabilities with high thermodynamic accuracy. This work enables operators to perform rapid, data-driven assessments of scaling risks to optimize chemical treatment programs and flow assurance.

Project 2: Reservoir Characterization & Modeling for Mining and Lithium Brine (2024–Present)

Scope of Work: Directed the characterization and multi-phase reservoir simulation of U.S. lithium brine and mining resources. The scope included developing automated data pipelines and web-based applications to integrate real-time subsurface sensing data for resource evaluation.

Achievements: Delivered innovative Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and machine learning applications to predict critical element production and optimize product leaching and co-extraction processes. This project pioneered the use of a "Cognitive Mine" architecture to replace static updates with dynamic, AI-driven closed-loop control systems.

Project 3: Unconventional Asset Optimization & Digital Transformation (2019–2023)

Scope of Work: Served as a Reservoir Engineering Advisor leading the evaluation and adoption of cloud-based digital solutions for most prolific unconventional basins onshore US and Canada. The project focused on integrating data analytics with traditional 3D modeling to improve well performance predictions.

Achievements: Delivered a physics-constrained machine learning model that improved the predictivity of legacy reservoir models. Additionally, spearheaded the evaluation and adoption of cloud-based solutions and automated production reporting workflows.

Project Idea Summary

Our project focuses on accelerating the domestic supply of critical minerals by integrating geospatial AI with 3D numerical modeling. We aim to create a predictive framework that identifies high-value extraction zones for lithium from unconventional subsurface sources, such as oil and gas brines. By leveraging agentic AI workflows, we will transform static geological datasets into dynamic models that predict fluid transport and mineralization behavior under extreme uncertainty.
