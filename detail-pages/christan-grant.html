Introduction

Christan Grant is an Arnold and Lisa Goldberg Rising Star Associate Professor of Computer Science at the University of Florida, where he directs the UF Data Studio. His research develops agentic AI systems, scalable data pipelines, and human-over-the-loop frameworks that accelerate scientific discovery.                                                                                                                                                                                    

Dr. Grant's group builds end-to-end computational pipelines spanning data acquisition, labeling, interactive machine learning, and visualization. These pipelines target domains where conventional tools fail on shifting distributions, multimodal measurements, and non-standard inputs like accented speech and non-alphabetic writing systems. His group designs agentic AI workflows pairing LLM-powered assistants with human researchers who retain authority to steer, audit, and correct each stage of analysis. This human-over-the-loop paradigm keeps domain experts embedded in AI decision processes, preserving scientific rigor as automation scales. Current projects include LLM-driven scientific assistants for chemical discovery, novel visualizations for anomalies, and interactive tools for trustworthy AI deployment.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

Grant specializes in building AI-driven data pipelines and agentic systems that operate where standard tools fail. His expertise spans fairness-aware machine learning under distribution shift, large language model evaluation, audio language models for diverse speech, and interactive visual analytics for hidden statistical phenomena. He develops human-over-the-loop frameworks that embed domain experts into automated ML workflows, enabling real-time steering and auditing of AI agents.

Relevant Projects

**1. NSF FAI: Fairness-Aware Deep Learning on Data Streams (2022--2025, $628K)**

Scope: This multi-institution project with UT Dallas and University of Arkansas developed robust ML frameworks that maintain fairness guarantees under distributional shift. The UF Data Studio led the design of adaptive online learning pipelines that detect covariate and dependence shifts simultaneously, then recalibrate models to restore accuracy and equity constraints without full retraining. The pipeline ingests streaming data, monitors drift, triggers fairness-aware recalibration, and surfaces diagnostics through interactive dashboards.

Achievements: Six peer-reviewed publications at top venues including ACM KDD (2023, 2024), IEEE ICDE (2025), and ACL (2025). The KDD 2024 paper introduced the first framework addressing covariate and dependence shifts jointly. The ACL 2025 paper extended fairness-aware methods to in-context learning with LLMs. Three PhD students received training in scalable ML pipeline development.

**2. Colgate-Palmolive: ML Prediction for Product Formulation (2024--2025, $73K)**

Scope: As sole PI, Grant built ML prediction pipelines for body wash foaming behavior. The project required an end-to-end data pipeline spanning raw experimental measurements, chemical feature engineering, model training with limited labeled samples, and interactive result exploration. Domain scientists retained steering authority over each pipeline stage, validating intermediate outputs before advancing to prediction, embodying a human-over-the-loop workflow for scientific R&D.

Achievements: Delivered deployable predictive models that reduced the experimental iteration cycle for new formulations. Established a reusable pipeline architecture pairing agentic model selection with expert oversight. The partnership catalyzed a pivot toward LLM-powered scientific assistants for materials discovery, extending the framework to multimodal laboratory data and natural language queries over experimental records.

**3. Interactive and Agentic AI Systems for Scientific Analysis (2023--2026)**

Scope: The UF Data Studio developed open-source tools embedding human researchers into AI-driven analytical workflows. InsightBoard provides real-time multi-metric fairness monitoring during model training. Wiggum enables interactive visual detection of Simpson's Paradox across subpopulations. OPTiCAL benchmarks vision-language model reasoning to identify failure modes before deployment. Each tool operationalizes a human-over-the-loop paradigm where agentic components flag anomalies and present structured diagnostics for researcher adjudication.

Achievements: InsightBoard (FLAIRS 2026) and Wiggum (Information Visualization 2026) are pip-installable packages for external adoption. OPTiCAL (NeurIPS Workshop 2025) revealed systematic positional reasoning failures in frontier VLMs. The ASRU 2025 Best Paper Award recognized the lab's audio LM evaluation pipeline. These projects demonstrate a repeatable methodology: agentic pipelines surface hidden phenomena, then interactive interfaces let domain experts validate and act on AI-generated insights.
