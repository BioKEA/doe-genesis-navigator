Introduction

Verde Technologies is a leading perovskite solar startup focused on rapid development and scale-up of next-generation solar panel technologies. We are the grand prize winner of the DOE Perovskite Startup Prize, a commercial partner on the ADDEPT Center (a DOE-funded hub accelerating the development and deployment of perovskite photovoltaics using ai), giving us direct ties to the nation's leading perovskite research infrastructure.

Our core differentiator is a proprietary manufacturing process and hardware architecture proven to be uniquely compatible with robotic, automated, and AI-driven production systems. Where conventional solar manufacturers require months to iterate across scales, Verde compresses lab-scale, pilot-scale, and full-scale manufacturing trials into weeks — creating the kind of rapid, closed-loop iteration environment that AI process optimization requires to be meaningful.

This infrastructure positions Verde as a natural industry anchor for Genesis Mission teams targeting AI-accelerated energy material manufacturing. We provide what lab and academic partners cannot: a commercially validated, AI-ready manufacturing testbed and pilot manufacturing facility with a direct path to high-volume production and significant cost reduction for next-generation solar.

We are seeking National Lab and university partners to co-develop AI-driven digital twins, agentic process control systems, and predictive models for perovskite manufacturing scale-up.

Relevant Projects

ADDEPT & the Verde Slot Coating System

Verde Technologies is an industry partner on ADDEPT (Accelerated Co-Design of Durable, Reproducible, and Efficient Perovskite Tandems), a $11.25M DOE SETO-funded research center led by MIT and including UC San Diego, Princeton, and CubicPV. ADDEPT's core mission is to break the reproducibility and scale-up bottleneck in perovskite solar manufacturing by deploying machine learning and robotic screening to co-optimize efficiency, durability, and manufacturability simultaneously — rather than treating them as sequential problems.

Verde's contribution goes beyond advisory. As part of the center's industry-to-lab technology transfer mandate, Verde brought its proprietary Verde Slot Coating tool — a simplified, iteration-optimized deposition system inspired by slot-die coating but purpose-built for rapid process development — into the ADDEPT ecosystem. Working closely with MIT, UC San Diego, and the broader ADDEPT team, Verde and its partners then co-developed the Auto-Verde Slot: a fully robotic implementation of the Verde Slot Coating system designed to interface natively with the center's AI-driven experimental workflows and closed-loop optimization pipelines.

The Auto-Verde Slot represents a rare example of industry-grade perovskite deposition hardware that was co-designed with automation and machine learning in mind from the ground up — not retrofitted afterward. This work is a direct proof point of Verde's core thesis: that scalable perovskite manufacturing and AI-driven process optimization must be built together, and that Verde's tooling is uniquely positioned to serve as the hardware backbone for that effort.

Project Idea Summary

Verde Technologies – Genesis Mission Proposed Project

Verde Technologies is proposing an AI-accelerated manufacturing initiative targeting one of the most critical bottlenecks in next-generation solar deployment: the gap between laboratory-scale perovskite process development and high-volume, commercially viable production.

Working with a consortium of leading national lab, university, and AI technology partners, Verde will serve as the industry anchor providing real manufacturing environments, proprietary process data, and AI-compatible deposition hardware — including the Auto-Verde Slot robotic coating platform developed under the DOE-funded ADDEPT center.

The project will develop and deploy AI-driven digital twins, closed-loop process control systems, and agentic optimization frameworks trained on real manufacturing data across multiple production scales. By integrating Verde's demonstrated ability to compress lab-to-full-scale manufacturing trials into weeks rather than months, the consortium will create a validated, deployable AI workflow for perovskite energy material manufacturing with a direct path to commercialization and significant cost reduction.

The project directly advances the Genesis Mission's goal of achieving genuine AI advantage in advanced energy material production — not as a simulation exercise, but grounded in active commercial manufacturing with measurable scale-up milestones.
