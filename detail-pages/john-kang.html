Introduction

Dr. John Kang is an Assistant Professor of Mechanical Engineering at San Diego State University (SDSU) and Director of the Smart Manufacturing and Metrology Lab. His research focuses on AI-enabled sensing, advanced manufacturing, and physics-informed machine learning, with a particular emphasis on real-time monitoring and control of laser powder bed fusion (LPBF) processes for defect detection and quality assurance.

San Diego State University is an R1 research university, a Hispanic-Serving Institution (HSI), and one of the largest public universities in California, with over 40,000 students. SDSU is committed to excellence in research, education, and community impact. At SDSU, Dr. Kang leads interdisciplinary efforts that integrate multi-modal sensor systems, data-driven modeling, and digital twin technologies. His work aligns closely with the Genesis Mission by advancing intelligent, resilient, and cost-effective solutions for manufacturing, infrastructure monitoring, and safety-critical systems.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

(1) Controllable data augmentation and training/data generation software; (2) multi-modal sensor fusion AI architecture (pending patent) with integrated training and inference capabilities; and (3) sensor data–driven AI software for training, inference, and comparative evaluation across various machine learning models, including domain-informed feature extraction and transformation functions.

AI/ML/Data science expertise

AI/ML expertise in sensor-based detection and prediction for additive manufacturing, machining processes, water and gas pipeline systems, and wearable EMG/EEG sensing applications.

Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Various NVIDIA GPU–based workstations for high-performance computing, enabling efficient AI/ML model training and real-time inference.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Comprehensive multi-modal, time-synchronized sensor datasets for laser powder bed fusion (LPBF) processes under diverse operating conditions. The datasets include three on-axis pyrometers, a coaxial camera, an off-axis camera, acoustic emission (AE) sensors, and vibration sensors, enabling detailed analysis, sensor fusion, and AI-driven monitoring and control.

Experimental or test facilities

A central capability is the Aconity Mini LPBF system, a research-grade metal additive manufacturing platform that enables flexible process control and in-situ experimentation. The system is instrumented with synchronized multi-modal sensors, including coaxial and off-axis cameras, multiple on-axis pyrometers, acoustic emission (AE), and vibration sensors, enabling high-fidelity, real-time monitoring of melt pool dynamics, defect formation, and process stability.

Project Idea Summary

This project aims to develop an integrated AI framework for pre-process control and real-time monitoring of laser powder bed fusion (LPBF) to minimize defects and achieve targeted microstructures. The approach integrates AI-based multi-modal sensor fusion (e.g., optical, thermal, acoustic, and vibration data) informed by domain-informed features to enable robust prediction and control across varying process conditions. A key focus is improving model generalization and reliability by incorporating fundamental process physics into AI models and leveraging controllable data augmentation.

The primary challenge area is achieving consistent, defect-free (zero-defect) manufacturing with predictable microstructures in LPBF, which remains limited by process variability, insufficient real-time control, and lack of transferable AI models across different machines and environments.

To address this, we seek partners with:
(1) high-resolution X-ray CT capabilities for microstructural and porosity validation;
(2) expertise in LPBF process physics and simulation for physics-informed model development;
(3) experience in real-time monitoring and closed-loop control of LPBF systems; and
(4) LPBF platforms with in-situ sensing to enable cross-validation and generalization of AI models across different machines.
