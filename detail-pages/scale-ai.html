Introduction

As the U.S. mobilizes to double American R&D productivity over the next decade, the greatest bottleneck isn't AI algorithms—it's preparing complex scientific data for AI consumption. Scale AI is the premier teaming partner to bridge the gap between frontier AI models and the unique scientific data of the national laboratories.

By partnering with Scale, academic and industry consortia gain access to the production-proven infrastructure that currently powers the Department of War's most critical AI initiatives. We bring essential capabilities to complete your Genesis Lighthouse proposals:

AI-Ready Data Curation: We transform raw, multimodal scientific data into machine-readable assets ready for model ingestion across all 26 Lighthouse challenges.

Agentic Scientific Workflows: We deploy intelligent agents that orchestrate research tasks, shifting scientists from manual data fusion "in-the-loop" to high-level "on-the-loop" oversight. We proven out this capability as a Prime contractor for multi-AI-agent systems on a variety of Government networks.

Rigorous Test & Evaluation (T&E): Our Safety, Evaluation, and Alignment Lab (SEAL) develops domain-specific benchmarks to guarantee models adhere to scientific principles and strict safety constraints. Our team is specialized in CBRN evaluations and model knowledge.

Secure, High-Side Deployment: We provide mature AI scaffolding deployed at scale on classified networks.

Partnership Request & Project Idea

Capabilities Seeking
Computing infrastructure or cloud resources

Scale seeks to integrate our software platform (SGP) with DOE High-Performance Computing (HPC) environments. We are looking for partners who can provide the compute-scale necessary to perform large-scale fine-tuning and rigorous evaluation of models using Lab-resident datasets.

Datasets or data infrastructure

Scale seeks access to high-volume, unstructured, or 'dark' scientific datasets (currently at DRL 1 or 2) including instrument logs, historical PDF records, and raw simulation outputs. Our goal is to partner with data owners to apply the Scale Data Engine to curate these into AI-ready (DRL 5) assets for training domain-specific foundation models.

Experimental or test facilities

To demonstrate a quantitative 'AI Advantage' during Phase I, we seek partners with physical testbeds (e.g., Autonomous Labs, Manufacturing Demonstration Facilities, or Test Reactors). We aim to deploy 'Scientific Agents' that can autonomously suggest experimental parameters or analyze results in real-time within these facilities.

Subject matter expertise

We are looking for domain experts (e.g., nuclear physicists, material scientists, or biologists) to serve as 'Human-in-the-Loop' evaluators. These SMEs will help define the 'Golden Test Sets' and provide expert feedback to fine-tune Scale’s agentic workflows, ensuring the AI’s outputs are scientifically rigorous and aligned with mission objectives.

Project Idea Summary

Challenge Focus Area: Primary: AI for Advanced Manufacturing (Topic 1) or Nuclear Energy (Topic 4) Cross-Cutting: AI-Ready Data and Evaluation Infrastructure

Project Summary: We propose to bridge the "Data-to-Discovery" gap by implementing a Data Readiness Level (DRL) framework. We specialize in transforming "dark data" including unstructured instrument logs, legacy records, and raw simulation outputs into AI-ready (DRL 5) assets. For Phase I, we aim to demonstrate a 5x reduction in data-to-insight time by deploying Agentic Scientific Workflows that automate hypothesis generation and experimental optimization. By integrating our Scale GenAI Platform (SGP) with Lab-resident HPC (e.g., ORNL’s Lux cluster), we enable secure RLHF and rigorous T&E using our SEAL Leaderboards to quantify the "AI Advantage" required for the Genesis Mission.

Partnering Requirements: We seek DOE National Lab and Academic partners to complete a Phase I multi-institutional team:

Data Owners (Labs): Partners with mission-relevant datasets in Manufacturing or Nuclear Science requiring curation for foundation model training.

Subject Matter Experts (Labs/Academia): PhD-level experts to serve as "Human-in-the-Loop" evaluators to validate the scientific reasoning of our agents.

Facility Leads (Labs): Access to testbeds (e.g., MDF or OPAL) to validate AI-driven experimental suggestions against physical outcomes.

Researchers (Academia): Partners specializing in physics-informed ML or novel evaluation metrics.
