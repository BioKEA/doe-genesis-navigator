    Introduction

    Petronela Radu is the Leland J. and Dorothy H. Olson Professor and Chair of the Department of Mathematics at University of Nebraska-Lincoln. She received her PhD in Applied Mathematics from Carnegie Mellon University.  Her research in applied mathematics (PDEs, nonlocal models, dynamics, machine learning) has been supported by several awards from NSF, Department of Energy, the Fulbright and Simons Foundations. In 2025 she was selected as a Fellow in the National Science Foundation-funded Advancing Research Impact in Society (ARIS) program. She has developed the NSF-supported interdisciplinary course Math in the City, which provides students with a hands-on learning experience by creating and analyzing models using real data provided by local businesses, research centers, or government organizations. She has led the design of 5 new options for math majors, and co-designed a new major in Data Science. She has mentored dozens of undergraduate students (most recently through the Polymath Jr. program), has advised 9 PhD students, 3 postdocs. She has delivered numerous research presentations as invited or plenary speaker at national and international meetings.
