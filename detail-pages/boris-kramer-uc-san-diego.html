Introduction

I am a computational mathematician (and now in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at UCSD) with broad interests in computational methods and numerical analysis for data-driven modeling, scientific and structure-preserving machine learning, model discovery, and optimal control. Our group leverages those methods in multifidelity optimization, design and uncertainty quantification of complex and large-scale systems. 

We have worked on a variety of applications, e.g., digital twins, combustion, space weather, plasmas, soft robotics, metal additive manufacturing, aircraft design optimization, semiconductor manufacturing, radiation-hardened electronics, and metamaterials.

Relevant Projects

DOE-NNSA PSAAP IV Program:  Co-PI, Center for Advancing the Radiation Resilience of Electronics, 09/2025-09/2030.

- Overall Objectives: The overall aim is to create a validated, multi-scale, multiphysics modeling and simulation capability to predict and assess the radiation resiliency of electronics. UCSD's objective is research impact on computational methods for uncertainty quantification and scientific machine learning of complex and large-scale systems, as well as high-performance computing, particularly optimizing, scaling, and analyzing performance of scientific applications on HPC machines available as a part of exascale computing.

 

DOE-NNSA PSAAP IV Program: Co-PI, Center for Simulation & Design of Heterogeneous Architectures for Performance and Energy Absorption, 09/2025-09/2030

- Overall Objectives: The goal of the proposed Focused Investigatory Center (FIC) is to develop a highly parallelized design optimization capability that will leverage exascale computing to enable the design of additively manufactured (AM) mesostructured materials for impact and shock loading at high strain rates. Our group will apply state-of-the-art multifidelity UQ methods to the RKPM solver and validate experiments with experimental data. Moreover, we will develop reduced-order models for the RKPM architecture.

 

DARPA, Co-PI, STORMLAP: Strong Turbulence and Rogue-Wave Modeling using Machine-Learning Assisted Predictions, 03/2025-03/2026.

- Overall Objectives: We aim to develop a novel tabletop simulator for investigating highly turbulent capillary wave fluid systems using high-speed digital holographic microscopy (HSDHM). The simulator will generate a large and affordable* high-resolution data set to train machine learning (ML) models for predictive modeling of turbulent phenomena, directly addressing challenges in fluid dynamics relevant to the Department of Defense.

 

DARPA, Co-PI, Predictive Real-Time Intelligence for Metallic Endurance (PRIME), 02/2025-02/2027

- Overall Objectives: The PRIME project aims to develop a physics-based digital twin interface for uncertainty-quantified lifetime prediction of parts produced using laser powder bed fusion additive manufacturing. The multidisciplinary team will collaborate on innovative developments, including multi-sensor data fusion for real-time defect detection, accelerated physics modeling using Kalman state estimation and machine learning, digital twins capturing microstructures at scales not currently possible, and comprehensive fatigue life assessment combining microstructure-based life models with multi-fidelity uncertainty quantification.

 

Air Force Oﬃce of Scientific Research, Co-PI, MURI: Mathematics of Digital Twins, Co-PI, 09/2024-10/2029.

Overall Objectives: To develop novel mathematical techniques, specifically nonlinear balanced truncation model reduction, infused with data, for a digital twin of metal-additive manufacturing.
