Introduction

We are a team of faculty members at Johns Hopkins University and our research interests broadly span across robotics, artificial intelligence, and materials science. Our team (Abhishek Cauligi, Anand Bhattad, Greg Hager, Krishna Jatavabhulla, Homanga Bharadwaj, KT Ramesh, Joe Moore) is interested in leveraging our expertise in materials science and robotics for advancing AI-driven autonomous laboratories.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI/ML/Data science expertise

Our research group offers expertise on the application of machine learning for embodied AI systems, in particular for applications with robotic systems that must operate under significant environmental and system uncertainty. Our research group and proposal team has a strong track record of rapidly iterating to develop, test, and validate new algorithmic capabilities and closing-the-loop with real hardware for data generation and AI deployment pipelines.

Experimental or test facilities

We plan to leverage our existing Artificial Intelligence for Materials Design Laboratory (AIMD-L), a closed-loop facility that combines artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data handling with robotic automation, cutting-edge testing and characterization equipment, and unparalleled human expertise.

Subject matter expertise

Our proposal team is comprised of robotics experts with significant industry, national research lab, and academic experience. We supervise graduate students who are engaging in cutting edge research at the forefront of embodied AI and mobile robotics. In particular, we have extensive expertise with robot planning and control using tools such as computer vision, reinforcement learning, imitation learning, among others.

We are interested in engaging DoE subject matter experts to more closely align our algorithmic expertise and capabilities in robotic systems with the needs of the DoE for advancing embodied AI capabilities for national labs.

Project Idea Summary

Our aim is to develop cutting edge capabilities for robots to operate in unstructured and uncertain environments. In particular, our focus is on robotic platforms that are capable of interacting with and modifying their surrounding environments to carry out laboratory tasks and scientific experiments. To accomplish this, our research team plans to leverage its expertise in algorithmic robotics using its core skillsets of machine learning, computer vision, and control for developing new planning, control, and dexterous manipulation algorithms. We aim to partner with the DoE stakeholders and industry to develop new robotic capabilities for use in dynamic laboratory environments.
