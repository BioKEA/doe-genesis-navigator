Introduction

The Acceleration Consortium is a global coalition of academia, industry, and government, led by the University of Toronto, that aims to transform how scientific discovery is conducted using AI and automation. Its core mission is to reduce the time and cost required to discover and develop new materials and molecules by designing, building, and using self-driving labs (SDLs). SDLs accelerate scientific discovery by integrating AI with automated robotic experimentation. The Acceleration Consortium's goals include:

1) advancing AI and automation for scientific discovery;

2) design and development of modular, flexible and low-cost SDLs;

3) training students and researchers to leverage SDLs for accelerated scientific discovery;

4) using SDLs to optimize or discover molecules for diverse applications, from clean energy, sustainable consumer products, green infrastructure, and human health; and

5) working to ensure that SDL accelerated discovery is done in responsible and ethical ways that promote broad, durable benefits while minimizing direct and indirect harms.

Offerings & Experience

Offerings
AI models, tools, or software

The Acceleration Consortium is developing AI tools and agents to accelerate scientific discovery, with a focus on predicting properties of materials and molecules. 

AI/ML/Data science expertise

The Acceleration Consortium has AI and data science expertise in computational chemistry, materials, molecular biology, quantum systems, and experimental design. This includes over 30 Staff Scientists and over 90 affiliated faculty members.

Datasets or data infrastructure

The AC develops and openly disseminates materials, chemistry, and life sciences datasets and is interested in partnering with collaborators to develop large experimental and computational datasets for materials discovery.

Experimental or test facilities

7 self-driving lab core facilities (open user facilities), and over 30 SDLs in faculty labs

Inorganic: Catalysis, energy storage, structural, (opto)electronics

Organic: Electronics, photonics, energy, and green products

Medchem: Chemical probe development and hit-to-lead for drug discovery

Polymers: Biodegradable polymers, sustainable materials, health, and energy storage

Formulations: Consumer and drug delivery

Human organ mimicry: AI-driven toxicity testing and therapeutics development

Scale-up: Drugs, agrochemicals, decarbonization

Subject matter expertise

The Acceleration Consortium is highly interdisciplinary with over 90 Full Faculty Members and 30 Staff Scientists spanning expertise in AI, quantum chemistry, lab orchestration, materials science, chemistry, physics, biology, and more.
